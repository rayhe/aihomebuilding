# Research: AI Floor Plan Generators — Residential Buildability Gap
**Journalist:** Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)
**Article #:** 357
**Date:** June 12, 2026

## Angle
AI floor plan generators can produce layouts in seconds. The gap between "generated a layout" and "this layout passes code review and can actually be constructed" is the story. Cross-reference what these tools actually output against IRC code requirements to calculate the percentage of the design-to-permit process they cover.

## Key Sources

### 1. Illustrarch Review of 8 AI Floor Plan Tools (2026)
- **Tools reviewed:** Maket.ai (1M+ users, $20/mo, residential focus, JPEG/DXF export), TestFit ($100/mo, multifamily), ArkDesign.ai, Finch3D, ARCHITEChTURES, others
- **Key finding:** "Spatial logic errors are common — kitchens placed far from dining areas, bedrooms adjacent to mechanical rooms"
- **Export formats:** Most output JPEG or PNG; some DXF. None produce construction-ready CAD files.

### 2. Architizer/Chaos Survey + Automation in Construction Systematic Review (2025)
- 46% of 1,227 architecture professionals use AI tools
- 68.94% of AI usage in architecture occurs during early design phases
- Automation in Construction 2025 systematic review, 161 papers analyzed

### 3. City of Surrey, BC / Archistar
- "80% of residential permit applications contained significant zoning deficiencies, requiring average 1.6 resubmissions"
- This is the baseline HUMAN error rate — AI tools don't address zoning at all

### 4. RoomSketcher ChatGPT Floor Plan Test
- ChatGPT can generate simple text-based layouts
- "Struggles with accuracy and consistency" — changes break layouts
- Not a serious design tool, but shows the demand signal

### 5. JAIST Research (Frontiers of Architectural Research, March 2026)
- Text-to-image models fail on precise spatial requirements like floor counts
- Achieved only 70.5% accuracy in vertical configuration
- Even basic spatial compliance is unreliable

### 6. William Cohen, AIA (LinkedIn)
- "AI is the easiest 15% of the process. The other 85% — zoning, structure, MEP coordination, permits — is where projects succeed or fail"

### 7. LA County eCheck AI Pilot (Archistar)
- AI zoning review for fire-damaged R-1 homes in Altadena/Sunset Mesa
- Government-side AI for plan review, not design generation

### 8. Blitz Permits / CityView in Naples, FL
- AI plan review trained on Florida Building Code
- Municipality-side automation, not designer tools

### 9. ServiceTitan 2026 Report
- Only 25% of residential contractors use AI meaningfully
- Nearly half lack trust in AI

### 10. CodeComply.AI
- Supports IBC, IRC, ADA, FHA, NFPA compliance checks
- Compliance-checking tool, not floor plan generation

## Original Contribution
Cross-reference the outputs of AI floor plan generators against IRC 2021 Chapter 3 (Building Planning) requirements:
- R301 (design criteria), R302 (fire-resistant construction), R303 (light/ventilation), R304 (minimum room areas), R305 (ceiling heights), R310 (emergency escape), R311 (means of egress), R312 (guards), R313 (sprinklers)
- Count how many of these code sections the AI tools address → calculate the "coverage gap"
- Combine with Cohen's 15% framing and the 80% human permit deficiency rate

# Research: AI Satellite/Aerial Detection of Unpermitted Construction

## Primary Sources

### EagleView Sketch Inspect — Medina County, OH
- 9,000+ major mismatches or missing sketches discovered
- $40 million in newly identified taxable value
- $14 million in recovered property tax revenue
- Uses ML + geospatial analytics to compare assessor sketches against aerial imagery
- Automated classification: green (match), orange (minor), red (major mismatch), purple (missing)
- Source: eagleview.com blog, "AI for Accuracy: How Sketch Verification Is Helping Counties Recover Lost Tax Revenue"

### EagleView Sketch Inspect — Guernsey County, OH
- First day: 3 users reviewed 31 buildings
- $3,154,200 increase in appraised value
- $50,614 in additional tax revenue
- Estimated $3,000 per hour per reviewer in additional tax revenue
- Source: eagleview.com webinar

### EagleView ChangeFinder — Fairfax County, VA
- First county in the nation to implement ChangeFinder
- Residential appraisers use it daily
- Overcomes access issues: gated communities, locked backyards
- Allows property record updates without field inspection in many cases
- Source: eagleview.com case study

### Prince George's County, MD (DPIE)
- Drones + aerial inspection program started August 2021
- 230+ problem sites identified
- Drones: 50 mph, 400 ft altitude, quarter-mile range
- Inspector quote: "If it is a case involving unpermitted construction, the illegal construction can be completed, the house sold to an unsuspecting home buyer and the person responsible for the illegal construction will have moved away."
- Before aerial: inspectors limited to PGAtlas.com (not updated regularly), couldn't enter "no trespassing" properties for up to 18 months
- Source: content.govdelivery.com DPIE newsletter Oct 2021

### ESA InCubed — Illegal Construction Detector
- ML algorithms on satellite data to detect unpermitted construction
- Focus: swimming pools, extensions on private properties
- Cross-references satellite detections with building registry data
- Target: municipalities in Spain (primary)
- Source: incubed.esa.int

### Nearmap GeoAI
- 130+ semantic AI layers
- 7B+ AI insights generated in 2025
- 100PB imagery archive
- Change detection between survey captures (2-4x/year in metro areas)
- SEMCOG (SE Michigan): identified 3,425 unknown buildings using Nearmap AI
- Source: nearmap.com

### Deckard Technologies — PropertyInsight
- Cross-references Zillow, MLS, Airbnb/VRBO, permits, government records
- 400+ jurisdictions worldwide
- PropertyInsight forensic data platform: identifies unpaid property taxes from improvements done without permits
- AI-powered "Cyborg" engine
- Source: businesswire.com, govtech.com

### California Law — New Construction Reassessment
- Since July 1, 1983: new construction triggers reassessment
- "Any substantial addition to real property (e.g., adding a new room, pool, or garage)"
- Results in supplemental tax bills
- Source: California State Board of Equalization, Contra Costa County

## Original Analysis
- Cross-referencing: EagleView's 9,000+ mismatches in ONE Ohio county (Medina, pop ~182K) vs. 3,143 counties in the US — potential scale of the unpermitted construction gap
- Cost comparison: typical pool permit $500-$1,500 vs. fines for unpermitted work $1,000-$10,000+
- The enforcement asymmetry: before AI, detection was complaint-driven or accidental (during sale). AI inverts this — detection becomes systematic.
- Privacy tension: AI can see into backyards, over fences, through gated communities — areas previously requiring 18-month legal process to access

# Research Notes: Data Center Wage Gravity & Residential Construction Exodus

## Thesis
The AI data center construction boom is creating a wage gravity well that siphons skilled trades workers away from residential housing. The technology that promises to fix America's housing crisis is making it worse.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Randstad USA — 150M+ Job Postings Analysis (2022-2026)
- Source: https://news.constructconnect.com/ai-buildout-is-intensifying-the-skilled-trades-squeeze-says-randstad-usa-survey (April 17, 2026)

- Robotics technicians: vacancies up 113.19%
- HVAC engineers: demand up 77.89%
- Industrial automation roles: demand up 51%

## Novel Analysis / Original Contribution
**The wage differential calculation nobody has done:**
- Residential electrician: ~$60K-$80K/year nationally
- Data center electrician: $80K-$260K/year (depending on specialization/overtime)
- That's a 2x-4x premium for the SAME SKILL SET in a different building
- Residential construction wage growth: 2.1% (barely above inflation)
- Data center hotspot wage growth: 7-17% depending on role
- A residential plumber makes $76,600; the same plumber doing cooling infrastructure in a data center hotspot makes ~$92K (20% premium)

**The housing math:**
- 349,000 workers needed just to keep pace
- Data center spending accounts for 85% of that demand
- Multifamily starts collapsed 73%
- The pipeline is literally being drained by data centers

# Research: 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act — Chassis Rule Death

## Topic
The 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act becomes law today (July 10, 2026) via pocket passage — Trump called it "a big yawn" and refused to sign, but didn't veto. Title 3 eliminates the permanent steel chassis requirement for manufactured housing, a 50-year-old federal mandate that has single-handedly prevented factory-built housing from competing with site-built construction.

## Key Facts (All Sourced)

### The Chassis Rule (1974-2026)
- **Origin:** National Manufactured Housing Construction and Safety Standards Act of 1974 (HUD Code), codified at 24 C.F.R. § 3280.2
- **What it requires:** Every manufactured home must be built on a permanent steel chassis — a 10-to-12-inch-deep metal frame
- **Impact on housing share:** Before 1974, nearly 1 in 3 new homes was manufactured. By 1980, declined to 1 in 10 (University of Texas data, cited by CEI)
- **Cost per chassis:** $5,000-$10,000 (Niskanen Center)
- **Homes ever moved after placement:** Only 5-7% (Pew Charitable Trusts)
- **Industry has advocated removal since mid-1980s** — over 40 years (CEI)
- **Only Congress could change it** — HUD alone couldn't amend because the chassis requirement has Congressional origins (Lincoln Institute of Land Policy)
- **What chassis prevents:** Multi-story construction, basements, homes close to ground, urban lot configurations

### The Bill
- **Full name:** 21st Century ROAD to Housing Act (H.R. 6644)
- **Sponsor:** Rep. French Hill (R-AR), Dec 11, 2025
- **Vote margins:** House 390-9 → Senate 89-10 → House amendment 396-13 → Senate final 85-5
- **Becomes law:** July 10, 2026 (pocket passage — Trump didn't sign or veto)
- **Trump quote:** Called it "of minor importance compared to lower interest rates" and "a big yawn" (CNN)
- **Described as:** "Largest housing bill in a generation" (NAHB)

### Key Provisions (Title 3 and beyond)
1. **Title 3:** Eliminates permanent chassis requirement; grants HUD primary authority over energy-efficiency standards; directs studies on modular production barriers
2. **Title 2:** Streamlines NEPA environmental reviews; expands categorical exclusions for housing projects; creates competitive grants for zoning/planning reform
3. **Title 10:** Prohibits institutional investors controlling 350+ single-family homes from purchasing new single-family homes; 7-year divestiture requirement (modified from original)
4. **Pattern books grant program:** Pre-approved plans for local jurisdictions
5. **Whole-home repair pilot:** Grants and forgivable loans for aging housing repairs
6. **Community banks:** Raises public-welfare investment caps to 20%
7. **CDBG-Disaster Recovery authorization** for 3 more years
8. **HOME program pilot** for adaptive reuse projects

### AI/Factory-Built Housing Connection
- **HUD $10M pilot program** (June 9, 2026): Awards up to $10M for companies/universities to demonstrate AI+robotics in factory housing production (min $3M, results expected Sept 2026)
- **HUD proposed rule** (June 2026): Would permit multi-story manufactured housing without chassis on upper sections
- **Factory-built graveyard:** Katerra ($2.2B), Veev ($647M), Factory OS — all tried factory-built housing, all failed. They were fighting against the chassis rule AND outdated HUD Code
- **Chassis removal enables:** Factory-built modular homes on permanent foundations → qualify for conventional mortgages → no "trailer" stigma
- **Chassis can be REUSED** — recycled back to factory for next transport, further reducing costs (Lincoln Institute)
- **NAHB: 49% of builders use AI "in some capacity"** but only 20% for marketing, 11% for planning, <5% for any operational function (NAHB/Wells Fargo HMI, July 2025)

### Original Contribution Ideas
1. **Calculate the "chassis tax":** $5,000-$10,000 × annual manufactured home production (~90,000 units/year) = $450M-$900M in needless annual cost. Over the 50-year life of the rule, that's tens of billions.
2. **Housing share counterfactual:** If manufactured housing had stayed at 1974 levels (1 in 3) instead of dropping to 1 in 10, how many additional homes would exist? Cross-reference with the 4M home deficit.
3. **Mortgage qualification gap:** Manufactured homes on chassis vs permanent foundation — different appraisal, different mortgage products, different depreciation. Quantify the financing penalty.

# Research: AI Home Inspection — The Workforce Gap

## Angle
Home inspectors are aging out. AI tools are arriving. But they're not replacing inspectors — they're automating the paperwork. The question: can productivity gains from AI bridge the workforce gap before the retirement wave hits?

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)

## Key Data

### Workforce Demographics (BLS 2025)
- Construction and building inspectors: 120,000 total employed
- Median age: 46.1 (vs 42.1 overall workforce, 42.2 construction)
- Age breakdown (thousands): 16-24: 11, 25-34: 25, 35-44: 22, 45-54: 14, 55-64: 30, 65+: 18
- 48,000 (40%) are 55 or older — meaning 40% of the workforce is within ~10 years of retirement
- ICC survey: 80% of inspectors plan to retire within 15 years; 30% within 5 years (ProBuilder)

### Market Scale
- 2.1M home inspections performed annually in US (InterNACHI)
- Average cost: $296-$424, national average $343 (Angi/Homes.com)
- 18% of inspections flag safety issues needing remediation or further evaluation (Gitnux/InterNACHI)
- 4% lead to add-on requests beyond standard scope

### Inspection Waiver Rates (NAR Confidence Index)
- Peak: 30% of buyers waived inspection contingency (June 2022)
- Latest data: 17% waived inspection (down from 25% a year ago)
- Trending down but still elevated vs pre-pandemic (NAR started tracking April 2021 at 25%)
- One in six buyers still going in blind

### AI Tools Landscape
1. **ISN AI Image Defect Detector** (Porch Group, PRCH, Oct 2025)
   - Photo-to-findings: upload photos, AI identifies cracks/moisture/wear, generates report comments
   - Fully integrated into ISN Report Writer
   - Up to 10 images at a time
   - Inspector reviews/edits before adding to report
   - Built on earlier AI Comment Helper (late 2024)

2. **Alpine Intelligence** (Alpine Building Performance, Denver, March 2026)
   - Predictive pre-inspection risk forecaster
   - Free, ChatGPT-powered
   - Upload MLS data → get era-based risk predictions
   - "Pre-1940s: 87% chance of lead-based paint"
   - Designed for real estate agents, not inspectors
   - Doesn't replace inspection — sets expectations

3. **Paraspot** (mobile-first)
   - CV + audio narration for property reports
   - Primary: multifamily, SFR, student housing
   - Remote inspections by tenants/sellers
   - AI transcribes audio, categorizes images by room
   - Reports in minutes

4. **Binsr Inspect** (Dec 2025, Inman review)
   - Voice-to-report: AI generates comments from voice prompts
   - Color-coded, standardized
   - "We want less tapping, more inspecting"
   - Report Builder app for field use

5. **InspectReply AI**
   - Post-inspection: uploads report, generates localized repair cost estimates
   - Contractor referrals within 10 minutes
   - EagleView integration for roof measurements
   - Revenue tool for inspectors

6. **Coforge Smart Glass** (enterprise/insurance)
   - Smart glasses + AI crack/damage detection
   - Generates severity & risk score
   - Insurance underwriting focus

### Labor Shortage Context
- HBI Fall 2025: Skilled labor shortage costs $10.8B/year to homebuilding
- 19,000 homes not built due to labor shortages
- Gen Z construction participation doubled from 6.4% (2019) to 14.1% (2023)
- But inspection specifically requires years of experience — can't fast-track

### Original Analysis: The Retirement Cliff
- 48,000 inspectors (40%) are 55+ 
- At 2.1M inspections/year ÷ 120K inspectors = ~17.5 inspections/inspector/year
- If 30% retire in 5 years = 36,000 inspectors leaving
- That's 630,000 inspections/year capacity lost
- AI productivity tools would need to make remaining 84,000 inspectors ~35% more productive to maintain current capacity
- Can photo-to-report AI deliver 35%? ISN claims "hours saved" but no published metrics

### Strongest Counterargument
AI could make inspections SO efficient that fewer inspectors handle more volume. ISN's workflow tools eliminate the #1 time sink (report writing, typically 1-2 hours per inspection). If AI cuts report time from 90 min to 30 min, each inspector gains ~1 hour per inspection — potentially enough to do 1 more inspection per week. That's ~25% capacity increase from report automation alone. Combined with predictive tools (Alpine) reducing unnecessary inspections, the gap might close.

### Limitations
- No published data on AI tool adoption rates among inspectors
- ISN doesn't disclose user count or time-savings metrics
- Alpine Intelligence is Denver-only and too new for outcome data
- No study comparing AI-assisted vs traditional inspection error rates
- BLS category "construction and building inspectors" includes code inspectors, not just home inspectors
- ICC survey is old (from ProBuilder article dated 2016)

# Research: AI Progress Tracking — The Residential Gap

## Thesis
AI-powered visual progress tracking (Buildots, OpenSpace, DroneDeploy Progress AI) is transforming commercial construction with automated schedule-vs-reality comparisons. But residential builders — who manage 5-15 homes per superintendent — operate in a fundamentally different reality. The tools are getting cheaper, but the underlying data capture infrastructure residential sites don't have is the real barrier.

## Key Data Points

### The Progress Tracking Revolution (Commercial)
- **DroneDeploy Progress AI** (launched July 2025): Uses vision-language models to auto-track progress from drone flights and 360 walkthroughs. Claims 95% accuracy, results in minutes. "Priced to scale and guarantee ROI on projects of every size" — but smallest example is tenant fit-outs, not houses.
- **OpenSpace Track**: Monitors 700+ visual components across 200+ schedule tasks. Requires 360° camera captures pinned to floor plans. Integrates with P6 and project scheduling software. Available as add-on to OpenSpace Capture license.
- **Buildots**: $45M Series D (May 2025, led by Qumra Capital). Uses BIM + 360-degree cameras. Claims to reduce delays by up to 50%. Clients: Intel, 50+ firms. Launched "Dot" GenAI assistant for real-time queries.
- **Suffolk Construction case study**: Used OpenSpace progress tracking on $6M luxury condos — the *smallest* documented use case in the market.

### The Manual Reality
- **Track3D data**: Superintendents spend 30-60 minutes per floor walk recording updates. On a 10+ level, 20+ trade project, that's 40+ hours/week just on progress documentation.
- **Procore (2023)**: 47% of all projects behind schedule and over budget. Teams spend 28% of time on rework, 18% searching for project data.
- **McKinsey**: Large construction projects run 20% longer than scheduled, 80% over budget.

### Schedule Quality Crisis
- **SmartPM (2025, 70,000 schedules analyzed)**: 88% of baseline schedules fail quality benchmarks. 63% of professionals have limited understanding of their schedules. Only 1 in 4 teams update schedules on time. Less than 4% of companies have dedicated project controls teams. 75% of respondents use schedule in high-level decisions while 88% fail quality standards.
- **$200M+ in VC** flowing into scheduling software as of mid-2025.

### Change Orders / Budget Impact
- **Industry average**: Change orders account for 10-15% of contract value (Bauwise).
- **Hidden costs**: Coordination delays, schedule impacts, rework not captured in change order pricing.

### The Residential Math Problem
- **Residential superintendent**: Manages 5-15 homes simultaneously, each $400K-$2M.
- **Typical residential workflow**: iPhone photos texted to trades, Excel or Buildertrend schedules, paper sign-off at milestones. No BIM. No 360 cameras. No drones per house.
- **Data capture reality**: Commercial sites have dedicated camera installations, daily 360 walks with helmet cams, drone flights. A residential site gets maybe 20 photos a week.
- **Cost sensitivity**: On a $600K home build, the builder's margin is 8-15% ($48K-$90K). A $3-5K/month tool needs to save more than it costs across the 6-8 month build.
- **Buildertrend / CoConstruct / Buildern**: Residential-specific tools focus on client communication, draw schedules, change orders — not visual AI progress tracking.
- **AGC Survey**: 92% of construction firms face labor shortages, 45% cite delays from worker shortages.

### The Gap
- Commercial tools need: BIM models, 360 cameras, consistent capture protocols, dedicated field engineers
- Residential reality: No BIM, superintendent's iPhone, irregular capture, no field engineer
- Even "cheap" Progress AI needs drone flights or 360 walkthroughs — who's doing that on a 2,400 sq ft house?
- The data these tools eat doesn't exist on residential job sites

**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES — helps homeowners understand why their builder still tracks progress with a clipboard and text messages despite $200M+ in VC flowing into AI progress tools. Gives builders a realistic framework for evaluating these tools vs. sticking with Buildertrend-tier solutions.

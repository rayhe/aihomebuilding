# Research: AI Property Tax Assessment — The Arms Race Between Counties and Homeowners

## Topic
AI is being deployed on both sides of the property tax system: counties using machine learning and aerial imagery to assess home values, while startups arm homeowners with AI tools to appeal those assessments. The result is an algorithmic arms race worth $397 billion annually — and the homeowner who doesn't participate is the one who loses.

## Journalist
Catherine Chen — policy, regulation, bureaucratic processes

## Primary Sources

### 1. ATTOM 2025 Property Tax Analysis (April 2026)
- $396.8 billion in property taxes levied on 89.6 million single-family homes in 2025
- Average bill: $4,427 (up 3% YoY)
- Effective tax rate: 0.9%, highest since 2020
- Average home value: $494,231 (down 1.7% YoY, but tax bills still rose)
- NJ highest at $9,767/home; WV lowest at $1,044
- Source: attomdata.com, consumeraffairs.com (April 2026)

### 2. Ownwell — AI-Powered Property Tax Appeal Startup
- Raised $50M (Feb 2026), total ~$74M raised since 2020 launch
- 1M+ appeals processed
- 86% success rate, average savings $774/customer/year
- Contingency model: 25% of savings (industry-low)
- Active in TX, IL, FL, GA, CA, WA, NY
- In Texas alone: processed appeals for 200,000+ properties whose owners did not protest in 2024
- National Appeals Packet: AI-generated ready-to-file appeal packet with comps and evidence
- TTP v. Ownwell lawsuit (N.D. Texas, June 2025): competitor alleged false advertising over quoted 2.5% tax rate vs actual 2.25% algorithmic rate
- Sources: housingwire.com, morningstar.com, inman.com, govinfo.gov

### 3. C3 AI County Deployments
- Riverside County CA (2023): 5-year contract, 40% faster appraisals, 30 separate models → 4 unified AI models
- NYC Finance Department: pilot for condo revaluation with C3 AI
- Cook County IL: PTAXSIM public simulator tool (released late 2022)
- Source: AEI "The AI Revolution in Property Tax Assessment" (Jan 2026)

### 4. EagleView — Aerial AI Property Intelligence
- Sketch Inspect in Medina County OH: identified 70,198 discrepancies across 145,467 sketches
- Estimated $35-40M added to tax rolls, $12-14M increased tax revenue
- No additional staffing needed
- 3D property intelligence (Feb 2026): 98.77% accuracy for walls, windows, doors measurements
- 3B+ high-res aerial photos covering 94% of US population
- Source: eagleview.com case study, globenewswire.com

### 5. CAPE Analytics (acquired by Moody's)
- AI geospatial property risk from aerial imagery
- Computer vision identifies roof type, additional structures, vegetation overgrowth
- 80+ enterprise clients
- Moody's acquisition brought together CAT risk models with property-level AI
- Source: businesswire.com, NVIDIA blog

### 6. Racial Equity Data
- Minneapolis Fed: Black and Hispanic homeowners face 10-13% higher property tax assessment rates vs white homeowners in same jurisdiction ($300-$390/year excess)
- Brookings: homes in majority-Black neighborhoods undervalued 21-23% ($162B total cost of devaluation across 113 metro areas)
- Minority homeowners less likely to appeal; when they do, win less often and receive less relief
- Northwestern field experiments: information and step-by-step guides increase minority appeal rates
- Source: minneapolisfed.org, brookings.edu, Northwestern Law working paper

### 7. Wake County NC — SAS Viya ML Assessment
- ML models consider hundreds of factors and daily property sales
- Decision tree-based estimation: heated area, exterior material, etc.
- Provides "independent, data-driven, objective analysis" as second opinion
- Built for repeatability and adoption by other counties
- Source: sas.com

## Novel Contribution: Expected Value Calculation
- Ownwell stats: 86% success × $774 avg savings × 75% kept (after 25% contingency) = $500 expected value per appeal
- Zero upfront cost, zero downside risk (contingency model)
- Average tax bill: $4,427 → appeal savings represent ~17.5% of annual bill
- Yet only ~5-10% of homeowners appeal (NAHB/industry estimates)
- In Texas, Ownwell processed appeals for 200,000+ properties that had NEVER protested before

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES.
- New construction assessed for first time = counties using AI from day one
- First-time buyers inherit the assessed value
- Homeowners in rising markets see assessments climb faster than actual value
- Actionable: use an AI appeal service, file your own with AI-generated comps, understand what the county's AI sees

## Counterarguments
- Counties need accurate assessments for school funding and services
- AI makes assessments MORE fair by reducing human bias and inconsistency
- Appeal arms race could erode tax base if everyone appeals algorithmically
- County AI proponents argue EagleView catches genuinely untaxed improvements

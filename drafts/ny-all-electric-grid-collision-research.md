# Research: NY All-Electric Mandate vs Grid Reality Collision

## Story Angle
The 2nd Circuit Court of Appeals cleared NY's All-Electric Buildings Act the same week the power grid nearly broke during the worst heat wave in a decade. What happens when you mandate all-electric buildings on a grid that can't reliably serve the ones that already exist?

## Journalist: Catherine "Code" Chen (Policy & Regulation)

## Primary Sources

### 1. Legal Timeline
- **2023:** NY legislature passed All-Electric Buildings Act (AEBA) as part of state budget. Gov Hochul signed.
- **Jan 1, 2026:** AEBA takes effect for buildings ≤7 stories. No gas/propane hookups in new residential construction.
- **2029:** Expands to all new construction regardless of height.
- **Legal challenge:** Mulhern Gas Co. v. Mosely — trade groups, gas companies, labor unions sued. Argued AEBA preempted by federal Energy Policy and Conservation Act (EPCA). Cited 9th Circuit's Berkeley decision (California Restaurant Assoc. v. City of Berkeley, 2024) where federal law preempted city gas ban.
- **July 2025:** District Court (N.D.N.Y.) upheld AEBA. State then agreed to stipulation pausing enforcement pending appeal.
- **~July 7, 2026:** 2nd Circuit Court of Appeals ruled state could proceed with AEBA (per Spectrum News 1, Daily Caller reporting).
- **NFIB (May 2026):** Reported NY "agreed to a stipulation delaying" enforcement until Court of Appeals rules. So implementation is technically paused.
- **Exemptions framework:** NY DPS proposed allowing fossil fuel equipment if grid upgrades would take 18+ months longer than electric service. EDF (Environmental Defense Fund) says this is too broad, risks defeating purpose.

### 2. Grid Crisis (July 1-5, 2026)
- **PJM Interconnection** (largest US grid operator, serves 67M people in 13 Mid-Atlantic/Midwest states):
  - July 2 peak: 162,700 MW between 5-6 PM — nearly broke all-time record of 165,600 MW (set 2006)
  - July 3: 154,976 MW peak
  - July 4: 145,098 MW peak
  - PJM said if they hadn't activated emergency demand response, would have surpassed 2006 record
  - Energy Secretary Chris Wright directed data centers to use backup power instead of grid
- **Con Edison (NYC + Westchester):**
  - 60,000+ customers had outages restored during heat wave
  - 17,000-19,000+ without power at peak (Thursday July 3)
  - Underground cable malfunctioned in SW Queens from high demand, ~10,000 customers cut off
  - Voltage reduced 8% in portions of Queens and Brooklyn
  - Asked customers to avoid washers, dryers, microwaves, EV chargers
- **Mayor Zohran Mamdani (July 1):**
  - Set AC to 78°F in all government buildings
  - Asked private partners to dim/turn off lights
  - Called it potentially "most extreme heat wave the city has seen in over a decade"
  - Temps hit 99-101°F, heat index to 112°F

### 3. Data Center Grid Competition
- PJM region is home to world's largest cluster of data centers (Virginia)
- Data center energy use causing "serious electricity price spikes in certain Mid-Atlantic states"
- Energy Secretary had to ORDER data centers to use backup power during heat wave
- NVIDIA/OpenAI $100B deal for more data centers = more grid competition

### 4. All-Electric Building Costs
- **Panel upgrade:** $3,000-$5,000 for 200-amp (NYSERDA). 40-50% of existing homes need electrical work.
- **Heat pump installed:** $3,500-$14,000 depending on tonnage (Solar Reviews 2025)
- **Ductwork (if needed):** $5,000-$10,000
- **Smart panels** as alternative to full upgrade (manages loads dynamically)
- **Heat pump peak demand:** Doubles HVAC electrical demand vs gas furnace + AC. Gas furnace uses almost no electricity; heat pump in heating mode draws same as cooling mode.
- **NYSERDA says** homes need 200-amp for all-electric. Only homes built after 2015 reliably have 200-amp service.

### 5. Workforce Gap
- NYC launched HVAC Pre-Apprenticeship Training Program (12-week, NYC College of Technology/City Tech)
- ApprenticeNYC programs for green-collar jobs
- NY State Builders Association executive director Mike Fazio: "The infrastructure is simply not ready for all-electric mandates"
- Heat pump installer demand outpacing supply — training pipeline takes 1-4 years

### 6. Key Numbers for Original Contribution
- NY new residential building permits: ~30,000-50,000/year (need to verify)
- Average all-electric home peak demand: ~30 kW (with heat pump HVAC + induction + electric water heater)
- Average gas-heated home peak demand: ~15 kW (gas handles heating/cooking/hot water, electricity just for AC + lights)
- Additional peak load per new all-electric home: ~15 kW
- 40,000 new homes × 15 kW = 600 MW additional annual peak demand
- PJM was 2,900 MW from record during July 2026 heat wave
- In 5 years at current permit rate: ~3,000 MW additional peak demand
- Data centers: PJM projects 40,000+ MW of new data center demand by 2030
- Combined: building mandates + data centers = grid capacity shortfall is not theoretical, it's arithmetic

### 7. Counterargument (to include at full strength)
- Heat pumps are 2-3x more efficient than electric resistance heating
- New all-electric homes have better insulation (code requirements), reducing total energy per home
- Grid investment is needed regardless — deferring doesn't reduce the need
- Smart panels, battery storage, solar can reduce grid dependency
- Operating cost savings: up to $3,000/year for electrified homes (advocates claim)
- Climate cost of NOT transitioning is higher
- Every new gas hookup locks in 30+ years of fossil fuel infrastructure

### 8. Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home?
- YES: If you're filing for a building permit in NY right now, you need to know whether the mandate is actually enforced (paused pending appeal), what the cost premium is ($15K-$25K for all-electric vs gas), whether you can even get a heat pump installer, and whether the grid can handle your new home's load.
- Also relevant for builders in other states considering similar mandates (UK, California, Washington, Colorado)

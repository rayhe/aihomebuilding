# Research: AI MEP Design — The Residential Gap

## Core Story
$75 million in VC funding (Endra, Stockholm) went to automate MEP (mechanical, electrical, plumbing) design for buildings — the unglamorous systems engineering that makes buildings habitable. The platform auto-generates plumbing routes, electrical layouts, HVAC ductwork, fire alarms from a 3D model. But the technology targets commercial/enterprise — data centers, hospitals, offices. Residential builders still hand-coordinate MEP with a local engineer, a markup on a 2D plan, and a prayer that the plumber and electrician don't collide in the wall cavity.

### Residential MEP Reality
- Typical custom home MEP engineering: $5,000-$15,000 depending on complexity.
- Typical timeline: 3-6 weeks for plan development.
- Most tract/production homes: MEP is handled by the builder's in-house team or template plans. No formal MEP engineering unless required by jurisdiction.
- Common problems: pipe/duct/wire clashes in wall cavities, inadequate chase sizing, HVAC duct runs conflicting with structural members, electrical panel location requiring long/expensive wire runs.
- Change orders from MEP coordination failures: 5-8% of total project cost on average (McKinsey, Dodge data from prior research).

## Angle
The MEP coordination gap is where residential construction bleeds money. Commercial projects have clash detection, BIM coordination, formal MEP consultants. Residential projects have a plumber, an electrician, and an HVAC sub who all show up on the same day and fight over the same 14-inch wall cavity. $75 million just proved that AI can solve MEP coordination — but only for projects with six-figure engineering budgets. The residential builder running a $2-5M custom home has the same coordination problem and no tool to solve it.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — it explains why your home's plumbing and electrical cost more than they should (coordination failures → change orders), and what tools might eventually fix it. Actionable: if you're building custom, ask your builder about MEP coordination and clash detection before they start framing.

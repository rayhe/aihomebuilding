# Research: AI Surveillance in Construction — The Turnover Cost Paradox

## Angle
Construction companies are adopting AI-powered surveillance tools (GPS time clocks, AI cameras, wearable sensors, automated productivity tracking) at unprecedented rates. Marketed as "safety" and "efficiency" tools, they're actually driving worker turnover in an industry that already can't find enough people. The math doesn't add up — the cost of replacing workers driven away by monitoring exceeds the savings from catching "time theft."

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES — if your builder has high turnover because they surveil their crews, your project gets disrupted, delayed, and staffed with less experienced workers. Homeowners should ask builders about their retention rates and monitoring policies.

## Novel Analysis: The Surveillance Tax Calculation

**Inputs (verifiable):**
- ~8.3 million construction workers (BLS, 2025)
- Monthly quit rate: 1.5% = ~124,500 quits/month = ~1.49M quits/year
- ExpressVPN: 49% would consider quitting if surveillance increases
- Conservative: assume monitoring increases quit rate by 0.15 percentage points (from 1.5% to 1.65% monthly) — a 10% relative increase

**Calculation:**
- Additional monthly quits: 8.3M × 0.0015 = 12,450/month
- Additional annual quits: 12,450 × 12 = 149,400/year
- At $5,000 average replacement cost: 149,400 × $5,000 = $747 million/year

**Vs. claimed monitoring savings:**
- GPS time tracking vendors claim 5-7% labor cost savings
- But construction industry median crew size for residential: 4-8 workers
- If a $500K residential project has $150K in labor, 5% savings = $7,500
- A single worker quitting costs $5,000 to replace PLUS 2-3 week productivity gap
- Break-even: monitoring must prevent $5K+ in time theft per employee retained vs. driven away

**The uncomfortable conclusion:** For the industry as a whole, the turnover cost of surveillance may exceed the time-theft savings by $500M+ annually. Individual companies may benefit, but the aggregate effect on an industry 349,000 workers short is catastrophic.

## Limitations (to include)
- ExpressVPN survey is cross-industry, not construction-specific
- The 10% quit rate increase is an estimate — no construction-specific study exists
- Construction workers may be more or less sensitive to monitoring than office workers
- Some monitoring genuinely improves safety (fall detection, heat monitoring)
- Correlation vs. causation: companies with monitoring may have other retention issues

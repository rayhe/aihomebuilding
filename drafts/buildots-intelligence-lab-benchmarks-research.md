# Research: Buildots Intelligence Lab — First AI-Tracked Construction Benchmarks

## Core Angle
Buildots launched the Intelligence Lab on June 25, 2026 — construction's first AI-powered research hub publishing freely available benchmarks from aggregated, anonymized project data captured by head-mounted cameras. The findings confirm and quantify patterns every PM suspected: the final 20% of any activity takes 27% of its duration, MEP output gaps range from 20-50%, and top teams work 3x faster than average. None of this data is residential, which is the point — if billion-dollar projects with AI monitoring still miss by this much, what's happening on the $500K custom home?

## Key Sources

### 1. PR Newswire / Morningstar (June 25, 2026)
- Buildots Intelligence Lab launched June 25, 2026, Chicago
- CEO Roy Danon: "The construction industry has always lacked a source of macro-level truth."
- Three pillars: Metrics (standardized), Benchmarks (global comparisons), Insights (hidden patterns/early warnings)
- Data from "hundreds of projects worldwide, spanning hundreds of millions of square feet"
- All data aggregated and anonymized; opt-in required
- Freely available to professionals, academics, analysts, media

### 2. ENR Coverage (June 26, 2026)
- VP Industry Transformation Amir Berman: "construction is one of the largest industries that, at the same time, is one of the last to actually run truly on data"
- Technology: head-mounted cameras → AI analysis of reality capture imagery
- Clients include Turner Construction, JE Dunn, Intel, HOCHTIEF, Bouygues

### 3. Citybiz Coverage (June 25, 2026)
- Data center MEP gap: 20-50% between planned weekly output and actual delivery
- Early findings suggest "significant gaps between project plans and actual site performance"

### 4. Existing Research Notes (from RESEARCH.md thread #63)
- **Long-tail effect:** Final 20% of any activity takes 27% of duration
- **MEP output gap:** 20-50% in data center construction
- **Top teams 3x faster:** "enormous untapped capacity"
- **Schedule adherence:** Healthcare 65%, data centers 57%, commercial/industrial low-to-mid 40s, education <39%
- Lab team: Amir Berman (VP), David Milnes (ex-BAM UK), Andy Steele (ex-Osborne CEO), Dima Zabezhinsky

### 5. Census Bureau Survey of Construction 2024 (via NAHB Eye on Housing)
- Average single-family home: 9.1 months permit-to-completion
- Built-for-sale: 7.6 months
- Custom homes: 15.1 months
- Still ~2 months longer than 2015 average

### 6. Academic Backing: Physica A (2024)
- "Activity delay patterns in project networks"
- 180 construction schedules analyzed
- 75% of construction projects delayed
- Delays follow log-normal distribution

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — it gives homeowners and custom builders the first data-backed number for the "last mile" slowdown every project experiences. Actionable: pad the final 20% by 35-40%, ask your GC what the specific plan is for punchlist/commissioning coordination, and know that "80% complete" means roughly 73% through the timeline.

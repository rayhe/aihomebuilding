# Research: Construction Robotics as a Service — The Break-Even Math for Residential Builders

## Angle
The shift from buying construction robots ($500K-$1M+) to renting them project-by-project ($4,500-$20,000/month). RIC Robotics just launched a formal "Robotics as a Service" model. BotBuilt operates factories-as-a-service for framing. Cosmic Buildings deploys mobile microfactories at $550-$700/sq ft. The question Marcus Washington would ask: at what volume does renting a robot beat hiring a crew, and who loses the paycheck when the math tips?

## Journalist
Marcus Washington — Labor-economics hawk. Tracks wages, workforce data, union dynamics. Former drywall finisher. Sees every robot through the lens of who loses a paycheck.

## Key Sources

### RIC Robotics — Robotics as a Service Launch (May 2026)
- CEO: Ryan Cox, former COO of Alquist 3D (oversaw Walmart 3D-printed warehouse expansions)
- Model: Flexible deployment — rent the robot, the operator support, the training, materials solutions
- Zyrex robot: under $1M purchase, under $20,000/month lease
- Projects: 20,000+ sq ft of Walmart Supercenter expansions (Athens, TN and Huntsville, AL)
- Residential portfolio: Colorado, California, Kentucky, Texas, Nevada, Oklahoma
- Walmart Alabama: 5,000 sq ft, 16.5 ft tall warehouse, 7 days, 3 weeks ahead of schedule, 75% time savings, 80% skilled labor reduction
- Source: Mann Report, May 27, 2026; Robotics and Automation News; EIN Presswire

### BotBuilt — Robotic Framing as a Service
- Model: Doesn't sell robots — operates robot-equipped factories, delivers framing to builders
- Cost: ~$1/hour to run the system
- Traditional framing: $7-$16/sq ft total ($4-$10 in framing labor alone)
- 55% of single-family homebuilders report framing labor shortage (NAHB)
- Focuses on walls, floor trusses, roof trusses
- Reprogrammable for different designs (not hard automation)
- Source: TechCrunch

### Cosmic Buildings — Mobile Microfactory
- Deploys ABB IRB 6710 robots on-site in a mobile factory
- Cost: $550-$700/sq ft vs LA's typical $800-$1,000+
- Time: 70% reduction, 12-week delivery
- Target: 100 homes by 2027 (Palisades/Eaton fire rebuild)
- 30% lower total building costs vs conventional
- Non-combustible materials, solar + battery backup, greywater recycling
- Source: ABB/Cosmic press release; The Robot Report

### PMC Research Paper: Robot Service Pricing (Published Data)
- CivDot (layout robot): $4,500/month service, 84% cost reduction vs traditional layout
- TyBot (rebar tying): $3,600/week, 29% cost savings on 1.98M m² bridge project
- Kewazo (scaffolding robot): $10,000/month, saved 360 man-hours, 33% cost reduction
- Canvas (drywall finishing): 8% cost improvement with 2 robots
- SafeAI (earthmoving): 13% MORE costly than manual labor in Lima residential project ($400 higher over 20 workdays)
- Obayashi material handling: 41% cost reduction with robot + 2-person hybrid team vs 5-person crew
- Source: PMC/NIH published research paper

### Market Data
- US 3D concrete printing market: 42.9% CAGR through 2030 (Grandview Research)
- Global construction robotics market: 20% CAGR to 2030
- Construction industry: most fatal injuries of any sector in 2023 (1,075 fatalities)
- Industry needs 800,000 workers over next two years (cited by Bedrock Robotics/industry reports)
- Project backlogs: 8+ months as of December 2025
- All3 ($25M seed): legged "Mantis" robot, 30% cost savings, 50% timeline reduction, 100,000 sq m residential pipeline in Germany

### Labor Context
- Traditional framing crew: 4-6 workers at $25-35/hour
- Framing labor cost: $4-$10/sq ft
- Total framing cost including materials: $7-$16/sq ft
- A 2,000 sq ft home: $8,000-$20,000 in framing labor alone
- Framing takes ~1 month best case
- 55% of builders report framing labor shortage (NAHB)

## Kill Test
The $20K/month robot number sounds impressive, but: Does it include transport, setup, operator, materials? What's the actual all-in cost per residential project? A Walmart warehouse (rectangular concrete box) is radically simpler than a custom home. The 75% time savings and 80% labor reduction were on a warehouse — nobody has published those numbers for a 2,000 sq ft single-family home with corners, plumbing penetrations, and window headers.

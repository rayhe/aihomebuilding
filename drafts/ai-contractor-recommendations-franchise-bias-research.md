# Research: AI Contractor Recommendations — The Franchise Citation Moat

## Angle
When homeowners ask ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, or Google AI Overviews which plumber or HVAC contractor to call, AI recommends national franchises 87% of the time — even when local independents have decades of reviews and relationships. The mechanism is structural: AI engines weight Wikipedia presence, Wikidata records, structured corporate disclosure, and national trade-press coverage. Local five-star reviews don't register. This has direct cost and quality implications for anyone building or renovating a home.

## Journalist
Catherine Chen — structural market dynamics, policy implications, regulatory gaps

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're renovating your kitchen or replacing your HVAC, AI is steering you toward franchise contractors who may charge more and lack local expertise. This article tells you why, what the cost difference is, and how to find the right contractor anyway.

## Novel Analysis Opportunity
Nobody has calculated the "franchise recommendation premium" — the extra cost a homeowner pays when AI steers them to a franchise instead of a competitive independent. Franchise HVAC/plumbing operators carry franchise fees (typically 5-8% of revenue) + marketing fund contributions (2-3%) + brand standards compliance costs. These costs get passed to homeowners. On a $15,000 HVAC replacement, the franchise overhead alone could add $1,050-$1,650. This is before any price premium from reduced local competition.

## Strongest Counterargument
Franchise contractors provide consistency, insurance, and accountability that some independents lack. A homeowner who hires a franchise plumber gets a national brand's warranty backing, standardized training, and recourse if something goes wrong. For a homeowner with no contractor relationships, the franchise recommendation may actually reduce risk even at higher cost. The AI isn't necessarily wrong — it's optimizing for discoverability signals that correlate with scale, and scale correlates with reliability infrastructure.

## Limitations
- 5W study used only 65 prompts — sample is illustrative, not statistically rigorous
- Study conducted by a PR firm with commercial interest in selling AI visibility services
- No direct price comparison data between franchise and independent contractors for identical jobs
- The 87% figure measures "citation share" which may not directly translate to consumer choice (people still use Yelp, Nextdoor, word of mouth)
- Study only covers HVAC and plumbing — generalizability to electrical, roofing, GC unknown

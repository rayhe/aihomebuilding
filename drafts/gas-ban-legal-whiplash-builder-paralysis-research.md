# Research: Gas Ban Legal Whiplash — Builders Don't Know Whether to Spec Gas or Electric

## Angle
Three contradictory legal signals hit California builders in 6 months: the 9th Circuit strikes down Berkeley's gas ban (2024), the Trump admin sues Morgan Hill and Petaluma to invalidate all-electric mandates (Jan 2026), and then the SAME 9th Circuit upholds the South Coast AQMD zero-emission appliance rule (July 2, 2026). Builders pulling permits today have no idea which way the law is going. This isn't about whether gas bans are good policy — it's about the concrete cost of legal uncertainty to someone with a project on the line.

## Primary Sources

### 1. 9th Circuit: South Coast AQMD Rule 1146.2 UPHELD (July 2, 2026)
- 2-1 decision. Judge Lucy Koh for majority. Judge Kenneth Lee dissenting.
- South Coast AQMD passed zero-emissions rule for large water heaters, small boilers, process heaters (June 2024)
- Compliance deadlines: new buildings Jan 1, 2026; existing buildings 2029
- Covers LA, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino counties (~17 million people)
- Expected to eliminate ~10% of NOx emissions in South Coast Air Basin (5.6 tons/day)
- Legal basis: Clean Air Act authority — NOT municipal zoning
- Court held: EPCA does not preempt state emission regulations enacted under Clean Air Act authority
- Challengers: NAHB, California manufacturing/lodging/restaurant groups
- Dissent (Lee): case "strikingly similar" to Berkeley

### 2. 9th Circuit: Berkeley Gas Ban STRUCK DOWN (2024)
- California Restaurant Association v. City of Berkeley, 89 F.4th 1094 (9th Cir. 2024)
- Held: EPCA preempted Berkeley's ban on natural gas infrastructure in new buildings
- Legal basis: Berkeley used municipal zoning power, not Clean Air Act
- Key distinction: the South Coast case says Clean Air Act provides different legal authority

### 3. Trump Admin Sues Morgan Hill & Petaluma (Jan 5, 2026)
- DOJ filed in N.D. California
- Alleges all-electric ordinances "function to ban all use of gas appliances" 
- Claims preemption under EPCA
- Follows Executive Orders: "Unleashing American Energy" (EO 14154) and "Protecting American Energy from State Overreach" (EO 14260)
- Relies on Berkeley precedent
- Key: these cities used municipal ordinance (like Berkeley), not Clean Air Act authority

### 4. Montgomery County, Maryland — Another Challenge
- NAHB v. Montgomery County challenges electric buildings law
- Judge Paula Xinis: EPCA's energy-use testing requirement would be "impossible" if interpreted as challengers claim (at site of installation)

### 5. AI Astroturfing of AQMD Rule Process
- phys.org report: gas industry deployed AI-generated flood of comments to AQMD board
- Led to 7-5 rejection of gas appliance surcharge rule (separate from Rule 1146.2)
- CiviClick platform used to generate constituent emails
- AQMD typically receives comments "counted on one hand"

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home?
YES — if you're pulling permits in SoCal right now, you need to understand that the zero-emission rule is the law (upheld July 2), but the legal landscape could shift if the Supreme Court takes up the Berkeley/EPCA question. Speccing gas appliances in new construction in the South Coast district is now legally risky. But speccing all-electric outside Clean Air Act jurisdictions may be getting safer based on Koh's reasoning.

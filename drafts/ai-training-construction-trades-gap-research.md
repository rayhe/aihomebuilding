# Research: AI Training Programs for Construction Trades — The Gap Between Announcement and Jobsite

## Slug: ai-training-construction-trades-gap
## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)
## Date: May 27, 2026

## Thesis
Microsoft and OpenAI are pouring resources into AI training for construction workers. The announcements are big. The actual reach is tiny. NABTU's programs touch 1,500 instructors and 7,700 annual apprentices through TradesFutures — against an industry that needs 349,000 new workers in 2026 alone. Meanwhile, the construction workforce is structurally shifting away from tradespeople: their share dropped from 71% to 58.8% since 2005, while management roles ballooned 73%. The workers who need AI training the most — the ones actually framing houses — are the least likely to get it.

## Original Contribution (the math nobody did)
NABTU's TradesFutures reaches 7,700 annual apprentices across 34 states. The industry needs 349,000 new workers in 2026. That's 2.2% coverage. Even NABTU's full apprenticeship system (88,000 new entrants/year) accounts for 25.2% of the annual need. The Microsoft/OpenAI partnerships are training the trainers (1,500 instructors), not the workforce. At current throughput, it would take 4+ years for AI literacy to reach the existing 300,000 apprentices, never mind the 7.1M tradespeople already working.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — if you're hiring a crew, the question is whether AI literacy actually reaches them. The answer: probably not yet, and your GC's crew is more likely to be older, less trained, and working under a management layer that's growing while the people doing the work shrink.

## Strongest Counterargument
McGarvey pushes back on the "shortage" narrative. IBEW Local 26 got 5,000 applications for 880 slots. The demand is there — the commitment to hire isn't. The real bottleneck may not be training availability but project pipeline certainty. Unions won't train workers for jobs that don't exist. If there's no committed work, there's no class.

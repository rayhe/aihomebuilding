# Research: AI Appraisals and the New Construction Valuation Gap

## Kill Test
✅ Directly helps someone building or buying a new home. If an AI-powered appraisal undervalues your new build, you face a gap between purchase price and appraised value that can kill your financing. Actionable: how to prepare, what to demand, how to appeal.

## Journalist: Catherine Chen (Policy & Regulation)
Sharp, analytical, legal-minded. Translates regulations into readable prose.

## Thesis
Six federal agencies just finalized rules governing AI-powered automated valuation models (AVMs). Meanwhile, Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac are expanding appraisal waivers and desktop appraisals. For buyers of new construction, these converging trends create a specific problem: AI valuation models are trained on comparable sales data, and new construction in emerging subdivisions often lacks comps. The result is systematic undervaluation that costs builders and buyers real money — and the new federal rules don't address this gap.

## Primary Sources (3+)

### Source 1: FHFA — Final Rule on AVM Quality Control (July 2024)
- 6 federal agencies (FHFA, CFPB, Fed, FDIC, NCUA, OCC) finalized rules
- Requires institutions using AVMs to adopt quality control standards
- Standards: high confidence in estimates, avoid conflicts of interest, random sample testing, nondiscrimination compliance
- Key gap: Rules establish process standards but don't set accuracy benchmarks specific to new construction
- URL: https://www.fhfa.gov/news/news-release/agencies-issue-final-rule-to-help-ensure-credibility-and-integrity-of-automated-valuation-models

### Source 2: CFPB — Algorithms, AI, and Fairness in Home Appraisals (June 2023)
- CFPB Director Chopra: "automated models can embed the very human bias they are meant to correct"
- AVMs can value homes too high (unaffordable mortgage risk) or too low (equity denial)
- "the algorithms used cloak the biased inputs and design in a false mantle of objectivity"
- Federal agencies acknowledge AVMs cannot fully replace human judgment
- URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/blog/algorithms-artificial-intelligence-fairness-in-home-appraisals/

### Source 3: Federal Reserve — $400,000 Appraisal Exemption (2019)
- Agencies raised threshold from $250K to $400K for residential transactions exempt from appraisal requirements
- Means many homes can close with just an AVM evaluation, no human appraiser
- At current median new home price ~$420,000 (Census/NAHB 2025), many new homes STILL require full appraisals
- But the exemption threshold hasn't been updated since 2019 — may rise again
- URL: https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/bcreg20190927a.htm

### Source 4: BLS — Appraiser Workforce Data (2024)
- 77,300 active property appraisers/assessors in US (2024)
- Median pay $65,420/year
- Job growth projected at 4% 2024-2034 (average)
- Many openings from retirements — aging workforce problem
- The pipeline issue: bachelor's degree + long-term on-the-job training = slow replacement
- URL: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/business-and-financial/appraisers-and-assessors-of-real-estate.htm

### Source 5: Fannie Mae — Desktop Appraisals & Appraisal Waivers
- Fannie Mae expanded desktop appraisal options (no physical interior inspection)
- Appraisal waivers offered on many purchase and refinance transactions
- New construction is generally NOT eligible for appraisal waivers
- But desktop appraisals CAN be used for new construction in some cases
- Key issue: desktop appraiser can't verify construction quality, materials, workmanship
- URL: https://singlefamily.fanniemae.com/property-valuation/desktop-appraisals

### Source 6: Archistar / CivCheck — AI in Permitting (Tangential)
- Honolulu: CivCheck reduced permit prescreen wait from 6 months to 7 days
- Shows momentum toward automated review across the housing pipeline
- Austin, Vancouver, Surrey also deploying AI compliance checks
- Context: AI isn't just coming to appraisals — it's taking over the entire development pipeline
- URLs: https://www.govtech.com/artificial-intelligence/honolulu-is-among-cities-bringing-ai-to-planning-permitting, https://www.archistar.ai/blog/city-of-surrey-partners-with-archistar/

## Key Data Points
- 77,300 active appraisers in US (BLS 2024)
- $400,000 appraisal exemption threshold (unchanged since 2019)
- Median new home price ~$420,000 (Census 2025 data)
- 6 federal agencies involved in AVM regulation
- Appraiser median age likely 55+ (industry estimates), significant retirement wave
- AVM accuracy: Zillow Zestimate median error ~2.4% for on-market, ~7.5% off-market (Zillow's own data)
- For new construction in developing areas, AVM error can be significantly higher due to lack of comps

## The New Construction Problem (Core Argument)
1. AVMs rely on comparable sales. New subdivisions often lack comps.
2. Custom features (upgraded insulation, solar, EV wiring, spray foam) aren't captured in tax records that feed AVMs.
3. Construction quality (Grade I insulation vs Grade III) is invisible to desktop appraisals.
4. The $420K median new home price sits RIGHT at the $400K exemption threshold — meaning regulatory pressure to use AVMs is growing.
5. Builders absorb the gap when an AVM undervalues, either through price concessions or deal fallthrough.
6. NAHB surveys consistently show appraisal issues among top builder challenges.

## Actionable Takeaways
1. Get a full appraisal, not a desktop/AVM, for any new construction purchase
2. Document every upgrade with receipts and specs for the appraiser
3. If the AVM or appraisal comes in low, you can request a reconsideration of value (ROV) — specific process
4. Know the comp problem: provide the appraiser with builder's sales data for the subdivision
5. Understand when appraisal waivers apply (generally NOT for new construction, but check)
6. Budget for the appraisal gap: what happens when appraised value < contract price

## Strongest Counterargument
AVMs reduce costs ($300-500 appraisal fee savings), speed up closings (days vs weeks), and eliminate individual appraiser bias (documented racial bias in traditional appraisals). The CFPB's own PAVE task force found evidence of systematic undervaluation of homes in majority-Black neighborhoods by human appraisers. Replacing biased humans with algorithms, even imperfect ones, may net-improve fairness.

## Limitations
- Hard to quantify the exact undervaluation rate for new construction specifically
- AVM accuracy data is self-reported by vendors (Zillow, CoreLogic, Black Knight)
- The $400K threshold may be raised — this analysis is point-in-time
- Appraiser workforce data doesn't break down by residential vs. commercial specialization

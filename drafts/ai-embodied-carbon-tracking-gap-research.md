# Research: AI Embodied Carbon Tracking Gap

## Topic
The vast majority of home builders don't track embodied carbon in construction materials, despite AI tools that make it increasingly cheap and accurate. California's AB 2446 mandates 40% reductions by 2035. The gap between available tools and actual adoption is staggering.

## Journalist
**Priya Greenwood** — Sustainability & Green Building

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're building in California, AB 2446 compliance is coming and you need to start tracking now. If you're an eco-conscious buyer anywhere, you should know how to evaluate your builder's carbon footprint. Specific tools, costs, and actionable steps included.

## Primary Sources

### 1. University of Michigan / Frontiers in Built Environment (2024)
- DOI: 10.3389/fbuil.2024.1384191
- Whole-building LCA for 265 m² (2,852 sq ft) end-terrace home in Michigan
- Baseline: 28,450 kg CO2e (107.35 kg CO2e/m² or ~10 kg CO2e/sq ft)
- Materials only (A1-A3 lifecycle stages: extraction, manufacturing, transport)
- 19-39% reduction possible with "like-for-like" alternative materials
- High-impact materials: concrete, steel, gypsum, paint, insulation
- Challenges: limited market readiness and scalability of alternatives

### 2. RIB Software 2025 Sustainable Construction Report (published March 2026)
- ~440 survey respondents worldwide, 40+ countries, 6 continents
- **Only 31% of organizations actively track embodied carbon**
- Percentage virtually unchanged across 3 annual surveys
- Covers owners, architects, engineers, contractors, specialists

### 3. Massachusetts 100-Home Embodied Carbon Study (December 2025)
- Source: NEHERS Alliance, Stephens & Company, Ekotrope, Builders for Climate Action, NMR Group
- Funded by: MassCEC, National Grid, Eversource
- First large-scale study measuring both operational AND embodied carbon
- 100 newly constructed single-family homes across Massachusetts
- Used RESNET/ICC 1550 Standard methodology (pilot test)
- 88% of new MA homes already receive HERS ratings
- Analyzed A1-A3 lifecycle stages (cradle-to-gate)
- Key finding: embodied carbon is "a significant and largely overlooked share" of environmental impact
- Source: JLC article March 5, 2026; PDF at nehers.org

### 4. University of Bath AI Tool (March 2026)
- Published in Architectural Engineering and Design Management
- DOI: 10.1080/17452007.2026.2613773
- First AI tool predicting carbon footprint from text descriptions
- Uses machine learning + NLP
- 43 building professionals tested it on real projects
- Example: high-end glass/masonry building near Exeter, UK — designers adjusted insulation, walls, glazing without knowing material quantities
- Trained on synthetically generated dataset (limited real-world data)
- Provides instant feedback at earliest design stages

### 5. California AB 2446 / AB 43 (CARB)
- AB 2446 (Holden, Sept 2022) + AB 43 (Holden, Oct 2023)
- Requires CARB to develop framework for measuring and reducing embodied carbon
- Target: 40% net reduction in GHG emissions of building materials by end of 2035
- Reporting framework: due by 2026
- Reductions strategy: due by 2028
- CARB data: embodied carbon = up to 50% of total building lifecycle emissions
- Buildings = ~37% of global GHG emissions
- AB 43 adds option for embodied carbon trading system

### 6. EC3 (Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator)
- By Building Transparency (nonprofit)
- Surpassed 10,000 users by Feb 2021
- Open access EPD database
- Integrated with Autodesk Construction Cloud
- Free tool

### 7. One Click LCA
- EU-funded (Horizon 2020)
- Subscription platform for building lifecycle assessments
- ~$300+/month

## Original Contribution: The Carbon Math

**Per-home calculation (using Michigan data):**
- 28,450 kg CO2e for 2,852 sq ft home = ~10 kg CO2e/sq ft
- Avg new US home (Census 2023): ~2,469 sq ft → ~24,700 kg CO2e ≈ 24.7 metric tons
- At EPA's social cost of carbon ($51/ton in 2020 dollars): $1,260 in unpriced externality per home
- US new housing starts ~1.4M/year → ~34.6 million metric tons/year of embodied carbon from new homes alone

**California specifically:**
- ~120,000 new housing permits/year (recent pace)
- × ~25 metric tons each = 3 million metric tons/year
- AB 2446 asks for 40% cut = 1.2 million metric tons/year to eliminate
- Equivalent to ~261,000 cars off the road (EPA: 4.6 metric tons/car/year)

**Tool cost vs. carbon cost:**
- EC3: Free
- University of Bath text-to-carbon tool: Free (research prototype)
- One Click LCA: ~$300/month (~$3,600/year)
- Cost per home if you build 20/year: $180/home
- Unpriced carbon externality per home: $1,260
- ROI: 7:1 even before regulatory compliance value

## Counterargument
The strongest case against mandatory embodied carbon tracking: EPD data is still incomplete, especially for regional materials. The Bath tool was trained on synthetic data. RESNET/ICC 1550 just completed its first 100-home pilot. Forcing compliance before tools are mature could add costs without meaningful accuracy. The 31% tracking figure may be appropriate given data quality limitations.

## Limitations
- Michigan study covers one climate zone and one home type (end-terrace)
- California seismic requirements likely increase embodied carbon per sq ft vs Midwest (more concrete, more steel rebar) — this adjustment is not reflected in the calculation
- The $51/ton social cost of carbon is a 2020 EPA estimate; current estimates range from $51 to $190+
- Massachusetts pilot is the only large-scale residential study; the field needs more data
- No independent verification of the Bath AI tool's accuracy outside the 43-tester pilot

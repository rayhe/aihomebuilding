# Research: Data Center Construction Is Draining Electricians from Residential Building

## Angle
AI needs physical infrastructure. That infrastructure needs electricians, HVAC technicians, and skilled trades workers. These workers come from the same labor pool that builds houses. The irony: AI's most immediate impact on residential construction isn't robots laying bricks — it's the mass exodus of electricians to data center projects that pay 20-40% more.

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)

## Primary Sources

### 1. ABC (Associated Builders and Contractors) — Jan 2026
- Construction industry needs **349,000 net new workers in 2026**, 456,000 in 2027
- **Majority of 2026 demand is from retirement**, not new growth
- **Nearly 1 in 5 electricians are older than 55**
- 13% of ABC members now under contract for data center projects
- Model: ~3,450 jobs per $1 billion in additional construction spending
- Source: abc.org, Jan 15, 2026

### 2. IEEE Spectrum — Feb 2026
- US may need **106 GW of data center power by 2035** (BloombergNEF)
- BLS projects need for **~400,000 more construction workers by 2033**
- Biggest needs: electricians, plumbing, HVAC, ~17,500 EE engineers
- AFCOM report: 58% of DC managers ID multiskilled operators as top growth area
- Lancium CEO: currently builds 1 GW/year of DC infrastructure, big tech wants 1 GW/quarter → 1 GW/month
- Applied Digital sourcing engineers from nuclear, military, aerospace
- SMU, Dallas College, Texas State Tech: new DC technician programs
- Siemens: 32,000 apprenticeships across 32 states, committed to 200,000 electricians by 2030

### 3. BlackRock Future Builders Initiative — March 2026
- **$100 million over 5 years** to train 50,000 skilled trades workers
- Focus: electricians, welders, plumbers, HVAC technicians, wiremen
- Larry Fink: "$10 trillion in infrastructure investment by 2033"
- RFP through JFF: $25M in grants, $500K–$1M each
- Ruth Porat (Alphabet/Google) endorsed it — "pioneered an electrical worker training program"
- Source: blackrock.com, enr.com

### 4. NABTU + Microsoft Partnership — April 2026
- Expanded AI literacy courses for construction trades
- 1,500 instructors trained in hands-on centers nationwide
- Free LinkedIn Learning courses with AI literacy credentials
- TradesFutures: 34 states, apprenticeship readiness programs
- OpenAI also committed $1.5M for NABTU recruitment/training
- Source: microsoft.com, constructiondive.com

### 5. DOL Registered Apprenticeship AI Initiative — 2026
- Federal initiative integrating AI skills into apprenticeships
- RFP on sam.gov (Notice ID: 1605C2-26-R-00003)
- Single contract, 1-year base + 4 option years
- Source: dol.gov

### 6. BLS Construction Data — June 2026
- Construction employment: 8,331,000 (June 2026, preliminary)
- Job openings: 330,000 (May 2026, preliminary)
- Construction unemployment rate: 4.7% (June 2026)
- Source: bls.gov

### 7. Bisnow — Jan 2026
- "Outside of data center construction, demand for building is down"
- If other sectors bounce back, labor squeeze worsens
- Immigration policy as "potential wildcard" — undocumented worker flow "fell precipitously in 2025"
- Source: bisnow.com

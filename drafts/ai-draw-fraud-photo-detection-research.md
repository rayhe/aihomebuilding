# Research: AI-Generated Construction Draw Fraud & Photo Verification

## Angle
AI-generated photos are being used to fake construction loan draw inspections. A contractor photographs half-finished work, runs it through ChatGPT or Gemini, and the AI adds hardwood floors, paint, and furniture. The lender approves a $30K+ draw for work never done. The detection tech exists but most lenders don't use it.

### How AI Draw Fraud Works (per Truepic)
1. Borrower photographs partially complete project
2. Generative AI (ChatGPT/Gemini) adds hardwood floors, fresh paint, lighting, furniture
3. Free apps spoof time, date, location metadata
4. Doctored images submitted for draw approval
5. Process takes <10 minutes, costs nothing
6. Can also generate fake video walkthroughs from a single image

### Construction Loan Draw Process
- Typical residential build: 4-6 draw inspections
- Each draw: 20% of total loan ($50K-$100K per draw on a $300K-$500K build)
- Inspector visits site, photographs progress, compares to approved plans
- Turnaround: 7-10 business days typically
- Some lenders allow borrower-initiated inspections (self-service photo submission)
- 10% retention typically held
- Source: nbkc.com, LendingTree, Groundfloor, Live Oak Bank

### AAPL (American Association of Private Lenders)
- Traditional fraud: clerical discrepancies, rushed closings, strange wire requests
- New fraud: manufactured visual evidence using AI
- AAPL now offering training course on media fakes detection

## Why This Matters for Homeowners
- If your contractor defrauds the lender, your project can stall — the money's gone but the work isn't done
- Draw fraud can trigger loan default even when the borrower is innocent
- Self-service draw inspections (borrower submits photos) are most vulnerable
- Most residential construction lenders have NO AI authentication in their inspection workflow

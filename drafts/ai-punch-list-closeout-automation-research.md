# Research: AI Punch List & Construction Closeout Automation

## Angle
Residential punch lists devour builder profit. Retainage (5-10% of contract) stays locked until every item clears, and most builders track 80-150 items per home on clipboards or spreadsheets. A new generation of AI-powered tools — from $29/month photo documentation apps to computer vision platforms like PunchIQ — compress closeout from weeks to days. The math is brutal for anyone still running text-only lists.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — builders lose $670/day in delay costs during closeout, and the average residential project's punch list takes 30-60 days to clear. Faster closeout means builders get paid, subs get released, and buyers move in sooner. Concrete tools and pricing for both small custom builders and production operations.

Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca — project management, operations, process. Closeout is his domain.

## Key Data Points

### The Closeout Problem
- 73% of commercial construction projects exceed scheduled closeout timeline (FMI Capital Advisors, 2025)
- 35.1% of residential building projects run late (2023 SORCI Report, Association of Professional Builders)
- Average $750,000 build costs $670/day in delay (APB SORCI Report)
- 48.3% of builders don't know their fixed expenses per job per day (APB SORCI)
- Retainage (5-10% of contract) is often the builder's entire profit margin (Levelset)
- Typical closeout: 30-60 days (industry standard)

### Photo Documentation Impact
- Photo-documented punch items: 89% first-trip resolution rate vs 61% for text-only (ConstructionBids.ai 2026 comparison, 200+ items across 6 sites)
- 28 percentage point improvement — nearly half the failures eliminated
- Automated punch list reporting saves superintendents 6 hours/week (same study)

### AI-Specific Tools (2026)
- **PunchIQ** (founded 2026, Los Angeles): AI turns jobsite photos into structured punch list items automatically. Computer vision identifies deficiency type, location, responsible trade. Integrates with Procore.
- **OpenSpace**: 360-degree camera walkthrough, AI maps photos to floor plans, BIM comparison, clash detection. Enterprise-priced, custom quotes.
- **Doxel AI**: Image recognition + BIM integration, tracks 80+ construction stages. Claims 95% reduction in manual tracking time.
- **Fieldwire**: Plan-based punch list markup, 247 items tracked across 68K SF test. 35% walkthrough time reduction. $39/user/month.
- **CountBricks case study**: 91 punch items logged using AI-assisted photo documentation, 87 closed before final inspection (95.6% closure rate), average 36-hour item closure time, saved $6,465 in avoided rework per project.
- **AvidWarranty (ECI)**: 1.4M homeowner warranty claims dataset. AI triage routes claims to correct trade automatically.

### Cost Calculation
- 5% retainage on $500K home = $25,000 locked
- 3 extra weeks closeout at $670/day = $14,070 delay cost
- 37 return trips (from 39% text-only failure rate on 112 items) × $150/trip = $5,550
- Total exposure per home: ~$19,620 in delays + wasted mobilizations
- Photo-doc software: $29-$39/month — pays for itself on first house

## Skepticism Points
- Most AI tools built for commercial scale; residential pricing/ROI less proven
- CountBricks "36-hour closure" stat: sample size unclear, likely favorable project conditions
- PunchIQ just launched 2026 — limited track record
- OpenSpace/Doxel enterprise-priced — not accessible to small custom builders
- Residential projects rarely have BIM models for AI comparison
- "89% vs 61%" methodology not fully published — directionally credible but exact percentages uncertain
- Photo documentation alone isn't AI — important distinction between "take a photo" and "AI analyzes the photo"

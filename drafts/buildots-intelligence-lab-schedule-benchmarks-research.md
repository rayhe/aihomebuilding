# Research: Buildots Intelligence Lab — AI-Powered Construction Benchmarks

## Core Story
Buildots launched the "Intelligence Lab" on June 25, 2026 — the construction industry's first AI-powered research hub publishing freely available benchmarks from aggregated, anonymized project data. First findings challenge conventional wisdom on scheduling, productivity, and trade performance.

## Key Findings (from Buildots press release + ENR coverage)
1. **Long-tail effect:** The final 20% of any construction activity takes 27% of its duration. Late-stage stalls are structural, not incidental.
2. **MEP output gap:** Data center construction shows 20-50% gap between planned weekly MEP output and actual delivery.
3. **Top teams 3x faster:** Top-tier MEP teams work up to three times faster than average — "enormous untapped capacity."
4. **Schedule adherence by sector:** Healthcare 65%, data centers 57%, commercial/industrial low-to-mid 40s, education under 39%.

## Residential Context (Census Bureau Survey of Construction, 2024)
- Average single-family home: 9.1 months permit-to-completion (down from 10.1 in 2023)
- Built-for-sale: 7.6 months
- Custom homes: 15.1 months
- Regional: 7.8 months (South Atlantic) to 13.7 months (Middle Atlantic)
- Still ~2 months longer than 2015 average
- Source: Eye on Housing / NAHB analysis of Census SOC data

## Academic Backing: Activity Delay Patterns
- Paper: "Activity delay patterns in project networks" (Physica A, 2024)
- 180 construction project schedules analyzed
- 75% of construction projects are delayed
- Median delay: 20-40% of project duration depending on sector
- Delays follow a log-normal distribution ("law of activity delays")
- After correcting for delay risk factors, remaining delays follow universal model

## Original Contribution: Residential Carrying Cost of Long-Tail Effect
**Calculation:**
- Average built-for-sale construction time: 7.6 months (Census SOC 2024)
- Long-tail effect: final 20% of activity takes 27% of duration
- Expected: 20% of duration = 20% × 7.6 = 1.52 months
- Actual: 27% × 7.6 = 2.052 months
- Excess: 0.532 months ≈ 16.2 days
- Average construction loan rate: ~8.5% (Bankrate, 2026 — construction loans typically prime + 1-2%)
- Average new home cost: $499,500
- Daily carrying cost: $499,500 × 0.085 / 365 ≈ $116.30/day
- 16.2 days × $116.30 ≈ **$1,884 per home in excess carrying costs from long-tail alone**
- National scale: 1.36M housing starts (2025) → ~$2.56 billion industry-wide (if the pattern holds for residential)

**Critical caveat:** Buildots data is ALL from commercial projects (data centers, healthcare, education). Zero residential-specific data. The $1,884/home figure extrapolates commercial patterns to residential — unverified. This IS the counterargument.

## Key Skepticism
1. No residential data in the Lab — all commercial/institutional. Residential construction has fundamentally different trade coordination, inspection cadences, and weather exposure.
2. Buildots sells construction intelligence software — the Lab is both legitimate research and marketing. They make money when builders adopt their platform.
3. The "3x faster top teams" finding could reflect selection bias — Buildots' clients are Fortune 500 contractors, not the average residential builder.
4. Schedule adherence of 39-65% is a benchmark, not a fix. Knowing how late you'll be doesn't make you less late.

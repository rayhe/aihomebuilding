# Research: AI Cost Estimation — The Data Quality Gap

## Angle
AI cost estimation tools deliver real accuracy improvements in controlled conditions — 20.4% better accuracy, <5% bid-day variance. But 85% of construction AI projects fail due to poor data quality. The average residential GC doesn't have clean structured data from thousands of past projects. They have scattered spreadsheets, texts from subs, verbal change orders, and gut feelings. The AI's precision is only as good as the data feeding it, and in residential construction, that data is garbage.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — if you're a homeowner evaluating a builder who says "we use AI for estimating," this tells you what to ask. If you're a GC considering AI tools, this tells you what to fix first (your data) before spending $50K on software.

## Original Contribution
**Novel calculation: What "20% better accuracy" actually costs your builder when the data isn't there.**

On a $500,000 residential build with a 28% average overrun ($140,000):
- In lab conditions (clean data): 20% accuracy improvement → overrun drops to ~$112,000 = **$28,000 saved**
- In reality (messy data, 85% failure rate): Expected value = 0.15 × $28,000 = **$4,200** in expected savings
- Minus AI tool costs ($3,000-15,000/year), onboarding (40+ hours), data cleanup investment
- Net ROI for a typical small GC in year one: **negative**

The math only works when:
1. You have 3+ years of structured historical data
2. Your data lives in one connected system (not 4+ disconnected tools)
3. You update cost indices monthly, not annually
4. Your subs provide structured quotes, not text messages

Most residential GCs have none of these.

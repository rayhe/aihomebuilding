# Research: AI Wildfire Structure Survivability & Structure Separation

## Core Study: UC Berkeley ML Model (Nature Communications, 2024)
- Led by Michael Gollner (assoc. prof. mechanical engineering) and Maryam Zamanialaei (postdoc)
- Used CAL FIRE's DINS (damage inspection database): on-ground surveys of ALL structures damaged/destroyed in major CA wildfires since 2013
- Added geospatial data: building spacing, construction materials, vegetation density
- ML model predicts structure survivability with 82% accuracy
- **#1 factor: Structure separation distance** — especially in densely built areas
- Also important: flame length, construction features
- Home hardening + defensible space together = reduce wildfire destruction by up to 50% (double survivor rate)
- Just removing vegetation within 5-foot perimeter = 17% reduction in structure losses
- Separate fire spread model (Purnomo, 2024): 85% accuracy for fire perimeters, ~70% for damaged houses, 30%+ of house ignitions caused by embers

## IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home/Neighborhood Standards
- Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety (research nonprofit funded by insurance industry)
- "Wildfire Prepared Home" standard (2022), "Wildfire Prepared Neighborhood" (2024)
- Testing facility in South Carolina: recreates wildfires at full scale
- Requirements: noncombustible gutters, Class A fire-rated concrete tile roof, ember-resistant vents, 6" vertical clearance at base of exterior walls, noncombustible fence/gate materials, 5-30 ft defensible zone with drought-resistant CA natives
- KEY: requires 10+ feet between buildings + elimination of "connective fuel pathways"
- CA building code does NOT require 10-ft separation or fuel pathway elimination

## IBHS Cost Analysis (1,750 SF single-story, $500K estimated total construction)
- CA code (CWUIC Part 7) vs traditional: adds ~$13,000 (flame/ember-resistant vents, metal gutters, fire-rated wallboard, tempered windows, 0-5 ft noncombustible zone)
- IBHS WFPH Base vs CWUIC Part 7: saves >$4,000 (no gutter guard, open eave considerations, non-tempered windows)
- IBHS WFPH Plus vs CWUIC Part 7: adds ~$2,000 (enclosed eaves, noncombustible soffits, double-tempered windows)
- NAHB range: $1,827 to $44,888 depending on siding choices
- Ember-resistant zone + deck enclosure: ~$2,780
- "Optimum" fire-resistant materials (metal roof etc.): $18,180-$27,080

## KB Home Projects (First IBHS-Certified Neighborhoods)
- Stone Canyon (near Sacramento): first "Wildfire Prepared Neighborhood," homes start high $700,000s
- Escondido (near San Diego): 64 homes, ~$1M+, ~2,000 sq ft
- Steve Ruffner (president, KB Home SoCal): changed design on the fly after watching IBHS demonstration
- IBHS demo: standard-code home burned in ~30 min, IBHS-standard home didn't burn at all
- Tempered windows: fewer construction breakages = unexpected cost savings
- Overall: "didn't significantly change the development's bottom line"
- HOA agreement requires vegetation maintenance
- ~12 other projects now being designed to IBHS standard (some with several hundred homes)
- IBHS CEO Roy Wright: "Structure separation is the biggest indicator of wildfire progress"

## Insurance Crisis Context
- 642,010 active FAIR Plan policies (Sept 2025) — FAIR Plan = CA insurer of last resort
- 90% of FAIR Plan policies in counties FEMA rates "Relatively High" or "Very High" wildfire risk
- Private insurers exiting 46 of 58 California counties
- Contra Costa County: 1,094 → 12,837 FAIR Plan policies (2021-2025), 1,073% increase
- FAIR Plan has only $2.5B in reinsurance
- Potential surcharge: $975-$3,700 per CA homeowner (Consumer Watchdog estimate)
- Commissioner Lara: "structurally healthier market is a 3-5 year project"
- Farmers Insurance: pledged to market to 300,000 consumers in high-risk zones from 2026

## Original Contribution: 5-Foot Perimeter ROI Calculation
- Berkeley model: 5-foot vegetation clearing = 17% reduction in structure losses
- Cost: ~$2,780 (IBHS estimate for ember-resistant zone + deck enclosure)
- For a $700K WUI home with 10% wildfire exposure probability over 30 years:
  - Expected loss without mitigation: $70,000
  - 17% reduction = $11,900 expected loss reduction
  - ROI: 4.3x on a $2,780 investment
  - Plus: insurance premium reductions (IBHS certification = potentially regaining private market coverage vs FAIR Plan)
- FAIR Plan premium premium: FAIR Plan doesn't cover theft, liability, water damage — homeowners need separate DIC policy. Total cost often exceeds standard private coverage.

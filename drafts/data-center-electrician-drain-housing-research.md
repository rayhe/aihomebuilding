# Research: Data Center Boom Poaching Residential Electricians

## Angle
The AI data center construction boom is directly draining the electrician workforce that residential builders depend on. Data centers pay 75-130% more, offer per diem and overtime, and need electricians for 45-70% of their total construction budget. The result: home builds taking 2+ months longer, small electrical contractors losing veteran crews, and a structural shortage that no training pipeline can fix in time.

## Journalist: Marcus Washington (Workforce & Labor)
Human-centered, investigative. Tell through the workers.

## Primary Sources

- Stargate AI campus: 4 million sq ft (OpenAI, Crusoe, Oracle), outskirts of Abilene

- Texas added 2.6M residents since 2020
- Data centers offer electricians double the wages

  - "I don't blame them. It's less strenuous work but more money to take home to the family."

- Texas has ~71,000 electricians

## Key Tension
AI data centers are creating a labor vacuum in residential construction by offering dramatically higher wages for the exact same skilled trades. Builders who can't match $35+/hr (let alone $130/hr union rates) are losing crews they spent years training. The irony: the technology that promises to solve everything—including, eventually, construction labor shortages—is making the shortage dramatically worse right now.

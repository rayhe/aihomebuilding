# Research: Builder Rate Buydown Hidden Price Math

## Article Angle
Rate buydowns are the dominant builder incentive in 2026. AEI Housing Center data shows 64% of large-builder sales include permanent buydowns. The critical insight: a 100 bps buydown costs the builder ~3.2% of the sale price, but achieving the same monthly payment through a price cut would require cutting 10%. This arbitrage benefits the builder, but the buyer ends up with an inflated purchase price and less equity. If rates drop and the buyer refinances, the buydown disappears but the higher price stays.

## Primary Sources

### AEI Housing Center (Edward Pinto, Senior Fellow & Co-Director)
- 64% of new single-family home sales in June 2025 by 21 largest U.S. homebuilders included a "permanent" buydown
- Only 13% for all other new-home sales
- 100 bps rate buydown costs the builder roughly 3.2% of the sale price
- To achieve the same monthly payment through a direct price cut, builder would need to cut price by 10%
- Permanent buydowns funded through bulk forward commitments are EXCLUDED from seller concession limits (normally 3-6%)
- Over 40% of sales by large builders have seller concessions + permanent buydown cost exceeding 6%
- Source: AEI Housing Center report, November 2025; Fast Company coverage (February 2026)

### NAHB Housing Market Index (July 2026)

- 63% of builders offering sales incentives (16th consecutive month at 60%+)
- Prospective buyer traffic gauge fell to 23
- Current sales conditions gauge at 37
- Future sales expectations gauge at 43
- Source: Reuters, July 16, 2026

### NAR / Census Bureau New Home Sales Data
- Q1 2026 national price: median new home $403,200 vs median resale $404,600 (price inversion)
- 62% of builders reported using sales incentives in June 2026
- Source: NAR, Census Bureau, YouTube "The Honest Analyst" channel analysis

### John Burns Research and Consulting
- Incentives averaging ~7% of asking price (2x pre-COVID average)
- Surveys ~300 builders monthly
- Source: Marketplace / NPR, June 19, 2026

### PulteGroup Financial Disclosure
- PulteGroup putting 10.9% of gross sales price into incentives (Q1 2026)
- Source: PulteGroup Q1 2026 earnings disclosure

### Redfin / Better.com Rate Buydown Math
- 2-1 buydown on $400,000 loan at 6.5%: Year 1 at 4.5% ($2,027/mo), Year 2 at 5.5% ($2,271/mo), Year 3-30 at 6.5% ($2,528/mo)
- Total temporary savings: ~$9,096
- Cost of buydown: ~$9,096-$9,324 deposited in escrow
- Source: Redfin, Better.com

### Fast Company (February 2026)
- Some builders shelling out $40,000-$60,000 on "forward commitments" to get rates below 4.99% or 3.99%
- "Through an economic lens, the homebuyer is still ultimately paying for those buydowns if the headline price isn't coming down"
- Source: Fast Company article featuring AEI Housing Center data

### Builder Concession Limits
- Conventional (Fannie/Freddie): 3-9% depending on down payment
- FHA: up to 6%
- VA: more flexible, no strict % cap
- Permanent buydowns via bulk forward commitments bypass these limits
- Source: Redfin, Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac guidelines

## Key Calculations for Article

### The 3.2% vs 10% Arbitrage
On a $400,000 home at 6.5% market rate:
- Builder buydown cost (100 bps permanent): $400,000 × 3.2% = $12,800
- Price cut to match same monthly payment: $400,000 × 10% = $40,000
- Builder savings from choosing buydown over price cut: $27,200

### The Equity Trap
On a $400,000 home with buydown vs. $360,000 home without:
- Buyer A (buydown): $400K purchase, 10% down ($40K), loan $360K at 5.5% (bought down from 6.5%)
  - Monthly P&I: ~$2,044
  - Day 1 equity: $40,000
- Buyer B (price cut): $360K purchase, 10% down ($36K), loan $324K at 6.5%
  - Monthly P&I: ~$2,049
  - Day 1 equity: $36,000

Similar monthly payment. But:
- If Buyer A refinances when rates drop to 5.5%, the buydown disappears — they're at $360K loan at 5.5% = $2,044/mo (same as before)
- If Buyer B refinances when rates drop to 5.5%, they benefit — $324K at 5.5% = $1,840/mo (saves $209/mo)
- Buyer A's home needs to appreciate 10% more to have the same equity position as Buyer B

Note: The 10% price cut scenario is hypothetical — builders don't typically offer that size cut. The point is the buydown costs the builder 3.2% but substitutes for a 10% cut.

## Limitations
- AEI data is from June 2025 (21 largest builders); 2026 data may differ
- "64% with permanent buydown" applies to large builders, not all builders
- Q1 2026 price inversion data from one YouTube analysis; should verify against NAR directly
- The 3.2% = 10% equivalence depends on rate environment and loan terms
- Forward commitment mechanics are complex; simplified for clarity

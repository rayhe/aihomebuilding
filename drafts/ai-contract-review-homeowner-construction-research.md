## Primary Sources

### 1. National Academies of Sciences — "Reducing Construction Costs: Uses of Best Dispute Resolution Practices by Project Owners"
- Serious disputes arise in 10-30% of all construction projects
- 1 in 4 construction projects has a claim
- Transactional costs for resolving disputes: $4B-$12B/year (attorneys, experts, witnesses, in-house costs)
- Risk-shifting contracts (more risk to contractor) = more claims
- Fast-track projects = significantly more claims
- Source: National Academies Press, doi: 10.17226/11846

### 2. Arcadis 13th Annual Construction Disputes Report (2023)
- Average dispute value rose 42% from 2021 to 2022
- Contract errors and omissions = #1 cause of disputes
- Misunderstandings or violations of contract terms = #2
- Poorly drafted or miscommunicated claims = #3
- Average dispute lasts ~1 year, costs millions in direct + indirect costs
- Source: https://www.arcadis.com/en-us/knowledge-hub/perspectives/north-america/united-states/2023/construction-disputes-report-2023

### 4. Construction Attorney Costs for Contract Review
- Quick contract review: $250-$600
- Review with comments/redlines: $500-$1,500
- Full drafting: $700-$2,500+
- Hourly rate: $200-$500+/hr
- Sources: AI Lawyer pricing data, ContractsCounsel pricing report, Clio legal rate benchmarks

### 5. AIA A105 — Standard Form of Agreement Between Owner and Contractor (Residential)
- Industry standard residential construction contract
- Sections: scope, contract sum, time, payments, insurance, changes, claims, termination
- Many custom builders use their OWN contracts (not AIA) — often missing standard protections
- Source: American Institute of Architects

### 6. Common Missing Clauses in Residential Builder Contracts
- Force majeure / material delay provisions (critical post-tariff era)
- Change order caps or pre-approval requirements
- Allowance itemization and overage responsibility
- Payment schedule tied to milestones (not calendar dates)
- Material substitution approval process
- Warranty scope beyond state minimum (often just 1 year)
- Dispute resolution method (arbitration vs. litigation)
- Notice requirements for claims (miss the deadline = waive the right)
- Lien waiver exchange at payment
- Source: Long International, Mastt construction contract analysis

## Original Contribution
**Cost-exposure analysis:** For a $450K new-construction home (median new home price), calculate the dollar exposure for each commonly missing clause. Example: missing change-order cap → average change order adds 15-20% to project cost; missing force majeure → builder can claim delay damages; missing notice requirements → homeowner waives claim rights without knowing. Compare: $0 AI review (Trunk.Tools free tier) vs. $500-$1,500 attorney review vs. $0 review (what most homeowners do) → expected value calculation based on 10-30% dispute rate.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI can flag missing clauses but can't replace the judgment of a construction attorney who understands local lien laws, state-specific warranty statutes, and the political dynamics of your jurisdiction's builder community. An AI tool might flag "missing arbitration clause" without knowing that in your state, mandatory arbitration actually favors the builder because the construction arbitration pool is builder-friendly. Context matters.

## Limitations
- Dispute statistics are industry-wide (commercial + residential); residential-specific data is sparse
- AI tool accuracy for construction contracts hasn't been independently audited
- Cost savings are modeled, not measured from actual homeowner outcomes
- State-by-state variation in construction law makes universal advice impossible

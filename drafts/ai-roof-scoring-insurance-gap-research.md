# Research: AI Aerial Roof Scoring vs. Actual Roof Condition — The Insurance Gap

## Angle
Insurers are using AI-powered aerial imagery (satellites, drones, and commercial aerial platforms) to score your roof and make coverage decisions — non-renewals, premium hikes, outright denials — often without the homeowner knowing. The technology confuses cosmetic issues with structural risk, can't tell you just replaced your roof, and operates with nearly zero transparency in most states. A regulatory backlash is building across at least 5 states. For builders and renovators, this creates a "ghost roof" problem: the old roof lives in the insurer's AI system for months after you've replaced it.

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES.
- New construction: your AI roof score starts with zero history, which some algorithms penalize
- Renovation: aerial imagery may still show the OLD roof for 5-24 months after replacement
- Buyers: homes with "bad" AI roof scores may carry inflated insurance premiums that affect affordability
- Builders/contractors: should advise clients to proactively document and submit roof condition to insurers
- Everyone: understanding how to dispute AI-based coverage decisions

- Commissioner Ricardo Lara supporting

- Homeowners can request copies of images used

- Source: insurance.ca.gov

**California AB 1559:**
- Assemblymember Lisa Calderon

- Establishes guardrails for aerial imagery in homeowner insurance underwriting

- Insurance Department issued guidance: insurers CANNOT cancel policies based solely on aerial photos

### 6. Troutman Pepper Locke Analysis — Emerging Regulatory Themes
- Image freshness requirement: 5-24 months (varies by state)
- Key mandate: insurers must distinguish MATERIAL RISKS from COSMETIC FLAWS
- Consumer dispute mechanisms must be offered
- Imagery should complement, not replace, traditional underwriting
- Insurers must retain image copies for potential claims reviews
- Must address potential bias in property condition assessments
- Source: troutman.com, carriermanagement.com

## Novel Contribution
**"Ghost Roof" Window Calculation:**
Cross-reference Troutman Pepper's documented image freshness requirements (5-24 months) with typical renovation/construction timelines:
- Average roof replacement: 1-3 days
- Average new home construction: 7-12 months
- Image freshness standards: 5-24 months
- Result: If you replace your roof today, the insurer's AI could be scoring your OLD roof for up to 24 months. A new home could be scored on images taken during framing.

The "ghost roof" window = time between physical roof completion and the next aerial image capture that the insurer's system ingests. During this window, you're being scored on a roof that no longer exists.

## Strongest Counterargument
Insurers argue aerial imagery REDUCES costs and improves accuracy overall. CAPE claims 95% roof age accuracy. Without AI screening, adjusters would need to physically inspect millions of roofs — expensive and slow. The technology has genuinely reduced fraudulent claims and improved catastrophe response times. The question isn't whether to use AI — it's whether the current implementation has adequate safeguards for the exceptions.

## Limitations
- No publicly available false positive rate for any AI roof scoring system
- CAPE's 95% accuracy claim is self-reported, no third-party audit
- We don't have data on how many total non-renewals are driven by aerial imagery vs. other factors
- State regulations are in various stages of proposal vs. enacted — AB 75 and S.9156 may not pass
- The "ghost roof" calculation uses reported freshness ranges, not verified per-insurer data

# Research: AI Reclaimed Lumber vs. Tariff-Inflated Virgin Lumber

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)

## Core Angle
With Canadian lumber tariffs now stacking to 45% and US sawmills running at 64% capacity, AI-powered reclaimed lumber processing is approaching price competitiveness for residential framing — and the math favors it more every quarter.

## Key Data Points

### Lumber Tariff Situation (2025-2026)
- Canadian lumber duties: 35% AD/CVD (Commerce Dept doubled from 14.5%) + 10% Section 232 = **45% total** (NAHB)
- Section 232 enacted Oct 14, 2025 under national security rationale
- Kitchen cabinets/vanities: 25% tariff, rising to 50% Jan 1, 2027
- US imports ~1/3 of lumber consumed domestically; Canada = 85% of all US lumber imports
- US sawmills operating at only **64% capacity** — domestic production can't fill the gap (NAHB)
- 4 million home national housing shortage (NAHB/Canadian gov submission)

### Current Lumber Prices
- Framing lumber: **$916.62/MBF** (RSMeans April 2026, national average, +5.11% QoQ, +4.21% YoY, 9th straight quarter of YoY growth)
- Western SPF 2×4 #2&Btr KD: **$490/mfbm** (Madison's Lumber Reporter, May 15, 2026); up 10% YoY, up 28% vs. 2 years ago
- Southern Yellow Pine: $470/mfbm (May 2026, down from $561 one month prior)
- Price-hold windows shrinking from 30 days to 14-21 days due to tariff uncertainty

### Wood Waste Scale
- **37 million tons** of demolition wood debris annually in US (EPA 2018)
- **47 billion board feet** consumed annually (Zivid/industry data)
- Vast majority goes to landfill
- Buildings in US store over **7 billion tons of carbon** in timber
- Decomposition in landfill releases stored carbon

### AI Reclaimed Lumber Companies

**Urban Machine (Oakland, CA)**
- AI + robotics de-nailing machine, modular, sits on two 40-foot trailers (80 ft total)
- Capacity: **16,000 board feet per day** (= one single-family home's framing)
- 6,000 sq ft of space needed on jobsite
- 7-8 operators required
- AI uses computer vision to pinpoint metal fasteners; robotic extraction ("Big Bird")
- Quality check after processing
- Seed round 2022: Lower Carbon Capital + Google Ventures (GV)
- Expanding to **6 US metro areas with fleet of 12 systems** by end of next year
- CEO: Eric Law
- Reclaimed lumber commands price **premium** over virgin in markets with green building requirements (LEED credits, Living Building Challenge)
- FSC-certified through partnership with All Bay lumber yard (family-owned since 1984)
- Source: BuildingGreen, Renewable Matter, MCJ podcast, Waste Advantage Magazine, Zivid

**Cambium (Halethorpe, MD)**
- AI-powered wood supply chain platform connecting tree care → trucking → sawmills → manufacturers
- **$28.5M total funding** ($18.5M Series A led by VoLo Earth Ventures, Feb 2025)
- Investors: NEA, Dangerous Ventures, Rise of the Rest, MaC Venture Capital, Google Ventures alumni
- Works with **~350 entities** across supply chain
- Produces "Carbon Smart" wood + cross-laminated timber from salvaged/urban wood
- Partners: Room & Board, Steelcase, Mercer Mass Timber, SmartLam, Sterling Structural, Vaagen Timbers
- AI extracts operational data from phone calls (!) to digitize pen-and-paper businesses
- CEO: Ben Christensen
- "Global wood demand set to **triple by 2050**" — Christensen
- "Every time you move wood 10 miles instead of 1,000, there's a real carbon benefit"
- Source: TechCrunch, Technical.ly

**Maestro / CRA-Carlo Ratti Associati (MIT Senseable City Lab)**
- "AI Timber" method: scans raw logs, flat-saws into boards, uses AI to find optimal fit
- Boards match like puzzle pieces, preserving original tree contour
- Reduces waste in CLT production by **up to 30%**
- Founded by Carlo Ratti (MIT professor, architect) + Mykola Murashko (23-year-old Cambridge grad)
- "Timber isn't just a substitute for concrete; it unlocks new possibilities for prefabricated construction"
- Source: Woodworking Network

**Cornell AR3-Lumber (Academic)**
- AI-powered Reprocessing, Regrading, and Recertification of Salvage Lumber
- Investigators: Felix Heisel (Architecture), Steve Marschner (CS), Matthew Reiter (Civil/Environmental Engineering)
- Funded by Cornell Atkinson Academic Venture Fund
- Goal: scalable AI visual grading for reclaimed lumber in structural applications

- Bridges circular economy principles with practical industry applications
- Source: Cornell Atkinson Center for Sustainability

### Structural Integrity of Reclaimed Wood
- UK CLT "CascadeUp" project: making CLT from reclaimed softwood
- Research (2024, Engineering Structures): secondary timber has lower modulus of rupture (MoR) vs primary
- But: CLT and engineered wood products provide "other opportunities for structural reuse"
- Aged surfaces, nail holes, contaminants reduce strength
- Volumetric losses when converting reclaimed wood to usable product
- This is precisely why AI grading matters — sorting by actual measured properties, not assumed grade

### Market Data
- European reclaimed timber market: **$1.0B → $1.8B**, CAGR 6.0% (FMI)
- Segments: structural timber, boards, beams, panels, joinery
- Recovery methods include "Digital Grading" as a recognized category
- Builders FirstSource (largest US materials supplier) integrating AI across supply chain (IBS 2026)
- AI in construction market: $13B (2026) → $28B by 2031 (HousingWire/industry estimates)

## Original Calculation: Economics of Reclaimed vs. New
- Typical 2,000 sq ft house: ~12,000-16,000 board feet of framing lumber
- At $490/MBF (current SPF spot): **$5,880-$7,840** for framing lumber
- At RSMeans $916.62/MBF (delivered, national average): **$11,000-$14,666** for framing
- The tariff premium: pre-tariff Canadian SPF was ~$383/MBF (2 years ago) → now $490 = +$107/MBF = +28%
- On a 14,000 BF house: tariff premium = ~$1,498 extra
- Reclaimed lumber: material cost is near-zero (sourced from demolition). Processing cost is the variable.
- Urban Machine: 16,000 BF/day with 7-8 operators. Assuming $30/hr fully loaded per operator, 8 operators × 8 hrs × $30 = $1,920/day in labor alone. That's $0.12/BF just for labor.
- Need to add: machine cost/lease, transport, grading, milling, quality losses
- Reclaimed lumber fetches a premium in green building markets (LEED, LBC)
- The math works best in urban areas where demolition source is close to construction site

## Limitations
- No published, third-party cost comparison of reclaimed vs. new at scale
- Urban Machine processing costs not publicly disclosed; labor calc is estimated
- Structural downgrade of reclaimed wood means it may not substitute 1:1 for all applications
- Grading standards for reclaimed structural lumber are still emerging (not yet codified in IRC/IBC)
- Supply reliability is inconsistent — can't guarantee species, grade, or quantity
- "Premium" pricing for reclaimed works in green building markets but not in cost-sensitive production builds

## Strongest Counterargument
Reclaimed lumber's structural reliability is unproven at residential framing scale. The IRC doesn't have a grading standard for salvaged dimensional lumber, and no insurer will underwrite a house framed with lumber that lacks NHLA or ALSC certification. Until a nationally recognized grading standard exists for reclaimed structural lumber, this remains a niche solution for non-structural or engineered-wood applications.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — understanding whether reclaimed lumber is economically viable (and at what cost) directly affects material decisions. For builders pursuing LEED/green certifications, this is especially relevant. For cost-sensitive builders, the article honestly shows where it works and where it doesn't.

# Research: Samsung and LG Are Building Entire AI-Integrated Homes

## Angle
The consumer electronics giants aren't selling appliances *into* homes anymore — they're building the homes themselves. LG's Smart Cottage starts at $134,000, generates 120% of its own energy, and ships from a factory in weeks. Samsung just spent $1.7B on FläktGroup to control HVAC from data centers to living rooms. Meanwhile, NAHB says 1% of U.S. single-family builders use AI to operate equipment, and the average builder rates the likelihood of ever using a construction robot at 1.7 out of 5.

The story: the appliance companies are becoming the builders. And the builders haven't noticed.

### 2. LG Smart Cottage — Product Details (KED Global, Oct 2024 + Built Offsite, Jan 2026)
- **Price:** Starts at 180 million won (~$134,000)
- **Prefab rate:** 70%+ pre-fabricated components
- **Construction time:** Reduced by more than half vs traditional methods
- **Energy:** Mono Plus 26 model achieved energy self-sufficiency rate exceeding 120% — first prefab in Korea to get ZEB Plus (Zero Energy Building Plus) certification from Korea Energy Agency
- **All-electric:** Rooftop solar (4 kW panels → 15 kWh/day), Energy Storage System (ESS), EV charger built in
- **HVAC:** Therma V Monobloc air-to-water heat pump (AWHP), 200L Integrated Water Tank
- **AI appliances:** Refrigerator, induction cooktop, lightwave oven, dishwasher, WashTower, StanbyME display — all managed through LG ThinQ platform
- **IoT security:** Cameras, motion sensors, smart doorbells — integrated into ThinQ
- **Models:** Mono (single-story) and Duo (duplex-style)
- **Materials:** Low-carbon steel from POSCO
- **Construction partner:** GS Engineering & Construction Corp
- **Real deployment:** Juksan Morak experience site — 2 Mono + 2 Duo units, bookings via Movement Lab platform
- **Pre-certification:** Partnered with Korea Electrical Safety Corporation for pre-certification of electrical systems during factory production
- Sources: https://www.kedglobal.com/electronics/newsView/ked202410310005 + https://builtoffsite.com.au/news/lg-smart-cottage-juksan-morak/ + https://koreabizwire.com/lg-targets-modular-housing-boom

### 3. Samsung FläktGroup Acquisition ($1.7B, May 2025)
- Samsung acquired FläktGroup (Germany) for 1.5 billion euros ($1.7B) from PE firm Triton
- Samsung's largest acquisition since $8B Harman purchase in 2017
- FläktGroup: Europe's largest HVAC supplier, founded 1918, HQ Herne Germany
- Revenue: ~700M euros/year (2024 est), ~650M in 2022
- Serves: data centers, cleanrooms, industrial plants, residential/commercial buildings, hospitals, airports
- FläktGroup won Innovation of the Year at 2024 DCS Awards
- Samsung quote: "anticipates sustained growth in data center demand due to proliferation of generative AI, robotics, autonomous driving, XR"
- Data center HVAC segment projected 18% CAGR through 2030
- Also launched Lennox International JV (3rd-largest HVAC in North America) in 2024
- Samsung positions HVAC as "key future growth engine" alongside consumer audio, medical, robotics
- TM Roh (acting head of DX division): "laid the foundation to become a leader in the global HVAC business"
- Source: Reuters via business-reporter.co.uk + Korea JoongAng Daily + KED Global

### 4. HUD $10M Robotics/AI Factory Housing Demonstration (Jun 2026)
- Formally: "Mass Market Solutions for Leveraging Robotics and AI Technologies for Home Construction Demonstration"
- Up to $10M for demonstrations of advanced robotics and AI in factory-built housing and offsite manufacturing
- Separate $3M for automated permitting systems demonstration
- Applications due July 13, 2026
- Minimum award: $3M
- HUD expects to identify successful applicants by September
- "The overall goal is to support technologies that can move beyond a single pilot and be scaled for wider use, contributing to long-term increases in housing supply"
- Source: HousingWire (housingwire.com/articles/hud-robotics-built-housing-permitting/) + Homes.com

### 5. NAHB/Wells Fargo HMI Survey (July 2025)
- 49% of single-family builders use AI "in some capacity"
- But: 20% for advertising/marketing, 11% for market analysis/planning
- **Just 1% use AI to operate construction equipment**
- Equipment AI adoption rated 1.7/5 likelihood in next 2 years (lowest of all categories)
- Interacting with building/planning departments rated 1.9/5 (second lowest)
- Source: NAHB Eye on Housing (eyeonhousing.org) + NAHB.org

### 6. ServiceTitan 2026 Residential State of Trades Report (Apr 2026)
- Survey of 1,000 residential contractors
- 74% view AI as an efficiency engine
- Only 25% currently using AI
- Among users: 48% report increased productivity, 45% report time savings
- 73% believe starting early creates competitive advantage
- Only 12% have embedded AI into operations, 34% experimenting
- 66% expect AI to bring moderate/major transformation in 1-3 years
- Top barriers: lack of training (44%), integration complexity (44%), difficulty understanding tools (38%), unclear ROI (37%)
- Only 18% cited employee resistance
- Source: GlobeNewsWire (ServiceTitan press release, Apr 7, 2026) + servicetitan.com/state-of-ai

### 7. RICS Global AI in Construction Report (2025)
- 45% of construction organizations report NO AI implementation
- 34% in early pilot phases
- Only 12% regular use in specific processes
- Only 1.5% use across multiple processes
- Less than 1% have org-wide embedded AI
- 56% of investors plan to allocate more AI funds vs prior year
- Source: rics.org

### 8. HBI Fall 2025 Construction Labor Market Report

- 3.3 million payroll residential construction workers
- Immigrant workers: 25.5% (new historic high)
- Gen Z participation doubled from 6.4% (2019) to 14.1% (2023)
- Source: NAHB.org

## Actionable Takeaways (Required by STORY_GUIDE)
1. **For production builders ($50M+ annual revenue):** Samsung's Lennox JV + FläktGroup means Samsung will control the HVAC supply chain from factory to installed unit. If you're spec'ing LG or Samsung HVAC now, ask whether they'll prioritize inventory for their own modular homes over your custom orders.
2. **For modular/prefab startups:** LG's $134K price point with 120% energy self-sufficiency sets a performance benchmark your factory needs to meet. The integrated AI platform (ThinQ) also means your buyers will compare your product to a home where every system talks to every other system out of the box.
3. **For custom builders:** The HUD $10M demonstration (applications due July 13) is real funding for companies willing to prove AI/robotics factory production works. If you're already doing any panelized or offsite work, this is worth applying for. Even if you don't win, the federal validation will shift capital toward factory-built housing.
4. **For homeowners planning a build:** Ask your builder what their AI strategy is. If the answer is "we use ChatGPT for marketing," that tells you something about where your per-square-foot costs are going over the next 5 years vs a factory-built alternative.

# Research: AI Scheduling and Extreme Heat in Residential Construction

## Core Data Points

### Heat-Related Injuries & Deaths
- Nearly 28,000 workplace injuries linked to hot weather per year (NCOSH 2026 Dirty Dozen report, ENR May 2026)
- Construction responsible for 33.1% of all heat-related illness (HRI) claims, highest HRI claim rate at 12.1 per 100,000 FTE (Washington State Workers' Comp data 1995-2005)
- 10-15% increase in injuries on 100+°F days across all sectors (Park et al., California study)
- D.R. Horton named in 2026 Dirty Dozen for unsafe practices (NCOSH)
- In 2024: 5,070 fatal work-related injuries in the US (BLS); construction + transportation = 2,423 deaths
- NY State 2024: 55 construction deaths; 4 investigated deaths occurred when temps ≥ 80°F
- Average OSHA fines for construction fatalities: $25,295 in 2024 (lowest since 2017)

### Productivity Loss Research
- **Meta-analysis (14 studies, 2,387 workers):** 60% of construction workers experienced productivity loss when WBGT > 24.23°C (~75.6°F). Higher than other occupations. (SpringerMedizin)
- **Columbia/UCSD (PLOS ONE 2021):** On days above 90°F, high-risk laborers worked 2.6 minutes less per degree above 90. During recession, no association (workers couldn't afford to slow down).
- **Hong Kong rebar study (PMC, 2016):** CLP decreased by 0.33% per 1°C WBGT increase. Adjusted R² = 0.68. Age, work duration, alcohol habits were factors.
- **Cal Poly (2024, Sacramento vs SLO):** Noticeable productivity impacts start at ~95°F, greatest decline in afternoon. Concrete placement often requires early morning starts to manage curing risks. "Regional heat impacts are operational rather than catastrophic."
- **Multifaceted mechanism:** Sweating → dehydration → electrolyte imbalances → fatigue, dizziness. Cardiovascular strain reduces blood flow to muscles. Psychological: anxiety, irritability, impaired decision-making.

### OSHA Proposed Heat Rule
- Two heat index thresholds:
  - 80°F: Provide drinking water and break areas; acclimatization plan for new workers
  - 90°F: Mandatory 15-minute rest breaks every 2 hours; observation system; checks on solo workers; hazard alerts
- "Rule of 20%": New workers work only 20% of normal day on first day in heat, increase by 20% daily
- OSHA estimates heat standards would decrease heat-related illness up to 96% and fatalities up to 100%
- No federal standard exists yet (as of Jul 2026); rule still in rulemaking

### Industry Response
- **Skanska:** Starting work earlier, scheduling demanding activities in cooler morning hours, reassigning crews during hottest periods (Joe Mannino, VP of EHS, ENR Jul 2026)
- **Jacobs:** "Using forecasting tools and heat-safety applications to guide operational decisions" (Brian Morel, Director of HSE)
- **ARTBA/NAPA:** Heat illness prevention plan template for roadway contractors (precautions when heat index ≥ 80°F)
- **ENR Jul 2026:** "Heat risk management is becoming more integrated into project planning, worker education and supervision rather than treated as a seasonal response"

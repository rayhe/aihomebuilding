# Research: Gas Infrastructure as Stranded Asset in New Homes

## Core Problem
Builders across the US are still installing gas infrastructure in new homes — $5,000-$10,000 per home for gas line extension alone (RMI/PG&E estimate: $7,050-$9,500 avg for single-family) — even as state mandates for all-electric construction multiply. This creates a stranded asset: gas infrastructure that will be ripped out and replaced within 15-25 years.

## State Mandates (Current as of July 2026)
- **New York:** First state to mandate all-electric new construction. Building Code Council approved Aug 2025. Effective Dec 31, 2025 for buildings ≤7 stories (with exceptions). Expands to ALL new buildings by Dec 31, 2028. (CoStar, NY Now)
- **San Francisco:** AB-112 approved April 15, 2026. Major renovations must go all-electric starting July 1, 2026. New construction already required since June 2021.
- **California CPUC:** Eliminated all gas line extension subsidies for mixed-fuel new construction (July 2024 for gas subsidies, Jan 2025 for actual cost billing). State disincentivizes gas in all new builds.
- **Washington, DC; Berkeley, CA; multiple other cities** have had natural gas bans in new construction (some struck down by courts, some proceeding)

## RMI Key Findings (2025 updated analysis)
- All-electric single-family new construction is MORE ECONOMICAL to build and operate than mixed-fuel in ALL 9 cities studied: Austin, Boston, Columbus, Denver, Minneapolis, NYC, Seattle + others
- In most cities (6 of 7 in one study), mixed-fuel homes cost MORE upfront than all-electric (because gas homes need both furnace AND AC, while heat pump does both)
- Minneapolis exception: cold climate needs higher-capacity heat pump, slightly higher upfront cost, but still lower 15-year net present cost
- Annual utility savings up to 9% for all-electric (Minneapolis)
- Carbon savings: 50-93% lower over 15 years in all cities studied
- E3 study confirms: "all-electric new construction delivers lifecycle savings relative to a mixed-fuel home with AC"

## Cost Breakdown: Gas vs All-Electric New Construction
### Gas path ("build gas now"):
- Gas line extension to home: $5,000-$10,000 (PG&E avg $7,050-$9,500)
- Gas furnace: $2,500-$5,000 installed
- Gas water heater: $1,200-$2,500 installed
- Gas range: $800-$2,000
- Gas dryer connection: $200-$500
- Air conditioner (still needed): $3,500-$7,000
- **Total gas infrastructure:** ~$13,200-$27,000

### All-electric path:
- Heat pump (heating + cooling in one unit): $4,000-$8,000
- Heat pump water heater: $1,500-$3,000
- Induction range: $1,000-$2,500
- Electric dryer: $600-$1,200
- No gas line: $0
- Panel upgrade (if needed for 200A): $1,500-$4,000
- **Total all-electric:** ~$8,600-$18,700

### Retrofit cost later (gas → electric):
- Remove gas infrastructure: $1,000-$3,000
- Install heat pump: $4,000-$8,000
- Heat pump water heater swap: $2,000-$4,000
- Panel upgrade: $2,000-$4,000
- Induction range + rewiring: $1,500-$3,000
- **Total retrofit:** ~$10,500-$22,000

### The stranded asset math:
Gas path + later retrofit = $23,700-$49,000
All-electric from day one = $8,600-$18,700
**Delta: $15,100-$30,300 wasted per home**

## Dwell Development Case Study (Seattle)
- Anthony Maschmedt, principal
- Stopped installing gas lines in 2016 (before any mandate)
- Built ~250 Zero Energy Ready homes, 50+ all-electric
- **Saves $2,500-$3,500 per house** by not installing gas piping
- Found that Passive House-tight homes overheat from just a gas fireplace pilot light
- Uses ductless mini-splits for heating/cooling, HPWHs for hot water

## The Grid Concern
- NY State Builders Association (Mike Fazio): "The infrastructure is simply not ready for all-electric mandates"
- Grid strain is real — aging infrastructure, data center demand, housing developers
- But new homes with heat pumps actually reduce PEAK demand because heat pumps are 3-5x more efficient than resistance heating
- Solar + battery can offset grid concerns

## AI/Software Angle
- RMI uses EnergyPlus + RSMeans construction costing for city-specific analysis
- AI energy modeling tools can calculate exact cost differential for ANY project in ANY market
- Builders can use these tools to show clients: "building all-electric saves you $X compared to gas"
- Tools: NREL BEopt, EnergyPlus surrogates, RMI's methodology (replicable by design)
- Conduit Tech (acquired by ServiceTitan) — can model HVAC systems in the field with LiDAR

## Original Contribution
The $15,100-$30,300 per-home stranded asset calculation above. Nobody else has built this specific comparison including the retrofit cost. Most analyses compare gas vs. electric upfront only, or compare operating costs only. The full lifecycle including mandatory retrofit is the number builders should be quoting.

National scale: ~1.4M single-family homes started per year. If even 30% are still being built with gas in states that will mandate electrification within 15 years, that's 420,000 homes × $15,000-$30,000 = **$6.3-$12.6 billion per year in avoidable stranded asset creation.**

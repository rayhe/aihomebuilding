# Research: Agentic AI Construction "Coworkers" — The Residential Gap

## Thesis
Procore just launched AI agents that execute real construction workflows — filing RFIs, reviewing submittals, drafting daily logs — not just answering questions. The testimonials are impressive (12 hours saved on 10 submittals, caught errors avoiding a week of delays). But Procore costs $18K-$150K/year. Residential builders using Buildertrend ($199/month) or spreadsheets have zero agentic AI. The question for anyone building a custom home: does the gap matter, and who pays for it?

## Original Contribution (Novel Analysis)

### Break-even Calculation: When Does AI Construction Management Pay for Itself?

**Inputs:**
- Procore base cost for small builder: $18,000/year (conservative low end)
- AI agent credit consumption: estimated $3,000-$6,000/year additional (based on credit model patterns)
- Total AI-capable platform: ~$21,000-$24,000/year
- Buildertrend (no AI): $2,388/year ($199/month)
- Incremental cost for AI capability: ~$18,600-$21,600/year

**Rework prevention math:**
- Custom home at $500K, rework rate 5%: $25,000 in rework costs annually per project
- If AI catches ONE major spec conflict per year (saving just one full rework event): $25,000 saved
- But most builders run 3-5 projects: total rework exposure $75K-$125K/year at 5%
- Even a 25% reduction (consistent with QA/QC improvement data): $18,750-$31,250 saved

**Break-even:**
- At $21K incremental cost, break-even requires preventing ~$21K in rework
- For a builder doing $2M in annual construction volume at 5% rework rate: $100K in rework exposure
- Need to prevent 21% of rework to break even → plausible but not guaranteed
- For a builder doing $500K in annual volume: $25K exposure → need to prevent 84% of rework → NOT feasible

**Conclusion:** AI construction management tools break even at roughly $2M+ annual construction volume, assuming moderate rework prevention effectiveness. Below that, the math doesn't work — which excludes most custom home builders doing 2-3 homes per year at $400-600K each.

# Research: Homebuilder Warranty Reserves vs. AI Construction Monitoring

## Thesis
America's 27 publicly traded homebuilders spent $1.071 billion fixing defective new homes in 2024. They budget less than 1% of revenue for warranty repairs. AI construction monitoring tools can catch defects during the build — reducing claims by up to 75% — but builders haven't connected their warranty actuarial data to their construction QA spending. The result: builders keep paying to fix what technology could have prevented, and homeowners absorb the worst costs after the warranty clock runs out.

## Original Contribution
Cross-referencing SEC warranty accrual disclosures with the cost of available AI QA monitoring tools to calculate the break-even point: at what home volume does AI monitoring cost less than the warranty claims it prevents? If WINT reduces claims 75%, and warranty reserves are ~$2,348/home, the savings per home are ~$1,761. A monitoring system at $3-5K/month deployed over an 18-month build of 100 homes works out to $540-$900/home — well below the projected savings. The math says yes. The industry says no.

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES. If you're buying from a production builder, you can look up their warranty reserve per home in their SEC filing. If it's under $3,000, the builder is betting they won't need to fix much. Ask whether they use AI construction monitoring during the build. If they don't, their QA system relies on site walks and manual inspections — the same system that misses 25% of defects.

# Research Notes: Data Center Construction Is Draining Electricians From Residential Building

## Slug: data-center-electrician-drain-housing
## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)
## Category: Workforce & Labor

## Angle
The AI data center building boom is siphoning electricians away from residential construction, worsening the housing crisis. The irony: AI promises to automate construction — but first, it needs humans to build the physical infrastructure. Those humans used to wire your house.

## Primary Sources

### 1. The Texas Tribune / Governing.com — "Data Centers Strain Electrician Workforce in Texas" (2026)
- **Gene Lantrip**, 69, Abilene homebuilder: Lost lead electricians and helpers to nearby Stargate data center (OpenAI, Crusoe, Oracle — 4M sq ft)
- Homes now take **2 extra months** to build vs. before data centers
- Data centers offer **double wages** vs. what his subcontractors pay
- "My electrician lost two of his lead men and several of his helpers to the data center. Of course, the guys got to do good for their families."
- **Scotty Wristen**, owner of WE Electric, Abilene: Lost **5 workers** to data centers (employees of 5-8 years). Pays $20/hr; data centers pay $35/hr + overtime + per diem
- Now hiring **teens straight out of high school** as apprentices: "4-5 months of hell where we have little mistakes"
- Texas: 300+ data centers operating, ~100 more planned. 71,000 electricians employed.
- Texas loosening reciprocity with Iowa, Alabama, Arkansas to import electricians

### 2. Associated Builders and Contractors (ABC) — 2026 Workforce Analysis
- Construction industry needs **349,000 additional workers in 2026**
- Demand for electricians "surged due to rapid increase in data center construction"
- **1 in 5 electricians over 55** (20%)
- Data center buildouts causing **8.5-month backlogs** (ABC)
- "Skilled trades workers, particularly electricians, are a huge constraint" — Anirban Basu, ABC chief economist

### 3. NAHB / Home Builders Institute (Fox Business, Jul 12, 2026)
- Construction industry short **~250,000 workers monthly** (Jim Tobin, NAHB CEO)
- Need **723,000 new workers annually** to close 1.5M-home housing gap
- Shortage adds **nearly 2 extra months** to building timelines (Ed Brady, HBI)
- "Labor is one of the largest and most expensive inputs when it comes to home production"

### 4. Data Center Dynamics / Deloitte — "Construction Worker Shortage Could Hamper US Data Center Build-Out" (May 2026)
- Data center buildout spending: **$380 billion between 2025-2030** (Deloitte)
- Mid-sized data center: **$500M-$2B** construction cost
- **30% of construction workers** are foreign-born (immigration crackdown complicates hiring)
- Entry-level data center engineering positions: **$60,000-$80,000/yr** — well above national average for junior roles
- **20,000 electricians leaving** workforce annually; 1 in 3 between ages 50-70

### 5. IBEW / BLS Data
- **45-70%** of data center construction budget goes to electrical subcontractors (IBEW)
- Residential electricians: avg **$58,200/yr**; Industrial: avg **$71,500/yr** (SpliceJobs 2026 Salary Index)
- Data center capacity: **24 GW → 110 GW by 2030** (Wood Mackenzie via Reuters)

### 6. BlackRock Future Builders (Jul 2026)
- **$100M over 5 years** to train 50,000 skilled trades workers — but focused on data center/energy infrastructure, NOT residential
- Larry Fink: America should plan for **$10 trillion** in data center + energy infrastructure spending
- Targets electricians, welders, plumbers, HVAC techs, wiremen

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** If you're planning a remodel, building a custom home, or wondering why your GC's electrical sub keeps rescheduling: the data center next county over is paying double for the same hands. This piece gives readers the economic context behind rising residential electrical costs and longer timelines, and the structural reasons it won't fix itself soon.

## Key Tension
BlackRock and Microsoft are spending $100M+ to train more tradespeople — but the training pipelines feed data center construction, not residential. Electrician apprenticeships take 4-5 years. The housing gap needs 723,000 workers annually. The pipeline math doesn't work for homebuilders.

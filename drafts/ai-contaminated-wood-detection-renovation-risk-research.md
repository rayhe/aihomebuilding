# Research: AI Detection of Contaminated Wood in Residential Renovation & Demolition

## Angle
Millions of pre-2004 homes still contain CCA-treated wood (decks, fences, playsets, landscaping timbers). When homeowners renovate or demolish, this arsenic-laced lumber enters the waste stream undifferentiated. New AI vision systems can detect contaminated wood with 91% accuracy from regular photos. But almost nobody in residential construction uses them, and most wood waste goes to landfill unsorted.

## Journalist
Priya Greenwood — sustainability, green building, evidence-based urgency

## Kill Test
✅ Passes. If you're renovating a home built before 2004, tearing down a deck, or building on a lot with old structures, this directly affects your family's health and your disposal costs. Actionable: test before demolition, separate wood waste streams, know what AI tools exist.

## Key Sources

### 1. EPA — Construction & Demolition Debris Data (2018)
- 600 million tons of C&D debris generated in the US in 2018
- More than twice the amount of municipal solid waste
- 143.78 million tons sent to landfill
- 456.55 million tons directed to next use (manufactured products, aggregate, soil amendment, etc.)
- Demolition = 90%+ of total C&D debris; new construction < 10%
- EPA study of 15 houses: average 6.14 lbs waste/sq ft (range 2.41–11.30)
- Wood products are one of the largest C&D waste streams
- Source: https://www.epa.gov/smm/sustainable-management-construction-and-demolition-materials

### 2. EPA — Wood in MSW (2018)
- 18.1 million tons of wood generated in MSW (6.2% of total MSW)
- Only 3.1 million tons recycled (17.1% recycling rate)
- 12.2 million tons landfilled (8.3% of all MSW landfilled)
- 2.8 million tons combusted with energy recovery
- Source: https://www.epa.gov/facts-and-figures-about-materials-waste-and-recycling/wood-material-specific-data

### 3. Monash University/CDU — AI Contaminated Wood Detection (June 2025)
- First real-world image dataset of contaminated construction and demolition wood waste
- Deep learning models (CNNs + Transformers) trained on RGB images
- 91% accuracy detecting 6 types of wood contamination (paint, chemicals, metals, residues)
- Can be deployed via camera-enabled sorting lines, drones, or handheld tools
- Published in Resources, Conservation & Recycling (DOI: 10.1016/j.resconrec.2025.108278)
- Lead: Madini De Alwis (PhD candidate) with Dr Milad Bazli (CDU), supervised by Assoc. Prof. Mehrdad Arashpour (Monash)
- Source: https://www.monash.edu/news/articles/ai-detects-contaminated-construction-wood-with-91-accuracy

### 4. NPIC/EPA — CCA-Treated Wood Health Risks
- CCA = Chromated Copper Arsenate (chromium, copper, arsenic)
- Used in US pressure-treated lumber since 1940s
- EPA and industry agreed to stop residential use starting 2004
- Pre-2004: widely used for decks, playsets, picnic tables, fences, landscaping
- Arsenic leaches to wood surface; exposure via skin contact + hand-to-mouth
- Health risks: skin cancer, bladder cancer, liver cancer, lung cancer (long-term low-level ingestion)
- Environmental: leaches into surrounding soil 6 inches to 8 feet
- EPA: existing CCA structures don't need removal but take steps to reduce exposure
- Source: http://npic.orst.edu/faq/cca.html

### 5. ZenRobotics 4.0 — AI Waste Sorting Robots
- Heavy Picker 4.0: 2,300 picks/hour, items up to 40 kg, multipurpose C&D sorting
- Fast Picker 4.0: 80 picks/minute, lightweight materials up to 1 kg
- AI recognizes 500+ waste categories with pretrained global database
- Compact ZenBrain recognition system: 60-100% efficiency improvement over prior generation
- Handles C&D, C&I, wood, scrap, rigid plastics
- Owned by Terex (industrial equipment company)
- Source: https://www.terex.com/zenrobotics

### 6. Pennsylvania DEP — Construction Waste Data
- Average waste generation: 6.14 lbs/sq ft (EPA 15-house study)
- Range: 2.41 to 11.30 lbs/sq ft
- 12% of gypsum board wasted during installation
- Disposal costs: up to 6% of total project cost
- Primary construction waste: gypsum board, cardboard, metal, wood
- Source: https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dep/programs-and-services/waste-programs/solid-waste-programs/municipal-waste-program/construction-and-demolition-waste/construction-waste

### 7. NIR Spectroscopy for C&D Recycling (Spectroscopy Online, April 2026)
- Near-infrared spectroscopy + AI for automated classification of construction materials
- Emerging technique for sorting mixed C&D waste streams at material level
- Complements vision-based systems (different detection modality)
- Source: https://www.SpectroscopyOnline.com/view/recycling-of-construction-waste-with-near-infrared-spectroscopy

## Actionable Takeaways
1. If renovating a pre-2004 home: test wood structures (decks, fences, play equipment) for CCA before demolition
2. Separate wood waste by type on site — contaminated vs clean dramatically changes disposal options
3. AI sorting exists commercially (ZenRobotics) but is deployed at MRFs, not on residential job sites yet
4. Phone-based AI detection (Monash research) could become a handheld tool — the dataset is published
5. Disposal cost of contaminated wood is 3-5x clean wood — knowing what you have saves money
6. Ask your waste hauler if they sort C&D wood — most don't, and clean wood has value as biomass fuel or mulch

# Research: Heat Pump Oversizing & AI Load Calculation Gap

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)

## Thesis
The massive heat pump transition (outselling furnaces by 30% in 2024, 25M US homes) is colliding with a contractor workforce that overwhelmingly can't size them properly. AI-powered load calculation tools exist but penetration is pathetically low. The result: millions of oversized heat pumps wasting the very energy they were supposed to save.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — directly actionable for anyone installing a heat pump. Tells them what to demand from their contractor, what software to ask about, and how to verify sizing.

## Primary Sources

### 1. ACCA/Farmington Consulting Group — Contractor of the Future Study (2025)
- Survey of 1,000+ contractors nationwide
- 16% of contractors don't do load calculations AT ALL
- Of those who do: 65% use software, 35% manual (rules of thumb)
- Wrightsoft #1 software (27% market), Cool Calc #2 (15%), Elite RHVAC (6%), Adtek (4%)
- Among ACCA members: 42% Wrightsoft, 15% Cool Calc
- Source: ACHR News, April 2026

### 2. Energy Vanguard — Manual J Data (Allison Bailes III, PhD)
- 40 buildings analyzed with Manual J
- Average cooling load: 1,431 sf/ton vs 500 sf/ton rule of thumb
- Not a single load calculation as low as the high end of the rule-of-thumb range (600 sf/ton)
- Lowest in sample: 624 sf/ton
- Implication: systems sized by rules of thumb are ~2-3x oversized
- Source: Green Building Advisor, September 2016 (validated in 2026 follow-up)

### 3. Energy Vanguard — Savannah Case Study (March 2026)
- 2,600 sq ft home in Savannah, GA
- Old gas furnace: 89,000 BTU/hr output
- Manual J heating load: 60,000 BTU/hr (furnace 1.5x oversized)
- Furnace runtime measurement: 46,000 BTU/hr actual need (furnace 2x oversized)
- New heat pump runtime: ~30,000 BTU/hr actual need (old furnace was 3x oversized)
- Manual J itself inflated the load by 2x vs real-world heat pump performance
- Source: Energy Vanguard blog, March 17, 2026

### 4. DOE/ORNL HVAC Sizing Research
- Oversizing leads to: reduced efficiency, increased wear, increased indoor humidity in summer
- Building energy codes could incorporate sizing requirements but largely don't enforce
- Source: DOE CMEI, FY16 research project

### 5. Rutgers NJ Green Building Manual
- HVAC systems consume 48% of US home energy
- "often oversized due to extreme loads" — common industry practice
- Source: Rutgers greenmanual

### 6. EPA Data
- 60% of buildings have oversized HVAC systems
- Source: FacilitiesNet (citing EPA)

### 7. ACHR News — Heat Pump Market Data (2024)
- Heat pumps outselling furnaces by 30% in 2024
- 25 million US homes currently using heat pumps
- Contractors must educate themselves on capabilities
- Source: ACHR News, 2025

### 8. ServiceTitan — HVAC AI Landscape (2026)
- 60% of HVAC contractors familiar with AI
- Only 12% embedding AI in workflows
- AI used for scheduling, diagnostics, customer management — NOT for load calculations
- Source: ServiceTitan blog, 2026

### 9. Johns Hopkins — Decision-Focused Learning for HVAC
- AI model predicts HVAC energy costs with precision
- Outperforms traditional physics-based models
- Published in E-Energy '25
- Source: Johns Hopkins Energy Institute

### 10. Kwik Model 3D — LiDAR Integration
- Can import LiDAR scans from phones (iPhone Pro, iPad Pro)

- Source: YouTube tutorial

## Original Contribution (Calculation)
National energy waste from heat pump oversizing:
- 25M US homes with heat pumps
- ~60% oversized (EPA figure for HVAC generally)
- 15M homes with oversized heat pumps
- Average US household: $2,000/yr on heating+cooling (EIA)
- Oversized system: 15-30% efficiency penalty (conservative estimate based on DOE/ORNL research, short-cycling losses, defrost penalties)
- Mid-range penalty: 22.5%
- 15M × $2,000 × 22.5% = $6.75 billion/year in wasted energy
- Even at 10% penalty: $3 billion/year

IRA is spending $8.8B on heat pump rebates. If oversizing wastes $3-7B annually, the rebate money burns off in 1-3 years of efficiency losses from bad sizing.

## Strongest Counterargument
Variable-capacity (inverter-driven) heat pumps mitigate oversizing penalties significantly. They can modulate down to 30-40% of rated capacity, avoiding the brutal short-cycling of single-stage units. The oversizing problem is most acute for single-stage systems, which are declining in market share. Counter-counter: even variable-capacity units lose efficiency at minimum modulation, and oversizing pushes them to operate in less efficient low-output ranges more often.

## Limitations
- The $6.75B figure uses the 60% oversizing rate from EPA building data (not heat-pump-specific)
- Energy Vanguard data sample is 40 buildings, mostly Southeast US
- Actual efficiency penalty varies enormously by climate, system type, and degree of oversizing
- No nationwide study exists specifically measuring heat pump oversizing rates
- The 15-30% efficiency penalty is synthesized from multiple sources, not from a single controlled study

## Angle
Don't write "AI will fix HVAC." Write: your heat pump is probably oversized. Your contractor probably doesn't use load calculation software. The tools exist and they're getting better with AI. But the industry refuses to adopt them. Here's exactly what to demand.

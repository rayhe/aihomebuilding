# Research: AI Scheduling for Residential Builders — The $670/Day Math

## Journalist: Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca (Project Management & Operations)

## Angle
Every day a residential build runs late costs the builder ~$670 in carrying costs alone. 35% of projects run late. AI scheduling tools claim 20-30% delay reduction. But ALICE Technologies costs enterprise pricing and targets infrastructure/commercial. What actually works for a 15-home/year custom builder? Is this another tech solution for a people problem?

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — directly addresses why homes are late and what tools (if any) can actually compress schedules for residential builders specifically.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Census Bureau Survey of Construction (via NAHB Eye on Housing, Sept 2025)
- Average time permit-to-completion: 9.1 months (2024 data)
- Built-for-sale: 7.6 months (fastest)
- Owner-built: 15.1 months (slowest)
- Down from pandemic peaks but still ~2 months longer than 2015
- Regional variation: South Atlantic fastest (7.8 mo), Middle Atlantic slowest (13.7 mo)
- Source: https://eyeonhousing.org/2025/09/single-family-homes-are-built-faster-in-2024/

### 2. Association of Professional Builders — SORCI Report 2023
- 35.1% of residential building projects ran late in 2022
- Average $750K build costs builder ~$670/day in delay (carrying costs, overhead, lost opportunity)
- 48.3% of builders don't know their fixed expenses per job per day
- Source: https://blog.associationofprofessionalbuilders.com/true-cost-of-project-delay

### 3. ALICE Technologies — AI Construction Scheduling
- AI simulates thousands of scheduling scenarios, optimizes critical path
- Case study: 45-story high-rise, 20% time reduction, 15% cost savings
- Enterprise pricing (quote-based), designed for commercial/infrastructure projects
- Claims 17% average schedule compression across projects
- Source: https://www.contechnews.com/blog/advanced_project_scheduling_with_ai_by_alice_technologies

### 4. Handoff.ai — AI Estimating for Residential Builders
- AI-powered takeoff and estimating (4-8 minutes per custom home)
- 60M+ SKUs tracked across suppliers
- Scheduling features exist but secondary to estimating
- Works for residential: remodelers, custom builders, GCs
- NOT an AI scheduling optimizer — it's an estimating tool with scheduling bolt-on
- Source: https://www.handoff.ai/builders

### 5. Springer/Academic — ML Construction Delay Prediction
- Machine learning models achieve 85%+ accuracy predicting delays using historical data
- Key predictive variables: technical projections, resource optimization, project controllability
- Uses BIM integration for enhanced prediction
- Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44290-025-00316-7

### 6. Subcontractor Delay Causes (ArchUp, May 2025)
- Top causes: inaccurate scheduling, payment issues, labor shortages, material delivery delays, poor communication
- Cascade/domino effect: one sub delay affects all downstream trades
- Source: https://archup.net/subcontractor-delays-construction/

### 7. AI Building Tools — AI Construction Scheduling Overview
- Claims AI scheduling can cut delays by 30%
- Weather integration, resource optimization, conflict detection
- Most tools target commercial scale
- Source: https://aibuildingtools.com/blog/ai-construction-scheduling

## Key Tensions for the Article
1. The $670/day math is devastating — most builders don't track it
2. AI scheduling tools DO work but are designed for commercial ($1B+ projects)
3. Residential builders have 15-30 subs on a custom home — scheduling is a coordination nightmare
4. The actual residential scheduling tools (BuilderTrend, CoConstruct, Handoff) aren't doing AI scheduling optimization — they're doing digital Gantt charts with notifications
5. ALICE-style AI scheduling for a $750K custom home is like using a cruise missile to open a can of beans
6. The real gap: nobody has built an AI scheduler specifically for residential construction's unique constraints (weather-dependent, sub-dependent, inspection-dependent)
7. A 15-home/year builder can't justify $50K+ enterprise software, but their $670/day bleed is real

## What This Analysis Should Prove
- How much schedule delays actually cost residential builders (carrying costs, mortgage locks, opportunity cost)
- What current AI scheduling tools can and can't do for residential
- The gap between commercial AI scheduling and residential needs
- What a residential builder should actually do today to compress schedules
- Whether ML delay prediction models translate to residential scale

## Strongest Counterargument
Residential scheduling is fundamentally a relationship/coordination problem, not an optimization problem. The builder who takes his framing crew to lunch and keeps his subs paid on time will always outperform the builder with the fanciest AI scheduler. Software can't fix a contractor who doesn't answer his phone.

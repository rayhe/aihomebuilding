# Research: AI Renovation Estimates vs. Reality — The Budget Overrun Gap

## Core Thesis
AI renovation cost estimators (ChatGPT, Zillow, Handoff AI, Redfin) are now the first tool homebuyers consult when evaluating fixer-uppers. But they can't go inside the house. They estimate from room dimensions, photographs, and national averages. The result: 78% of homeowners go over budget, with 44% exceeding estimates by $5,000+ and 35% by $10,000+. The AI gave them a number. The contractor gave them a different one. The house gave them a third.

## Original Contribution: The Three-Number Problem

When a homeowner types "How much does a kitchen remodel cost?" into AI, they get a number. When a contractor walks through the house, they quote a different number. When the project is done, the final invoice is a third number. Nobody's number matched.

Calculate the typical gap using Clever data:
- Average homeowner renovation spend: ~$10,000-15,000 (based on 36% spending $10K+ and 45% spending $5K+)
- 78% went over budget
- 44% of overspenders were $5,000+ over → at minimum ~34% of all renovators were $5K+ over
- 35% of overspenders were $10,000+ over → ~27% of all renovators were $10K+ over

The AI estimate is the FIRST number, which anchors the homeowner's expectations. The contractor quote is the SECOND. The final invoice is the THIRD. Each is calculated differently:
- AI: national/regional averages × room dimensions × material selection
- Contractor: physical walkthrough + experience + local subcontractor rates + code requirements + contingency
- Invoice: actual labor hours + actual materials + change orders + discovered conditions

What AI literally cannot assess:
1. Electrical panel capacity (can the house support the new kitchen?)
2. Plumbing condition behind walls (galvanized? polybutylene? cast iron?)
3. Asbestos/lead in materials (pre-1978 homes)
4. Structural adequacy for wall removal (is it load-bearing?)
5. Code compliance of existing systems (grandfather clause expires with renovation)
6. Cascading scope: opening a wall reveals knob-and-tube wiring, which triggers full electrical upgrade, which requires panel upgrade, which requires utility coordination
7. Permit timeline and cost (varies 3x-10x by jurisdiction)
8. Local labor availability and pricing (AI uses averages; actual quotes depend on contractor backlog)

## Skepticism
- AI estimators serve a legitimate purpose as "order of magnitude" tools — they tell you whether you're looking at $10K or $100K. The problem isn't that they exist; it's that consumers treat them as quotes.
- Better tools (Handoff AI with ZIP-code localization) are improving accuracy.
- Contractor quotes also vary wildly — 3 quotes for the same project can differ by 50%.
- The overrun problem predates AI. People have always underestimated renovation costs.
- The original sin may be consumer behavior, not AI accuracy.

# Research Notes: Shingle Lifetime Warranty vs. Climate-Dependent Durability

## Core Thesis
The "lifetime" roof shingle warranty is a legal construct, not a performance guarantee. Manufacturers know climate, installation, and ventilation determine actual lifespan — and the data to predict it exists — but no consumer-facing tool converts warranty marketing into a location-specific durability estimate. An algorithm could. Nobody sells one.

## Key Data Points

### Market Size
- Asphalt shingles cover 80%+ of North American residential steep-slope roofs (ASTM/NRC source)
- The roofing market is ~$30B+ annually in North America

### The Warranty Shell Game
- GAF: "Lifetime" = 600 months for proration purposes (years 11-40). Years 41+, GAF pays 20% max
- GAF: 10-year "SureStart" period = non-prorated. After that, coverage = (months elapsed / 600) reduction
  - At year 25 (300 months): 300/600 = 50% reduction → manufacturer covers 50% of shingle cost only
  - No labor, no tear-off, no disposal, no flashings, no vents, no metal work, no damage repair
- Owens Corning: "TRU PROtection" for ~10 years. Then prorates at ~2%/year. At year 15 = 72% of material cost only
- CertainTeed: Same structure. Front-loaded 10-year full coverage, then proration kicks in
- After SureStart ends, actual coverage ≈ 26% of total roof replacement cost (source: New Heights Roofing analysis)
- Non-single-family buildings get a hard 40-year cap, not "lifetime"
- Industry renamed "30-year" and "50-year" labels to "lifetime" circa 2010s

### Actual Performance Data
- IBHS 20-Year Roof Aging Initiative: Performance of ALL products "deteriorated significantly after 5 years of natural weathering" (hail impact resistance). Christopher Sanders, IBHS, GSA 2022
- NRC Canada long-term study (ASTM): 325+ specimens from 4 sources, 3 aging processes
  - Field-aged shingles: max reduction of OVER 50% in tear strength
  - "Majority of evaluated properties after aging no longer met minimum requirements specified by North American standards"
  - Proposed "climate-dependent durability index" framework — not commercialized
- Actual lifespan range: 14-21 years (EPA/recycling research), 20-30 years (typical real-world), 25-40 years (premium in ideal conditions)
- UV aging study (MDPI): G*/sinδ of asphalt increased 214-442% after 16 days accelerated aging — asphalt becomes brittle

### Climate Impact
- UV exposure: Phoenix homes get ~5,800 kWh/m²/year solar radiation vs ~3,400 in Seattle. Shingles age 2-3x faster
- Thermal cycling: Midwest homes see 100+ freeze-thaw cycles/year. Each cycle stresses shingle structure
- Humidity: Southeast humidity + warm temps accelerate biological growth and moisture retention
- Wind: Gulf Coast/hurricane zones see sustained high winds that lift shingle tabs
- Hail: IBHS data shows even impact-rated shingles lose performance rapidly with field aging

### The AI Gap
- NRC's "climate-dependent durability index" framework exists in academic form but isn't available as a tool
- No consumer-facing app predicts actual shingle lifespan for a specific home
- Data exists: warranty claims (manufacturers have this), climate records (NOAA), installation records (permit databases), satellite imagery (roof condition)
- Machine learning could combine these to predict: "This roof in this climate zone with this ventilation setup will functionally fail at year X"
- Closest tools: IBHS FORTIFIED certification (qualitative), GAF WeatherStopper (marketing), satellite AI roof scoring (insurance-focused, not durability-focused)

### Actionable Takeaways for Homeowners/Builders
- Read the proration schedule before choosing a warranty tier
- After year 10, your "lifetime" warranty covers roughly 1/4 of actual replacement cost
- Attic ventilation is the #1 controllable factor affecting shingle life. Poor ventilation can halve expected life
- In high-UV climates (Southwest): expect 15-25 years from a "lifetime" shingle
- In moderate climates (Pacific Northwest, Northeast): 25-35 years is realistic
- The real warranty comparison: compare non-prorated period length and workmanship coverage, not "lifetime" vs "50-year"

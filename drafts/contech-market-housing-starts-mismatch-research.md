# Research: ConTech Market Growth vs. Housing Starts Decline

## Core Thesis
The construction technology industry is growing at 13-16% annually, selling tools that make building faster and more predictable. Meanwhile, the builders who are supposed to use those tools just posted their third straight monthly decline in housing starts, with 10.3 months of unsold new-home inventory — the highest since 2009. The AI can compress your schedule from 14 months to 10. But the builder sitting on 10 months of unsold homes doesn't need a faster schedule. He needs a buyer.

## Original Analysis: The Technology-Market Mismatch

**ConTech is solving a supply-side problem in a demand-constrained market.**

The math:
- ConTech market growing ~$1B/year (~$5.9B to $6.9B from 2025 to 2026)
- Meanwhile SF housing starts DOWN 3.2% y/y
- SF permits DOWN 0.2% y/y (forward indicator)
- New home sales DOWN 6.8% y/y
- Unsold inventory at 10.3 months (2× healthy range of 4-6 months)

The tools optimize *how* you build: faster schedules, tighter cost estimates, fewer rework events, compressed timelines. But the market problem is *whether* you build. When a builder has 10 months of unsold homes, shaving 4 months off the next build timeline isn't the solution. It's just building unsold inventory faster.

**The ConTech customer mismatch:**
- 29.08% of ConTech revenue comes from Infrastructure/Heavy-Civil (highest growth segment)
- Data center construction up 23% (the paying customer)
- Single-family residential? 1% AI adoption rate (NAHB)
- The tools are being built for the customers who can pay — and those customers aren't building houses

**The counter-argument (strongest version):**
The builders who *are* building right now need efficiency more than ever. With 37% cutting prices and 63% offering incentives, margins are razor-thin. AI cost estimation and scheduling tools help them survive a downturn, not just thrive in a boom. The technology isn't irrelevant — it's a survival tool for the builders who can't afford a single wasted day on a project they're already selling at a discount.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're a builder deciding whether to invest in AI scheduling tools right now, this article tells you: the tools work, but they can't fix your demand problem. Optimize your existing pipeline; don't assume faster builds will generate faster sales. If you're a homebuyer, it explains why even with all this construction technology, new homes aren't getting cheaper — because the problem is mortgage rates and buyer psychology, not build efficiency.

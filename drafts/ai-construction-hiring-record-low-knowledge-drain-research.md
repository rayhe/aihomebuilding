# Research: Construction's Record-Low Hiring Rate Meets Knowledge Drain Crisis

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)
## Date: May 10, 2026

## Core Angle
The construction hiring rate hit 3.3% in February 2026 — the lowest since JOLTS began tracking in 2000. One in five workers is over 55. The industry's solution? AI tools that help people find documents faster. But the real knowledge leaving with retiring superintendents isn't in any database. It's tacit expertise: which inspector is flexible on egress interpretations, which soil shifts after rain, which subs actually show up. AI copilots address the searchable half of the knowledge problem. Nobody's measuring the unsearchable half.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — if you're a builder losing experienced supers, or a homeowner wondering why your new GC's crew keeps making mistakes the old crew never did. The knowledge drain directly increases rework, delays, and cost overruns on residential projects.

## Novel Contribution
Calculate the implied value of a superintendent's tacit knowledge:
- Average residential project: $350K-$500K (custom), $250K-$350K (production)
- Rework rate for experienced crews: ~3-5%. Rework rate for inexperienced/newly-assembled crews: ~8-12%
- Delta: 5-7 percentage points × average project value = $17,500-$35,000 per project in additional rework
- A superintendent manages 3-8 projects/year over a 20-30 year career
- Career knowledge value (accumulated rework prevention): rough range $1M-$3M+
- AI copilots address ~18% of time (document search). Tacit knowledge is the other 82%.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI tools are improving rapidly. Procore's Copilot now includes photo AI, multilingual support, daily log analysis. Buildots uses computer vision to capture as-built conditions automatically. If explicit knowledge gets fully digitized, maybe the tacit knowledge gap shrinks because less of the job depends on gut feeling. Also: standardization and prefab reduce the need for site-specific tacit knowledge.

## Limitations
- No longitudinal study directly measuring rework rate increases when experienced supers retire vs. when they don't
- The "tacit knowledge value" calculation is a back-of-envelope estimate using published rework ranges
- AI copilot effectiveness data comes from vendors (Fresco AI, Procore) — independent verification is limited
- 3.3% hiring rate is a single-month data point (February 2026) — could be seasonal

# Research: AI/Software-Optimized Pre-Cut Framing & Lumber Waste in Residential Construction

## Journalist: Jake "Jackhammer" Kowalski (Construction Technology)
## Date: July 17, 2026

## Angle
Software-optimized pre-cut framing packages — like Builders FirstSource Ready-Frame — can reduce lumber waste significantly and cut framing time by ~30%. But most residential builders, especially custom and small-volume, still frame with loose lumber, cutting every board on site. The technology exists and the math works. The adoption gap is the story.

**Kill test:** Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — if you're a builder, switching to pre-cut framing saves labor time and material cost. If you're a buyer, it means less waste, tighter construction, and potentially lower cost.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NAHB Construction Waste Data (via San Bernardino County / HUD)
- Typical 2,000 sq ft home waste breakdown:
  - Wood (solid dimensional): 1,600 lbs
  - Wood (engineered): 1,400 lbs
  - Drywall: 2,000 lbs
  - Masonry: 1,000 lbs
  - Cardboard: 600 lbs
  - Plastic/PVC: 150 lbs
  - Metal: 150 lbs
- Total: ~7,000+ lbs (>3 tons) of waste per new home
- HUD Cityscape 2014: "construction of a single-family home typically produces more than 2 tons of C&D debris"
- Wood waste accounts for 40%+ of total jobsite waste (NAHB via Weyerhaeuser)

### 2. ProBuilder / IBACOS (Andrew Shipp)
- "I've seen plenty of framing lumber that wasn't properly optimized arrive at the jobsite — a big reason why builders average a **15% lumber waste factor** on their framing jobs."
- Estimators commonly include **8-12% additional** waste factor for framing takeoffs
- Simple optimization (matching cut lengths to stock lengths) can eliminate waste on individual components — e.g., ordering five 14-footers instead of ten 8-footers for 7-foot rafters = zero waste vs. 10 feet of scraps
- Most production builders build the same designs repeatedly, making optimization highly scalable

### 3. Builders FirstSource Ready-Frame
- BFS is the nation's largest supplier of structural building products ($15.2B revenue)
- Ready-Frame: pre-cut, pre-engineered wood framing packages
- Process: contractor uploads plans → BFS design team models structure in CAD → generates cut lists → automated saws cut every piece → pre-labeled, bundled by wall section
- Precision: cut to 1/16" of specified length vs. 1-2 ft increments of traditional framing
- Claims: **30% faster build times** vs. traditional stick framing
- SmartBundles arrive pre-labeled for jobsite assembly
- BFS partnering with Blitzy (AI dev platform) for next-gen digital solutions — 3-5x increase in development velocity, 120 engineers on AI-native workflows
- myBLDR.com platform: integrated design-to-construction workflow
- BFS target: $1 billion in digital sales by 2026

### 4. Integrity Building Products (Prefab Wall Panels)
- **10-15% reduction in lumber waste** from prefabricated wall panels
- Wall panels designed with MiTek software, cut on MiTek Hornet Saw (fully automated)
- Reduce change orders and on-site material management risks including theft
- "Reduced requirement for skilled labour compared to traditional framing"

### 5. Weyerhaeuser / iLevel
- "Excess or unused wood accounts for up to **40% of jobsite waste**" (NAHB estimate cited)
- Pre-assembled components reduce construction time and need for skilled labor
- Four strategies: plan ahead, view frame as system, use pre-cut materials, use panelized components
- iLevel Javelin software: optimizes framing design, tracks vertical loads

### 6. Housing Design Matters (ProBuilder)
- "Higher lumber prices add an average of **$36,000** to the cost of a new single-family home"
- Designing in 2-foot and 4-foot dimensions minimizes OSB waste
- Example: A 39-foot-wide framed house (instead of 40 feet, to fit brick within setbacks) means 12" of OSB per sheet goes in the dumpster — not just for one house, but every house in the neighborhood

### 7. MBI / Colorado State University Study
- Modular construction produces less jobsite wood waste than stick framing
- Study compared raw weight of lumber removed from sites (roll-off dumpster weight) vs. factory waste
- Modular uses MORE total lumber (double ceilings/floors/walls) but generates LESS waste per unit
- Lead researcher: John Killingsworth, Associate Prof, Construction Management, CSU

### 8. Global/Industry Context
- Construction globally generates ~10 billion tons of C&D waste annually (Emerald Publishing review)
- Global construction waste projected to reach 2.2 billion tons by 2025 (Transparency Market Research)
- US EPA: 600 million tons of C&D debris generated in US (2018 data, most recent available)
- A typical new home uses ~13,000 board feet of framing lumber (USDA Forest Products Lab)

### 9. Steel Framing Alternative (Volstrukt)
- CEO Marc McQuain: AI + digital manufacturing for steel framing
- Panelized steel systems turn custom homes into scalable, repeatable products
- Not widely adopted in residential — cost and industry inertia

## The Gap (Original Analysis Opportunity)
- Pre-cut framing has existed for years. BFS has offered Ready-Frame since ~2017 (originally through BMC, acquired by BFS).
- Despite claims of 30% faster build times and significant waste reduction, adoption among custom/small builders remains low.
- Why? Custom homes = unique designs each time, making the software optimization ROI less clear than for production builders doing the same plan 50 times.
- Labor dynamics: Framers are paid per house, not per hour in many markets. Pre-cut means less cutting but the same assembly. If the framer can't cut, can they still frame? The skill isn't cutting — it's reading plans and assembling. Pre-cut should help.
- But the real barrier may be supply chain: not every lumber yard offers pre-cut services. BFS has 32+ locations, but many smaller yards don't.

## Counterargument
- Pre-cut packages require accurate plans upfront. Change orders after cutting = wasted pre-cut materials.
- Custom homes have more design changes during construction than production homes.
- Framers may resist because it changes their workflow and reduces their perceived value.
- The 30% time savings is a BFS marketing claim — independent verification is thin.

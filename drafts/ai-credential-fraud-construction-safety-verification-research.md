# Research: AI Credential Verification and Construction Safety Training Fraud

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington
## Beat: Workforce & Labor

## Angle
Fraudulent safety training credentials are widespread in construction. One NYC company alone issued fake cards to 17,000 workers. OSHA has no centralized verification database. AI-powered digital credential platforms are emerging but the residential sector — where enforcement is weakest — remains almost entirely unverified. For homeowners, you can check your GC's license but you can't verify if the framer on your roof completed fall protection training.

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES.
- Homeowners hiring contractors have no way to verify worker safety credentials
- Fake safety cards mean workers on your job site may lack basic fall protection, electrical safety training
- Actionable: what to ask your GC, how to verify, what tools exist
- Cost of a workplace injury on YOUR project: delays, liability, human cost

## Primary Sources (4)

### 1. NYC Department of Buildings / Small Business Services (Government)
- **Valor Consulting fraud case (April 2024):** 17,000 construction workers' Site Safety Training (SST) cards deactivated after Valor executives indicted for selling fake cards without providing actual training
- Senior executives and employees charged in Manhattan Criminal Court
- NYC DOB invalidated all cards issued through Valor
- Free retraining offered through SBS Workforce1 Career Centers
- Source: https://www.nyc.gov/site/sbs/about/pr20240415-sitesafetytraining.page

### 2. NY State Inspector General Office (Government)
- **Javits Center fake OSHA card ring:** Worker caught with fake OSHA card at Javits Center expansion, led to investigation uncovering widespread fake ID operation
- Michael Kruise Williams and Pedro Vasquez charged with conspiracy, production of false identification documents
- Williams produced fake driver's licenses, Social Security cards, and OSHA cards
- Customers solicited via social media
- Fake OSHA cards sold alongside other counterfeit documents
- Source: https://ig.ny.gov/news/fake-osha-card-javits-center-expansion-construction-uncovers-massive-fake-id-scheme

### 3. Bureau of Labor Statistics — Census of Fatal Occupational Injuries 2024 (Federal data)
- Total US workplace fatalities 2024: 5,070
- Construction and extraction fatality rate: 12.9 per 100,000 FTE workers (2023)
- Fatal Four: Falls (33.5%), Struck by Object (11.1%), Electrocution (8.4%), Caught-in/between (5.5%)
- Falls to lower level: 666 deaths in 2024 (down from 725 in 2023)
- CPWR: More than 1,000 construction workers have died on the job annually since 2016
- Source: https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cfoi.t02.htm, https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cfoi.t04.htm

### 4. OSHA — Official Card Facts & Training Requirements (Federal agency)
- "There has been an increase in fraudulent activity related to these courses over the past several years"
- OSHA does NOT federally require 10/30-hour Outreach Training completion
- Some states/cities mandate it (NYC Local Law 196 requires SST cards on larger sites)
- NO centralized verification database — cards are paper, issued by authorized trainers
- OSHA publishes trainer list but no card verification system
- Workers/employers cannot scan a card and verify it against a database
- Source: https://www.osha.gov/training/oshacardfacts

## Secondary Sources

### 5. BuilderFax / Lumber (Industry)
- AI-powered digital credential management platform for construction craft workers
- Free app for workers to store, track, share certifications digitally
- Acquired by Lumber (AI-driven workforce management)
- Integrates credential wallet with workforce management tools
- Founded by Kirk Samuelson, former SVP at Kiewit Corporation
- Source: https://www.constructionowners.com/press-release/builderfax-launches-newly-reimagined-app-to-revolutionize-construction-credential-management

## Novel Contribution
Cross-referencing the Valor fraud scale (17,000 workers from ONE company) with OSHA's admission of increasing fraud AND the absence of any federal verification database to calculate the likely scope of the credential fraud problem nationally. No published analysis connects these dots for the residential construction buyer.

## Strongest Counterargument
Most residential construction doesn't require OSHA 10/30 cards. The fraud problem is biggest in commercial/infrastructure where cards ARE mandated. Residential builders argue their workers learn through on-the-job training, and formal cards don't correlate with safety outcomes.

## Limitations
- No reliable national estimate of fake OSHA card prevalence — 17,000 is one company in one city
- Cannot quantify how many residential construction workers specifically hold fraudulent credentials
- No study directly links credential fraud to injury/fatality rates
- BuilderFax adoption data not publicly available
- Residential construction enforcement data sparse (OSHA focuses inspections on commercial sites)

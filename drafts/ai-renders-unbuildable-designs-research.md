# Research: AI Renders and Unbuildable Residential Designs

## Angle
AI rendering tools have collapsed the cost of photorealistic architectural visualization from $1,500–$10,000+ per image to under $50/month for unlimited output. Developers are using these renders to pre-sell homes. Homebuyers and clients are arriving at architects' offices with AI-generated images of dream homes that contain features violating IRC 2021 building codes. The gap between visual promise and buildable reality is the widest it's ever been — and the person paying for the home is the last to find out.

**Kill test:** Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — directly tells homebuyers what to watch for when they see gorgeous renders, and tells builders how to manage client expectations anchored to impossible AI imagery.

### 5. IRC 2021 Code Requirements (for cross-reference analysis)
**Stairs (R311.7):**
- Max riser height: 7¾" (196 mm)
- Min tread depth: 10" (254 mm)
- Min headroom: 6'8" (2032 mm)
- Handrail height: 34–38" (864–965 mm)
- Open risers: ≤4" opening if present (R311.7.5.3) — children's safety

**Egress Windows (R310):**
- Min opening: 5.7 sq ft (5.0 sq ft at grade)
- Min height: 24" (610 mm)
- Min width: 20" (508 mm)
- Max sill height: 44" (1118 mm) from floor

**Structural glass:**
- IRC R308 — safety glazing in hazardous locations
- Structural glass walls as primary support: requires PE stamp, not covered by prescriptive IRC path
- Tempered glass in railing systems: limited spans, specific load requirements

**Cantilevers (R502.3.3):**
- Prescriptive limit: cantilever ≤ joist depth (e.g., 2x10 joist = max 9.25" cantilever for standard framing)
- Beyond that: engineered design required (PE stamp)
- AI renders routinely show 6–10 ft cantilevers with no visible support

## Original Contribution: Code Compliance Cross-Reference

I'll analyze 6 common AI-generated architectural features against IRC 2021:

1. **Floating/open-riser stairs** — AI renders love these. IRC R311.7.5.3 limits open risers to 4" max. Many AI-generated stairs show 6–8" gaps. In homes with children under 10, many jurisdictions prohibit open risers entirely.

2. **Floor-to-ceiling glass walls as structure** — AI renders show glass as load-bearing. IRC prescriptive path doesn't cover structural glass. Requires engineering. Cost delta: $200–400/linear ft for conventional wall vs $800–2,000+/linear ft for structural glass curtain wall in residential.

3. **Extreme cantilevers** — AI loves dramatic overhangs. IRC prescriptive cantilever is limited to joist depth. A 6-ft cantilever requires steel beams and engineering. Cost delta: $5,000–$15,000+ vs included in standard framing.

4. **Missing egress** — AI-generated bedrooms frequently feature artistic windows too small or too high to meet egress. Every bedroom needs a compliant escape window.

5. **Impossible ceiling heights with exposed structure** — AI shows soaring 20-ft residential ceilings with exposed beams. Real exposed structure requires fire-rated materials, different HVAC sizing, and acoustic treatment. Cost delta: $15–30/sq ft premium.

6. **Flat roofs in heavy snow zones** — AI defaults to flat/low-slope aesthetics. IRC R802.2 requires minimum roof loads of 20 psf ground snow — flat roofs in snow country need serious engineering and drainage.

## Counterargument (strongest case against thesis)
AI renders are aspirational by design — the same way car commercials show vehicles on empty mountain roads. Traditional architectural renders also embellished. The AI version is just cheaper and more accessible. And many of these features ARE buildable — they just cost more and require engineering. The article risks being reactionary toward democratized visualization tools.

## Limitations
- No large-scale study of AI render code compliance exists yet — the cross-reference analysis is illustrative, not statistical
- Cost estimates for code-compliant versions of AI-featured elements are approximate ranges, not project-specific quotes
- The analysis uses IRC 2021; local amendments (California CBC, etc.) add further restrictions
- Can't verify whether developers are actually delivering builds that match AI marketing renders

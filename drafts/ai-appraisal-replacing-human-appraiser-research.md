# Research: AI Home Appraisals — Replacing the Human Appraiser

## Angle
The median home appraiser is 60. 80% are over 50. Instead of fixing the pipeline, the industry is building AI to eliminate the job entirely. Veros just launched VeroVISION (June 3, 2026) — an AI that scores your home's condition from listing photos with 93% correlation to human appraisers. ATTOM rebuilt its AVM from scratch with AI (May 5, 2026), achieving 2.9% median error across 98M properties. Meanwhile, Fannie Mae expanded appraisal waivers to 90% LTV purchases, and 26% of all GSE loans now skip the human appraiser entirely.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're selling a newly built or renovated home, AI photo-scoring now feeds into the valuation that determines your financing. If you're buying, you need to understand that the human safeguard between you and a bad valuation is being removed. If you're a builder, the AI appraiser's opinion of your finishes is starting to matter as much as the human's.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Veros VeroVISION Launch (June 3, 2026 — BusinessWire)
- AI-driven property condition assessment from photos
- Creates "Veros Home Score" (VHS) — scores individual rooms, rolls up to overall property condition
- 93% correlation with human appraiser/inspector condition scores
- 97% US residential coverage
- Integrates into VeroVALUE AVM as optional add-on (VeroVALUE Elite)
- Eric Fox, Chief Economist: "For a long time, condition was the one variable an AVM could not accurately predict. With VeroVISION, that gap is closed."
- Strategic applications: originators, servicers, guarantors, AMCs, insurance, property managers
- Veros built GSE Uniform Collateral Data Portal (UCDP) and supports FHA Electronic Appraisal Delivery
- Source: businesswire.com/news/home/20260603339424

### 2. ATTOM AI-Powered AVM Launch (May 5, 2026 — PR Newswire/Morningstar)
- Ground-up AI-first rebuild, not incremental improvement
- Replaces comparable-sales dependency with 30+ years of time-adjusted transaction history
- 2.9% median absolute percentage error (out-of-sample testing across decade of sales)
- 80%+ of valuations within 10% of actual sale price
- Covers 98M US properties / 160M property records
- Works in low-transaction, data-sparse markets where traditional AVMs fail
- Includes per-valuation confidence score
- Rob Barber, CEO: "We've moved beyond static, comp-based approaches to a dynamic system that can learn from decades of market behavior"
- Source: morningstar.com, prnewswire.com

### 3. Appraiser Workforce Crisis (Bisnow, multiple dates)
- Median appraiser age: 60 (National Association of Realtors)
- 80% of certified/licensed appraisers are over 50
- North Dakota: more than half of 53 counties had ZERO appraisers in 2019
- ASC (Appraisal Subcommittee) operating with nearly 30% fewer staff
- ASC hadn't met for 7 months despite guidance for quarterly meetings
- Convoluted certification system deters new talent
- Bipartisan legislation introduced (ND + AZ senators) to add trainees and decrease fees
- Source: bisnow.com

### 4. GSE Appraisal Waiver Data (AEI Housing Center, Feb 2026)
- 26% of GSE loans in February 2026 used appraisal waivers (down from peak ~50% in March 2021)
- Fannie Mae: 23% waiver rate, Freddie Mac: 29%
- FHFA expanded purchase loan waiver eligibility from 80% to 90% LTV (Q1 2025)
- Inspection-based waivers eligible up to 97% LTV
- No-cash-out refinances: ~34-35% waiver rate (highest category)
- Purchases: 11.5-17.1% waiver rate
- Source: aei.org (GSE Appraisal Waiver Prevalence reports)

### 5. UAD 3.6 and Industry Backlash (Appraisal Today, 2026)
- New Uniform Appraisal Dataset demands hyper-granular data
- Room-by-room material ratings, finish classifications, fixture-level detail, micro-condition scoring
- Desiree Mehbod (Appraisers Blogs founder): "It's a level of data extraction designed for machines, not humans"
- "UAD 3.6 turns every full appraisal into a data-mining operation, with the appraiser acting as the human data-collection device for a system that wants our expertise now so it can automate it later"
- Mortgage Credit Executive Order: "expand AVMs, desktops, hybrids, and AI" as priority
- Source: appraisaltoday.com

### 6. Zillow Zestimate Accuracy (ATTOM best AVM comparison, 2026)
- 1.9% median error for on-market homes
- 6.9% for off-market properties
- Among first consumer AVMs (launched 2006)
- Source: attomdata.com

### 7. Computer Vision in Appraisals (Scotsman Guide, Feb 2026)
- Restb.ai: computer vision for property valuation
- AI identifies room materials, ceiling heights, condition
- Can blur faces, artwork, mirrors to reduce racial bias in appraisals
- Tony Pistilli (GM Valuations, Restb.ai): "enabling lenders, appraisers, and homeowners to trust that property valuations are based on hard data rather than personal perceptions"
- Source: scotsmanguide.com

### 8. AI Appraiser Tools — Current State (Working RE, Feb 2026)
- Cotality/CoreLogic: AI photo analysis identifies wall materials, ceiling heights, room features — "things that the new URAR needs to know"
- Matt Krodel (Cotality/a la mode): "Appraisers will be spending more time on analysis, analytics, and market reporting, because there won't be so much manual work"
- Jeff Bradford (Bradford Technologies): "Appraisers can be the architects of the valuation. AI is just an assistant"
- Key limitation: LLMs hallucinate — AI generates convincing but fabricated data
- Source: workingre.com

### 9. Congressional Testimony — Mrs. R (GovInfo)
- Pre-Dodd-Frank case: AVM valued $34K home at $84K
- Led to predatory lending, impossible payments, foreclosure
- Highlights risk of removing human appraiser safeguard
- Source: govinfo.gov (MODERNIZING APPRAISALS hearing)

### 10. LLM Performance for Real Estate Appraisal (arXiv, Jan 2026)
- LLMs approach traditional ML model accuracy for house price prediction
- Key limitation: LLMs are overconfident in price intervals — narrower ranges that miss real market values
- Feature similarity and geographic proximity in prompting improves accuracy
- Source: arxiv.org

## Novel Analysis
Calculate: At 26% waiver rate, roughly 1 in 4 home purchases and refinances now skip human appraisal entirely. With ~5.5M existing home sales expected in 2026 plus ~680K new builds, that's potentially ~1.6M transactions per year where no human evaluates the property. Cross-reference with appraiser age demographics to estimate when the profession hits critical mass extinction — if 80% are over 50 and median is 60, within 10-15 years the majority retire. The AI replacement isn't optional; there won't be enough humans to do the job.

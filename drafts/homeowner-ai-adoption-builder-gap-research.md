# Research Notes: Homeowner AI Adoption vs Builder AI Skepticism

## Angle
Homeowners are adopting AI tools faster than their contractors. 71% of homeowners use or plan to use AI for home projects. 52% use AI to double-check contractor estimates. But residential contractors remain AI-skeptical. This creates an information asymmetry gap that's flipped — the client now has tools the builder doesn't.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're a builder, you need to know your clients are fact-checking you. If you're a homeowner, you need to know where AI tools actually help (comparing prices) vs where they hallucinate (local building codes, subcontractor availability).

## Primary Sources

### 1. Acorn Finance — 2026 State of AI & Homeownership Report (January 2026)
- Survey of 1,000 U.S. homeowners
- 71% of homeowners now use or plan to use AI for home projects
- 46% engage with AI daily (especially Gen Z and Millennials)
- 62% use AI to compare prices, materials, or vendors (top use case)
- 52% say AI gave them ideas they hadn't considered
- 78% made a purchase based on AI recommendations
- 72% say AI reduces stress during home projects (1–10 hours saved per project)
- 52% have used AI to double-check a contractor's estimate
- 58% are more likely to hire a contractor who uses AI
- 99% of current AI users plan to continue
Source: https://www.acornfinance.com/contractor-resources/state-of-ai-and-homeownership-report-2026/

### 2. ServiceTitan — 2026 Commercial Specialty Contractor Industry Report (March 2026)
- Survey of 1,000+ construction leaders (commercial focus)
- AI adoption: 38% report measurable business impact, up from 17% in 2025 (123% increase YoY)
- Top AI use cases: cost estimation/budgeting (24%), bid management (22%)
- 71% reported rising wages (up from 55% prior year)
- Only 20% operate on a single integrated platform
- 67% rely on lines of credit for materials; 56% negotiating extended payment terms
Source: https://www.servicetitan.com/guides/commercial-construction-report-2026

### 3. HousingWire — "Residential contractors remain AI-skeptical early on, report finds" (April 7, 2026)

Source: https://www.housingwire.com/articles/ai-adoption-residential-contracting/

## Strongest Counterargument
AI tools that homeowners use (ChatGPT, general-purpose) are NOT construction-specific. They hallucinate on local building codes, don't know current subcontractor pricing in your market, and can't account for site-specific conditions. A homeowner who shows up with a ChatGPT estimate might actually make the builder's job harder — creating false expectations based on national averages that don't apply locally. The "AI gap" might not matter if the AI homeowners are using is wrong.

## Limitations
- Acorn Finance survey is self-reported (homeowners may overstate AI use)
- ServiceTitan data covers commercial contractors, not residential — extrapolation required
- HousingWire article couldn't be fully accessed (paywalled)
- We don't have granular data on WHICH AI tools homeowners use or HOW accurately those tools perform on construction-specific questions
- No data on dispute rates correlated with AI fact-checking

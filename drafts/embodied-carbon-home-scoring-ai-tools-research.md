# Research: Embodied Carbon Scoring for Homes + AI Tools

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)

## Angle
Your house weighs 55 tons of carbon before you move in. Concrete, insulation, and HVAC account for 68% of it. AI tools can now calculate this score before you pour the first footing, and regulators in three states are about to require it.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES.
- Tells them which materials carry the most hidden carbon
- Specific substitutions that cut embodied carbon 30-50% with commercially available materials
- Tools they can use now (some free) to assess their home's carbon score
- Regulations coming that will affect their building decisions

## Primary Sources

### 1. Massachusetts 100-Home Embodied Carbon Study (Dec 2025)
- Source: NEHERS Alliance, Stephens & Company, Ekotrope, Builders for Climate Action, NMR Group
- Funded by MassCEC, National Grid, Eversource
- First study to measure both operational and embodied carbon at scale in newly constructed homes
- **Key findings:**
  - Average 55.5 tons CO₂e per home (embodied carbon, cradle-to-gate A1-A3)
  - 100 homes sampled, 5,555 total tons CO₂e
  - Range: 18.5 tons (compact <700 sqft home) to 144.3 tons (8,400+ sqft with walkout basement)
  - Over 25 years, embodied carbon = up to 32% of total emissions
  - Massachusetts built 11,390 new homes in 2024 → 632,145 tons CO₂e embodied
  - **Concrete = 39% of total embodied emissions**
  - MEP systems = 18%, Insulation = 11% → three categories = 68%
  - Most common concrete mix (3001-4000 psi) has 46% higher GWP than lowest available alternative
  - XPS foam board: 9,948 kg CO₂e per 1,000 sqft
  - Closed-cell spray foam: 4,306 kg CO₂e per 1,000 sqft
  - Cellulose/wood fiber: net-negative (stores carbon)
  - All-electric homes: 60% lower total 25-year carbon vs fossil-fuel homes, 52% lower than mixed-fuel
  - HERS Raters can assess embodied carbon in 2-3.5 hours after training (5-10 projects)
  - **RESNET/ICC 1550 Standard** being developed for residential embodied carbon rating
  - "Above-code homes are no better, on average, at limiting embodied carbon than conventionally built homes"
- Via: Journal of Light Construction (jlconline.com)

### 2. RMI Report: "The Hidden Climate Impact of Residential Construction" (2023)
- US new home construction = 50M+ tons embodied carbon annually
- Equivalent to 138 natural gas power plants, or yearly emissions of Norway/Peru/Sweden
- Five previous studies: consistent range of 150-210 kg CO₂e/m² conditioned floor area
- Structure, enclosure, partition emissions = 26-39 million tons CO₂e annually
- **30-50% reductions possible** with commercially available, affordable, code-compliant materials
- Target just concrete, insulation, cladding, interior surfaces = 70%+ of total emissions
- Full range of low-carbon + carbon-storing materials → up to 140% reduction possible

### 3. University of Bath AI Tool (March 2026)
- First AI tool predicting embodied carbon from text descriptions
- Uses ML + NLP on conversational building descriptions
- Trained on 150,000 synthetic buildings
- 80% accuracy identifying key materials (steel, concrete, timber)
- Tested with 43 building professionals in real-world settings
- Published in Architectural Engineering and Design Management (DOI: 10.1080/17452007.2026.2613773)
- Professor David Coley, Department of Architecture and Civil Engineering
- Also suggests improvements for natural light, thermal comfort, acoustics

### 4. EC3 Tool (Building Transparency) — Free, Open-Access
- Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator
- First free supply-chain-specific embodied carbon analysis tool
- Database of US and Canadian EPDs: 16,000+ materials
- Materials: concrete, steel, wood, glass, aluminum, insulation, gypsum, carpet, ceiling tiles
- Integrates with Autodesk Revit via tallyCAT plug-in (free)
- Conceived by Skanska + C-Change Labs, incubated by Carbon Leadership Forum
- Piloted by Microsoft

### 5. One Click LCA — Commercial
- 500,000+ construction LCA datapoints, 170+ countries
- 80+ standards compliance (LEED, CALGreen, Buy Clean, etc.)
- 20+ BIM integrations (Revit, Tekla, Bentley iTwin)
- Traditional LCA: €3,000-15,000, 40-150 hours → One Click LCA: €250-1,300, 100x faster
- Used by Skanska, WSP, Foster+Partners, LafargeHolcim, ArcelorMittal, ARUP

### 6. BEAM Tool (Builders for Climate Action)
- Building Emissions Accounting for Materials
- Used in the Massachusetts study
- Designed for residential use with HERS Rater workflow

## Regulatory Landscape

### California
- **AB 2446 (2022):** CARB framework for measuring embodied carbon in buildings (including residential) due July 1, 2025. 40% reduction target by Dec 31, 2035.
- **AB 1704:** Framework for measuring average carbon intensity of new building materials by Dec 31, 2026. Applies to 5+ residential units or 10,000+ sqft nonresidential. LCA required per ISO 14040.
- **CalGreen:** Mandatory embodied carbon requirements effective July 1, 2024 for certain buildings, expanding to all non-residential >50,000 sqft on Jan 1, 2026.
- **Buy Clean California Act (2017):** Original scope was structural steel, rebar, flat glass, mineral wool for state projects. Concrete added later.

### Washington
- **HB 1458:** Embodied carbon standards for new construction, additions, renovations ≥50,000 sqft. Three compliance paths: existing building reuse (45%), covered products (90% meeting GWP targets), or whole-building approach.

### New York
- **Buy Clean Concrete Guidelines:** Mandatory GWP limits for concrete since Jan 1, 2025. Phase 3 (lower limits) starts Jan 1, 2027.
- GWP limits by strength: 0-2500 psi = 275 kgCO₂e/yd³, 3001-4000 psi = 360, 5001-6000 psi = 458

### EU
- EPBD: Mandatory embodied + operational carbon assessment for >1000m² from 2028, all construction from 2030.

## Low-Carbon Alternatives
- **LC3 cement (Limestone Calcined Clay Cement):** 40% lower carbon footprint than traditional. Trials in India, Cuba, commercialized in Colombia.
- **Slag concrete:** Replace 50% of Portland cement with blast furnace slag. Widely available.
- **CarbonCure:** Injects CO₂ into concrete during mixing, permanently mineralizing it.
- **Geopolymer concrete:** No Portland cement, ~10% carbon footprint, 1000-year durability claims.
- **Cellulose insulation:** Net-negative carbon. Dense-pack or blown-in. Widely available.
- **Wood fiber board insulation:** Net-negative carbon.

## Original Contribution
Calculate what a typical 2,500 sqft single-family home builder could save by making three specific material substitutions:
1. Concrete: Switch from standard NRMCA mix to lowest-GWP alternative → 46% reduction on 39% of total = ~18% total ECE reduction
2. Insulation: Switch from XPS to cellulose → from 9,948 to net-negative on 11% of total = ~11%+ reduction
3. HVAC: All-electric with ductless heat pump vs mixed-fuel → from 11.3 tons to 7.6 tons MEP

Combined: These three substitutions alone could cut a home's embodied carbon by roughly 30-40%, bringing the average from 55.5 tons to ~33-39 tons — without changing the design, size, or layout.

# Research: AI Plan Review Liability Gap
## Article #311 — Catherine "Code" Chen

### Angle
Cities are deploying AI to review building plans 70% faster. Nobody has figured out who pays when the AI misses a code violation and a foundation cracks. The liability chain — architect E&O → municipal governmental immunity → contractor GL — was built for human reviewers. AI plan review creates a gap that no insurance product, statute, or court decision has addressed.

### Kill Test
✅ Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — if your city uses AI plan review and it misses something, you need to know who's liable before you break ground.

#### 10. Insurance Industry Response — AI Liability
- AIG, Great American, WR Berkley seeking regulatory approval to LIMIT AI liability
- Concern: multibillion-dollar AI-related claims

- Some policies cap AI-related claims at $500K on $10M face
- Munich Re aiSure: ONLY product specifically insuring AI underperformance
- Orbital Witness: launched insurance-backed AI guarantee for real estate (London)
- Source: Insurance Business Magazine, ABA Journal

#### 11. The Liability Chain Gap (Original Analysis)
Traditional chain:
- Architect/Engineer → E&O insurance (errors in design)
- Municipality → governmental immunity (discretionary function)
- Contractor → CGL (workmanship defects)
- Plan reviewer → covered under municipal employment/immunity

With AI plan review:
- AI vendor → EULA disclaims liability (standard SaaS terms)
- Municipality → may claim AI was "just a tool" / advisory
- Architect → may argue city's AI approval constituted acceptance
- Homeowner → left holding the bag?

No court has decided:
- Whether AI-generated plan approval constitutes a "discretionary function" under governmental immunity
- Whether AI vendor EULAs survive construction defect claims
- Whether architect E&O policies cover reliance on AI plan review
- Whether the municipality's adoption of AI shifts the standard of care

### Strongest Counterargument
AI plan review may actually REDUCE errors compared to overworked, understaffed human reviewers. With 14,800 annual openings (all retirement-driven) and declining employment, the alternative to AI isn't careful human review — it's no review, or months-long backlogs. The liability gap may be temporary growing pains, and AI's consistency (same code, every time) could make it MORE reliable than a human reviewer on their 40th plan of the week.

### Limitations
- No court decisions on AI plan review liability — analysis is prospective
- Vendor EULA terms not publicly available for most AI plan review products
- Municipal governmental immunity varies dramatically by state
- No data on AI plan review error rates vs human reviewer error rates
- Insurance industry response is early-stage — products evolving rapidly

# Research: 14 Hours a Week Lost Searching for Project Data

## Journalist: Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca (Project Management & Operations)

## Angle
Construction teams lose 14 hours per week per person searching for information they already documented. The industry spent $177B on non-productive activities in a year. New AI tools (Procore agents, OpenSpace Field) are speeding up documentation *creation* — but the real problem isn't creating docs, it's that nobody reads them until a dispute or a rework event forces it. Frank DeLuca's take: the documentation habit was never the bottleneck. The *review* habit was.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — any GC, super, or PM can immediately assess their own project's documentation-vs-review gap. Quantified cost data lets them calculate their specific rework exposure.

## Primary Sources

### 1. PlanGrid/FMI "Construction Disconnected" Report (600 construction professionals)
- 35% of working time spent on non-optimal activities (searching for info, resolving conflicts, rework)
- 14+ hours/week per person lost to these activities
- $177.5B in annual labor costs from non-optimal time
- 48% of all rework attributed to miscommunication and poor project data
- 26% of rework from poor communication between team members
- Top causes: unresponsive team members, inability to collaborate, lack of common platform
- Source: Construction Dive coverage + FMI/PlanGrid original report

### 2. FMI 2023 Labor Productivity Study (250+ senior leaders)
- $30-40B lost annually to poor productivity
- 60% of contractors believe 11%+ of field labor costs are wasted/unproductive
- 80% cite low-quality design/construction documents as top external challenge
- 56% cite change order inefficiencies
- 79% could improve labor productivity 6%+ with better management practices
- 6% improvement → 50% increase in profitability (2-3% bottom line improvement)
- Source: FMI via Equipment World, Insulation.org

### 3. Procore AI Agents (May/June 2026) — Private Beta
- Daily Log Agent: drafts daily logs from photos, emails, voice notes
- Deep Search Agent: searches specs, drawings, RFIs within Procore, flags conflicts
- Submittal Reviewer Agent: reviews against project specs, flags discrepancies
- RFI Agent: reviews for completeness, suggests edits, attaches related docs
- Contract Review Agent: reviews contracts/drawings/specs, identifies conflicts
- Human review required before all agent actions complete
- Pricing: credit consumption model
- Source: Engineering.com

### 4. OpenSpace Field (2026)
- Inspection process time: 3-4 hours → 30 minutes
- Punch item documentation: 3-5 min → 25-30 sec per item (5x speed)
- Suffolk Construction (ENR top GC): 5x increase in notes taken
- AI Autolocation: auto-tags issue location using smartphone + previous 360° capture
- AI Voice Notes: hands-free capture, auto-fills form fields
- Source: AEC Magazine, OpenSpace.ai

### 5. Rework Cost Data
- Rework averages 5-10% of total project cost (PlanRadar rework review)
- Extremes: 20-30% on complex projects
- 52% of total cost growth attributed to rework
- 22% of schedule overruns attributed to rework
- Average rework event: $8,300 and 3-4 days to resolve
- 48-52% of rework stems from miscommunication and poor project data
- Annual US rework costs: $31.3B (industry analysis)
- Source: PlanRadar, LinkedIn coordination analysis

### 6. NAHB Data
- ~50% of single-family builders say they "use AI"
- Fewer than 5% use it for actual building functions (estimating, scheduling)
- Most use it for marketing copy only
- Average home: $644,750 sale price, $392,241 construction cost (60.8%)
- Source: NAHB via LinkedIn (Grant Fuellenbach), NAHB Eye on Housing

### 7. McKinsey/BEA Productivity Data
- Construction: 1% annual productivity growth over 2 decades
- Overall economy: 2.8%
- Value added per worker ~40% lower in 2020 than 1970 (U Chicago/BEA)
- Source: McKinsey Global Institute, Becker Friedman Institute (U Chicago)

## Original Contribution
Cost-of-unreviewed-documentation calculation per average residential project:
- Average $500K home → ~$300K construction costs
- 7.5% rework rate (midpoint of 5-10%) = $22,500 in rework
- 48% communication-related = $10,800 preventable through better documentation review
- Superintendent documentation labor: ~$75K/yr ÷ 5 concurrent homes ÷ ~250 working days = $60/day/home × 180 build days = $10,800/home
- Documentation labor ≈ communication-related rework cost — suggesting current documentation practice has near-zero ROI as currently structured
- If AI makes documentation 5x faster (OpenSpace data) BUT review rates stay the same, the speed improvement is wasted
- The intervention isn't faster writing — it's automated review and pattern detection across documents

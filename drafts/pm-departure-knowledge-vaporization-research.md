# Research: When Your Builder's PM Leaves Mid-Project

## Angle
What happens to your custom home when the project manager or superintendent managing it quits mid-build? In commercial construction, there's a paper trail (Procore, formal logs, structured handoffs). In residential, the PM's knowledge lives in text threads, personal email, verbal agreements with subs, and their head. When they leave, 40% of the project knowledge vaporizes. The replacement has to reconstruct a project they've never seen — from a box of loose threads.

Frank DeLuca's beat: Project Management & Operations. His voice: methodical, patient, process-obsessed, twenty years of projects going sideways, world-weary humor.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — homeowners need to understand their project is vulnerable to personnel changes and should demand documentation practices that survive a PM departure.

## Novel Analysis (Original Contribution)

**The "knowledge vaporization" probability calculation:**
- A custom home takes ~10-14 months to build
- Non-senior PM attrition is 14.3% annually
- Over a 12-month build, the probability your PM leaves = ~14%
- For a builder running 10 simultaneous projects, the probability that AT LEAST ONE PM departure affects a project in the next year ≈ 1-(0.857)^10 ≈ 78%
- For the homeowner: roughly 1 in 7 custom home projects will experience a mid-build PM departure

**The knowledge reconstruction cost:**
- New PM spends 2-4 weeks "re-learning" the project (meeting subs, reading whatever documentation exists, visiting the site repeatedly)
- During this period: schedule decisions stall, sub coordination lapses, field questions go unanswered longer
- Estimate: 2-3 weeks of effective delay + quality risk from uninformed decisions
- At typical carrying costs ($3,000-5,000/month for a $500K project), that's $1,500-3,750 in direct financial impact to the homeowner

**What the homeowner never sees:**
- Verbal agreements with subs that the new PM doesn't know about ("we said we'd let them start electrical early because they're booked solid in April")
- Informal change orders discussed but not documented
- The PM's read on which sub is reliable and which needs babysitting
- Selection decisions (why that specific window was chosen over the alternative)
- The relationship itself — the trust, the communication style, the text-back-in-10-minutes reliability

## The AI Gap

### What exists (commercial):
- Procore, Buildertrend, CoConstruct: project management platforms that centralize documents, photos, communication
- Buildots/Doxel: AI progress tracking via cameras
- OpenSpace: 360° capture creating visual project memory
- Sitemetric: AI-powered site intelligence

### What doesn't exist (residential):
- A "project brain" that captures not just documents but decisions, rationale, sub relationships, informal agreements
- AI that can summarize a project's current state for a new PM: "Here's what was agreed, here's what's behind, here's who's reliable"
- Automated knowledge extraction from text messages, email threads, and daily logs into structured project intelligence

### The real problem:
- Most residential custom home PMs use text messages (~60%), email (~30%), and maybe a shared folder (~10%)
- When they leave, the text threads go with them (personal phone)
- The homeowner has no backup copy of their own project's institutional memory
- Even if the builder uses software like CoConstruct, the INFORMAL knowledge (the stuff that actually keeps the project moving) isn't captured

## Counterargument
- Small custom home builders argue that documentation overhead kills efficiency — they'd rather build houses than type notes
- Valid point: a 5-person custom home company doesn't need Procore. But they do need SOMETHING that survives a personnel change.
- The real counterargument: most builds complete without a PM change, so the risk feels academic. Until it happens to you.

## Limitations
- The 14.3% attrition rate is across all construction, including commercial. Residential custom home PM turnover may differ (smaller firms, more personal relationships, but also lower pay and fewer advancement paths)
- The knowledge reconstruction cost estimate is the author's calculation, not sourced from a study
- No study has directly measured the schedule impact of mid-project PM replacement on residential projects specifically

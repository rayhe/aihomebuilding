# Research: Smart Water Shutoff in New Construction — The $550 Device Nobody Installs

## Thesis
Water damage is the #1 driver of homeowner insurance claims by frequency and the leading cause of construction losses. AI-powered water monitoring has been proven by Munich Re to reduce claims 73% and payouts 90% on commercial sites. A $550 consumer device (Flo by Moen) installed during construction — when the plumber is already on-site — could protect residential builds at near-zero marginal cost. Almost nobody does it. The incentive structure explains why: builders don't pay homeowner insurance claims, and homeowners don't know what they should have asked for.

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES — tells homeowners to demand a smart water shutoff during construction (when install cost is lowest), tells builders the insurance math justifies spec'ing it standard, tells custom builders the liability math favors it.

## Original Contribution

### 1. Break-Even Calculation (New Construction Install)
- Flo by Moen: $550 device
- Marginal installation cost during construction: ~$100-$200 (plumber already on site, adding one inline device)
- Total: ~$650-$750 during construction vs. $1,500-$2,500 retrofit after move-in
- Average water damage claim: $15,400
- Annual claim probability: 1/67 = 1.49%
- Expected annual water damage cost per home: $15,400 × 0.0149 = ~$230/year
- Break-even: ~3 years during construction, ~7+ years for retrofit
- Over 30-year mortgage: expected avoidance = ~$6,900 in water damage costs
- Device cost as percentage of $500K home: 0.13%

### 2. Builder's Risk Premium Impact
- If WINT's 73% claim reduction translates to residential (conservative 50% assumption):
- $500K build with $5K builder's risk premium
- Water damage = ~35% of losses → water portion of premium = ~$1,750
- 50% reduction = ~$875 savings per project
- Device pays for itself during construction phase alone

### 3. The Misaligned Incentive Structure
- Builder pays builder's risk premium (construction phase)
- Homeowner pays homeowner's insurance (occupancy phase)
- Builder has NO cost incentive to install $550 device that saves the homeowner $230/year
- Homeowner doesn't know to ask for it during construction (when it's cheapest)
- Result: technology exists, proven, affordable, universally ignored

### 4. Insurance Discount Quantification
- Smart water shutoff discounts: typically 5-15% of homeowner premium
- Average homeowner premium for new construction ($300K): $133/month = $1,596/year
- 5-15% discount = $80-$240/year
- Combined with damage avoidance: $310-$470/year total benefit
- Device payback: 1.4-2.1 years (during construction) or 3.2-4.8 years (retrofit)

## Strongest Counterargument
Residential plumbing systems are simpler than commercial — fewer failure points, shorter pipe runs, lower pressure. The 73% claim reduction from WINT on commercial sites may not translate 1:1 to residential. Additionally, false positive shutoffs during construction activities (concrete pours, exterior washing) are a real operational nuisance. And at $550 per home, production builders building 10,000+ homes/year face a $5.5M+ annual cost for a benefit that accrues to the homeowner, not the builder.

## Limitations
- Munich Re/WINT study is on commercial sites, not residential. No peer-reviewed residential-specific data exists.
- Insurance discount amounts vary by carrier and state. Not all carriers offer smart water device discounts.
- Flo by Moen requires WiFi and AC power — not available during all construction phases.
- AlertLabs (cellular, battery) solves the construction-phase connectivity problem but is positioned for commercial.
- Break-even calculations use national averages; regional variation in claim frequency and severity is significant.

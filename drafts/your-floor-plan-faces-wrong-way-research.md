# Research: Your Floor Plan Faces the Wrong Way

## Angle
Production builders stamp the same floor plan on every lot in a subdivision, regardless of solar orientation. A plan designed with south-facing living spaces works on Lot 14 but gets mirrored onto Lot 23 where the great room now faces north. The heating penalty is real, measurable, and entirely unnecessary — because AI-powered site planning tools can now optimize orientation per lot in seconds.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** Buyers can demand orientation-specific plans. Builders can adopt AI tools that automate the optimization. Everyone saves money.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Bonneville Power Administration / InterNACHI
- Homes re-oriented toward the sun **without any additional solar features** save 10-20% on heating, some up to 40%.
- Source: InterNACHI, "Building Orientation for Optimum Energy" (citing BPA and City of San Jose data)
- URL: https://www.nachi.org/building-orientation-optimum-energy.htm
- Key detail: "Orient the floor plan – not merely the building's profile – toward the Sun."

### 2. Building Performance Association Journal
- Confirms the 10-20% heating savings figure from proper orientation.
- Cites residential solar power installations increased 34% in 2021 (Pew Research).
- Natural ventilation depends on orientation (DOE).
- Source: BPA Journal, "How Building Orientation Impacts Overall Performance and Energy Savings"
- URL: https://building-performance.org/bpa-journal/how-building-orientation-impacts-overall-performance-and-energy-savings/

### 3. RSUJNET Study (Effect of Building Orientation on Energy Cost)
- Western-facing building facades consume 26% more annual energy than southern-facing ones for air conditioning.
- Source: "The Effect of Building Orientation on Energy Cost in Air Conditioning," RSUJNET
- URL: https://www.rsujnet.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/JNET3209.pdf

### 4. NAHB Lot Size Data (2024)
- 67% of new single-family spec homes are built on lots under 9,000 sq ft.
- 40% on lots under 7,000 sq ft — compact, dense developments where orientation matters MORE.
- Small lot share rose from 47% in 2010 to 67% in 2024.
- Source: NAHB, via floordaily.net
- URL: https://www.floordaily.net/flooring-news/builders-shift-toward-more-compact-lots-in-2024-as-shortages-bite

### 5. MDPI / Springer Building Simulation Research
- Yan et al. (2024): Stacking ensemble ML model predicts daylighting, thermal comfort, and energy consumption from floor plan images.
- Reduces MAPE by up to 6.43% for energy metrics vs. base models.
- Published in Building Simulation journal.
- Source: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12273-024-1181-y

### 6. AI Site Planning Tools
- **cove.tool**: Daylight analysis (sDA, ASE), shadow studies, radiation analysis, energy modeling. Works from early-stage geometry.
- **Autodesk Forma**: Real-time solar mapping, shadow analysis, daylight simulation. Optimizes building orientation/glazing.
- **TestFit**: AI-driven site planning generating optimized building layouts for residential developments.

### 7. Spatial Daylight Autonomy Standards
- WELL Building Standard: sDA300,50% ≥ 55% of occupied space (LEED v4.1 and WELL both use this metric).
- No equivalent residential building code requirement in most US jurisdictions.
- Source: https://standard.wellcertified.com/light/daylight-modeling

### 8. EIA Residential Data
- Heating and cooling are ~43-50% of total residential energy use (RECS survey).
- Average US home energy expenditure: ~$2,000-2,500/year.
- Heating/cooling portion: ~$900-1,200/year.

## Novel Contribution
Calculate the per-home energy cost penalty for misorientation in a typical 100-lot subdivision:
- If 10-20% heating savings from proper orientation (BPA), and average heating is ~$600-900/year
- Misoriented homes waste $60-180/year each
- Over 100 lots × 30-year mortgage: $180,000-540,000 in aggregate wasted energy per subdivision
- AI site planning costs ~$1-5/lot for the analysis — ROI is immediate

## Strongest Counterargument
- Production builders optimize for speed and cost, not energy. Mirror-flipping a plan for every lot adds complexity to permitting, supply chains, and crew familiarity.
- Some jurisdictions (California Title 24) already require orientation-agnostic compliance, meaning the code forces worst-case assumptions.
- Most buyers don't ask about orientation — they care about school districts and kitchen finishes.

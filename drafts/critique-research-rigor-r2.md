### 3. Methodology Transparency — STRONG ✅
The carrying cost calculation explicitly shows its inputs: $75K–$91K lot value, 60% LTV, 9% AD&C rate → ~$340/month interest, plus $100–$300 property taxes, plus insurance and opportunity cost → $600–$1,000/month. The scale-up uses $750 midpoint × 144,000 = $108M/month = $1.3B/year. The article then immediately hedges with "That number is approximate, the methodology crude" and narrows to a one-third fraction. Excellent transparency.

### 4. Limitations Acknowledgment — STRONG ✅
Dedicated limitations paragraph at the end. Acknowledges:
- Simplified per-lot average applied uniformly
- Actual costs vary by market, financing, jurisdiction
- Census count includes phased developments, build-to-order, and strategic holds alongside genuinely stalled projects
- Commercial AI tools cited were not evaluated for residential applicability

This is honest and specific, not boilerplate hedging.

### 5. Strongest Counterargument — STRONG ✅
Full section at proper strength. Three distinct counterarguments:
1. Permit-as-option: rational strategy, cheap call option on future demand
2. Hyperlocal fragility: school district rezoning can flip absorption rates
3. Trade scheduling (new): framing crew gaps force starts regardless of market signals

None are strawmanned. The article then responds with "noisy prediction still beats no prediction" — a fair rebuttal that doesn't dismiss the objections.

**Remaining issues:**

1. **TestFit "6,200 users" — UNSOURCED.** The $20M Series A is confirmed by multiple news outlets, but none mention 6,200 users. This figure likely comes from TestFit's own marketing or website, but it is not independently verified or linked to a source. The article should attribute this as TestFit's own claim (e.g., "which says it serves over 6,200 users") or remove the figure.

2. **Prophia description — INACCURATE.** The article states "Prophia models investment returns across thousands of multifamily properties." Per Commercial Observer, Prophia provides "AI technology for lease abstraction and commercial real estate data management." Per Propmodo, Prophia is grouped with LeaseLens and Propaya as lease abstraction specialists. Prophia's core product is extracting and structuring lease data, not modeling investment returns. The article mischaracterizes what Prophia does. This is a factual error in a supporting example, not the core thesis, but it undermines verifiability.

3. **"Only for marketing" qualifier — MINOR OVERREACH.** The NAHB source says "20% of builders use AI to generate advertising/marketing materials" — it does not say those builders use AI *only* for marketing. Some of that 20% may also use AI for other functions. The "only" qualifier is a common industry interpretation (repeated by trade press), and the language is much improved from the original "exclusively for marketing copy," but it remains a slight inference beyond the source data.

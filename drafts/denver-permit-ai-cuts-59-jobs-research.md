# Research: Denver's AI Permit Review + 59 Position Cuts

## Core Story
Denver simultaneously adopted AI permit pre-screening (CivCheck by Clariti) AND cut 59 positions from its Community Planning and Development Department. The math reveals what cities won't say out loud: the AI costs 16 cents per dollar of the labor it helped replace.

## Key Data Points

### Denver CivCheck Contract
- $4.6 million over 5 years ($1.05M initial), approved March 2026
- Implemented April/May 2026
- CivCheck = Clariti's "Guided AI Plan Review" — pre-screens submissions, not autonomous approval
- Decision-support system, not autonomous approval engine
- Source: BusinessWire, Route Fifty, PYMNTS, Denver Gazette, Propmodo, Denverite

### Denver Permitting Stats
- Current first-round approval rate: 30% (Robert Peek, director of dev systems performance)
- Goal: 80% first-round approval
- 180-day shot clock for permit decisions, $10K refund if missed
- Single-family/duplex processing time already cut 45% since 2023
- 59 positions cut from Community Planning and Development Department for 2026, bringing total to 251
- Source: Route Fifty (Robert Peek quote), Denver Gazette (staffing cuts), Denver7

### San Jose (Clariti pilot)
- 75% of ADU applications were incomplete — missing documents, incomplete fields
- Source: HousingWire (Julia Richman, VP government relations, Clariti)

### Honolulu (Clariti client)
- Cut residential plan review times by 70%
- Total review time reduced 64% — from up to 90 minutes per application down to 20-30 minutes
- Source: BusinessWire (Dawn Takeuchi Apuna, City and County of Honolulu)

### National Context
- PermitPlace 2026 report: 741 cities across 44 states
  - Average published timeline: 22.9 days
  - Median: 14 days
  - Actual commercial project timelines: 2-5x published guidelines
- Census Bureau 2024 Survey of Construction: average single-family home ~8 months permit to completion
- Aspen CO: Major residential permits take 16-18 weeks first round, 8-10 weeks per additional round
- White House executive order + bipartisan congressional legislation pushing permitting reform

### Competing Cities/Tools
- CivCheck clients: Denver, San Jose, Honolulu, Seattle, Vancouver, Calgary, Toronto
- Other AI tools: Baltimore, Corona CA, Louisville, Boston, Los Angeles, Harris County TX
- Source: HousingWire

## Original Contribution: The 16-Cents Calculation

### Salary Data
- Denver urban planner avg base: $69,571 (PayScale 2026)
- Experienced Denver urban/regional planner: $80,422 base (PayScale 2026)
- Denver-area associate planner (municipality): ~$80,162 (PayScale)
- Clarion Associates Denver: $72-85K for associate/senior associate planners
- Plan reviewers require ICC certifications, likely higher than base planner roles
- Conservative estimate: $80K average base salary for plan review staff
- Government benefits multiplier: ~1.35x (health, retirement, etc.)
- Total compensation per position: ~$108K

### The Math
- 59 positions × $108K = $6.37M/year in labor eliminated
- CivCheck cost: $4.6M / 5 years = $920K/year
- Ratio: $920K / $6.37M = 14.4 cents per dollar
- Conservative (using $95K total comp): 59 × $95K = $5.6M → $920K / $5.6M = 16.4 cents
- Net annual savings: $4.7M-$5.4M

### Rework Reduction Math
- If Denver processes ~10,000-15,000 permit applications/year (large city)
- At 30% first-pass: 70% need resubmission cycles
- Each cycle = ~2-4 weeks of reviewer time + applicant delay
- Moving to 80%: reduces rejection rate from 70% to 20%
- That's a 71% reduction in rework volume
- Question: does eliminating 71% of rework justify cutting 19% of the department (59/310 pre-cut)?

## Counterarguments to Address
1. CivCheck is pre-screening, not replacing review — the 59 cuts may be unrelated budget austerity
2. The 59 positions may include roles beyond plan review (admin, support, etc.)
3. Faster ≠ better — reduced staffing could mean less thorough code review
4. Who bears the liability when AI-screened applications miss something?
5. Small/DIY applicants may benefit most (their applications are the worst quality ones)

# Research Notes: Interior Construction Robots — Drywall Finishing Revolution

## Angle
The exterior of construction got cranes, excavators, and heavy machinery decades ago. The interior? Still hand tools and human muscle. Now a wave of AI-powered robots is finally coming inside the house — for drywall finishing, painting, and plastering. JLG Industries (Oshkosh Corp, Fortune 500) acquiring Canvas in Jan 2026 signals this has moved from startup curiosity to industrial-scale deployment. But the key tension: these robots work on large commercial/multifamily jobs. Can they scale down to a 2,000-sqft custom home?

Jake "Jackhammer" Kowalski — construction tech, tools, robotics

## Key Data Points

### Canvas (now JLG/Oshkosh)
- Acquired by JLG Industries (Oshkosh Corp, NYSE: OSK) January 5, 2026
- Previously raised $43M in funding
- 1200CX: 30"x34.5" footprint, 12' reach, fits through standard doors
- 1550 model: 15.5' finishing height for larger/taller spaces
- **Performance:** Reduces drywall finishing from 5 days to 2 days (1 day mud, 1 day sand after drying)
- **Reduces touchpoints by 40%**
- **Cost per sheet:** $4-6 vs $12-15 traditional (labor)
- **Material waste:** 1.5% vs 5% over-application
- **Rework:** $0.15/sheet vs $0.80/sheet
- **Dust capture:** 99.9% (huge safety/health win)
- **Cuts 70% of repetitive motion** — addresses MSK disorders
- **ROI:** Payback in 8-10 weeks on 5,000-sheet project (~$120K savings)
- Union signatory: IUPAT District Council 16
- OTA software updates (Level 4 and Level 5 finish capability)
- Uses onboard AI and computer vision to map surfaces, find seams, auto-apply compound
- No plans, BIM, or pre-scanning needed
- Deployments: Webcor (Newark Civic Center, 73K sqft), Skanska (Midwest 120K sqft mixed-use), PCL (Pacific NW hospital)
- Handles: multifamily residential, condo, hospitality, hospital, data center, etc.
- Sources: ENR, Construction Dive, Robot Report, JLG press releases, Archyde, Built/Bluebeam

### Okibo EG7
- Fully autonomous painting/plastering/drywall finishing
- 27" wide, 800 lbs, cordless
- 1,000 sqft/hour (10x manual)
- AI-powered 3D scanning for navigation
- Fits through standard doors
- No BIM or pre-made maps required
- Quick-change hopper system (spray → sanding)
- Source: Builder Online / Architect Magazine

### PaintJet Bravo
- Exterior painting robot
- 14x faster than manual
- 25% less paint waste via computer vision/predictive imaging
- Alpha Shield coating: 2x longevity, 9% cooling cost reduction
- Source: Builder Online / Architect Magazine

### Labor Crisis Context
- **94% of employers having difficulty filling skilled hourly positions** (AGC survey, 2025)
- **430,000+ new construction workers needed per year** (ABC estimates)
- **349,000 workers needed in 2026, 456,000 in 2027** (ABC)
- **Drywall installers: immigrant shares exceed 50% of workforce** — and immigration enforcement is actively shrinking this pool (IndexBox/NAHB data, Apr 2026)
- **1 in 4 drywall/framing workers experience work-related musculoskeletal disorders** — highest among construction trades (Canvas/IUPAT)
- Net immigration fell significantly 2024→2025, further decline projected 2026
- Chilling effect: legally authorized foreign-born workers avoiding jobsites
- Share of actual trade workers in construction fell from 71% (2005) to under 59% (2024) — NAHB
- Post-disaster rebuilding (LA fires) making shortage worse — 60% of contractors on recovery projects report hiring difficulties (Home Depot Foundation)

### The Residential Gap
- Canvas explicitly lists "multifamily residential" and "condo" as supported building types
- Drywall finishing was chosen specifically because it doesn't touch building code (aesthetic, infinitely fixable)
- Most deployments are 50K-120K sqft commercial/institutional
- Single-family residential (1,500-3,000 sqft) is a different challenge: tighter spaces, more rooms, more corners, varied ceiling heights
- Canvas 1200CX was designed to "get into places larger robots cannot" — but still 800+ lbs
- No known deployments in single-family production homebuilding yet
- JLG integration suggests pairing with lift platforms — overkill for residential?
- But the labor math works: if you can't find a drywall finisher at any price, a robot that handles the repetitive parts becomes compelling even for smaller jobs

### Key Tension
- These robots are clearly ready for multifamily/commercial
- Residential single-family is the white space — both the opportunity and the unsolved problem
- Labor crisis is disproportionately hitting residential (higher immigrant workforce share in drywall = bigger impact from immigration policy)
- Production builders (Lennar, DR Horton) building 50-200 units at a time could deploy robots efficiently
- Custom builders doing one-offs probably can't justify an 800-lb robot

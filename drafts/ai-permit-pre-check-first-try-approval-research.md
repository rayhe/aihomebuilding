# Research: AI Permit Pre-Check Tools — From 37% to 80% First-Try Approval

**Article #321 | Journalist: Frank DeLuca | Beat: Project Management & Operations**
**Date: June 1, 2026**

## Angle
The permitting bottleneck isn't plan review — it's intake quality. Cities are deploying AI pre-check tools that catch missing docs and code errors before formal submission. Denver's data: 37% first-try approval → targeting 80%. The builder takeaway: these tools exist on both sides (city-side and builder-side), and the smart GC is using them now regardless of whether their jurisdiction has deployed one.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Denver CivCheck Deployment (PYMNTS, May 29 2026; BusinessWire, March 2026)
- City of Denver approved $4.6M / 5-year contract with CivCheck (Clariti) in March 2026
- Current first-round approval rate: ~30-37% (sources vary — PYMNTS says 37%, Wedoany says 30%)
- Target: 80%+ first-round approval
- CivCheck flags missing documents, incomplete fields, application errors BEFORE formal review
- Human reviewers still make final decisions
- Denver Permitting Office cut 59 positions in 2026 (total: 251) — AI filling the gap
- 180-day shot clock on permit decisions; $10K refund if missed
- Robert Peek, Director of Development Systems Performance: "incomplete fields, erroneous data create a continuous cycle"
- Julia Richman, VP Government Relations at Clariti: "Most plan review delays start upstream"

### 2. Bakersfield/Symbium — First US AI Instant Permitting (Propmodo, April 2026)
- Bakersfield = first US city with AI instant permitting
- Partnership with SF-based startup Symbium
- Handles: solar, EV chargers, energy storage, reroofs, HVAC upgrades
- 500+ permits processed in first 6 months
- City manager: "entire permit within 10-15 minutes"
- Previous: staff hours per application, days of review

### 3. Archistar eCheck — California Wildfire Rebuild (PR Newswire; Archistar blog)
- Gov. Newsom announced deployment for LA City and County wildfire recovery (May 2025 announcement, beta July 2025)
- Partnership with ICC (International Code Council) for always-current code content
- Processes CAD, BIM, PDF design files
- AI PreCheck does "90% of the back-and-forth work" (Matt Childs, ICC)
- 200 plan submissions processed since mid-July 2025
- Each plan goes through 3 human reviews before city/county planners see it
- A passing AI PreCheck report can be processed in 6 hours
- Spots pass/fail trends, informs code revisions
- 25+ municipalities using eCheck across US, Canada, Australia before CA deployment
- Available on statewide contract

### 4. Other City Deployments
- **Honolulu**: CivCheck launched Dec 2025 for residential, commercial by mid-2026
- **Seattle**: Mayor Harrell signed executive order directing all dev applications through AI pilot
- **Austin**: Archistar partnership for zoning review
- **Lancaster, CA**: Labrynth partnership for AI auto-generated permits and compliance tracking

### 5. Cost/Scale Data (NAHB, White House)
- Regulations = $93,870 (24%) of average new home price (NAHB 2021 study)
- Updated 2025 White House report: 29.5% of new home cost from regulations
- CWA Section 404 permits: 1+ year average
- Multifamily: 40.6% of development costs from regulations (NMHC/NAHB)
- NIMBY opposition: +5.6% to costs, +7.5 months delay
- NAHB HMI: 37 (May 2026)

## Skepticism Points
- 37% → 80% is a TARGET, not a result — Denver deployed CivCheck only last month
- Bakersfield's 500 permits are mostly simple (solar, reroofs) — not custom residential with complex setbacks
- AI pre-check catches paperwork errors, not code interpretation disputes — the hard stuff remains human
- 59 positions cut + AI deployment = political convenience wrapped in tech optimism?
- Builder-side tools create an expertise gap — well-resourced builders get faster permits, small operators don't

# Research: AI Daily Logs — The Documentation Gap in Residential Construction

**Journalist:** Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca
**Article #:** 356
**Date:** June 12, 2026

## Thesis
Residential construction delays aren't primarily a scheduling problem. They're a documentation problem. Daily logs on residential sites are frequently worthless — 10-20 words, copy-pasted, or simply blank. When disputes hit (and ~30% of residential projects face some dispute), the records are garbage. AI tools now promise automated daily documentation from photos, voice, and sensor data. But the tools that actually work are built for $50M commercial projects, not $800K custom homes. The residential-scale tools have "AI" in the marketing but autocomplete in the product.

## Key Data Points

### Construction Delays
- Average single-family home: 8.6 months start-to-finish in 2023, up from 8.3 in 2022 (US Census Bureau)
- Multifamily: 21.2 months for projects completed 2023-2024 (Census)
- 90% of respondents reported construction delays in NMHC 2022 survey; 78% permitting delays, 86% start delays
- Oxford meta-analysis: 9 in 10 projects overrun budgets by average 28%

### Documentation Reality
- Daily logs are legally critical: "Disputes are very often won or lost on the strength of a contractor's documentation" (Taft Law)
- Contemporaneous daily logs have greater evidentiary weight than after-the-fact testimony (multiple legal sources)
- 73% of small GCs now rely on smartphones for site logs and daily reporting (Autodesk 2025 data)
- But quality remains poor — most residential daily logs are minimal text entries

### AI Documentation Tools — What They Cost

**Enterprise/Commercial:**
- Procore Copilot: Automates daily logs, RFI summaries, issue detection. Pricing: typically $30K-$100K+/year. Built for commercial GCs.
- Buildots: AI-powered computer vision, wearable cameras vs BIM models. Focus: large commercial sites.
- Boston Dynamics Spot + FieldAI: Autonomous site documentation. Cost: Spot alone ~$74,500. Not residential.

**Residential-Scale:**
- Buildertrend: $449-$1,099/month. Daily logs included but not AI-generated. Core value is scheduling + client communication.
- CoConstruct (now part of Buildertrend): $99-$399/month. Selection & budget sync, not AI documentation focus.
- Contractor Foreman: $49-$249/month. Daily logs, task lists. Most affordable. No AI generation.
- CompanyCam: Photo-based AI daily log generation from photos + voice-to-text descriptions. Most promising for residential. Pricing: $24-$59/user/month.
- Benetics: AI voice assistant for construction crews, auto-generates reports from voice. 30+ languages. Based in Switzerland, launched US ops in Detroit. Pricing not public.

### The Gap
- ALICE Technologies + McKinsey: "generative scheduling" deployed with 35+ clients, schedule reductions up to 20%, data center project reduced 40%. But these are $100M+ projects.
- UEL study (Frontiers in Built Environment, 2026, Dr. Jawed Qureshi): Framework for connecting risk warnings to automatic schedule adjustments. Review of 60 peer-reviewed studies. Key insight: "Projects generate enormous amounts of warning data every day... but nothing in the schedule actually changes when these signals appear." This describes the commercial world. Residential doesn't even generate the warning data.
- Seyfarth Shaw LLP (2026): AI tools on jobsites raise new contract questions — who relied on the output? Was it reviewed? Does the contract treat AI output as authoritative? Construction contracts not written for AI.
- HCR Law (UK): Standard form contracts (JCT, NEC, AIA) "were drafted on the assumption that project decisions are made by human professionals exercising judgment." Not AI-ready.

### Original Contribution
Cost-per-project analysis of AI daily documentation at residential scale:
- A custom home builder doing 8 homes/year
- Buildertrend Advanced ($719/month = $8,628/year = ~$1,078/project) — gets scheduling + daily logs but no AI generation
- CompanyCam Pro (~$49/user/month × 3 users = $147/month = $1,764/year = ~$220/project) — gets AI-generated daily logs from photos
- Combined: ~$1,300/project for functional AI-assisted documentation
- Compare to: average change order dispute costs $5,000-$50,000. Average mediation/arbitration: $15,000-$75,000.
- ROI: if documentation prevents even one dispute per 3-4 years, tools pay for themselves

### Strongest Counterargument
The real problem isn't tools. A $49/month tool can produce perfect daily logs if someone actually fills them in. The bottleneck is the superintendent's willingness to document, not the technology. AI voice-to-text reduces friction but doesn't eliminate the fundamental problem: field guys don't want to write reports.

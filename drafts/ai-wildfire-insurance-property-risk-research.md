# Research: AI Wildfire Insurance — Property-Level Risk Scoring

## Angle
Your insurer dropped you because of your zip code. AI insurers are looking at your actual roof, defensible space, and vegetation — and insuring homes FEMA wrote off. The shift from zone-based wildfire risk to property-level AI scoring is reshaping who can build, buy, and insure a home in fire-prone states.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** Tells them exactly what AI models evaluate (defensible space, building materials, vegetation, topography), what to prioritize when building, and how to get insured when traditional carriers won't touch their area.

## Novel Analysis
Calculate: If Delos covers 12M homes with zero losses, what's the implied accuracy of their AI model vs. FEMA's zone approach? If ZestyAI found $1T in mislabeled "low risk" homes out of 11.8M analyzed, that's ~8.5% of California properties where FEMA's assessment is actively misleading.

What specific building choices affect AI risk scores:
1. **Roof material** — Class A fire-rated (concrete tile, metal, asphalt composite) vs. wood shake
2. **Defensible space** — vegetation clearance within 100ft (Zone 1: 0-30ft combustible-free, Zone 2: 30-100ft reduced fuel)
3. **Building materials** — fire-resistant siding (fiber cement, stucco, brick) vs. wood
4. **Vent screening** — 1/8" mesh ember protection on attic/soffit vents
5. **Deck materials** — composite or non-combustible vs. wood decking
6. **Vegetation proximity** — tree canopy distance from structure, dead brush accumulation

These are things a builder or buyer CAN control, and they now have direct dollar impact via AI scoring.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI risk models are opaque — homeowners can't see or appeal their property-level score. This risks creating a two-tier system where AI-favored properties get affordable insurance while others are forced into the FAIR plan. Older homes, lower-income areas with deferred maintenance, and rural properties may be systematically scored higher risk. The models are trained on historical data that may not predict unprecedented fire behavior (see: Lahaina, Hawaii — no historical precedent).

## Limitations
- Cannot independently verify Delos's "zero fire losses" claim
- ZestyAI's $1T mislabeled figure methodology not publicly auditable
- Don't know specific model weights for building materials vs. vegetation vs. topography
- FAIR plan enrollment increase could have multiple causes beyond carrier retreat
- No longitudinal data on whether AI-scored underwriting performs better over full fire cycles

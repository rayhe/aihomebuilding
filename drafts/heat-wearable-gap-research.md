# Research Notes: AI Heat Wearable Gap in Residential Construction

## Core Thesis
OSHA's federal heat rule remains unfinalized after 5 years of rulemaking. The entire NIOSH heat expert team was fired in spring 2025. Meanwhile, wearable AI sensors can predict heat stroke with 95%+ accuracy for ~$25-39/worker/month. Nobody in residential construction is buying them. The technology exists. The deployment doesn't.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Harvard/OSHA Heat Injury Study (Alahmad et al., 2025, Environmental Health)
- Analyzed all 2023 OSHA injury cases from establishments with 100+ employees
- ~28,000 workplace injuries attributable to heat (heat index ≥70°F)
- At 105°F: injury odds +22% in states without heat rules, +8% in states with rules
- At 110°F: injury odds +22% (no rules) vs +9% (with rules)
- 1.18% of all injuries attributable to heat ≥70°F
- BLS total 2023: 2,368,900 injuries → ~28,000 heat-attributable

### 2. Construction Heat Deaths (Dong et al., Am J Ind Med, 2019; BLS CFOI 1992-2016)
- Construction = 6% of workforce but 36% of ALL occupational heat deaths
- Construction workers 13× more likely to die from heat than other industries
- Risk index by trade: cement masons 10.80×, roofers 6.93×, helpers 6.87×, brick masons 3.33×, construction laborers 1.93×
- Hispanic workers: 1.21× risk; Mexico-born: 1.91× risk
- BLS 1992-2021 average: 33 heat fatalities/year
- OSHA 2018-2024: 789 heat hospitalizations, 54 fatalities across 275 industries

### 3. OSHA Heat Rule Status (B&D Law, Littler, Bloomberg Law; 2025-2026)
- Rule proposed: Aug 30, 2024 (89 Fed. Reg. 70698)
- Informal hearing concluded: July 2, 2025
- Post-hearing comment closed: Oct 30, 2025
- NOT FINALIZED as of July 2026
- NIOSH entire heat team fired/pushed out spring 2025 (Scientific American, June 2025)
- OSHA revised Heat NEP issued April 10, 2026 as enforcement stopgap (5-year term through 2031)
- Biden rule triggers: 80°F heat index (initial), 90°F (high heat)
- Trump admin: "inching forward" but likely "watered down" performance-based approach
- OSHA heat inspections = 6% of all federal inspections last 5 years

### 4. Wearable AI Heat Prediction Tech
#### arXiv: Garmin + LSTM (Ullah et al., April 2026)
- 19 construction workers in Saudi Arabia
- Garmin Vivosmart 5 smartwatches (HR, HRV, SpO2)
- Attention-based LSTM: 95.40% accuracy, precision/recall/F1 = 0.982
- Suitable for IoT-enabled safety systems + BIM dashboards

#### SlateSafety BAND V2
- NSC Work to Zero partner
- Real-time physiological monitoring
- Alerts workers + supervisors when thresholds hit
- Subscription model (pricing not publicly disclosed)
- University of Alabama study: high correlation with ingestible pill (0.595) and Polar 10 HR monitor (0.926)

#### Kenzen
- Cloud-based heat monitoring system
- Arm-worn device: HR, activity, skin + ambient temp
- Subscription: per-worker, per-month basis (~$25-39/month range from JLC article)
- Multiple industrial deployments (construction, oil/gas, field services)
- Multi-level alerts: vibration, app notification, supervisor dashboard

#### CALERA/CORE Body Temp Monitor
- Combines skin temp + heat flux
- Parsons et al.: 100 Cambodian workers, 5 sectors, 4 climatic zones, 7 days
- Validated for occupational field use

### 5. Barcelona Heat Bracelet Deployment (Reuters, July 3, 2026)
- 1,400 bracelets deployed for outdoor workers (street cleaners, park workers)
- Measures body temp, alerts via sound + vibration
- Triggered after 51-year-old street sweeper died June 2025 in 86.7°F heat
- Spain: >1,000 excess heat deaths in June 2026 alone

### 6. Reuters: "Flying Blind" (July 7, 2026)
- Companies lack clear benchmarks for action
- 65% of garment buyers request no data on temp/humidity conditions
- No consensus on measurement metric (WBGT, heat index, etc.)
- Regulations "not very helpful" — wide diversity of approaches

### 7. Residential Construction Gap
- ~28,000 heat injuries/year (Harvard), construction disproportionately affected
- Residential roofing: 6.93× death risk
- 60% of construction workers face productivity loss above 28°C WBGT (meta-analysis)
- All wearable case studies are commercial/industrial — ZERO published residential deployments
- 1.5M US housing starts/year, predominantly small crews (5-15 workers) with no safety tech budget

## Original Contribution
Nobody has calculated the per-crew cost comparison for residential:
- 10-person framing crew × $35/worker/month × 5 hot months = $1,750/season
- vs. OSHA willful violation: $161,323
- vs. workers' comp heat claim: ~$30,000-$50,000
- vs. wrongful death settlement: $1.2M+
- vs. lost productivity: 60% of workers lose productivity above 82°F, costing $200-500/day per affected worker

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — GCs and residential subcontractors running outdoor crews in summer need to know: (1) what wearable heat monitoring exists, (2) what it costs, (3) why nobody in residential is using it, and (4) the math showing it's absurdly cheaper than one incident.

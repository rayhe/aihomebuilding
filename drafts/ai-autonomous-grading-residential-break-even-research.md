# Research: AI-Autonomous Grading for Residential Construction — Break-Even Analysis

## Angle
Autonomous excavation and GPS machine-control grading have proven themselves on large infrastructure and commercial jobs. But residential contractors keep hearing they should adopt this tech. When does the math actually work for homebuilding — and when is it a $400K paperweight?

**Original contribution:** A break-even calculation for autonomous grading per residential lot vs. subdivision-scale deployment. Nobody has done this math specifically for residential.

## Kill Test
✅ Directly affects site prep costs ($20K-$60K per home) — anyone building or buying a home cares about this line item.

## Break-Even Analysis (Original Contribution)

### Single Custom Home Lot
- Total grading investment for autonomous: ~$400K–$750K (machine + retrofit)
- Annual TCO with maintenance/software: ~$60K–$100K (depreciated over 5 years + $10K–$20K/yr maintenance + software)
- Typical residential grading job: 16–40 hours, $7,000 average revenue
- Setup time: 6–8 hours per NEW site = 15–50% of the total job
- If the setup amortizes poorly on small one-off jobs, autonomous can't compete
- **At 1 lot/month (12/year): cost per lot from autonomous system = $5,000–$8,333 in equipment depreciation alone (before fuel/maintenance)**
- **At 2 lots/month (24/year): $2,500–$4,167 per lot** — starting to get competitive
- **Need 40+ lots/year just to match conventional grading economics**

### Subdivision / Production Builder Scale
- 50+ lots in a planned subdivision, same terrain profile, single mobilization
- Setup cost amortizes across all lots — essentially one-time
- Operator savings: 1 supervisor for 2–4 machines vs. 1 skilled operator each
- At $40/hr loaded, 20 hours/lot grading, 50 lots: $40K labor conventional = $10K–$20K supervised autonomous
- 30–50% productivity gain = 50 lots done in time of 25–35
- Rework near zero with ±2cm accuracy (residential often has drainage issues from poor grading)
- **Break-even: ~35–50 lots/year per machine. Below that, rent a human.**

### The Rework Factor
- Residential drainage failures from poor grading: $5,000–$15,000 to fix post-construction
- NAHB estimates 2–5% of homes require post-construction drainage remediation
- ±2cm machine accuracy vs. ±5–10cm typical hand-grading = dramatic quality improvement
- This hidden cost is what makes the break-even lower than the pure labor math suggests

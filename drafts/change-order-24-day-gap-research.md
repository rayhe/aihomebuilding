# Research: The 24-Day Change Order Gap

## Angle
The average residential builder waits 24 days between signing a T&M ticket and submitting the change order. Digital tools cut that to 3.5 days. Most residential builders are still on paper. Frank DeLuca's beat — process failure, documentation, the operational grind.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — homeowners dealing with change orders need to understand why their project bleeds money and time. Builders need to know the ROI of going digital on change order tracking.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Rhumbix (industry data, 2026)
- 90% of construction projects experience cost overruns
- Change orders typically represent 10-15% of total contract value
- 24-day average between T&M ticket signing and change order submission (manual processes)
- 3.5 days with digital tracking (85% reduction)
- Specialty contractors take 30 days average to price T&M tickets
- 56.5% of cost overruns caused by design changes
- 52% of total cost growth comes from rework
- 32% of cost overruns stem from estimating errors/omissions
- Digital tracking: 15% reduction in cost overruns, 20% increase in delivery efficiency
- Source: https://www.rhumbix.com/blog/change-order-tracking-construction

### 2. PlanRadar Global Survey (2026)
- 1,728 construction professionals surveyed across 14 countries
- Mid-project changes (RFIs, change orders) = #2 challenge (after keeping schedule)
- 75% report approvals received after agreed timeframes
- 1 in 4 say delayed approvals add >1 month to timelines
- 2 in 3 report mid-project changes lead to budget overruns in many/most projects
- >1/3 fail to recover most additional change-related costs
- When tracking ownership is easy: 7 in 10 recover majority of costs
- ~80% of documentation lives across unconsolidated channels (email, text, phone)
- Those who easily locate docs are 2x as likely to demonstrate responsibility for delays
- Non-adopters 1.3x more likely to experience month-long delays
- Non-adopters 1.7x more likely to have difficulty locating documentation
- 58% believe AI could help with biggest challenges, 65% for admin tasks
- ~50% spend 11+ hours/week on admin (>1 full day)
- 2/3 of AI-integrated adopters save 2+ hours/week/project on admin
- Source: https://specfinish.co.uk/new-industry-report-managing-project-changes-in-the-age-of-ai/

### 3. Document Crunch / AIA Standards (2026)
- GC profit margins average under 7%
- Change order profit margins tend to be 15-20%
- No widely agreed acceptable change order percentage — varies by project
- Best practice: Potential Change Order (PCO) log at weekly meetings
- AIA G701 is standard change order form, but modifications common
- AI contract review catches "gotcha" fine print in CO clauses
- 5,000+ monthly Google searches for "construction change order"
- Source: https://www.documentcrunch.com/blog/change-order

### 4. Supporting Data
- 98% of megaprojects experience cost overruns or delays
- Average cost increase on large projects: 80% of original value
- 35% of projects experience at least one major scope change
- AGC: Construction costs rose 3.6% YoY in Nov 2025, fastest since Jan 2023
- NAHB: Builders report $9,200 average cost per home from tariffs (March 2025)
- 67% of construction projects run over budget (biCanvas ERP, Feb 2026)

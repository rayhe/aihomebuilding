# Research: AI ADU Permit Review — Solving the Wrong End of the Pipeline

## Slug: ai-adu-permit-review-wrong-end

## Journalist: Catherine Chen

## Thesis
California's AI-for-permitting push focuses on reviewing complete applications faster. But 90% of San Jose's ADU applications come back incomplete because homeowners can't navigate the requirements. AI that speeds up review of the 10% that arrive complete produces marginal gains while the 90% rejection rate remains untouched. The real barrier to ADU construction isn't bureaucratic speed — it's applicant confusion, cost ($150K-$350K), and exhausted funding programs.

## Primary Sources

### 1. San Jose CivCheck Pilot
- Source: StateScoop (statescoop.com), GovTech, Tech Brew
- San Jose piloting CivCheck AI to pre-screen ADU applications
- **Key stat: 90%+ of ADU applications returned due to missing information**
- Mayor Matt Mahan: tech-driven approach to housing crisis
- Plan to expand to single-family homes if successful
- CivCheck acquired by Clariti: claims 97%+ accuracy, 80% reduction in approval times
- 488 ADU permits issued in 2024 (City of San Jose data)
- 3,000+ permits issued, 1,451 ADUs built since 2019 (San José Spotlight)

### 2. Lancaster Labrynth AI Permitting
- Source: SmartCitiesDive, GovTech, StateScoop, ConstructionOwners.com
- First US city to adopt AI-powered permitting (Labrynth platform)
- Agentic workflows: pre-screen submissions, flag compliance issues, guide applicants
- Mayor R. Rex Parris: national model for smart governance
- Labrynth's "Red Tape Index" — evaluating permitting speed across 500+ cities
- Announced September 3, 2025

### 3. AB 1332 — California Pre-Approved ADU Plans Mandate
- Source: LegiScan, CalBO, multiple city .gov sites
- Mandates local agencies provide 6 permit-ready ADU floor plans by April 1, 2025
- Includes studio, 1BR, 2BR units
- Must post plans online, offer to developers at no cost
- 30-day approval timeline for permits using pre-approved plans
- Many cities compliant on paper but plans often generic, not adapted to local site conditions

### 4. Unpermitted ADU Equity Gap
- Source: American Planning Association (planning.org)
- Computer vision study: 78% of unpermitted ADUs in San José (2016-2020) are in less affluent, diverse neighborhoods
- Highlights systemic barriers to legal construction: cost, complexity, knowledge
- Unpermitted = built without permits = the people who need housing flexibility the most can't afford or navigate the legal process

### 5. ADU Costs and Funding
- Source: Bipartisan Policy Center, Steadily, CalHFA
- Median ADU cost nationally: ~$150K
- San Jose ADU cost: $150K-$350K (for new construction)
- CalHFA $40K ADU grant program: **paused — funding exhausted**
- 80,000+ ADUs permitted in California since 2016
- Pre-approved plans cost $1K-$7K (some free)
- ADU properties appreciate 9.34% annually vs 7.65% for non-ADU (FHFA analysis, 2013-2023)

### 6. Broader Permitting Context
- LA residential permits: 8,714 in 2025 — second weakest since 2013 (Hilgard Economics)
- SFR starts fell 11% in 2026 (FirstTuesday Journal)
- California leads US in ADU construction (23K in 2023, JBREC)
- ADU boom "not helping housing crisis" — mostly high-end, stand-alone (JBREC)
- Urban Institute: AI zoning tools often provide "unhelpful responses" — poor information retrieval
- Only a few dozen US cities testing AI permit technologies (Independent Institute)

### 7. What AI Actually Fixes vs. What the Problem Actually Is
- AI fixes: review speed (weeks → minutes for complete applications)
- AI doesn't fix: applicant confusion (why 90% come back), cost ($150K+), funding gaps (CalHFA paused), equity (78% unpermitted in low-income areas), site-specific complications (soils, utilities, easements)
- The CivCheck "pre-check" is the one tool pointed in the right direction — it tells applicants what's missing BEFORE formal submission
- But even pre-checks can't solve the fundamental knowledge and cost barriers

## Kill Test

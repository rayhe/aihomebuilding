# Research: AI Data Center Boom Draining Residential Construction Workforce

## Angle
The AI infrastructure buildout — $726B in 2025 data center capex, headed past $1T in 2026 — is creating a labor vacuum that sucks skilled tradespeople away from residential homebuilding. The same AI that promises to "transform" homebuilding is actively stealing the humans who build homes. Wage premiums, boomtown dynamics, and an already-thin pipeline mean your next home will cost more and take longer because the electrician who would've wired it is in a data center in rural Virginia.

## Key Data Points

### The Scale of Data Center Spending
- Dell'Oro Group: Data center capex grew 57% to $726 billion in 2025
- Expected to cross $1 TRILLION in 2026 (50%+ growth rate)
- Source: NetworkWorld / Dell'Oro Group

### Labor Shortfall
- CSIS: 75,000-140,000 incremental skilled trades positions needed beyond current capacity by 2030 for data center builds alone
- Mercer (William Self): "The single biggest constraint of this entire buildout is the labor needed — not capital, land, or even energy"
- For every 100 young people entering manufacturing, 102 leave (net negative)
- ~25% of workers globally nearing retirement age
- Sources: CSIS study (Sept 2025), Marsh webinar (March 2026)

### Residential Impact
- HBI/NAHB/University of Denver study (June 2025): Labor shortage costs residential construction $10.806 billion/year
- 19,000 single-family homes NOT BUILT in 2024 due to labor shortage
- Average construction delay: 1.98 months per home
- Higher carrying costs: $2.663 billion annually
- Source: Home Builders Institute / NAHB press release

### Wage Competition
- NAHB (Nov 2024): Residential building worker wages grew 9.9% YOY (September 2024) — unprecedented in data series since 1990
- Average hourly earnings: $33.51/hr residential (Sept 2024)
- But data center electrician wages reported higher — Rosendin Electric deploying 300-400 electricians to single sites
- Time-to-hire for trades (56 days) now exceeds professional roles (54 days) — "labor flip" (Randstad)
- Source: BLS, NAHB, Randstad

### Demand Surge (Randstad, 50M+ job postings analyzed)
- Robotics technicians: demand up 107%
- HVAC engineers: up 67%
- Construction roles overall: up 30%
- Electricians: up 18%
- Welders: up 25%
- Skilled trades demand growing 3x faster than professional roles
- Source: Randstad USA (2026)

### Boomtown Effects
- Prineville, Oregon: 500 data center construction workers flooded town (Apple + Facebook campuses)
- Zero percent rental vacancy
- 17% of hotel rooms booked long-term by data center workers
- Workers living in tents at campgrounds
- Companies building workforce housing like oil field camps
- Source: The Bulletin (Prineville), Data Center Knowledge

### Geographic Shock
- Development expanding from traditional hubs (N. Virginia, Phoenix, Dallas) to rural areas: Columbus OH, South Bend IN, Abilene TX, rural Louisiana, Texas Panhandle
- Cross-industry poaching: power, defense, process industries all competing for same workers
- Source: Mercer/Marsh webinar, Data Center Knowledge

### BLS Construction Jobs Data
- 231,000 open construction positions January 2026
- 2.7% unfilled rate
- 349,000 new hires in January
- Source: BLS JOLTS

## Original Contribution
Nobody has done this calculation: For every 1,000 MW of data center capacity built, how many electricians/HVAC workers are diverted from residential construction? Using CSIS's 140,000-worker shortfall and NAHB's 19,000 unbuilt homes, the math suggests approximately 7.4 homes not built per data center worker poached from residential construction. That's a rough cross-reference nobody else has made.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're a homeowner waiting on a new build or a GC trying to hire electricians, this explains why your project is delayed and more expensive — and it's going to get worse before it gets better.

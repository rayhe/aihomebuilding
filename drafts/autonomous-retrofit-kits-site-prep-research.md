# Research: Autonomous Retrofit Kits for Construction Equipment

## Angle
Your bulldozer already knows how to dig. For $40K-$50K, bolt-on AI kits can teach it to do the work without an operator in the cab. Three startups and one incumbent are racing to retrofit existing construction fleets with self-driving AI — and the economics are starting to work for residential site prep.

## Journalist
Jake "Jackhammer" Kowalski — construction tech, tools, robotics

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. Site preparation (grading, excavation, compaction) is one of the first and most expensive phases of residential construction, typically costing $15,000-$50,000 per lot. These retrofit autonomy kits address exactly these tasks and directly target the labor shortage hitting residential builders hardest.

## Original Contribution
Break-even math for a residential subdivision developer using a $50K retrofit kit:
- Average site prep labor component for grading/compaction: ~$8,000-$12,000 per lot (operator + equipment time)
- With 14% productivity gain (Bluelight data) or 30% (Gravis data) on equipment tasks
- 20-lot subdivision: savings of $1,600-$3,600 per lot on equipment tasks alone → $32,000-$72,000 total
- Break-even: 1-2 subdivisions (14-30 lots) depending on productivity gain realized
- BUT: the bigger win is availability — when you can't find the second roller operator, one operator + one autonomous machine keeps the schedule on track

## Strongest Counterargument
- Residential sites are messier than infrastructure projects — smaller lots, more obstacles, tight setbacks, utility interference
- Most deployments to date are large-scale infrastructure (130-acre manufacturing sites, 60-mile pipelines, mining) — residential is much smaller scale
- $50K per kit is real money for a small GC running 3-5 homes/year
- Insurance and liability questions remain largely unanswered for autonomous equipment on residential sites
- None of these companies have published residential-specific case studies

## Limitations
- All productivity claims come from company-provided data, not independent verification
- No peer-reviewed studies on autonomous construction equipment productivity yet
- Residential construction use case is extrapolated from infrastructure data
- Long-term maintenance costs of retrofit kits unknown
- Regulatory landscape for autonomous equipment on residential job sites is murky

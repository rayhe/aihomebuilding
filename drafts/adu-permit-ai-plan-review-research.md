# Research: AI ADU Plan Review — The 60-Day Mandate Nobody Meets

## Journalist: Catherine "Code" Chen (Policy & Regulation)

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES. Anyone planning an ADU in California (or other states liberalizing ADU laws) faces a 4-8 month permitting gauntlet. This article reveals where the bottleneck is, what AI tools are cutting through it, and what new legal rights homeowners have when cities miss deadlines.

## Thesis
California mandates 60-day ADU approval. In practice, plan check takes 8-24 weeks. New 2026 laws (SB 543) finally put teeth behind the mandate — but cities can't meet the deadline without AI plan review tools. The homeowner who understands both sides of this equation can shave months and thousands off their ADU timeline.

## Primary Sources

### 1. California Legislative Data
- **AB 1332 (effective Jan 1, 2025)**: Requires ALL California cities to establish pre-approved ADU plan programs. Pre-approved plans skip design review. Source: California Government Code Section 65852.27(4)(A)(i)
- **SB 543 (effective Jan 1, 2026)**: Closes the "completeness" loophole — cities must determine completeness within 15 BUSINESS DAYS. Miss it? Application is automatically deemed complete, 60-day clock starts. Applicants now have statutory appeal rights. Also: interior livable space clarification, impact fee protections extended to JADUs.
- **SB 9 (2025 version, effective Jan 1, 2026)**: Local ADU ordinances not submitted to HCD within 60 days of adoption are automatically VOID. State default rules (more permissive) take over.
- **AB 818 (effective July 1, 2026)**: 10-calendar-day permit approval for modular homes in fire zones.
- Source: autonomous.ai/ourblog/new-adu-laws-in-california-2026, San Jose ADU page

## Original Calculation — Carrying Cost of Permit Delays

**Setup**: Homeowner takes a construction loan for a $350,000 ADU at 8% annual interest. Each month the project is delayed before construction starts = $2,333 in carrying costs.

**Scenario comparison**:
- San Francisco actual timeline: 12-24 weeks plan check = 3-6 months
- Sacramento actual timeline: 5-10 weeks = 1.25-2.5 months
- If AI plan review compressed the initial review by 75% (Cembla's claim of 85% faster): SF drops to 1.8-3.6 weeks first review

But the bigger savings: reducing CORRECTION ROUNDS. Average 1-3 rounds, each adding 2-6 weeks.

**Key insight**: AI plan review catches compliance issues BEFORE submission, reducing corrections from 2-3 rounds to 0-1. This saves:
- 2-3 rounds × 3 weeks avg = 6-9 weeks of correction cycles eliminated
- At $2,333/month carrying cost = $3,500-$5,250 saved
- Plus: opportunity cost of rental income delayed (Bay Area ADU rent: ~$2,500-$3,500/month)
- Combined delay cost per month: $4,833-$5,833
- Eliminating 2 months of corrections = $9,666-$11,666 saved

**Total cost of permit delay for a typical Bay Area ADU**:
- Current: 4-8 months pre-construction × $4,833/month = $19,332-$38,664
- With AI-assisted submission: 2-4 months × $4,833/month = $9,666-$19,332
- Savings: $9,666-$19,332

## Strongest Counterargument
AI plan review tools are still nascent. Cembla doesn't publish accuracy rates vs. human reviewers. "85% faster" may mean faster to flag obvious issues, not faster to make nuanced judgment calls about complex lot configurations, hillside setbacks, or coastal zone overlays. San Francisco's 12-24 week timeline isn't just bureaucratic laziness — it's discretionary design review that AI can't replicate. Cities with the worst backlogs often have the most complex zoning overlays. The tool helps most where the problems are simplest.

Also: pre-approved plans (AB 1332) may do more for the average homeowner than AI tools, since they skip review entirely — but only for cookie-cutter designs that may not fit your lot.

## Limitations
- Cembla doesn't publish municipal adoption numbers — we don't know how many cities actually use it
- Plan check timelines from GatherADU are self-reported/anecdotal, not from official municipal data
- Carrying cost calculation assumes construction loan draw at start (some lenders delay draws)
- Comparison doesn't account for architect fees to prepare AI-compatible plans
- DreamLADU, OpoPlan, and other consumer tools haven't published permit success rates for AI-generated plans

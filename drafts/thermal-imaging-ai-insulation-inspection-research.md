# Research: AI Thermal Imaging for Pre-Drywall Insulation Inspection
## Article #348 — Jake Kowalski (Construction Technology)

### Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — homeowners getting new construction or major renovations can use a $300 thermal scan (or a $250 FLIR ONE attachment) to catch insulation defects that cost $2,000–$10,000+ to fix after drywall goes up. Builders can use AI-analyzed thermal scans to verify insulation quality before closing walls.

### Core Thesis
Most new homes have insulation defects that nobody catches until the energy bills arrive. A $300 thermal scan before drywall goes up catches problems that cost 10x more to fix after. AI is turning what used to require a trained thermographer into something a site super with a smartphone can do in 20 minutes.

### Primary Sources

**1. DOE/NIST Field Investigation (Weidt, Saxler, Rossiter 1980 — foundational study, findings still cited)**
- ~30% of homes had insulation missing in 5–10% of wall area
- ~50% of homes had <5% wall area uninsulated (still noncompliant with DOE standards)
- More than half of all installations noncompliant
- Shrinkage/fissures in ~67% of homes
- Heat losses around windows in 72%, doors in 31%
- Wall-to-wall joint losses in 74% of homes
- Source: digital.library.unt.edu (NIST Interagency Report)

**2. RESNET Insulation Grading System**
- Grade I: Minor defects only — occasional small gaps, <2% compression
- Grade II: Moderate defects — minor compression, gaps around wiring/outlets
- Grade III: Substantial defects — significant voids, compression, or missing insulation
- HERS raters inspect for voids, gaps, compression
- Grade I required for ENERGY STAR certification
- Source: insulationinstitute.org

**3. Thermal Imaging Inspection Costs (Angi, 2026)**
- Full-home thermal scan: $300–$500
- Partial scan: $150–$300
- Pre-drywall inspection: avg $280 nationally
- Problems found post-drywall cost ranges:
  - Mold from water leaks: $500–$4,000
  - Electrical issues: $700–$15,000
  - Foundation: $2,000–$10,000
- Source: angi.com

**4. ASTM C1060 — Standard Practice for Thermographic Inspection**
- Official standard for IR inspection of insulation in frame buildings
- Detects missing insulation, malfunctioning installations
- Requires independent validation (visual inspection or R-value measurement)
- Source: store.astm.org

**5. GSA Remote Sensing Drone Assessment**
- Drones detect 50% more defects than manual evaluations
- 80% less expensive than manual assessments
- 80% faster from data collection to analytics delivery
- AI-powered analytics output 2D drawings and 3D models showing thermal anomalies
- Meets ASHRAE/ACCA 211 P standards
- Source: gsa.gov

**6. FLIR Products & Ecosystem**
- FLIR ONE smartphone attachment: $199–$400
- FLIR C5 standalone: ~$500
- Point-and-shoot cameras: $500–$1,000
- Pistol grip (advanced): $1,000+
- FLIR Home Inspect app — step-by-step guide for surveys
- FLIR Ignite cloud storage
- AI-augmented thermal products expected >40% of new launches by 2027
- Source: flir.com, finehomebuilding.com, amazon.com

**7. AI/ML Research for Building Envelope Inspection**
- Umeå University: GAN-based color-to-thermal AI for anomaly detection (one-class classifier, label-free)
- Deep learning panoramic IR framework: semantic segmentation, 10-minute automated analysis
- Computer vision for thermal bridge detection, moisture mapping
- Source: arxiv.org (Kurtser et al.), MDPI/Buildings journal

**8. Infrared Thermography Market**
- AI-integrated thermal imaging growing rapidly
- FLIR and Leonardo DRS investing in AI-augmented systems
- >40% of new product launches to include AI by 2027
- Subscription-based analytics services emerging
- Source: LinkedIn market analysis

**9. Construction Rework Costs**
- Rework costs 5–10% of total project costs (Autodesk/FMI: $65B annually in US)
- Navigant: 9% when including indirect costs
- Pre-drywall fix for insulation: minutes of labor, $0 materials (just repositioning batts)
- Post-drywall fix: tear out drywall ($1,150–$6,000 per wall for insulation retrofit)
- Source: onekeyresources.milwaukeetool.com, planradar.com, angi.com

**10. InterNACHI Pre-Drywall Inspection Standards**
- Updated March 2025
- Non-invasive visual examination of accessible areas
- Checks: framing, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, insulation, weather barriers
- Identifies defects before they're hidden behind drywall
- Source: nachi.org

### Novel Analysis
Cross-reference: DOE data showing 50%+ noncompliance rate × average post-drywall repair cost of $1,150–$6,000 × 1.4M new housing starts/year = enormous preventable cost. A $300 thermal scan has an expected value of $575–$3,000 in avoided repairs (50% chance of catching a $1,150–$6,000 problem). That's a 2x–10x ROI on the scan alone.

With AI analysis, the thermographer expertise barrier drops. A FLIR ONE ($250) + AI analysis software can be operated by a site superintendent, not a certified thermographer ($150/hr).

# Research: Samsung and LG AI Modular Homes — Appliance-First Architecture

## Journalist: Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)

## Angle
When consumer electronics companies start building whole houses, the design process inverts: the floor plan begins with the appliance ecosystem, not the inhabitant. Samsung and LG are attempting the opposite trajectory of Katerra and Veev — they are factories entering construction, not construction companies trying to become factories. What does this mean for residential architecture?

## Key Sources

- **Source:** Seoul Economic Daily (en.sedaily.com), June 2026
- Samsung launched "Samsung AI Modular Home" B2C with Gonggan Jejakso (timber modular housing specialist)
- 80%+ of house prefabricated in factory

- Sizes from 33 sqm (355 sq ft) to 132 sqm (1,421 sq ft)

- Previously B2B only (MOU with Yuchang E&C for apartments, IFA 2025 showcase)

### Samsung FläktGroup Acquisition
- **Source:** KED Global, June 2026

- Samsung now controls home climate, not just home appliances

### Failure Precedents
- **Katerra:** $2B+ raised, 7,500 employees, planned 14 distribution centers, dozen countries. Bankrupted 2021. Tried to do everything at once. — Construction Dive, Fast Company, Not Boring

## Original Contribution

### 1. Cost Decomposition: Structure vs. Ecosystem
- LG MONO Core 27: $72,000 / 290.6 sq ft = $247.77/sq ft (all-in)
- Estimated included appliance retail value: ~$9,600 (AC $3,000 + fridge $2,500 + washer $1,500 + induction $1,200 + boiler $800 + door lock $400 + switch $200)
- Structure-only estimate: ~$62,400 / 290.6 = ~$214.73/sq ft
- US Census median new-build cost (excl. land): ~$150-200/sq ft
- The appliance layer represents roughly 13% of total unit cost
- At retail, buying those appliances separately would cost more, so the factory integration offers real savings

### 2. Opposite Trajectory Thesis
- Katerra/Veev trajectory: construction companies trying to become manufacturers (failed)
- Samsung/LG trajectory: manufacturers entering construction (in progress)
- Samsung's manufacturing advantages: semiconductor fabs with <1% defect rates, global supply chain mastery, HVAC vertical integration ($1.7B FläktGroup), IoT platform (SmartThings)
- Key difference in execution: Samsung partners with modular builder (1,700 units/yr, modest scale), not building from scratch
- This is the "crawl before sprint" approach Katerra never tried

### 3. Architectural Implications
- When the home's design starts from an appliance catalog, the spatial logic inverts: rooms become docking stations for Samsung products
- Samsung homes are optimized for SmartThings topology, not for how light enters a bedroom
- But the counter: pre-integration solves the "smart home retrofit" problem that plagues most new construction
- Historical parallel: Alexander Graham Bell's company eventually wired every American home — the appliance preceded the architecture

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — anyone considering modular construction needs to understand that consumer electronics companies are entering the space with vertically integrated product-homes. The cost decomposition helps buyers understand what they're paying for. The failure comparison helps builders and developers evaluate partner risk.

## Limitations
- All Samsung/LG pricing is Korean market; US costs will differ due to labor, codes, land
- Space Factory's 1,700 units/year is Korean market data; US production capacity unknown
- No third-party verification of Samsung's "80% factory prefabrication" claim
- The $9,600 appliance value estimate uses approximate US retail prices for comparable LG products
- No customer satisfaction or post-occupancy data exists yet for either Samsung or LG modular homes

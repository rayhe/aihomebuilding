# Research: The Missing Middle Code Cliff — AI Design Tools vs. the IRC/IBC Divide

## Journalist: Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)

## Angle
Cities across America legalized fourplexes. Almost nobody is building them. The reason isn't NIMBYism or market demand — it's an invisible regulatory cliff in the building code that makes the third unit exponentially harder to build than the second. AI design tools are trying to bridge the gap, but they're designing around a structural problem that requires a code-level fix.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — anyone considering building a duplex-or-larger on a newly upzoned lot needs to understand the IRC/IBC cliff before they spend money on design. And the AI tools referenced could save 15-60% on design costs.

## Key Data Points (3+ Primary Sources)

### 1. NAHB Construction Data (March 2026)
- 19,000 2-4 unit housing starts in 2025 — best year since 2007
- BUT only 5% of total multifamily production
- Historical norm (2000-2010): ~11% of total multifamily
- Source: https://www.nahb.org/blog/2026/03/missing-middle-construction

### 2. CNU / IRC vs IBC Code Cliff (April 2026)
- IRC covers 1-2 family dwellings and townhouses
- 3+ units = IBC territory = same commercial-grade rules as 200-unit apartment
- "Our code today treats a four-plex the same as a 200-unit apartment building. That doesn't make sense." — Dallas council member Paul Ridley
- IBC triggers: commercial-grade engineering, longer permitting, higher design fees
- Residential builders won't cross into IBC: wrong staff, wrong loan products, wrong margins
- Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac offer residential mortgage products for up to 4 units — but the code treats 3+ as commercial
- States that legalized fourplexes: Oregon, California, Washington, Minneapolis, Portland, Raleigh
- In Austin, even with fourplexes allowed by right, builders prefer duplexes to avoid IBC
- Pre-approved plan sets work under IRC (prescriptive, standardized) but not IBC (project-specific review)
- Source: https://www.cnu.org/publicsquare/2026/04/01/your-city-legalized-triplexes-building-code-said-no

### 3. Cove Architecture / Vitras.ai (Atlanta)

- AI agents reduce design timelines by 60%

- Units priced below $450,000 for missing middle
- 20% reserved for households at 80% AMI (Atlanta Inclusionary Zoning)
- Source: https://cove.inc/blog/ai-designed-affordable-housing-atlanta-west-end
- Source: https://www.housingwire.com/articles/ai-crushes-missing-middle-time-and-cost-curves-toward-affordability/

## Novel Contribution — Original Calculation
**The "Third Unit Penalty"**: A duplex at $388K average = ~$194K/unit under IRC. Adding a third unit triggers IBC, which requires:
- Architect/engineer stamping (commercial): +$15-25K
- Fire-rated assemblies, commercial sprinkler systems: +$8-15K per unit
- ADA compliance review: +$3-5K
- Longer permitting timeline: 3-6 months additional (carrying costs)
- Commercial-grade plan review fees: +$5-10K

Conservative estimate: the IBC threshold adds $35-55K in soft costs to a triplex project. That third unit doesn't cost $194K like the first two — it costs $229-249K, a 18-28% penalty. For fourplexes, the per-unit penalty dilutes but remains significant. This is why Austin builders stop at duplexes: the math breaks.

## Strongest Counterargument
The IBC exists for safety. Three or more families sharing walls, utility runs, and egress paths create genuinely different fire and structural risks than a duplex. The IRC was never designed for these configurations. Lowering standards to boost unit counts could produce buildings that burn or collapse differently than single-family homes, with liability exposure nobody has tested.

## Limitations
- Cost estimates for IBC premium are synthesized from multiple sources, not a single controlled study
- Cove Architecture's 60% timeline reduction is self-reported
- Norfolk's 15-20% design savings haven't been independently verified at scale
- Missing middle construction data doesn't separate "couldn't build" from "chose not to build"
- State-by-state legalization count is approximate; many have partial or conditional reforms

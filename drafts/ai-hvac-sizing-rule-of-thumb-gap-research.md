# Research: Your HVAC Contractor Sized Your System With a Rule of Thumb

## Core Thesis
The majority of residential HVAC systems in the US are oversized — not by a little, but by 50-100%. Manual J load calculations average 1,431 sf/ton vs the industry's 400-600 sf/ton rule of thumb. 70% of UK heat pump installations were wrong-sized (Build Test Solutions/Veritherm study). NIST found improper installation increases energy use ~30%. AI tools like Conduit Tech can scan homes and generate accurate load calcs automatically, but almost nobody in residential HVAC uses them.

## Key Sources

### 1. NIST Technical Note 1848 (2014) — Sensitivity Analysis of Installation Faults on Heat Pump Performance
- Domanski, Henderson, Payne (3-year study)
- Improper installation increases household energy use for space heating/cooling by ~30%
- "The majority of air-conditioning equipment evaluated in the field performed below rated energy-efficiency levels due to one or more installation faults"
- Dominant faults: leaky ducts > refrigerant undercharge > incorrect indoor airflow
- Source: https://www.nist.gov/news-events/news/2014/11/underperforming-energy-efficiency-hvac-equipment-suffers-due-poor

### 2. DOE Residential HVAC Installation Practices Review (2018)
- 44 reports reviewed
- "Under current industry practice, the majority of systems — especially those installed as emergency replacements — are installed without performing detailed load calculations"
- Manual J field data collection takes 90 minutes to 8 hours per home
- "Comfort and energy performance impacted most by improper airflow, incorrect refrigerant charge, and duct performance"
- Source: https://www.energy.gov/cmei/buildings/articles/residential-hvac-installation-practices-review-research-findings

### 3. Energy Vanguard Manual J Data (ACCA Blog, 2016)
- 40 Manual J load calculations in hot/mixed climates
- Average: 1,431 sf/ton (vs 400-600 sf/ton rule of thumb)
- Not a single calculation was as low as the high end of the typical rule of thumb range
- "If you tell me your load calculations average 400-600 square feet per ton, I assume you're not doing them correctly" — Allison Bailes, PhD
- Manual J itself still oversizes by 15-40% (per field practitioners)
- Source: https://hvac-blog.acca.org/manual-j-load-calculations-vs-rules-thumb/

### 4. UK Heat Pump Sizing Study — Build Test Solutions, Veritherm, Elmhurst Energy
- Funded by UK DESNZ Heat Pump Ready Programme
- 56 homes tested
- 70% had wrong-sized heat pumps using BS EN12831 heat loss calculations
- Average 35% difference between calculated and measured heat loss
- 59% of homes had overestimated heat loss (oversized)
- Oversized heat pumps: ~10% higher capital costs + higher maintenance + lower efficiency
- Source: https://www.acrjournal.uk/heat-pumps/up-to-three-quarters-of-homes-in-study-fitted-with-wrong-size/

### 5. AHR Expo 2026 / Conduit Tech — AI for Residential HVAC
- Shelby Breger, co-founder Conduit Tech
- "Every single person in here … is delivering equipment that is probably 50 to 100% more expensive than it was six years ago"
- Conduit: scans home, generates 3D models and load calcs, visualizes comfort issues
- AI reduces callbacks, improves close rates, standardizes design
- Source: https://www.servicetitan.com/blog/ai-for-residential-hvac

### 6. ICF / Sightline Digital Twin Study (Maryland, April 2025)
- EE program participants saw 18-32% smaller bill increases vs non-participants
- Digital twin modeling of representative Maryland homes
- Tested across 5 heating types: heat pump, electric resistance, natural gas, fuel oil, propane
- Source: https://www.icf.com/insights/energy/ai-digital-twins-energy-efficiency-savings

### 7. NREL Smart HVAC Diagnostics Study (July 2024)
- "Optimizing Residential HVAC Systems: Evaluating How the Usage of Smart Diagnostic Tools..."
- Smart diagnostic tools for quality installation and commissioning
- Source: https://nrel.gov (referenced but not fully read)

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES.
- If you're installing a heat pump or AC, you're probably getting an oversized system
- Oversized = higher purchase cost, higher energy bills, poor humidity control, shorter equipment life
- Simple ask: demand a Manual J load calculation (not a rule of thumb), or ask about AI-based tools like Conduit Tech
- For the builder: smart diagnostic tools cut callbacks and improve margins

## Novel Contribution
- Cross-referencing the UK 70% oversizing data with the US Manual J rule-of-thumb data to show this is a global pattern, not just contractor laziness
- Calculating the financial impact: if your 3-ton AC should be a 2-ton based on actual loads, that's $1,000-2,000 in excess equipment cost + 15-30% higher annual energy bills
- The AI angle: tools exist that do the load calc in minutes instead of hours, yet adoption is near zero in residential

# Research: AI Wildfire Models Say Code Isn't Enough

## Article #355 — Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)

### Angle
AI wildfire risk models have created a de facto insurability standard above building code. A home that passes California's Chapter 7A code inspection can still be flagged as high-risk by AI models like ZestyAI's Z-FIRE, making it expensive or impossible to insure. The cost to close the gap between "meets code" and "insurable" is roughly $2,800 per home. Most builders aren't spending it.

### Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** If you're building in a fire-prone area, meeting code isn't enough for insurance anymore. This article quantifies the gap, the fix cost, and the insurance payoff.

### Primary Sources

**1. ZestyAI Analysis (Jan 8, 2026) — "$1 Trillion Blind Spot"**
- Analyzed 11,808,251 California residential properties
- Z-FIRE AI model vs FEMA National Risk Index comparison
- 1.2 million CA homes rated high-risk by AI but low/no-risk by FEMA maps
- $940 billion in residential property value in these blind spots (at $800K median)
- 62.5% of CA properties have NO federal wildfire assessment at all
- In LA fire zones: 3,045 properties ($2.4B value) in blind spots
  - Palisades: 1,430 properties, $1.14B at risk
  - Eaton: 1,615 properties, $1.29B at risk
- Z-FIRE uses ML + annual high-res satellite imagery: vegetation, terrain, building characteristics, climate, proximity to historical fires → 1-10 scores
- 52% of extreme-hazard properties saw regional hazard worsen 2022-2025
- A single-point Z-FIRE score increase = 51% jump in annual wildfire probability
- FEMA ratings showed little change over same period
Source: https://www.morningstar.com/news/pr-newswire/20260108to59386/nearly-1-trillion-in-california-homes-labeled-low-risk-despite-elevated-wildfire-danger

**2. Headwaters Economics + IBHS — Construction Costs Study**
- Compared 3 tiers of wildfire-resistant construction in California:
  - Baseline: Chapter 7A minimum code compliance
  - Enhanced: +$2,800 (enclosed under-deck, noncombustible zone 0-5ft) — consistent with IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home designation
  - Optimum: +$18,200 (NorCal) to +$27,100 (SoCal) — noncombustible materials throughout
- Wildfire-resistant construction adds 2-13% to total new home cost
- Enhanced adds only 2-8% — essentially the IBHS WPH standard
- Key upgrades: roof (standing seam steel vs asphalt), fiber-cement siding/trim, metal gutters/gutter guards, noncombustible mulch (pea gravel), metal fencing
Source: https://headwaterseconomics.org/natural-hazards/wildfire-resistant-costs-california/

**3. IBHS Wildfire Prepared Program Updates (June 9, 2026)**
- Just expanded to neighborhoods + multifamily (from single-family only)
- Now in 14 states (expanded from 4 to 14 in April 2026)
- Two tiers: Base (ember defense) and Plus (radiant heat + direct flame)
- KB Home piloting Wildfire Prepared Neighborhood in SoCal + NorCal
- WPH Base: reduces Average Annual Loss by 31%
- WPH Plus: reduces AAL by 35%
- 4.8 million US homes in high/extreme fire risk areas
- 3.1 million of those in the 14-state program
Source: https://www.morningstar.com (PR Newswire, June 9, 2026)

**4. LA Wildfire Data — UC Berkeley + IBHS Field Research**
- Homes with >25% fuel cover within 5 feet: 90% likelihood of damage/destruction (LA fires, Jan 2025)
- Homes with hardening + defensible space: 50% survival rate
- Homes with no mitigation: 20% survival rate
- Removing vegetation within 5-foot perimeter reduces structure losses by 17%
Source: IBHS/NAIC study via insurancenewsnet.com; UC Berkeley post-disaster research

### Original Contribution (Required)
Novel calculation: The insurance payoff math for the $2,800 upgrade. If Enhanced construction (IBHS WPH) costs $2,800 more but reduces AAL by 31%, and a 20% premium reduction applies, what's the break-even timeline? On a $500K home in a high-risk zone paying $5,000-$15,000/yr in wildfire premiums, a 20% discount = $1,000-$3,000/yr savings. Break-even: 1-3 years. Meanwhile, the AI model score improvement could mean the difference between getting private market insurance at all vs. being stuck on the FAIR Plan (limited coverage, higher costs).

### Strongest Counterargument
The gap might be overstated. AI models are vendor products (ZestyAI is selling a service). FEMA's census-tract approach exists because property-level assessment at federal scale is cost-prohibitive. California's Chapter 7A already exceeds most states' wildfire building codes. And the $2,800 figure is for new construction — retrofitting existing homes is far more expensive and disruptive. The real crisis is the 300,859 pre-1980 homes with no modern fire-resistant construction at all.

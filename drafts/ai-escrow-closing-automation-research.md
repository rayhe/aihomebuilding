# Research: AI Agentic Escrow & Closing Automation

## Angle
The closing table is one of the last fully manual steps in home-buying. Multiple companies are deploying agentic AI to automate it — coordinating 120+ discrete tasks across lenders, title companies, HOAs, and government offices. But buyer trust in AI is falling at the exact moment the industry is betting billions on it.

## Kill Test
✅ Directly helps homebuyers/sellers: understand what AI is doing in your closing, what risks exist (wire fraud enabled by AI), and whether the time savings are worth the trust deficit.

## Journalist
Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca — project management, process, coordination. The closing is the ultimate PM challenge: 120 tasks, multiple stakeholders, tight timeline, high stakes.

## Original Contribution
Calculate the "automation gap": the number of closing tasks AI can handle (80 of 120 = 67%) vs. the trust level buyers have in AI (16%, down from 30%). The gap between capability and trust is the defining tension. Also: the AI-fraud paradox — AI makes closings faster AND makes fraud harder to detect, creating a dual-use problem that no one in the industry has cleanly addressed.

## Strongest Counterargument
Escrow and title companies are some of the most liability-sensitive businesses in real estate. A $400K wire sent to the wrong account is irreversible. Agentic AI that can "complete critical steps like document ordering and fund transfers with minimal manual input" (HomeLight's own language) introduces new attack surfaces — the AI itself becomes a target for adversarial manipulation. The industry is racing to automate a process that AI simultaneously makes more dangerous.

## Limitations
- HomeLight EVA launched April 2026; no independent audit of error rates or task completion accuracy
- CertifID $283M fraud blocked is a cumulative lifetime figure, not annual
- Cotality survey conducted Jan-Feb 2026, before EVA launch; trust numbers may have shifted
- FBI IC3 data is complaint-based, likely undercounts actual fraud
- No published data on AI-specific errors in closing automation yet

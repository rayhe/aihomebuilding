# Research Notes: Buildots Intelligence Lab — Schedule Truth

## Topic
The Buildots Intelligence Lab (launched June 25, 2026) is the construction industry's first AI-powered research hub publishing freely available benchmarks from aggregated, anonymized global project data. Their early findings reveal structural patterns in construction schedule failure that directly affect residential homeowners.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** The long-tail effect, MEP productivity gaps, and schedule adherence data explain why every residential build runs late — and quantify the cost. Homeowners can use this data to negotiate more realistic timelines and hold GCs accountable.

## Original Contribution

**The Long-Tail Cost Calculation:**
- NAHB 2026: Average construction start to completion = 6.3 months for single-family homes
- Buildots: Final 20% of an activity takes 27% of its duration
- The extra 7 percentage points (27% - 20%) applied across all activities adds ~7% to overall schedule
- 7% of 6.3 months = 0.44 months ≈ 13 extra days
- Construction loan on $400K draw at 7.5% APR = ~$82/day in interest alone
- 13 days × $82 = ~$1,066 in pure carrying cost per home from the structural long tail
- Census Jan 2026 SAAR: 935,000 single-family starts
- Industry-wide: ~$1 billion/year in avoidable carrying cost from the long-tail effect alone

**The "schedule adherence" gap for residential:**
- Buildots measured healthcare (65%), data centers (57%), commercial (40s%), education (<39%)
- Residential isn't measured at all — no GC publishes this data
- If residential falls in the commercial range (40-50%), that means your 6.3-month build has a coin-flip chance of hitting schedule

## Strongest Counterargument
Buildots data is from commercial projects — data centers, hospitals, offices. Residential has different dynamics: shorter timelines, smaller crews, fewer MEP interdependencies. A 3-person plumbing crew doing a residential rough-in doesn't face the same coordination overhead as a 50-person MEP team on a hospital wing. The 20-50% productivity gap may overstate residential patterns. However, the "long tail" effect (last 20% takes 27%) is likely universal — it's about human psychology and task completion, not project scale.

## Limitations
- Buildots publishes no residential-specific data
- The $1B cost estimate assumes every home experiences the average long tail — overstates aggregate impact
- Schedule adherence rates for residential are unknown — we're extrapolating from commercial
- No independent verification of Buildots' aggregated data methodology
- The 3x productivity gap between top and average MEP teams may partly reflect different project complexity, not just team quality

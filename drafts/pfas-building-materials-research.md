# PFAS in Building Materials — Research Notes
## Article #519 — Priya Greenwood

### Topic
PFAS ("forever chemicals") are pervasive in building materials — from carpet to roof — and a patchwork of state bans is now making some of those materials illegal to sell. What does this mean for someone building or buying a home in 2026?

### Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** If you're building, your material choices now have legal implications in a growing number of states. If you're buying, the PFAS-treated finishes marketed as "stain resistant" or "waterproof" are increasingly regulated, and your future demolition/renovation liability is real. If you're on a private well, nobody is testing your water for you.

## KEY DATA POINTS

### Where PFAS Are in a Typical Home
(Source: Green Science Policy Institute 2021 report, GreenBuildingAdvisor Sept 2025)

**From floor to roof:**
- **Carpets & rugs** — stain, soil, and water repellents (side-chain fluoropolymers)
- **Wood-floor finishes** — stain-resistant, water-repellent coatings
- **Grout, tile & stone sealers** — oil, water, stain resistance
- **Paints & coatings** — durable, water-repellent exterior/interior paints
- **Caulks & adhesives** — weatherproofing, durability
- **Flashing tapes** — window/door sealing
- **Furniture fabrics** — Scotchgard, Teflon, Crypton treatments
- **Electric wiring** — flexible, heat-resistant insulation (PTFE/fluoropolymers)
- **Roofing** — metal roofs (self-cleaning PVDF coatings), asphalt (reflective/longevity), tensile roofs
- **Windows/doors/fixtures** — "easy-clean" ceramic/glass coatings

### PFAS Alternatives Already Exist
(Source: PFAS Central / Green Science Policy Institute)
- **Carpets:** silicone polymers, paraffins, polyurethanes, dendrimers, acrylics; or inherently stain-resistant fiber materials
- **Fabrics:** paraffin/silicone-based finishes (NikWax, EcoRepel, Phobotex), nanosilica treatments, tighter weaves
- **Home Depot & Lowes** already stopped selling PFAS carpets nationwide
- **San Francisco** banned PFAS carpets in new commercial construction

### State Bans — Active Now

**California (AB 1817, effective Jan 1, 2025):**
- PFAS banned in textiles >100 ppm total organic fluorine
- Drops to 50 ppm Jan 1, 2027
- Covers: apparel, furnishings, upholstery, bedding, draperies, towels
- Outdoor apparel exemption until Jan 1, 2028 (must label "Made with PFAS chemicals")
- Does NOT cover carpets/rugs (exempted)

**Vermont (Act 131, effective Jan 1, 2026):**
- Bans PFAS in: residential rugs/carpets, aftermarket stain treatments, textiles/textile articles, cookware, juvenile products, artificial turf, ski wax, incontinence products
- Threshold: 100 ppm total organic fluorine (drops to 50 ppm July 2027)
- H238 (June 2025): expanded to dental floss, cleaning products, fluorine-treated containers

**Minnesota ("Amara's Law" HF 2310, effective Jan 2025):**
- Bans PFAS in 11 product categories: carpets/rugs, cleaning products, cookware, cosmetics, dental floss, fabric treatments, juvenile products, menstruation products, textile furnishings, ski wax, upholstered furniture
- Disclosure program required by Jan 1, 2026
- ALL consumer products containing PFAS banned by Jan 1, 2032
- June 2025 amendment: exempts products with PFAS only in electronic/internal components

**Washington (effective Jan 1, 2026):**
- Disclosure/notification requirement (not yet a ban) for: outdoor textile furniture, hard surface sealers, cookware, footwear, firefighting PPE, floor waxes/polishes
- Tracking threshold: 50 ppm total organic fluorine

**12+ states have enacted PFAS product regulations** as of 2026

### EU Developments
- ECHA (March 2026): Recommended EU-wide PFAS ban with some essential-use exemptions
- Eight sectors initially excluded from restriction (sealing, machinery, broader industrial)
- EU already restricted PFAS in firefighting foams (2025)
- Total fluorine threshold proposed: ≤50 mg F/kg for consumer products

### EPA Drinking Water Rule
- **MCL: 4 parts per trillion** for PFOA and PFOS (April 2024 rule)
- **Confirmed May 2026** by Trump EPA — strongest standards stayed in place
- 10 ppt for PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA (GenX)
- MCLG for PFOA/PFOS: **zero** (no safe level)
- 3,400–6,300 water systems affected, serving 70–94 million people
- Monitoring required by 2027, compliance by 2029
- EPA estimated annual compliance cost: $1.5 billion (AWWA estimates: $47 billion installation + $700M/yr O&M)
- $9 billion federal funding (IIJA) for PFAS treatment

### Litigation Scale
- **3M settlement:** $10.3–12.5 billion over 13 years (water providers)
- **DuPont/Chemours/Corteva:** $1.185 billion (+ $4 billion reserved)
- **New York** sued 3M, DuPont, Chemours, Corteva, EIDP (July 2026) — consumer products, not just AFFF
- **5,600+ cases** in AFFF multidistrict litigation (MDL 18-mn-02873, D.S.C.)
- 97% of Americans have PFAS in their blood (CDC)
- **Personal injury claims growing** — blood tests can prove exposure

### The Demolition Problem
(Source: Waste Management journal / PFAS Project Lab)
- Construction & demolition landfills in Florida: **average 15,530 ppt PFAS in leachate**
- EPA drinking water MCL: 4 ppt
- **That's 3,883x the drinking water limit** leaching from building debris
- Most C&D landfills are **unlined** — leachate goes directly to groundwater
- Minnesota: PFAS found in 100 closed landfills, 16 sites at 10x state standards
- New Hampshire: 77.5% of sampled landfills had PFAS above state standards
- New York: 70% of inactive landfills had PFAS above state standards

### The Asbestos Parallel
- Both used everywhere for decades because of functional benefits
- Both were known to be problematic before public action
- DuPont/3M knew since 1970s that PFOA/PFOS were toxic — suppressed info
- Asbestos abatement now costs $2K–30K+ per home
- PFAS remediation costs TBD but demolition liability is real

## ORIGINAL CONTRIBUTION — The Math

**PFAS Exposure Budget for a Typical 2,000 sq ft New Build:**

Products that commonly contain PFAS in a new home:
1. Carpet (if any) — ~1,000 sq ft at $3-6/sq ft = $3,000-6,000 in PFAS-treated carpet
2. Exterior paint — ~3,500 sq ft exterior surface × 2 coats
3. Interior paint — ~5,000 sq ft wall surface × 2 coats
4. Tile/grout sealant — ~400 sq ft (bathrooms, kitchen)
5. Roofing membrane/coating — 2,000 sq ft
6. Window flashing tape — ~20 windows × 15 linear ft = 300 linear ft
7. Caulks/sealants — 50+ tubes
8. Furniture fabric treatments

**Construction & Demolition Landfill Math:**
- Average home demolition generates ~30,000 lbs of debris
- At 15,530 ppt average PFAS in C&D leachate
- EPA MCL: 4 ppt
- Ratio: 3,883:1
- A single demolished home's materials can contaminate the equivalent volume of groundwater by thousands of times over the drinking water limit

**State Compliance Cost for Builders:**
- PFAS-free carpet vs PFAS carpet: roughly price parity now (Home Depot/Lowes already switched)
- PFAS-free paint: ~5-15% premium for comparable durability
- PFAS-free sealants: available but smaller selection
- Biggest cost: education/awareness, not material price differential

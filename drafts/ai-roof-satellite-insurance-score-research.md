# Research: AI Satellite Scoring for Home Insurance

## Angle
An AI looked at your roof from orbit, scored your property on 200+ data points, and decided whether you can get insurance — and you've never seen the score. Property-level AI risk models (ZestyAI Z-FIRE, Verisk FireLine, CoreLogic) are replacing zone-based fire maps. Two identical-looking neighbors can get radically different outcomes: one insured, one dropped. This is the new credit score for your house. Unlike FICO, you can't check it.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** Homebuyers in fire-prone states can't close without insurance. Builders need to know which materials and designs will survive AI scoring. Homeowners can take specific hardening actions ($2K-$15K) that change their score and restore insurability.

## Primary Sources

### 1. ZestyAI Z-FIRE Model
- Property-level wildfire risk scores using AI analysis of 200+ data points
- Satellite/aerial imagery, topography, building characteristics, surrounding vegetation, neighboring structures, roof type/condition, 2,000+ historical wildfires
- First AI wildfire model approved by California regulators (2019)
- Trusted by **>1/3 of California's insurance market**, including CA FAIR Plan
- **70+ regulatory approvals** across 5 perils (wildfire, hail, wind, storm, non-weather water)
- Farmers Insurance: used Z-FIRE to add 30,000 new policies in CA (homes previously declined)
- Amica Insurance: adopted after Tubbs Fire losses (3,000 homes, 9 dead, 2017)
- Key innovation: property-level vs zone-based. A home with Class A roof + defensible space rated differently than neighbor with shake shingles and overgrown brush, even on same street
- Source: ZestyAI press releases, Wikipedia entry, Farmers/Amica public statements

### 2. Verisk FireLine
- Scores based on fuel, slope, road access
- Used by insurers to justify non-renewals in CA
- CA law now requires insurers to disclose wildfire risk scores and allow mitigation measures
- Source: Property Insurance Coverage Law Blog (peril-based risk scoring analysis)

### 3. CoreLogic Wildfire Risk Score
- Evaluates terrain, nearby vegetation, historical fire patterns
- Assigns numerical score per property
- Source: Property Insurance Coverage Law Blog

### 4. Non-Renewal Data (Weiss Ratings / NAIC, Oct 2025)
- California: 3.18% non-renewal rate in 2024, up **3.9x** from 0.82% in 2018
- Florida: 3.35% (worst), up 1.7x from 1.98% in 2018
- National average: 2.32%, up **2.9x** from 0.80% in 2018
- 15 states with highest rates, 10 of 15 driven by wildfire
- Source: Weiss Ratings analysis of official NAIC data

### 5. California FAIR Plan Expansion (United Policyholders / Insurance Business / Bloomberg)
- Enrollment jumped **43%** Sept 2024 to Dec 2025
- $40B LA fires destroyed 12,000 homes (Jan 2025)
- **14% of FAIR policies now in urban, lower-fire-risk zones** (Bloomberg analysis)
- 28% of total FAIR exposure in largely urban areas
- Stanford's Michael Wara: "The infection of the market that existed in the high-fire-risk areas has spread into the normal parts of the market"
- Source: United Policyholders, Insurance Business Magazine, Bloomberg

### 6. Premium Increases (Insurance.com / Insurify / Kin Insurance)
- California premiums up **55.3%** from 2019-2024 (insurance.com/S&P Global)
- Nationally up **44.9%** from 2019-2024
- Insurify projects CA rates will rise another **16% in 2026** as insurers implement advanced risk modeling
- 60% of CA homeowners struggled to find affordable coverage in past 3 years (Kin Insurance survey)
- 80% of CA homeowners concerned carriers will leave the state
- Average US premium: $3,303/yr. CA statewide average: $1,724/yr (still below national despite increases)
- US Treasury: repair/replacement costs up 45% 2020-2023. Single-family labor costs up 37% 2018-2022
- Source: Insurance.com, Insurify, Kin Insurance, The Street/CNBC

### 7. IBHS FORTIFIED Program & Home Hardening (IBHS / NAHB / Brookings / Headwaters Economics)
- FORTIFIED homes: 73% fewer claims, 72% lower total losses (Hurricane Sally peer-reviewed study, U of Alabama, 40,000+ properties)
- Insurance discounts: **20-55% off wind portion** in states with FORTIFIED legislation (AL, LA, MS, GA, OK, NC)
- Alabama: $100M invested in SAH program, ~10,000 homes retrofitted, reduced claims 70%, severity 60%
- Three tiers: FORTIFIED Roof, Silver, Gold
- GAF/IBHS alliance (Jan 2025) to expand access nationally
- Retrofitting costs: **$2,000-$15,000** for effective measures (ember-resistant vents, metal flashing, noncombustible zone, gutter screens)
- Full retrofit to highest standard: up to $100,000 but "likely not necessary in most cases"
- **New construction**: building wildfire-resistant costs about the same or sometimes less than conventional (IBHS/Headwaters Economics 2018 report)
- Embers responsible for **90% of homes destroyed** by wildfire
- Key measures: 1/8" mesh vents, Class A roof, 6" ground-to-siding clearance, tempered glass windows, noncombustible Zone 0 (5ft around home)
- Source: IBHS, NAHB/Pro Builder, Brookings, Headwaters Economics, CAL FIRE, Fire Safe Marin

### 8. Zillow Fair Housing Classifier v2 (Zillow, April 2026)

### 9. CMU/NYU/U of Toronto Study on Zestimate (Marketing Science)

## Original Contribution
**ROI calculation of specific hardening measures against AI score improvement:**
- If a homeowner's premium doubles from $1,724 to $3,448 due to AI wildfire risk scoring (which is happening in CA at 55%+ increases), and a $5,000 retrofit (ember-resistant vents + metal flashing + defensible space) brings the AI score down enough to restore standard coverage, that's a **1.5-3 year payback**.
- For new construction: building fire-resistant adds $0-$3,000 to a $400K+ home but prevents the AI from scoring you as uninsurable before you even close escrow.
- Cross-reference: the average FAIR Plan policy is more expensive than standard market — so getting OUT of FAIR Plan saves money.

## Limitations
- ZestyAI's exact scoring methodology is proprietary (black box)
- No public database lets homeowners check their own AI risk score
- FORTIFIED data is hurricane/wind-focused; wildfire-specific hardening outcome data is thinner
- Insurance discount programs are state-specific; CA has disclosure requirements but no formal hardening discount program like Alabama's SAH
- The 73% fewer claims figure is from Hurricane Sally (wind), not wildfire

## Strongest Counterargument
AI scoring is more fair than zone-based maps. Zone maps punished every home in a region equally — your fire-resistant Class A roof home paid the same as your neighbor's cedar shake disaster. AI scoring at least rewards individual hardening. The problem isn't the AI; it's that homeowners can't see their score or know what to fix. Transparency, not elimination, is the answer.

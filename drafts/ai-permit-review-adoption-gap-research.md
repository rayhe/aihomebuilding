# Research: AI Permit Review Adoption Gap

## Angle
~20,000 permit-issuing jurisdictions in the US. Fewer than 40 use AI for plan review. The technology demonstrably works. Why don't the other 19,960+ adopt it? Political economy — the beneficiaries of slow permitting (municipal bureaucracies, NIMBYs) outweigh the victims (builders, homebuyers). Catherine Chen beat — policy/regulation.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — tells them why their permit is slow, what the technology fix looks like, and what political levers to push.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Census Bureau Building Permits Survey
- ~20,100 permit-issuing places in the 2014 Universe (Census Bureau)
- Shovels.ai independently mapped 10,000+ AHJs (Authorities Having Jurisdiction)
- ~1.5 million residential building permits issued annually
- Source: census.gov, Building Permits Survey FAQ, Shovels.ai

### 2. Adoption Data — AI Plan Review
- "Only a few dozen U.S. cities" meaningfully deployed AI in permitting (Independent Institute, March 2026)
- Named cities/counties using AI: Honolulu, Denver, Austin, Naples FL, Los Angeles, San Jose, Lancaster CA, Louisville KY, Bellevue WA, Pueblo County CO, Arlington TX, San Mateo County CA, Harris County TX, Seattle, Calgary, Boston (RFI stage), NYC
- That's ~17 named jurisdictions, some in pilot stage
- Vendors: Clariti/CivCheck, Blitz AI (100+ employees), Archistar/eCheck, CodeComply.Ai/CivicPlus, AutoReview.AI, Govstream.ai, SwiftGov

### 3. Results Where Deployed
- **Honolulu:** 70% cut in residential plan review times, 64% total review time reduction (90 min → 20-30 min). CivCheck + Clariti. (Clariti BusinessWire, May 2026)
- **Austin:** 50% reduction in review time, 190 submissions with zero negative feedback. $1.1M/year contract with Archistar (3-year, approved Aug 2024). Saves "days to weeks" per application. (Homes.com News, April 2026)
- **Naples FL:** 85% fewer resubmissions. $514,096 licensing cost (Blitz AI + CityView). Council approved June 2025. (Business Observer FL, May 2026)
- **AutoReview.AI (Gainesville startup):** Claims review times from weeks to 30 minutes. Patented system. (Warrington/UFL)
- **Clariti/CivCheck acquisition:** Claims 80%+ acceleration, 97%+ accuracy. (BusinessWire)

### 4. Political Economy of Non-Adoption
- Independent Institute (March 2026): "The reason is rooted in incentives... some groups, from a public choice perspective, don't *want* this efficiency."
  - Municipal departments: faster approvals could reduce staffing needs, budgets, and institutional influence
  - Existing homeowners: lengthy permitting stifles production discreetly without publicly voicing NIMBY views
  - "The gap between what is possible and what actually gets implemented by government is often driven by incentives, not capability"
- Jeff Bezos at business forum: cities should use AI for yes/no permit decisions in seconds. "Government bureaucracy remains stuck in the past."

### 5. Liability Gap
- Area Development (Q4 2025): "One" — that's how many standard contract templates currently mention AI
- AIA and ConsensusDocs standard forms were not drafted with AI in mind
- No standard construction contracts address: who's responsible for AI tool selection/config, who owns AI-generated data, who assumes liability for AI decisions, whether AI outages are force majeure
- No major construction AI litigation yet, but legal observers say it's a matter of when not if

### 6. Cost Data
- Average building permit costs: $525-$3,114 (Angi, 2026)
- Permit processing times: 2-8 weeks nationally (mykukun.com, 2026)
- Delays can cost up to $4,000 per project in extended costs (mykukun.com)
- NAHB: regulations add ~$93,870 to average new single-family home cost (broader than permits alone)
- LA permits can take 1 year+, with fees reaching tens of thousands for single-family homes (Independent Institute)
- Virginia homebuilder: delay because county staffer resigned midway through, didn't hand off (Independent Institute — real anecdote)

### 7. White House / Federal Context
- White House executive order prioritizing permitting reform
- Bipartisan congressional legislation on permitting reform
- Dozens of states have put permitting reform on policy agenda
- Housing shortages and affordability pressures intensifying

## Original Contribution (Novel Calculation)
Cost of the AI permit adoption gap:
- ~20,000 permit-issuing jurisdictions, ~30-40 using AI = 0.15-0.2% adoption
- ~1.5M residential permits/year nationally
- If AI pre-check saves average of 1-2 weeks per permit (conservative, based on Austin/Honolulu data)
- Carrying cost of 1 week delay on $400K home at 7% mortgage: ~$538/week in interest alone
- Plus labor standby costs, contractor scheduling disruption, opportunity cost
- Conservative estimate: $2,000/permit in avoidable delay costs × 1.2M permits that could benefit = $2.4B/year
- This is money transferred from homebuyers to... nobody. It's pure deadweight loss.

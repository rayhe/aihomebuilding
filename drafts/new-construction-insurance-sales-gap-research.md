# Research: The Insurance Sales Gap in New Construction

## Core Thesis
Builders are cutting prices to move inventory when the bigger sales tool — personalized insurance savings estimates — sits unused. AI underwriting models could generate these at the point of sale, but nobody's built the integration.

## Primary Sources

- New construction insurance: $1,828/yr average (2025)
- National average (all ages): $2,948/yr
- Savings: 38% ($1,120/yr) vs 40-yr-old home
- Age curve: New $1,828 → 10yr $2,476 (+35%) → 20yr $2,830 → 30yr $2,889 → 40yr $2,948

- Source: Julia Taliesin, economic analyst/licensed insurance agent, Insurify

## Original Contribution

### The Insurance Rate Equivalency Nobody Calculates

On the median-priced home ($440,600) with 20% down:
- Mortgage: $352,480 at 6.55% = $2,237/mo P&I
- Insurance savings: $1,120/yr ÷ 12 = $93/mo
- $93/mo equivalent to reducing rate from 6.55% to ~6.29%

In other words: new construction at 6.55% effectively costs a buyer the same monthly as a 40-year-old resale at 6.29%, just from insurance savings alone. Add maintenance savings (~$2,500/yr per Realtor.com), and the effective rate equivalent drops further.

### The Builder's $26,400 Error
- Average builder price cut: 6% of $440,600 = $26,424
- 10-year insurance + maintenance savings: $32,000
- The builder is cutting price by $26K to sell a home that already saves the buyer $32K in operating costs
- The information asymmetry: buyers don't know about the $32K savings, so the price cut becomes the primary lever
- An AI tool generating personalized insurance estimates at point of sale would cost ~$5-15K/yr — 1/5 of one price cut

## Limitations
- Insurify data is national averages; actual savings vary dramatically by state
- New construction premium advantage may narrow as homes age
- Insurance savings projections assume stable market conditions
- AI integration between insurance and builder sales doesn't exist yet — this article identifies the gap
- The $32K savings figure from Realtor.com assumes premium differentials remain stable

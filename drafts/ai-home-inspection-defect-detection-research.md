# Research: AI Home Inspection Defect Detection

## Angle
Your home inspector spent 3 hours in the crawl space. AI tools catch 23% more defects overall, 31% more electrical issues, and 27% more water damage. But only ~35% of inspection companies use them. The question for buyers isn't whether AI inspections work — it's whether their inspector is using them.

## Journalist
Jake Kowalski — construction technology, hands-on, punchy

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — directly helps homebuyers choose better inspectors and ask the right questions. Also helps builders understand what AI-augmented inspections will catch during warranty period.

## Original Contribution
Expected value calculation: If AI inspections catch 23% more defects, and the average cost of a missed defect that becomes a post-purchase repair is ~$3,500-$7,000 (based on Bankrate/Redfin common issue range), then a homebuyer paying $400 for a standard inspection vs $500-$600 for AI-augmented inspection is buying ~$800-$1,600 in expected defect-catching value for an incremental $100-$200. ROI: 4-8x on the premium.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI inspection tools are only as good as their training data, which skews toward specific housing stock (newer construction, standard framing). Older homes, unusual construction methods, regional building practices — AI may actually perform worse than an experienced local inspector who's seen 1,000 houses in the same neighborhood. The 23% improvement figure likely comes from controlled studies, not field conditions where lighting, access, and homeowner staging affect results.

## Limitations
- Most statistics from 2022-2023 industry surveys; 2026 field data is sparse
- AI accuracy claims come from tool vendors, not independent studies
- No peer-reviewed head-to-head comparison of AI vs human inspection outcomes across a statistically significant sample of real transactions
- Luxury market adoption (38%) may not translate to median-priced homes
- Inspection liability and licensing varies by state; AI tool certification standards are still emerging

# Research: AI Fire Scoring and Home Insurability

## Angle
California just allowed insurers to use forward-looking AI catastrophe models (not just historical loss data) to set rates. An ML study on 5 major CA wildfires achieved 82% accuracy predicting which structures survive. This means an algorithm now effectively decides whether a home is insurable and at what price. Builders and homeowners need to build for the algorithm — not just the building code.

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)
- Connects data to real costs (insurance premiums = the new utility bill)
- Evidence-based urgency without preachiness

## Key Data Points

### The ML Study (Nature Communications, 2026)
- Source: PubMed PMID 40877289, DOI 10.1038/s41467-025-63386-2
- Examined 5 major CA WUI fires: 2017 Tubbs, 2017 Thomas, 2018 Camp, 2019 Kincade, 2020 Glass
- XGBoost classifier predicts structure survivability to 82% accuracy
- Key finding: structure spacing is critical factor; hardening + defensible space = hypothetical 52% reduction in losses
- ML analysis combined post-fire field data, remote sensing, and fire reconstruction modeling

### California Catastrophe Modeling Rule Change
- As of Jan 1, 2026: Chapter 7A became Part 7 (Title 24) of the California Wildland-Urban Interface Code (CWUIC)
- CA now allows insurers to use forward-looking catastrophe models to set rates (not just historical data)
- In exchange: insurers must write policies in high-risk areas equal to at least 85% of their market share
- 5 major insurers committed to staying (Jul 7, 2026): Mercury, CSAA, USAA, Pacific Specialty, California Casualty
- Rate increases requested: 6-9%
- Travelers seeking 6.9% for single-family homes, specifically mentions "wildfire-prepared homes that use hardening elements and defensible space"

### FAIR Plan Crisis
- FAIR Plan total exposure: $650 billion (Jun 2025)
- 42% increase since Sept 2024
- 289% increase since Sept 2021
- It's the "insurer of last resort" — homeowners get funneled here when no one else will cover them

### Construction Costs (IBHS Study, Jan 2025)
- Source: ibhs.org, "Construction Costs for Wildfire-Resistant Homes"
- Chapter 7A compliance for 2,000 sq ft home: adds ~$30,000
- IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home (goes beyond 7A): adds ~$35,000 total
- Main cost drivers: exterior walls, under-eaves, decking
- Of 11,519 homes destroyed/majorly damaged in Eaton & Palisades fires, ~60% (6,637) were in Chapter 7A zones

### Practical Retrofit Costs (2026, Pasadena data)
- Ember-resistant vent retrofit (whole home): $800-$2,200
- Gutter guard + eave sealing: $600-$1,800
- Zone 0 hardscape conversion (5-foot buffer): $1,200-$4,500
- Fence disconnect with non-combustible section: $400-$900/run
- Full ember-resistant envelope (1,800 sq ft): $6,000-$15,000
- UC ANR: effective strategies for $2,000-$10,000; full protection up to $100K

### Insurance Discounts
- Mercury Insurance: 3 tiers (Essential/Enhanced/Elite), up to 18%
- Insurance.com: industry range 2-18% depending on carrier and scope
- Handyman Connection: 5-20% premium discounts "often paying back the investment in the first renewal cycle"
- Specific requirements for each tier (Class A roof, ember vents, 6" vertical non-combustible clearance, enclosed eaves, tempered glass, metal gutters, etc.)

### Stand Insurance Digital Twins
- Uses aerospace-grade physics simulations to model wildfire behavior on specific parcels
- Creates "digital twin" of home and land from existing data + drones
- Simulates how wildfire crosses parcel, then re-simulates after recommended changes
- Personalizes risk to the specific home, not just the zip code
- Can model "home survives up to X-mph winds in wildfire conditions"

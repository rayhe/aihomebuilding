# Research Notes: Net-Zero Home Performance Gap

## Proposed Headline
**"Your 'Net-Zero' Home Uses Twice the Energy the Model Promised. Here's What No One Measured."**

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)

## Kill Test
- Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES** — buyers paying $30K-$80K premiums for "net-zero" labels deserve to know 1 in 5 certified buildings fail performance targets. Builders marketing net-zero need accountability mechanisms. AI monitoring tools are the verification layer that closes the loop.
- Novel contribution: connects the well-documented commercial performance gap to residential, with specific AI monitoring tools that homeowners can deploy to verify claims.

## Core Angle
The "net-zero" label on a new home is based on an energy model, not metered reality. Academic research consistently shows a 30-150% gap between modeled and actual energy use. AI-powered home energy monitors (Sense, Emporia, Span) can finally close this gap by providing real-time disaggregated data — but almost no builder offers post-occupancy verification. The article asks: why isn't metered performance part of the net-zero promise?

## Primary Sources (3+ required)

### 1. NBI Getting to Zero Database (2025/2026)
- Source: New Buildings Institute, January 2026 update
- 922 verified and emerging zero-energy/zero-carbon buildings across 50 countries
- Median EUI for verified net-zero: 29 kBtu/ft²/yr (65% below US commercial avg of 83)
- **Key finding: nearly 1 in 5 buildings targeting net-zero certification failed to meet designed EUI within first two years of operation**
- URL: sustainableatlas.org analysis of NBI data

### 2. CIBSE/UCL Performance Gap Meta-Analysis (2024)
- Source: Chartered Institution of Building Services Engineers + University College London
- **Documented gaps of 30-150% in commercial buildings (actual 1.3x to 2.5x modeled)**
- Root causes: occupant behavior, HVAC commissioning failures, envelope defects, plug loads underestimated
- "The performance gap" is now an established term in building science literature

### 3. MDPI Sustainability Study — Occupant Behavior Impact (2023)
- Source: MDPI Sustainability journal, Vol 15, Issue 3, Article 2440
- Occupant behavior can cause up to 80% variation in energy consumption between identical homes
- Identical houses showed one using 2x heating energy of neighbor — only variable was occupant behavior
- 12% energy reduction + 5% comfort improvement achievable by coupling occupant behavior models into building simulation
- Key insight: energy models assume "standard" occupant behavior that doesn't exist

### 4. ScienceDirect — Residential Energy Performance Gap (2024)
- Source: Building and Environment journal
- "Expectations vs. Reality: Investigating the Energy Performance Gap of Residential Buildings between Simulation and Actual Use"
- Systematic study of residential buildings comparing simulation to metered data

### 5. AI Home Energy Monitoring Tools (2026)
- **Sense Energy Monitor**: $250-350, ML-based disaggregation, identifies individual appliances from electrical signature
- **Emporia Vue**: $150-220, per-circuit monitoring with up to 16+ branch circuits
- **Span Smart Panel**: $3,500 + installation, full circuit-level control + monitoring
- Real-world results: 5-12% energy reduction from active monitoring over 12-18 months
- Payback: 3-5 years for engaged users (at $0.20/kWh, 9,000 kWh/yr home)
- Utility trials (Hydro One Ontario): 6.5% reduction from simple in-home display across 500 homes

### 6. California 2026 Building Code (Title 24)
- Electric systems becoming standard, gas not banned but efficiency standards make electric obvious
- California targeting 6 million homes on heat pumps by 2030
- New EV charging requirements for all new construction
- Relevant: code mandates design performance but no post-occupancy verification requirement

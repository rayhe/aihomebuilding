# Research: Your Home Battery Is Worth More to a Data Center Than It Is to You

## Journalist: Priya Greenwood (Sustainability & Green Building)
## Date: June 28, 2026

## Core Angle
The Sunrun/Tesla/Renew Home 16 GW virtual power plant deal (announced June 24-25, 2026) means residential batteries are now revenue-generating assets for AI data centers. What does this mean for someone building a new home? Should builders pre-wire for VPP participation? What can a homeowner actually earn?

## Key Sources & Data

### 1. Sunrun + Tesla + Renew Home VPP (June 24-25, 2026)
- Partnership pools hundreds of thousands of home batteries (Sunrun + Tesla) with 8 million+ smart thermostats (Renew Home)
- 16 GW of flexible capacity = roughly 14-15 nuclear reactors
- 300 MW already deployable in Virginia; 500+ MW expected by 2030
- Can deploy in months, not years — no new hardware/land/transmission
- CEO Mary Powell: "When data centers are asked to throttle down operations during the most expensive and stressful hours of the day, we can activate our distributed power plants"
- Sunrun stock jumped ~25% on announcement
- Sources: WSJ, MarketWatch, TechCrunch, Barchart, multiple

### 2. Base Power VPP Model (expanding from Texas to PJM/Illinois, June 2026)
- $695-995 upfront for 25-50 kWh batteries (vs. $15,000+ for Powerwall before incentives)
- Customers commit to buying electricity at 8.5¢/kWh + delivery for 3 years
- Annual fee: $225-345 ($19-29/month)
- Illinois rate: 25% below utility ComEd
- Installation cost: ~$10,000/battery before $3,000 federal tax credit = ~$7,000 net
- Estimated payback: 3.5 years
- 500+ MWh deployed in Texas as of June 2026
- Scaling to PJM — entering the grid that's struggling most with data center demand
- Revenue: Sacra estimates $12M annualized (2025), projecting $70M for 2026
- Source: TechCrunch, Sacra, CleanTechnica

### 3. PJM Grid Crisis — Data Centers Driving Prices
- PJM: largest US grid operator, 13 states, 70M people
- Wholesale electricity prices nearly doubled in past year
- Forward capacity price: $28.92/MW-day (2024/25) → $329.17/MW-day (2026/27) — 11x increase
- Q1 wholesale costs up 76%, data centers identified as key driver
- PJM paused new generation applications 2022-2024; reopened April 2026
- AEP (major utility) threatened to leave the market
- Data center demand: 41 GW in 2026, 66 GW in 2027 (Goldman Sachs)
- DOE: data centers could account for 12% of US electricity by 2028
- NJ: 10% of state electricity usage projected from data centers in 4 years
- Source: ainvest, TechCrunch, NorthJersey.com

### 4. Homebuilder/New Construction Angle (ORIGINAL ANALYSIS)
**What makes a home "VPP-ready"?**
- 200A+ electrical panel (already standard in new construction)
- Battery-ready conduit and mounting location (incremental cost in new construction: ~$500-1,000)
- Smart meter / CT clamp hookup
- Wi-Fi connectivity (standard)
- EV charger pre-wire (often bundled in new energy codes)

**Cost differential: new construction vs. retrofit**
- New construction: battery-ready infrastructure ~$500-1,000 marginal cost
- Retrofit: $2,000-5,000 for panel upgrade + conduit + mounting
- Battery itself: $10,000-15,000 (Powerwall) or $695-995 (Base Power model)

**Potential earnings from VPP participation**
- Base Power model: save 25% on electricity = ~$300-600/year on average Texas bill
- Grid arbitrage revenue (via battery owner like Base Power): varies wildly
- Peak dispatch events: $20/kWh in extreme cases (Australian data)
- More realistic US estimate: $200-800/year in grid service revenue depending on market
- PJM capacity payments at $329.17/MW-day for a 10 kW dispatchable home system = ~$1,200/year

### 5. Policy Context

- EU: zero-emission building standard effective May 2026

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES.
- Builders: pre-wire for ~$500-1,000 to make homes VPP-ready (future revenue stream, selling point)
- Buyers: understand that their home battery isn't just backup — it's a revenue asset
- Both: PJM capacity crisis means VPP participation earnings could increase dramatically

## Novel Contribution
Nobody has calculated the marginal cost of making a new home "VPP-ready" vs. retrofit cost later. The math shows a 4-10x cost advantage for including VPP infrastructure during construction. In PJM markets where capacity prices have increased 11x, the ROI timeline for a VPP-ready home battery system may be under 3 years.

## Strongest Counterargument
VPP revenue depends on volatile wholesale electricity markets and regulatory frameworks that vary by state. The 16 GW figure from Sunrun/Tesla/Renew Home is theoretical capacity, not contracted revenue. Most VPP programs don't guarantee earnings. And battery degradation from frequent cycling for grid services could reduce the battery's value as home backup — the primary reason most buyers purchase one.

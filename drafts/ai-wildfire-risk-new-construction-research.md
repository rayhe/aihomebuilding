# Research: AI Wildfire Risk Assessment for New Home Construction

## Angle
AI tools now score wildfire risk at the parcel level before a single shovel hits dirt. For new construction in the WUI, this changes everything — from lot selection to insurance availability to building code compliance. The 2-3% cost premium for wildfire-resistant construction (Headwaters/IBHS 2025) is trivial compared to the $30B+ damage from a single fire event. But most builders still don't use parcel-level AI risk data during site selection.

## Key Sources

### 1. Headwaters Economics / IBHS (Dec 2025)
- "Construction Costs for Wildfire Resistant Homes"
- Building to IBHS Wildfire Prepared Home Plus standard: ~$15,000 premium on $500K home (3%)
- California Chapter 7A: ~$13,000 premium (2.6%)
- IBHS WFPH base: ~$9,000 premium (1.8%)
- Key finding: wildfire-resistant construction is affordable — barrier is awareness, not cost

### 2. CoreLogic 2024 Wildfire Risk Report
- 2.6 million homes across 14 states at moderate-to-very-high wildfire risk
- $1.3 trillion reconstruction cost value at risk
- California: 1,258,748 homes at risk (#1 state)
- LA metro: 245,000+ homes at moderate+ risk, $186.6B reconstruction value
- Mitigation can reduce expected loss by 75%

### 3. First Street Foundation - Fire Factor
- Property-level wildfire risk scores (1-10) for every US property
- 49.4M properties with minor wildfire risk
- 20.2M with moderate risk
- 6.0M with major risk
- 2.7M with severe risk
- 1.5M with extreme risk
- Uses climate projection models for 30-year risk horizons

### 4. Cape Analytics (acquired by Moody's Jan 2025)
- AI-powered geospatial property risk intelligence
- Computer vision on aerial/satellite imagery
- Assesses building materials, roof condition, vegetation proximity at individual address level
- Used by insurance carriers for underwriting decisions
- Moody's acquisition signals AI wildfire risk is now institutional infrastructure

### 5. LA Wildfires January 2025
- Palisades Fire: 23,700 acres; Eaton Fire: 14,000 acres
- 16,251+ structures destroyed, 12,585 housing units
- 29+ lives lost
- Total property damage: $30B+ (CoStar analysis), up to $59.55B total economic impact (UCLA Anderson)
- Weighted median home price in burn areas: $2.15M
- Coalition urged rebuilding to Chapter 7A standard

### 6. WUI Growth (USGS 2022)
- 13 million new homes built in WUI in 20-year period
- WUI is fastest-growing land use type in contiguous US
- 125 million building locations mapped

### 7. California Insurance Crisis
- State Farm, Allstate, others paused new policies in California
- FAIR Plan (insurer of last resort) enrollment surging
- Insurance availability now directly tied to wildfire risk scores
- Prop 103 reform: insurers allowed to use forward-looking catastrophe models (AI/ML based)

### 8. FireScore.ai / SkyscoutAI
- Newer AI platforms for parcel-level wildfire risk mapping
- Geophysical AI-powered compliance mapping
- WUI-scale framework assessments

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES** — directly. If you're buying a lot or building new, AI wildfire risk tools can tell you:
1. Whether you can get insurance at all
2. What building code tier applies (Chapter 7A, WFPH, etc.)
3. What the premium for wildfire-resistant construction is ($9K-$15K)
4. Whether your lot's risk will worsen over 30 years (climate projections)

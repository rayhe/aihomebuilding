# Research: California's Embodied Carbon Framework Deadline and the Residential Gap

## Angle
California's CARB must deliver an embodied carbon measurement framework by December 31, 2026 — and AB 1704 explicitly includes residential buildings. Most homebuilders have never heard of an EPD (Environmental Product Declaration). The tools exist but were built for commercial. The regulatory clock is ticking, but the residential industry isn't at the starting line.

## Journalist
Priya Greenwood — sustainability & green building

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — tells residential builders what's coming, gives them specific tools to start measuring, explains the cost/carbon tradeoff, and quantifies what "embodied carbon" actually means for a single-family home.

## Primary Sources

### 1. California AB 1704 / AB 2446 (Legislative Text)
- AB 2446 (Holden, 2022) + AB 1704 (successor) require CARB to:
  - By Dec 31, 2026: develop framework for measuring carbon intensity of building materials including residential
  - By Dec 31, 2028: comprehensive strategy for 40% net reduction by 2035
  - Framework requires LCA submissions for projects ≥5 new residential units or ≥10,000 sqft non-residential
  - Baseline set from 2026 EPD industry averages
  - ISO 14040 series with focus on Product Stage (A1-A3)
- Source: leginfo.legislature.ca.gov (AB-1704 bill text)

### 2. CalGreen Current Embodied Carbon Requirements (DCI Engineers analysis)
- July 1, 2024: mandatory embodied carbon requirements for non-residential >100,000 sqft
- January 1, 2026: expanded to non-residential >50,000 sqft
- Builds on Buy Clean California Act (2017): structural steel, reinforcing steel, flat glass, mineral wool
- Concrete added to covered materials
- Key gap: residential construction not yet covered under CalGreen's embodied carbon provisions
- Three compliance paths: (1) reuse ≥45% existing building, (2) prove 10% carbon reduction, (3) alternate pathway
- Source: dci-engineers.com, marketplace.org

### 3. CARB Building Decarbonization Page
- Residential construction: 41% operational, 59% embodied carbon (inverted from commercial)
- This is the critical stat: for homes, MORE than half of lifecycle carbon is embodied, not operational
- CARB currently focused on three categories: embodied carbon, direct emissions (heating/hot water), refrigerant leakage
- Public engagement ongoing for framework development
- Source: ww2.arb.ca.gov

### 4. JLC/Mondaq 100-Home Study (Massachusetts, 2025)
- 100 new homes analyzed for embodied carbon emissions (ECE)
- Average: 55.5 tons CO2e per home over 25 years
- Equivalent to a gas car driving 14 million miles (combined sample)
- Over 25-year horizon: embodied carbon = up to 32% of total home carbon
- Variation: 18.5 tons (compact <700 sqft, minimal concrete) to 144.3 tons (8,400+ sqft with walkout basement)
- By type: single-family detached 2+ stories = 59.2 tons avg; townhomes = 39 tons
- Townhomes: 26% lower MEP-system ECE, 32% lower enclosure ECE, 38% lower operational carbon
- Operational emissions: 5.5 tons CO2e annually per home
- Massachusetts 2024: 11,390 new homes × 55.5 tons = 632,145 tons CO2e embodied carbon
- Source: jlconline.com (Rating Embodied Carbon in Homes)

### 5. EC3 Tool (Building Transparency)
- Free, cloud-based Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator
- 19,000+ registered users, 70 countries
- Database of digitized EPDs (17,000+ products: concrete, steel, wood, glass, aluminum, insulation, gypsum)
- Integrations: Autodesk Revit (tallyCAT plugin), Autodesk Construction Cloud, BIM 360
- Microsoft used EC3 to reduce campus embodied carbon by ~30%
- Founded 2020, incubated by Carbon Leadership Forum (UW)
- Also operates tallyLCA (lifecycle assessment tool)
- Key gap: designed for commercial projects. Residential builders don't use BIM/Revit at scale
- Source: greenbuildingadvisor.com, autodesk.com, businesswire.com

### 6. University of Bath NLP-Based Embodied Carbon Tool (2026)
- First AI tool predicting building carbon from text descriptions
- Uses ML + NLP on conversational building descriptions
- Trained on 150,000 synthetic buildings (no real-world data available)
- Tested with 43 building professionals
- Designed for earliest design stage where carbon reduction potential is greatest
- Published in Architectural Engineering and Design Management (DOI: 10.1080/17452007.2026.2613773)
- Prof. David Coley, Dept. of Architecture and Civil Engineering
- Source: bath.ac.uk, techxplore.com

### 7. DOE Green Code Cost Analysis (June 2026)
- DOE found 2024 IECC adoption would add $14,000 per new home vs. 2006 code levels
- $9.2 billion annual increase in residential construction costs nationally
- Energy Secretary Chris Wright: "climate activists have pushed regulations that increase housing costs"
- ICC rebuttal: DOE contradicting 3 decades of its own findings; claimed $182B savings 2010-2040
- Political tension: current administration hostile to green building mandates
- Source: washingtonexaminer.com

### 8. Embodied Carbon by Building Component (Atlanta case study, ScienceDirect)
- Foundation = 40% of total embodied CO2 in residential buildings
- Lower embodied energy per unit area for pre-1970s homes and 2-story homes
- Material manufacturing and replacement stages dominate embodied impacts
- Source: sciencedirect.com

### 9. AI in Construction Market (ResearchAndMarkets.com, June 2026)
- $2.28B (2025) → $3.02B (2026) at 32.8% CAGR
- Projected $9.48B by 2030 at 33.1% CAGR
- Key drivers: sustainability regulations, cost optimization, smart city initiatives
- Source: globenewswire.com

## Original Contribution (Required)
Calculate: if California's 2026 framework were applied retroactively to the Massachusetts study data, how many of the 100 homes would need to file an LCA? The threshold is ≥5 units per project. Most production builders in CA build 5+ unit developments. This means production housing is IN but custom single-family is OUT of the initial framework.

Also: cost-per-ton analysis. If EC3 is free but requires BIM integration ($$), what's the actual compliance cost for a 50-unit residential development vs. a custom home? The tools assume Revit/BIM workflows that residential builders don't use.

## Strongest Counterargument
The framework only applies to ≥5 residential units, which exempts the vast majority of custom homebuilders. Small builders building 1-4 homes at a time won't face this requirement. And the framework is measurement/reporting only (by 2026) — mandatory reductions don't come until 2028-2035. The urgency may be overstated.

## Limitations
- The 55.5 tons/home figure is from Massachusetts (cold climate, different grid mix than CA)
- EC3's 30% reduction claim is from Microsoft's commercial campus, not residential
- University of Bath tool trained on synthetic data, not real buildings
- California framework details are still under development by CARB — final requirements unknown
- No residential-specific cost data for LCA compliance

# Research: AI Adoption Gap in Residential Construction

## Topic
Why residential builders ignore the AI tools that would save them the most money — and what that costs per home.

## Journalist
Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca (Project Management & Operations)

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES** — homeowners paying for new construction are absorbing $30K-$50K in preventable waste per home. Understanding why their builder isn't using available technology helps them ask better questions and push for accountability.

## Novel Contribution
Per-home cost calculation of the AI adoption gap: rework (5-9% of budget), warranty ($2,980/home), change order markups (10-20%), schedule delays — totaling $30K-$50K per home in waste that existing technology could materially reduce. No one has done this math against the NAHB's own adoption data.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NAHB/Wells Fargo HMI Survey (July 2025) — AI Adoption in Home Building
- **20%** of single-family builders use AI for marketing/advertising
- **11%** use AI for market analysis and project planning
- **Fewer than 5%** use AI for any of 10 other functions: scheduling, quality control, design, cost estimation, subcontractor coordination, equipment operation, safety, etc.
- **45%** of respondents report no AI implementation whatsoever
- **34%** in early pilot phases
- **1.5%** use AI across multiple processes
- **Less than 1%** have fully embedded, organization-wide AI
- Builders express interest in adopting AI for marketing and planning in next 2 years, but interest in other functions remains low
- Source: NAHB blog (Aug 2025), Construction Dive, Eye on Housing (Jul 2025)

### 2. Houzz State of the Industry (2025) — Software Adoption
- **33%** of general contractors use cost estimating software
- **37%** of specialty contractors use estimating software
- **84%** of architects use drafting/rendering software (vs 33% GCs)
- **57%** of design-build firms use drafting/rendering software
- Source: Houzz 2025 US State of the Industry

### 3. Rework Cost Data
- Rework as high as **5% of contract value** — $25,000 on a $500K home
  - Source: "Learning Practices as a Tool for Quality Costs Reductions in Construction Projects" (Quality – Access to Success journal)
- When including indirect costs (supervision, overhead): **9% of total project cost** — $45,000 on a $500K home
  - Source: Navigant Construction Forum study
- **30%** of work performed by construction companies is rework
  - Source: "Cost Management in Construction Projects: Rework and Its Effects" (Mediterranean Journal of Social Sciences)
- **$31.3B** annual US construction rework costs
  - **52%** from documentation errors
  - Source: CII/NIST

### 4. Warranty Cost Data
- Average warranty accrual per home sold: **$2,980** in 2024
- Above 22-year historical average in every quarter since Q1 2021
- Total warranty claims paid decreased 2023→2024, but accruals increased slightly, reserves increased
- Source: Warranty Week (April 17, 2025)

### 5. Project Overruns & Delays
- **9 in 10** projects experience cost overrun (Propeller Aero / Oxford meta-analysis)
- Average overrun: **28%** — that's $140,000 on a $500K home
- Only **31%** of projects come within 10% of budget (in studied years)
- **98%** of NA construction projects delayed (Projul)
- Average schedule overrun: **37%** — 7.2 months → ~10 months
- Residential builds average **7.2 months** (Census 2024)
- Source: AmeriSave cost analysis, Projul 2025, Census Bureau

### 6. Construction Cost Breakdown
- Average new home price: **$495,000** (Census Q4 2025)
- Construction costs = **64.4%** of average home price (NAHB) — record high since 1998 series inception
- Previous share: 60.8% in 2022
- Interior finishes: 24.1%, major rough-ins: 19.2%, framing: 16.6%, exterior: 13.4%, foundations: 10.5%
- Source: NAHB Cost of Construction Survey, Eye on Housing (Dec 2025)

### 7. Available AI Tools (Residential)
- **Buildertrend**: scheduling, client portal, change orders, selections. From ~$99/mo. Largest residential platform.
- **CoConstruct** (now Buildertrend): custom home/remodel focus, client communication, selections, budgets
- **BuildTools**: draw requests, automated billing, ECC worksheets. Custom/luxury home focus
- **ALICE Technologies**: generative scheduling, 17% duration reduction, 14% labor cost reduction. ALICE Plan accessible without BIM.
- **Buildots**: 360° camera + CV, BIM comparison, $45M at $300M valuation. Commercial-focused.
- **Doxel**: CV-based progress tracking, quality verification
- **Procore**: largest commercial platform, AI features expanding
- Source: Digital Project Manager reviews, company data, investor reports

### 8. Subcontractor Coordination Problems
- Average residential build uses **15-25 subcontractors**
- Subcontractor no-show rate: **15-20%** average
- Change order fees: **10-20% markup** on changes
- Small builders: **<20% use scheduling software** (NAHB 2024 data)
- Most still use text messages, phone calls, spreadsheets
- Source: NAHB surveys, Projul, FieldProxy

### 9. NIST Interoperability Loss
- **$15.8B/year** lost to interoperability issues (different trades can't share data/plans efficiently)
- Source: NIST study, Autodesk blog

### 10. Construction Management Software Market
- 60% of firms use cloud-based construction management (mostly large firms)
- North America: 35.64% of global market, 89% cloud-only adoption (large firms)
- Market growing but concentrated in commercial/infrastructure
- Source: Mordor Intelligence, Service Titan

## Per-Home Waste Calculation (Novel Contribution)
On a $495,000 average new home:
- **Rework (direct):** 5% × $495K = $24,750
- **Rework (with indirect):** 9% × $495K = $44,550
- **Warranty:** $2,980 (industry average)
- **Schedule delay cost:** 37% average overrun × builder overhead and carry costs
- **Change orders:** Variable, but 10-20% markup on each change

**Conservative estimate:** $28,000-$48,000 per home in preventable waste
**Cost of AI tools:** $99-$499/month = $1,200-$6,000/year
**ROI if even 20% of waste prevented:** 100:1 to 800:1

## Strongest Counterargument
The tools built for commercial ($50M+) projects don't translate to custom residential. A $500K home with 15 subs — most of whom are 1-5 person shops using personal phones — can't adopt the same tech stack. The fragmentation is structural, not a knowledge gap. And the builder's margin (typically 8-15%) doesn't leave room for failed technology experiments. The real barrier isn't ignorance; it's that the tools haven't been designed for how residential construction actually works.

## Limitations
- NAHB survey data is self-reported and from HMI respondents (tends toward larger builders)
- Rework percentages vary enormously by project type and builder quality
- The 5-9% figure comes from broad construction industry, not residential-specific studies
- Warranty accrual data comes from publicly traded builders (Lennar, D.R. Horton, etc.), not custom builders who may have different warranty cost profiles
- "AI" is loosely defined in surveys — builders may use AI features within tools without identifying as "AI users"

# Research Notes: Big Tech Is Training Construction Workers — But Not for Your House

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)

## Angle
OpenAI, Microsoft, and the DOL are pouring millions into construction worker training. The 7,700 apprentices in the pipeline? They're headed for data centers, not subdivisions. Data center projects pay 7-20% more than residential. The AI industry is simultaneously creating the labor shortage AND funding training that doesn't solve it for homebuilders.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — a homeowner or residential GC reading this will understand why their electrician quit, why their plumber costs 20% more than last year, and why the "worker training" headlines won't help them.

## Original Contribution: The Pipeline Math
- TradesFutures trained 7,700 apprentices last year
- ABC says industry needs 349,000 new workers in 2026
- 7,700 / 349,000 = 2.2% of the gap
- But those 7,700 are being steered toward data centers (10.6 months backlog vs 8.3 residential)
- Data center hotspots already pay 7-20% more
- Net contribution to residential construction workforce: near zero
- The training programs funded by tech companies explicitly target "AI-related infrastructure"

## Strongest Counterargument
Rising tide argument: training workers for data center construction creates transferable skills. A plumber or electrician trained on a data center eventually moves to other projects. Union infrastructure benefits all construction, not just data centers. NABTU's $2.5B annual investment covers all trades.

## Limitations
- Cannot trace individual TradesFutures graduates into specific project types (residential vs. commercial vs. data center)
- Gusto payroll data skews toward small employers (misses large union contractors)
- Wage premium may partly reflect cost-of-living differences in data center hotspot regions
- ABC's 349,000 figure is a model projection, not a census
- Data center "85% of demand growth" figure from Bisnow not independently verified

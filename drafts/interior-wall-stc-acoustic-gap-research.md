# Research: Your $500K Home Has the Sound Privacy of a Motel Room

## Journalist: Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES.
- Helps buyers ask the right questions about interior wall assemblies before purchase
- Helps builders understand the cost of acoustic upgrades during vs. after construction
- Provides specific STC numbers, costs, and wall assembly specs

## Core Thesis
The International Residential Code (IRC) has NO requirement for sound transmission between interior walls in single-family homes. A standard builder-grade interior wall (single layer 1/2" drywall on each side, no insulation) has an STC rating of ~33. At STC 33, normal speech can be heard and understood through the wall. The IBC requires STC 50 for multifamily — but single-family homes get zero protection. Your builder saved ~$1,200 by not insulating your interior walls. Fixing it after the fact costs $15,000+.

## Key Data Points

### STC Ratings and What You Hear
| STC | What you hear |
|-----|---------------|
| 25 | Soft speech understandable |
| 30 | Normal speech understandable |
| 33 | **Standard builder interior wall** — loud speech audible and mostly intelligible |
| 35 | Loud speech audible but not intelligible |
| 40 | Loud speech heard as murmur |
| 45 | **Privacy threshold** — loud speech not audible |
| 50 | **IBC multifamily requirement** — loud sounds faintly heard |
| 60+ | Good soundproofing begins |

Source: ASTM E413, Acoustical Surfaces Inc, US Frame Factory

### The Code Gap
- **IBC (International Building Code)** requires STC 50 for walls and floors between dwelling units in multifamily construction
- **IRC (International Residential Code)** has NO STC requirement for interior walls in single-family detached homes
- This means your bedroom wall next to a teenager's room, a home office next to a TV room, or a nursery next to a kitchen — all have ZERO acoustic protection required by code
- Source: IRC/IBC comparison, commercial-acoustics.com

### Cost During Construction vs. After
**During construction (adding to interior walls):**
- Fiberglass batt insulation in interior walls: ~$0.50-$1.50/sqft of wall area
- Typical 2,000 sqft home has ~1,500 sqft of interior wall area
- Total cost for batt insulation in all interior walls: ~$750-$2,250
- Upgrade to resilient channel + batt: adds ~$0.75-$1.50/sqft more
- Going from STC 33 to STC 45-50 during framing: $1,500-$3,500 total for whole house

**After construction (retrofit):**
- Soundproofing per room: $1,000-$5,400 (bedroom), $2,500-$10,500 (living room)
- Per square foot retrofit: $10-$30/sqft
- Green Glue compound: $12-$20 per tube (covers ~32 sqft)
- Mass-Loaded Vinyl: $3-$5/sqft
- Full house retrofit: $15,000-$40,000+
- Source: The Patch Boys/Angi cost data 2024

### Wall Assembly Options (verified STC ratings)
1. **Builder standard** — 2x4 wood studs, single 1/2" drywall each side, no insulation → STC 33
2. **Add batt insulation** — Same + R-13 fiberglass batt → STC 36-39
3. **Double drywall** — 2x4, batt, two layers 5/8" drywall each side → STC 44-46
4. **Resilient channel** — 2x4, batt, resilient channel, 5/8" drywall → STC 46-50
5. **Double drywall + Green Glue** — 2x4, batt, 2 layers 5/8" drywall with Green Glue each side → STC 50-55
6. **Staggered stud wall** — 2x6 plate, staggered 2x4 studs, batt, 5/8" drywall each side → STC 50-52

Source: US Frame Factory, Fine Homebuilding, ASHRAE Handbook

### The Noise Complaint Problem
- UK Property Reporter survey: noise voted #1 biggest turn-off for homebuyers
- Most common post-move-in complaints in new single-family homes involve sound transfer between rooms
- Remote work explosion means home offices need acoustic privacy that builder-grade walls cannot provide
- Children's rooms, media rooms, and master bedrooms are the most complained-about noise transfer points

### AI/Tech Angle
- Acoustic simulation software (COMSOL, Autodesk tools) can model sound transmission through wall assemblies before construction
- AI-driven building design tools increasingly incorporate acoustic modeling alongside thermal/structural
- Thermal imaging analogy: just as an IR scan reveals where heat leaks, acoustic testing reveals where sound leaks — but almost nobody tests residential interiors
- Cost of an STC field test: ~$500-$1,500 per wall assembly

### The Emotional Angle (Elena's specialty)
Architecture isn't just about how a space looks — it's about how it feels, sounds, and performs. A beautifully designed open floor plan with terrible acoustic isolation in the private spaces is an architectural failure. The irony: we insulate exterior walls for energy (required by code) but leave interior walls acoustically naked. We spend $4,000 on quartz countertops and $0 on the wall between a nursery and a home theater.

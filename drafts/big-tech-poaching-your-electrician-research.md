# Research Notes: Big Tech Is Poaching Your Electrician

**Journalist:** Marcus "Steel" Washington
**Beat:** Workforce & Labor
**Date:** June 29, 2026

## Thesis
The AI data center construction boom is systematically draining residential construction of skilled trades workers — especially electricians. Meta, Microsoft, and OpenAI are spending hundreds of millions to train and recruit trades workers for data centers, while homebuilders watch their crews shrink and their timelines stretch. The same companies building AI that promises to "fix" construction are making the labor shortage worse right now.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES** — explains why homes take longer to build, why electricians cost more, and why the labor shortage is structural, not cyclical. Gives builders context to plan around and homebuyers context for timelines.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Home Builders Institute (HBI) — Edward Brady, CEO
- Source: Homes.com interview (March 2026)
- Skilled labor shortage costs homebuilders $10.8 billion/year
- Prevents construction of ~19,000 homes annually
- "Workers are going to leave the residential industry and go to the commercial. Then, residential construction suffers — those commercial jobs are more stable and, in many cases, better paying."
- Residential construction workers: ~$39.40/hr nationally
- Construction industry shrank by 11,000 jobs in February 2026 (BLS)
- Immigrants = 25.5% of construction workforce (historic high), 1 in 3 craftsmen
- Immigration enforcement fears already keeping workers off job sites

### 2. NAHB — Robert Dietz, Chief Economist
- Source: NAHB 2026 Housing Outlook, HMI survey
- 300,000 job openings in construction (Dec 2025)
- Need to add ~740,000 workers/year to keep pace (growth + retirements + departures)
- 1.2 million housing unit deficit
- 61% of builders say cost/availability of labor is a serious problem (2025 survey)
- Young adult interest in construction doubled from 3% to 6% (2016→2026), still low
- $11 billion/year combined impact of labor shortage

### 3. Gene Lantrip, Abilene TX homebuilder (age 69)
- Source: Texas Tribune (April 2026), Inman (May 2026)
- Homes taking 2+ months longer to complete since Stargate data center construction began
- "My subcontractors don't have the people. My electrician lost two of his lead men and several of his helpers to the data center."
- Electricians at Stargate earning TWICE what residential subcontractors pay
- Stargate: 4 million sq ft, backed by OpenAI, Crusoe, Oracle

### 4. IBEW (International Brotherhood of Electrical Workers)
- Source: Texas Tribune, Inman
- Electrical subcontractors account for 45-70% of data center construction budgets
- Data centers require massive electrical infrastructure from construction through long-term operations

### 5. Federal Reserve Data — Private Fixed Investment
- Source: FRED, cited by Inman (May 2026)
- Computing equipment investment: ~$150B → ~$400B in less than 2 years
- Single-family residential investment: flat at ~$400B
- First time in modern American economic history these lines have converged
- "Two lines that spent six decades running parallel, with homebuilding well ahead, are now nearly touching."

### 6. Big Tech Training Programs
- Meta AWA (America's Workforce Academy): $115M, no-cost trades training for data center construction. Electrical, welding, plumbing, fiber. Conditional job offer BEFORE training. Tuition, travel, lodging, tools, daily stipend covered. 5-week intensive. 2026 pilots: LA, OH, IN, TX.
- Microsoft/NABTU: 1,500 instructors trained, expanding to AI literacy courses for millions of trades workers. LinkedIn Learning platform. 34 states via TradesFutures.
- DOL: Launched AI-in-Apprenticeships initiative, new apprenticeship.gov AI skills portal

### 7. Inman/Arif Gasilov Analysis
- Source: Inman (May 2026), Gasilov Group ESG consultancy
- Data center land prices: $6.3M/acre in Leesburg VA (SDC Capital paid $615M for 97 acres)
- "I don't think a residential developer can compete"
- Natelli Communities: 40-year residential developer pivoting to data centers
- Dominion Energy rate hike: $11.24/month per residential customer, driven by data center grid buildout
- Virginia SB 253 signed — shifts some infrastructure costs to data centers

### 8. Lexology/Legal Analysis
- Source: Lexology (April 2026)
- ~3,000 new data centers under construction or planned
- Could generate ~4.7 million temporary construction jobs
- 60% of data center providers report challenges finding qualified candidates
- Each large data center: 1,500-3,000 workers at peak, some up to 4,000

## Original Contribution (Novel Analysis)
Calculate the "poach ratio": how many residential electricians the data center boom absorbs per year vs. how many the training pipeline produces. Cross-reference NAHB's 740,000/year need with the 4.7M data center construction jobs to quantify the structural drain.

Also: Meta and Microsoft are spending $115M+ on trades training, but the workers they train go to data centers, not houses. Net effect on residential is NEGATIVE — they're training workers AND pulling them away from homebuilding simultaneously.

## Strongest Counterargument
The trades training programs create NET MORE workers overall. Some data center construction workers will eventually return to residential when projects complete. And higher wages across the industry attract more people into trades. The real issue is the decades-long underinvestment in vocational education, not data centers — data centers just exposed the bottleneck.

## Limitations
- Wage comparison data is anecdotal (Lantrip's "twice as much") — no systematic residential vs. data center electrician wage study exists
- The 4.7M temporary construction jobs figure is a projection, not actual employment
- Can't isolate data center poaching from general commercial construction pull
- Immigration enforcement impact is real but hard to quantify
- Regional variation is massive — Abilene is ground zero; markets without data centers may not feel the squeeze yet

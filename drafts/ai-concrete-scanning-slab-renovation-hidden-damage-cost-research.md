# Research: AI-Powered Concrete Scanning Before Slab Penetration

## Topic
AI-enhanced GPR (ground-penetrating radar) concrete scanning before cutting, coring, or drilling into residential slabs. Focus: post-tension cable damage prevention during bathroom/kitchen renovations.

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?** YES — directly actionable for any homeowner renovating a slab-on-grade home (72.4% of new US homes). One GPR scan prevents a $2,000-$8,000 cable repair.

### 2. Post-Tensioning Institute (PTI)
- Post-tensioned slabs common in expansive soil regions (TX, FL, AZ, CA)
- Damage causes: corrosion, accidental impact, coring/drilling after construction
- Repair requires structural engineer evaluation + specialized PT contractor
- Source: https://www.post-tensioning.org/education/ptapplications/repair.aspx

### 3. GPRS (Ground Penetrating Radar Systems) — Industry Data
- GPR accuracy: ±¼ inch to center of object, ±½ inch to depth (concrete scanning)
- Average utility strike cost: $56,000 (commercial — residential lower but still significant)
- Scan time: 5-15 minutes for a concrete slab or room
- GPR vs X-ray: single-side access, no radiation, no site evacuation
- Color-coded marking: paint, flags, chalk for visual reference
- Source: https://www.gp-radar.com/explained/concrete-scanning-explained

- Source: https://www.screeningeagle.com/en/products/proceq-gp8000, certifiedmtp.com

## Cost Analysis (Novel Contribution)

### GPR Scan Cost
- Professional service: $300-$800 per residential visit (industry estimate, varies by region)
- Rental: $500-$1,000/day for equipment like StructureScan Mini
- AI-equipped devices (GP8000): $15K-$25K purchase price

### Post-Tension Cable Repair Cost
- Single cable repair: $1,500-$3,000 (minor, accessible)
- Complex repair with structural engineering: $3,000-$8,000+
- Multiple cables: $5,000-$15,000+
- Does NOT include: fixing the finished surfaces damaged during repair access

### Break-Even Calculation
- Typical bathroom remodel on PT slab: plumber needs 3-5 slab penetrations (drain relocations, supply line additions)
- Without scanning: estimated 5-15% chance of hitting a cable per penetration (depends on cable spacing, typically 24-48 inches on center)
- With scanning: near-zero chance (GPR accuracy ±0.25 inches)
- Expected cost without scan: 3 penetrations × 10% hit rate × $5,000 avg repair = $1,500 expected loss
- GPR scan cost: $500
- ROI: 3:1 on expected value alone — but the real number is binary: you either hit one ($5,000+) or you don't

### The Real Math
- 5 penetrations × ~10% each = 41% cumulative probability of at least one strike
- 41% × $5,000 = $2,050 expected loss
- $500 scan → net savings: $1,550 expected value
- If you hit a cable AND it damages plumbing work already completed: $8,000-$12,000

## Strongest Counterargument
- Most experienced plumbers in PT-heavy markets already probe/check for cables
- X-ray has been used for decades (though GPR is superior for residential)
- Many bathroom remodels on PT slabs go fine without scanning
- Equipment cost ($15K+) makes AI-powered scanning impractical for individual homeowners — must hire service

## Limitations
- No centralized database of PT cable strikes during residential renovations
- Repair cost estimates vary widely by market and contractor
- Hit probability (5-15%) is estimated from cable spacing patterns, not from tracked incident data
- AI auto-tagging accuracy claims come from manufacturers, not independent validation
- GPR struggles with closely spaced objects and congested rebar areas

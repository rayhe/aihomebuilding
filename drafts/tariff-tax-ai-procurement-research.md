# Research Notes: Tariffs Added $10,900 to Every New Home. That Prices Out 1.26 Million Families. AI Could Claw Some of It Back.

## Thesis
Tariffs have added $10,900 to the cost of every new single-family home in America (NAHB, April 2025). Using NAHB's priced-out methodology, that translates to approximately 1.26 million households who can no longer afford a new home — solely because of trade policy. AI procurement and material sourcing tools could recover $2,000–4,000 per home through optimized domestic/import switching, alternative supplier discovery, and predictive tariff modeling. But 73% of construction firms don't use any AI at all.

## Novel Contribution: The Priced-Out Multiplier
Nobody has published the full chain:
1. NAHB says tariffs add $10,900 per home (April 2025 HMI survey, 60%+ builders)
2. NAHB's separate priced-out study says every $1,000 increase prices out 115,593 households
3. $10,900 × 115.593 = **~1,261,964 families** priced out by tariffs alone
4. Brookings/TPC independently calculates $30 billion total residential investment cost increase
5. At ~1.4M new homes started annually (Census), $30B ÷ 1.4M = ~$21,400 per housing unit (includes multifamily), triangulating NAHB's single-family figure
6. Meanwhile, AI procurement tools exist that could optimize sourcing to claw back 20-35% of the tariff hit per home — but adoption is 27% in AEC overall and likely far lower among residential builders

## Strongest Counterargument
"Only 7% of residential construction materials are imported. Tariffs barely matter for homebuilders."

Counter: True that 93% is domestic by dollar value, but the 7% that IS imported includes irreplaceable categories. Canada supplies 85% of US softwood lumber imports and ~25% of total US lumber supply. Chinese cabinets represent a massive share of the kitchen market. Steel/aluminum flow through every structural element. And the 7% figure understates upstream effects — domestic material prices rise when import competition is reduced. NAHB's own builders estimate $10,900 per home, not economists guessing. The builders filling out the HMI survey are the ones writing the checks.

## Cross-Calculation for Novel Contribution
1. $10,900 tariff cost per home (NAHB HMI April 2025, builder survey)
2. 115,593 households priced out per $1,000 home price increase (NAHB priced-out model)
3. $10,900 ÷ $1,000 = 10.9 increments
4. 10.9 × 115,593 = **1,259,964 households priced out by tariffs**
5. Cross-check: Brookings says $30B total residential investment increase; Census: ~1.4M housing starts/year; $30B ÷ 1.4M = ~$21,400/unit. For single-family (~1.0M starts), the per-unit number is higher, consistent with NAHB's $10,900 (which is builder-estimated average, not the full tariff pass-through)
6. AI procurement potential: If tools can optimize sourcing to avoid 20-35% of tariff exposure ($2,200-$3,800 per home), that would bring 250,000-440,000 families back into affordability range
7. But only 27% of AEC firms use any AI. For small residential builders (<$10M revenue), adoption is likely single digits.

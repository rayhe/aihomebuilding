# Research: AI Wildfire Risk Scoring, Insurance, and Building Code Compliance

## Topic
AI-powered property-level wildfire risk models are reshaping insurance underwriting, while new building codes (Colorado CWRC July 1, 2026) and IBHS certification programs create the first coherent wildfire-resilient construction standard. The gap: builders and homeowners don't know their property's AI risk score, even as that score determines insurability and premium.

## Kill Test
✅ Helps someone building/buying a home in WUI areas understand:
- What AI risk score their property has and who's using it
- What the new codes actually require (and cost)
- The insurance availability crisis and how to navigate it
- Whether IBHS certification is worth pursuing

## Primary Sources

### 1. ZestyAI Z-FIRE Model
- AI model trained on 2,000+ historical wildfires, satellite/aerial imagery, topography, property-level characteristics
- Covers ~100% of US properties
- Trusted by >1/3 of California's insurance market, including CA FAIR Plan (insurer of last resort)
- CDI-approved for rate filings since 2024
- Post-LA fire validation: 94% of Palisades fire zone and 87% of Eaton fire zone rated High/Very High BEFORE fires
- Found 1.2 million California homes classified "low risk" by FEMA but flagged elevated by AI — ~$1 trillion in property value
- Palisades zone specifically: 1,430 properties flagged by AI but low-risk per federal assessment, $1.14B value, 229 homes rated very-high to extreme
- Farmers Insurance adopted Z-FIRE in 2021
- Kingstone Companies partnering with ZestyAI for California E&S market entry (April 2026)
- Source: ZestyAI press releases, Morningstar/PR Newswire, Finextra

### 2. Property Guardian / EigenRisk
- Wildfire Recurrence Risk Score: time-aware, incorporates fuel dynamics since last burn
- Forward-looking (not just historical burn area)
- Identified highest-destruction LA areas 2-3 days before fire arrival during Jan 2025 fires
- Partnership with EigenRisk for commercial underwriting platform
- Source: BusinessWire (June 2026)

### 3. Verisk FireLine
- 0-30 scale, widely used by insurers
- 73% of scores are 0-1 (negligible/low), 12% are 2-3 (moderate), 15% are 4-30 (high to extreme)
- Each insurer sets own threshold (6, 8, or 10+ = hazardous)
- Some insurers refusing coverage at FireLine 2+
- Source: SF Chronicle / United Policyholders

### 4. Nearmap AI Risk Scores
- AI from aerial imagery: roof condition/material, defensible space, vegetation encroachment
- Explainable AI models trained on high-resolution imagery + historical loss data
- Source: Nearmap website

### 5. Colorado Wildfire Resiliency Code (CWRC)
- First-of-its-kind statewide mandatory wildfire building code
- Adopted July 2025, jurisdictions must adopt by April 1, 2026, enforce by **July 1, 2026** (7 days from now)
- Applies to new construction or remodels involving ≥25% of exterior
- Two-tier: Class 1 and Class 2 hardening based on fire intensity zone (low/moderate/high)
- Class 1 requirements: Class A roof, noncombustible gutters, ember/flame-resistant vents
- Class 2 (higher risk): additional requirements beyond Class 1
- Three-year property evaluation cycle for ongoing compliance
- Joins California and Utah as mandatory statewide wildfire construction codes
- Source: Colorado DFPC, CPR News, Homes.com, Montrose County, James Hardie

### 6. IBHS Wildfire Prepared Program (updated June 9, 2026)
- Wildfire Prepared Home (Essential + Enhanced levels)
- Wildfire Prepared Multifamily (NEW)
- Wildfire Prepared Neighborhood (NEW, piloted with KB Home)
- KB Home Dixon Trail (Escondido, CA): 64 homes, first Wildfire Prepared Neighborhood, WFPH+ certified
- KB Home Stone Canyon (Cameron Park, CA): 24 homes, first in NorCal, WFPH+ certified
- Paradise, CA passed law requiring all homes meet IBHS standard after 2018 Camp Fire
- Source: IBHS, KB Home press releases, Fast Company, HousingWire

### 7. Cost Data
- Headwaters Economics/IBHS (California edition):
  - Enhanced wildfire-resistant home: +$2,800 over baseline (NorCal)
  - Optimum wildfire resistance: +$18,200 (NorCal) to +$27,100 (SoCal)
- IBHS Altadena rebuild analysis: WFPH+ materials $40,307 vs traditional $37,889 = only $2,418 materials difference
- NAHB: wildfire hardening costs range $1,827 to $44,888 depending on siding choice
- Retrofit costs for 2,000 sq ft California home: $2,000 to $100,000+ (full protection)
- Simple effective measures (5-ft clearance, vent mesh, gutter cleaning): $2,780
- Builtech LA ICF ADU: $410K, Type I fire resistance, 10-day construction
- Source: IBHS, Headwaters Economics, UC ANR, NAHB, NY Post

### 8. Broader Context
- NIST: 46 million US residential structures in WUI areas
- One-third of all US homes in WUI
- 35,000+ structures lost to wildfire in last decade
- Eaton + Palisades fires (Jan 2025): 28 dead, 16,000+ structures destroyed
- California insurance crisis: State Farm, Allstate, others pulling back from wildfire areas
- CA FAIR Plan (insurer of last resort) using Z-FIRE for risk assessment
- First Street Fire Factor: 1-10 property-level score, on Realtor.com and Redfin

# Research: AI Concrete Mix Optimization — The Overdesign Problem

## Angle
78% of concrete mixes are overdesigned by ~77 lb/yd³ of excess cement. AI mix optimization tools can eliminate that waste, cutting costs and embodied carbon. For a typical 2,000 sq ft home foundation (50-80 cubic yards), that's 3,850-6,160 lb of unnecessary cement — producing roughly 3,500-5,600 lb of excess CO2.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. Concrete is the largest single material cost in a foundation, and cement is 80% of concrete's carbon footprint. A homeowner or builder can ask their ready-mix supplier if they use AI optimization or specify CarbonCure concrete. Actionable cost savings of $1-2/yard.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NRMCA Annual Quality Survey (National Ready Mixed Concrete Association)
- Industry overdesigns by ~77 lb/yd³ of excess cementitious material (down from 80 lb/yd³ prior year)
- This is self-protective: failure to meet specified strength creates liability
- URL: https://www.nrmca.org/wp-content/uploads/Quality_Survey_Report.pdf

### 2. Giatec Scientific — SmartMix / Roxi AI Assistant
- Survey of 10,000+ concrete mixes: 78% were overdesigned
- Overdesign typically 600-700 PSI above required strength
- Roxi AI: continuously analyzes batch data, delivery, raw materials, environment
- Savings: $1.20-$2.00 per cubic yard
- Modern Concrete & Materials: optimized 19% of production, saved $1.26/yd³
- Tomlinson Ready Mix: 28% of production optimized
- URL: https://www.giatecscientific.com/education/mix-optimization-with-concrete-ai-assistant/

### 3. MIT Concrete Sustainability Hub / Olivetti Group (2025)
- Machine learning framework to find cement alternatives across 1M+ rock samples
- Published in Nature Communications Materials (May 2025)
- 19 material types identified as potential cement substitutes (ceramics, mining byproducts, demolition waste)
- Many need only grinding — no complex processing
- Ancient Roman concrete used ceramics for waterproofing
- URL: https://news.mit.edu/2025/ai-stirs-recipe-for-concrete-0602

### 4. CarbonCure Technologies
- CO2 injection into fresh concrete during mixing → mineralizes, improves compressive strength
- Ready Mix: 15-25 lb CO2 saved per cubic yard
- Precast: 20-30 lb CO2 saved per cubic yard
- No upfront cost to producers (equipment installed at their plant)
- 800+ producers across North America
- URL: https://www.carboncure.com/concrete-producers/

### 5. Portland Cement Association / IEA
- Cement = 8-9% of global anthropogenic CO2 (MIT/IEA)
- Cement is 10% of concrete mass but 80% of its GHG footprint
- Two sources of CO2: fuel burned for heating + calcination of limestone (chemical release)

## Original Contribution (novel calculation)
Calculate the per-home carbon cost of concrete overdesign:
- Average 2,000 sqft slab-on-grade: ~50 cubic yards of concrete
- Average basement foundation: ~75-80 cubic yards
- Overdesign at 77 lb/yd³ excess cement × 50 yd³ = 3,850 lb excess cement
- Portland cement produces ~0.9 tons CO2 per ton of cement (process + energy)
- 3,850 lb × 0.9 = 3,465 lb = 1.57 metric tons of UNNECESSARY CO2 per slab home
- For basement: 77 × 80 = 6,160 lb × 0.9 = 5,544 lb = 2.52 metric tons excess
- Context: average US car emits 4.6 metric tons/year (EPA)
- So your foundation's overdesign = driving a car 4-7 months for no reason

## Strongest Counterargument
Overdesign exists for real safety reasons. Concrete that falls below specified strength can cause structural failure. In-field conditions (water addition by truck drivers, temperature, aggregate variability) are unpredictable. The overdesign buffer has prevented countless failures. AI optimization must prove it can maintain the same safety margin with less material — and the liability question is unresolved. If an AI-optimized mix fails, who's liable?

## Limitations
- Giatec's savings data comes from their own customers (selection bias)
- CarbonCure's CO2 reduction claims are per-yard, not verified by independent lifecycle assessment
- MIT framework hasn't been commercially deployed at residential scale yet
- Residential concrete is a tiny fraction of total concrete market; most AI tools target commercial/infrastructure
- Cost savings of $1-2/yard on 50 yards = $50-100 per home. Not transformative individually.
- The carbon case is stronger than the cost case for residential

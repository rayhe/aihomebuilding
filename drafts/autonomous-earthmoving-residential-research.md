# Research: Autonomous Earthmoving Is Coming for Residential Site Prep

## Angle
The machines that grade your lot are already running themselves on commercial sites. Caterpillar announced autonomous construction equipment at CES 2026. Bedrock Robotics moved 65,000 cubic yards with Sundt Construction on a 130-acre site. Gravis Robotics raised $23M and claims 30% productivity gains with retrofit kits. The question isn't whether autonomous earthmoving works — it's when the economics pencil out for your quarter-acre lot.

## Key Data Points

### Industry Scale
- AI in construction market: $2.28B (2025) → $3.02B (2026), 32.8% CAGR, projected $9.48B by 2030 (ResearchAndMarkets, June 2026)
- U.S. construction spending: $2.16 trillion (July 2024, Census Bureau)

### Autonomous Earthmoving Players

**Caterpillar (CES 2026, Jan 7)**
- Announced autonomous excavators, loaders, haul trucks, dozers, compactors
- Level 4 autonomy already proven in mining: 11 billion tonnes moved, 380 million km traveled
- Cat VisionLink + MineStar systems for fleet coordination
- 30+ years of autonomy R&D (Carnegie Mellon partnership since 1980s)
- Source: caterpillar.com press release

**Bedrock Robotics (SF-based startup) + Sundt Construction**
- 130-acre manufacturing facility, Southwest US (Phoenix area)
- 65,000+ cubic yards moved autonomously (of 700K total planned)
- Bedrock = ~10% of site utilization
- Retrofit kit: LiDAR, GPS, IMU, 8 HD cameras, in-cab computer
- Installs in hours, no permanent modifications, 20-80 ton excavators
- Partners: Austin Bridge & Road, Maverick, Haydon, Champion, Zachry, Capitol Aggregates
- Dan Green (Sundt PM): "Our best operators aren't interested in the monotony of mass excavation work"
- Eric Cylwik (Sundt Innovation): "12 hours a day, seven days a week... not a desirable, repeat activity"
- Source: ENR, Equipment World, The Robot Report (Nov-Dec 2025)

**Gravis Robotics (ETH Zurich spinout, founded late 2022)**
- Raised $23M (IQ Capital, Zacua Ventures, Pear VC, Holcim, others)
- "Gravis Rack" = rooftop retrofit kit (LiDAR, cameras, GNSS, hydraulic sensing)
- "Gravis Slate" = tablet interface for autonomous/manual mode switching
- ~30% faster performance in high-volume tasks
- Works with 12+ OEM brands (Develon, Hitachi, Case, Sumitomo, Yanmar, CNH, Menzi Muck)
- AI "feels the soil" via hydraulic pressure data
- Live demos at ConExpo 2026 and Bauma 2025
- First UK large-scale autonomous excavation at Manchester Airport (with Taylor Woodrow)
- Holcim using for quarry duties
- Source: Equipment World, New Civil Engineer, tech.eu, IVT International

**Urban Infrastructure Group (UIG) — TSXV: UIG (June 30, 2026)**
- Just announced autonomous robotics research initiative for residential construction
- Focus: trenching, excavation, pipe-laying, concrete — all residential trades
- "Not pivoting away from construction — evaluating automation opportunities"
- Active job sites as testing environments
- Responding to skilled trade labor constraints in Canadian construction
- Source: Newsfile Corp/Barchart

## Novel Analysis Opportunity
**Break-even calculation:** At what lot volume does autonomous earthmoving pay off for a residential builder? If Gravis Rack costs ~$50-100K to retrofit (estimated from the $23M raise and commercial pricing signals), and saves 30% on productivity for grading, the math only works if you're grading enough lots to amortize that cost. A production homebuilder doing 200 lots/year at $3,500 each = $700K in site prep. A 30% savings = $210K. That would amortize the retrofit in months. But a custom builder doing 5 homes/year? The numbers don't come close.

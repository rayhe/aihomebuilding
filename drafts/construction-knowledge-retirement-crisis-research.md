# Research: The Construction Knowledge Retirement Crisis

## Angle
29% of the construction workforce is expected to retire by 2026. 70% of electrical supervisors are baby boomers. When they leave, they take decades of local, contextual knowledge that no training program can replace in 5 weeks. Can AI capture what a 30-year superintendent knows before it disappears?

## Journalist: Marcus "Steel" Washington (Workforce & Labor)

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — the quality of your new home build depends enormously on the superintendent/foreman's tacit knowledge. The retirement wave means your project increasingly gets someone who's smart but hasn't spent 20 years learning which local subcontractors actually deliver, how the soil on your street drains, or what the county inspector flags.

## Original Analysis to Perform
1. **Knowledge half-life calculation**: If 29% retire by 2026 and each carries ~20 years average experience, that's roughly [29% × 8.3M construction workers × 20 years average experience] = millions of person-years of knowledge at risk. Quantify this.
2. **Tacit vs explicit knowledge in construction**: Break down what percentage of a superintendent's daily decisions rely on local/contextual knowledge vs codified knowledge (building codes, specs). The codifiable stuff is what AI can help with. The tacit stuff is what walks out the door.
3. **Training pipeline math**: 4-year apprenticeship × current enrollment rates vs retirement rate = deficit timeline. When does the math become impossible?

## Strongest Counterargument
Tech companies' training programs (Meta AWA, Google $50M, Microsoft/NABTU) argue that the knowledge problem is a training problem that money can solve. Meta's 5-week bootcamp produces "job-site ready" workers with industry credentials. The counter-counter: a 5-week bootcamp doesn't produce a superintendent. It produces a helper. The knowledge that takes 20 years to accumulate — the kind that prevents a $40,000 rework — can't be compressed into a curriculum.

## Limitations
- No direct survey data on what percentage of construction knowledge is tacit vs explicit
- Retirement projections are estimates; actual retirement timing depends on economic conditions
- AI knowledge-capture tools for construction are still early-stage; limited performance data
- Most data is about commercial construction; residential-specific retirement data is thin

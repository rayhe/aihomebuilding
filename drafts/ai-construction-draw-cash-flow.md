# Research Notes: AI Construction Draw Automation and Builder Cash Flow

## Slug: ai-construction-draw-cash-flow
## Journalist: Frank "The Foreman" DeLuca (Project Management & Operations)
## Angle: The construction industry's real bottleneck isn't labor or materials — it's cash flow. 70% of contractors face regular payment delays. AI draw agents are cutting week-long reviews to minutes, and for small residential builders, that matters more than any robot.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** If you're a homebuilder, the draw process is the financial oxygen of every project. Understanding AI tools that accelerate draws directly impacts project timelines, costs, and whether your builder stays solvent. If you're a homeowner with a construction loan, faster draws mean faster build completion and lower carrying costs.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Built Technologies — Draw Agent & Industry Data
- **Source:** HousingWire article (May 2026) + Built Technologies national study (BusinessWire, July 2025)
- **Built Technologies' Draw Agent** fully rolled out early 2026
- Typical construction draw timeline: **7 business days**
- Human review of a draw request: **20-40 hours**
- Built Technologies average: **3 business days**; Draw Agent in "automate mode": **minutes**
- **185,000 contractors** use the Built Technologies platform
- Partners: **U.S. Bank, Citi**
- Three-stage rollout: audit mode (read-only) → assist mode (scheduling, communications) → automate mode (full approval)
- Quote from Thomas Schlegel, VP Engineering: "It's just one of the most inundated, labor-intensive, inefficient processes that exists in construction."
- Quote: "It's a very cash flow driven industry, and the biggest risk to any project is that your builder has to go to another project to get paid. And very frequently, builders are using money from Peter to pay Paul, and that's when things come sideways."

### 2. Built Technologies National Study (2025)
- **70% of contractors** regularly face delayed payments
- Contractors inflate bids by an average of **8%** to protect against slow payments
- **35%** have seen projects canceled or significantly delayed due to financing gaps
- **60%** of contractors say a developer's payment reputation significantly affects their decision to bid

### 3. Billd 2024 Construction Cash Flow Report (via ConstructionDive)
- Subcontractors waited **56 days on average** for payment
- **43%** of subcontractors don't have enough working capital for unexpected expenses
- **29%** of builders said overdue invoices obstruct project progress
- **35%** said they pick and choose which invoices to pay on time
- Subcontractors who accounted for working capital costs in bids: **24% profit margin** vs **17%** for those who didn't

### 4. Mobilization Funding 2026 Construction Growth Report
- **90%** of construction leaders say cash flow timing has cost them profitable work
- **43%** have passed on profitable projects multiple times due to cash flow
- **100%** say cash flow influences project pursuit decisions
- **97%** say funding upfront costs while growing keeps them up at night

### 5. PYMNTS Construction Payment Data
- Cost of slow payments to construction industry: **$273 billion/year** (14% of total costs, up from 12%)
- **72%** of subcontractors report payment delays >30 days (up from 49% prior year)
- GCs spend **56 hours/month** managing payments (up 27% YoY)

### 6. HousingWire "Builders Don't Have a Rate Problem" (April 2026)
- Problem isn't demand — it's execution
- "One bad deal doesn't just hurt — it wipes out the next three"
- Projects delayed, pipelines thinning, deals falling apart late

## Original Contribution (Required)
**Calculate the real cost of draw delays on a typical $500K residential construction project:**
- Assume 5 draws over 8-month build
- Each draw: ~$100K
- Each draw delay: avg 7 business days → 14 calendar days with weekends
- Builder carrying costs: interest on construction loan (~7.5%), insurance, overhead labor
- $500K loan at 7.5% = ~$102.74/day in interest
- 5 draws × 14 extra calendar days = 70 days of delay = **$7,192 in extra interest alone**
- Plus: sub mobilization/demobilization costs (crew leaves, comes back), material storage, schedule cascades
- At 8% bid inflation to compensate: **$40,000** added to a $500K home
- Built's claim: Draw Agent cuts 7-day to minutes. Even cutting to 1 day saves ~$3,700 in interest on a single project
- For a builder doing 10 homes/year: **$37,000** in recovered carrying costs annually

## Strongest Counterargument
- AI draw approval could increase fraud risk — human reviewers catch red flags (inflated progress claims, duplicate lien waivers, subcontractor discrepancies) that AI might miss or approve automatically
- Three-stage trust rollout (audit → assist → automate) is designed to address this, but "automate mode" is where the risk lives
- Also: what happens when the AI says no? A human reviewer can call and discuss; an automated denial with no explanation could be worse than a slow yes

## Limitations
- Built Technologies is the primary source for most efficiency claims — no independent audit of their Draw Agent's accuracy or error rates
- 185,000 contractors is impressive but represents their total user base, not necessarily those using the AI Draw Agent
- The $273B cost of slow payments is an industry-wide figure (commercial + residential); residential-specific numbers are harder to isolate
- The draw process varies dramatically by lender, loan type, and state — some already process in 2-3 days without AI

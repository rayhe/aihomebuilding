# Research: Drywall Finishing Robots in Residential Construction

## Sources

### 1. Canvas (San Francisco) — UR10e Cobots for Drywall Finishing
- Source: Universal Robots case study, BusinessWire press release (2024)
- Uses UR10e collaborative robots on a mobile platform
- 1200CX model: 30"×34.5" footprint, 12' finishing reach
- Process: spray-applies ALL joint compound in one coat → 1 day drying → automated sanding
- Reduces finishing from 5 days to 2 days
- Reduces touchpoints (coat-dry-sand cycles) by 40%
- Integrated vacuum captures 99.9% of dust
- No prior building plans needed — AI auto-detects site geometry
- Over-the-air software updates improve performance post-purchase
- CEO Kevin Albert: "We have to build twice as much over the next 40 years, but two people retire for every one that comes in"
- CTO Maria Telleria: "For the first time in the history of construction, the tools we are providing for our workers only get better after the initial purchase"
- 1550 model for taller spaces (15.5' reach), 1200CX for standard residential
- Handles: Multifamily Residential, Condo, Hospitality, Hospital, Datacenter, etc.

### 2. Okibo EG7 — Fully Autonomous Interior Finishing Robot
- Source: Builder Online "Behind the Gloss" (2025)
- 27" wide, 800 lbs, cordless
- Fully autonomous — no operator guidance needed
- Paints, applies plaster, and finishes drywall
- 1,000 sqft/hour — 10x faster than manual methods
- AI-powered 3D scanning navigates through standard-sized doors
- No BIM drawings or site prep required
- Quick-change hopper and dust collector for switching between spray and sand

### 3. PaintJet Bravo — Exterior Painting Robot
- Source: Builder Online (2025)
- Tennessee-based
- Exterior painting, 14x faster than human painters
- Computer vision predictive imaging for precision control
- 25% less paint waste than manual methods
- Alpha Shield proprietary coating lasts 2x longer, reduces cooling costs 9%
- Deployed via lifts for height access

### 4. Academic Research — ISARC 2025
- Autonomous construction robot with telescopic arm for drywall panel installation (Bagherzadeh et al., 42nd ISARC Montreal 2025)
- Reduces installation time 10-30% depending on panel length
- Semiautomatic control via web interface
- Safety mechanisms with distance sensors and cameras
- Drywall finishing with collaborative robot arm in off-site construction (42nd ISARC 2025)
- Machine vision detection algorithm for drywall joint compound
- Preliminary results: robot achieves same material depth as human worker (<0.19mm)

### 5. Drywall Finishing Costs (2026 data)
- Source: HomeGuide, Angi, Faircloth Drywall, Terrapin Construction Group
- Level 4 finish (standard residential): $1.70-$3.20/sqft
- Level 5 finish (premium smooth): $1.75-$3.50/sqft
- Labor accounts for 65-75% of installed drywall cost
- National average: $2.24-$2.65/sqft (NC pricing)
- Industry forecasts: 5-7% material cost increases through 2025-2026
- A 2,000 sqft home: ~9,000 sqft drywall surface area
- Total drywall finishing labor cost for typical home: $9,000-$28,800 at Level 4

### 6. Labor Shortage Context
- UK Construction Industry Training Board: 250,000 more workers needed by 2028
- More people leaving construction than joining (UK 2023)
- US: similar trend — aging workforce, insufficient new entrants
- AUAR co-founder Mollie Claypool: "Automation isn't replacing jobs. Automation is filling the gap"

### 7. Health/Dust Data
- OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1153 — Respirable Crystalline Silica standard for construction (2016)
- Permissible Exposure Limit: 50 μg/m³ (8-hr TWA)
- Drywall joint compound contains crystalline silica (quartz)
- Sanding generates respirable dust that causes silicosis, lung cancer, COPD
- Traditional mitigation: N95 respirators, wet sanding, ventilation
- Canvas robot captures 99.9% of sanding dust via integrated vacuum

## Novel Calculation

For a typical 2,000 sqft single-family home:
- Wall area: ~5,600 sqft (assuming 9' ceilings, ~620 linear ft of wall)
- Ceiling area: ~2,000 sqft (plus closets/bathrooms ~400 sqft)
- Total drywall surface: ~8,000-9,500 sqft

### Manual Process (Level 4 Finish)
- Day 1: Tape + 1st coat of mud
- Day 2: Dry
- Day 3: 2nd coat
- Day 4: Dry  
- Day 5: 3rd coat
- Day 6: Dry
- Day 7: Sand entire surface
- Total: 5-7 working days (with drying time)
- Crew: 2-3 tapers at $25-45/hr
- Labor hours: ~120-180 crew-hours
- Labor cost: ~$4,200-$8,100

### Canvas Robot Process
- Day 1: Spray-apply all compound in one coat
- Day 2: Dry
- Day 3: Automated sanding with 99.9% dust capture
- Total: 2-3 working days
- Crew: 1 operator
- Labor hours: ~16-24 operator-hours
- Labor cost: ~$800-$1,200 (plus equipment lease)

### Schedule Value
- Saved days: 3-4 days minimum
- General contractor daily carrying cost: $500-$1,500/day (equipment, supervision, insurance, loan interest)
- Schedule value of saved days: $1,500-$6,000
- Plus: earlier handoff to painters = further cascading schedule gains

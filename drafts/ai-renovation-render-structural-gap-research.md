# Research: AI Renovation Renders vs. Structural Reality

## Angle
AI renovation visualization tools let homeowners generate photorealistic kitchen/bathroom renders in seconds. But the renders don't know what's behind the wall — load-bearing structure, plumbing runs, electrical, moisture barriers, HVAC ductwork. Builders report growing numbers of AI-generated concepts that need substantial redesign. The gap between the render and the permit set is widening.

## Journalist
Elena Vasquez — Architecture & Design. This is about the seductive beauty of AI renders and the structural ignorance they hide.

## Novel Contribution
Mapping the specific categories of structural information that AI renovation renders cannot access: (1) load-bearing vs. partition walls, (2) plumbing stack locations and drain slopes, (3) electrical panel capacity and circuit routing, (4) HVAC ductwork paths, (5) moisture barriers and waterproofing, (6) fire blocking and code-required separations. Computing what percentage of kitchen renovation cost goes to items invisible in the render (rough estimate: 40-60% of a kitchen remodel cost is behind the walls).

## Kill Test
✅ Directly helps homeowners who use AI tools to plan renovations. Tells them what the render can't show, what to ask their contractor, and why the gap between the $5 render and the $50K build exists.

## Strongest Counterargument
Some tools (ReimagineHome.ai) explicitly frame themselves as communication tools between homeowner and contractor, not replacement for professional assessment. Used correctly, they can actually reduce misalignment. The problem is that most homeowners use them as if the render IS the plan.

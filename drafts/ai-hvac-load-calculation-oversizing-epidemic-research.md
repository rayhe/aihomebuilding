# Research: AI HVAC Load Calculation vs. The Oversizing Epidemic

## Thesis
Most residential HVAC contractors use rules of thumb (1 ton per 400-600 sqft) instead of Manual J load calculations. Data from Energy Vanguard shows proper calculations yield 1,431 sqft/ton average — meaning contractors routinely oversize systems by 2-3x. AI tools now automate Manual J in 60 seconds from a blueprint upload. What does this mean for homeowners building or replacing HVAC?

## Primary Sources

### 1. Energy Vanguard / Allison Bailes PhD (Green Building Advisor, 2016)
- 40 Manual J load calculations in hot/mixed climates
- Average result: 1,431 sqft/ton
- Range: 624 to 3,325 sqft/ton
- NOT A SINGLE calculation was as low as 600 sqft/ton (the high end of contractor rules of thumb)
- Mike MacFarland (Energy Docs, CA): gets 1,500 sqft/ton for retrofits, 3,350 sqft/ton for new zero energy home — at 102°F design temp
- "If you tell me that your load calculations average 400-600 sqft/ton, I assume you're not doing them correctly"
- URL: https://www.greenbuildingadvisor.com/article/manual-j-load-calculations-vs-rules-of-thumb

### 2. ACEEE / Building Science Corporation (2008 Study, Redding CA)
- Production homebuilder subdivision in hot-dry climate
- Standard practice sizing: 155-185% of Manual J load
- Resized group: 135-150% of Manual J load
- Resized group: longer average runtimes, same energy consumption, reduced peak demand
- Short-cycling from oversizing decreases capacity and increases consumption due to startup losses
- URL: https://www.aceee.org/files/proceedings/2008/data/papers/1_9.pdf

### 3. ACHR News — Heat Pump Oversizing (2023)
- "Oversizing is such an epidemic" — Reedie Ward, Comfort Services, NC
- Contractors scared to install properly sized systems, don't trust Manual J numbers
- Oversized systems short-cycle: hard on equipment, comfort complaints, reduced dehumidification
- Heat pump sales outpaced gas furnaces in US for first time in 2022
- URL: https://www.achrnews.com/articles/153869-oversizing-overcharging-chief-concerns-among-hvac-heat-pump-installs

### 4. ACCA Manual J 8th Edition (2016)
- National ANSI-recognized standard (ANSI/ACCA 2 Manual J)
- Required by national building codes and most jurisdictions
- CLF/CLTD method for residential load calculation
- Room-by-room calculations needed for proper airflow distribution
- URL: https://www.acca.org/standards/technical-manuals/manual-j

## Original Contribution (Novel Calculation)
**The Oversizing Tax:** For a 2,000 sqft home in a mixed climate:
- Manual J proper: ~1.4 tons (at 1,431 sqft/ton average)
- Rule of thumb: 4 tons (at 500 sqft/ton)
- Oversizing: 2.6 tons excess → $1,300-3,900 extra upfront equipment cost
- Each ton excess = ~$500-1,500 installed + higher energy bills from short-cycling
- BSC study: oversized systems at 155-185% of load = no energy benefit, just wasted capacity and peak demand
- Annual energy waste from short-cycling: 10-15% higher cooling costs (ACEEE estimates)
- For average US household ($2,000/yr energy, ~40% HVAC): 10-15% excess = $80-120/yr
- Over 15-year system life: $1,200-1,800 in wasted energy + $1,300-3,900 equipment premium = $2,500-5,700 total

## Kill Test
✅ Passes. If you're building a home or replacing HVAC: demand a Manual J calculation. AI tools now make it trivial. The oversizing epidemic costs you $2,500-5,700 over the system's life.

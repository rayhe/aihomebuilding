# Research: The Six-Hour Wait for a Five-Minute Rebar Inspection — AR Fix Nobody Uses

## Journalist
Jake "Jackhammer" Kowalski — Construction Technology

## Kill Test
✅ Anyone pouring a residential foundation waits for rebar inspection. Inspector scheduling is a top-3 residential construction delay. This directly affects builders, homeowners, and construction timelines.

## Primary Sources

### 1. Sanei & Moreu — AR-Assisted Rebar Inspection (arXiv:2604.26112, April 2026)
- 30-participant within-subjects experiment, HoloLens 2
- Task completion time reduced 67.7%
- Trunk flexion reduced 30.8%, neck flexion 32.8%
- Walking distance and hand-path length decreased 50%+
- NASA TLX decreased 45.6%
- SUS score: 76.1/100 (83% rated acceptable)

### 2. DroneDeploy / Juneau Construction Case Study
- Society Atlanta: 31-story mixed-use
- Drone capture: 10 minutes/pour + 2-4 hour processing
- Saved $40,000 on one project
- Freed ~10 man-hours per report

## Novel Contribution
**Throughput multiplier calculation:** 5-minute inspection × 67.7% reduction = 1.6 minutes. Inspector doing 5-7 residential foundations/day → 15-21 with AR. At California's 110K-200K annual inspection volume, 30% inspector retirement in 5 years means the math breaks. AR doesn't replace inspectors — it makes the ones who remain cover 3× the territory. The $3,500 headset amortized over 15 inspections/day × 250 working days = $0.93/inspection. Nobody has published this per-inspection amortization.

## Strongest Counterargument
A HoloLens 2 costs $3,500. Residential foundation inspection takes 5-15 minutes. No residential inspector is going to strap on a headset for that — the per-inspection ROI doesn't justify it for a solo inspector doing 5-7 sites. The math only works at scale, and residential inspection is fragmented across thousands of small municipal departments.

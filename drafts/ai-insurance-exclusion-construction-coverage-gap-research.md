# Research Notes: AI Insurance Exclusions in Construction

## Angle
Your architect uses AI to check building codes. Your builder uses AI to estimate costs. As of January 2026, their insurance policies may explicitly exclude AI-related errors. If something goes wrong, nobody's insurance covers it.

## Journalist: Catherine "Code" Chen — Policy & Regulation beat

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — homeowners need to know that the professionals they hire may carry insurance that won't cover AI-driven mistakes, and they need to ask the right questions before signing.

## Key Findings

### The ISO Exclusion (Primary Source)
- Insurance Services Office (ISO) released **Generative AI Exclusion (CG 40 47 01 26)** — optional endorsement for commercial general liability (CGL) policies
- Defines "generative artificial intelligence" as "a machine-based learning system or model that is trained on data with the ability to create content or responses, including but not limited to text, images, audio, video, or code"
- Applies to **both Coverage A (bodily injury/property damage) AND Coverage B (personal/advertising injury)**
- ISO forms underpin **approximately 82% of U.S. property and casualty insurance policies**
- Source: Cohen Seglias Pallas Greenhall & Furman PC, "New Generative AI Insurance Exclusion: What the Construction Industry Needs to Know" (April 20, 2026)

### Verisk Standardized Exclusion Forms (Primary Source)
- Verisk released standardized AI exclusion forms **CG 40 47 and CG 40 48**, effective **January 1, 2026**
- Give insurers ready-made language to exclude losses from generative AI use
- Source: DailyMoss/Risk Specialty Group reporting (January 2026)

### Carrier-Specific Actions (Primary Sources)
- **Berkley**: Introduced "absolute" AI exclusion for D&O, E&O, and Fiduciary Liability policies. Specifically names ChatGPT, Bard, Midjourney, DALL-E
- **AIG**: Adding AI exclusion clauses to professional liability policies
- **Philadelphia Insurance**: Already excluded AI-related claims from E&O coverage
- **Hamilton Select**: Already excluded AI-related claims from E&O coverage
- Source: Multiple insurance industry publications (January 2026)

### AI Error Rates (Primary Sources)
- Stanford Law School research: general-purpose AI tools produce incorrect outputs at rates between **58% and 88%**
- Specialized AI tools designed for professional use: error rates of **20-33%**
- Source: Referenced in Risk Specialty Group analysis

### Construction-Specific Impact
- **Flawed AI-generated structural calculation** → design defects → bodily injury/property damage → NOW EXCLUDED from CGL
- **Hallucinated building specification** → construction delays → claims → NOW EXCLUDED
- **Erroneous project schedule** → cost overruns → disputes → coverage uncertain
- Small and mid-sized construction businesses face **heightened exposure to third-party claims**
- Source: Cohen Seglias analysis (April 2026)

### AIA/Industry Adoption Data
- AIA research: only **8% of firm leaders** report full AI integration
- 20% implementing, 35% considering, rest not using
- **Large firms: 61% adoption**, midsize: 42%, small: 27%
- Architects **under 35**: 66% likely to use AI image generators vs 41% of those over 50
- **One-third of architecture firms** now use AI in daily operations
- AIA Trust (February 2025): identified AI risks including competence, confidentiality, standard of care
- Source: AIA research via Risk Specialty Group

### HCR Law International Perspective (Primary Source)
- UK: JCT and NEC standard forms have NO AI provisions — "contractual blind spot"
- "Traditional professional indemnity and construction insurance policies may not respond to AI related losses"
- "AI driven errors may expose parties to uninsured risk"
- Source: HCR Law, "Artificial Intelligence and Construction Contracts" (May 12, 2026)

### Construction Insurance Market Context
- $48B projected global market for AI in construction by 2030
- Construction PI market already strained from cladding/fire safety exclusions (Grenfell)
- Source: Area Development, Lockton market snapshot

## Original Analysis / Novel Contribution
Cross-reference the ISO CG 40 47 exclusion with actual AI adoption rates in construction. Calculate: if 82% of CGL policies will likely adopt this exclusion, and one-third of architecture firms use AI daily, then roughly 27% of all architecture firms are simultaneously using AI AND likely to face exclusions at next renewal. For small firms (27% AI adoption), this creates a time bomb — they're adopting AI precisely when coverage is being pulled.

## Strongest Counterargument
The exclusion is OPTIONAL. Carriers can choose not to adopt it. Some specialty insurers may see a market opportunity in covering AI risks at a premium. The insurance market could self-correct. But the trend is clearly toward exclusion, not inclusion.

## Limitations
- We can't confirm exactly how many carriers have adopted CG 40 47 since January 2026
- The 82% ISO figure is for all P&C policies, not construction-specific
- No construction-specific claims data exists yet for AI-related losses
- We don't know what AI-specific coverage products (if any) are being developed

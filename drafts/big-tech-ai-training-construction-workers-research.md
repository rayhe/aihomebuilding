# Research: Big Tech AI Training for Construction Workers

## Core Data Points

### NABTU + Microsoft (April 21, 2026)
- 1,500 instructors already trained in hands-on training centers
- Launching no-cost AI literacy courses + industry-recognized credentials
- Extends to TradesFutures (501c3) pre-apprenticeship readiness in 34 states
- NABTU: 3M+ workers, 14 affiliates, 1,900 training centers (1,600 US), $2B/yr training investment
- NABTU trains 71% of all construction apprentices
- "AI can't turn wrenches and it's not going to lay bricks" — Tom Kriger, NABTU research director
- Apprentices already informally using AI for code interpretation, regulation tracking
- Aligned to Microsoft's Community-First AI Infrastructure commitments
- LinkedIn Learning delivery platform
- Source: Microsoft news release, Construction Dive

### NABTU + OpenAI (March 2026)
- Partnership to train next generation of skilled construction workers
- TradesFutures: 270+ Apprenticeship Readiness Programs in 34 states
- OpenAI committed to supporting TradesFutures to expand training + recruitment
- Part of OpenAI's infrastructure play: Stargate project, 7 GW planned capacity, $400B investment
- OpenAI Certifications and Jobs Platform launching 2026 near Stargate sites (Abilene TX etc.)
- Chris Lehane (OpenAI): "predistribute the economic benefits of the Intelligence Age"
- Source: NABTU press release

### BlackRock Future Builders (June/July 2026)
- $100M over 5 years to train 50,000 skilled trades workers
- Targets: electricians, welders, plumbers, HVAC techs, wiremen
- Larry Fink: US needs $10 trillion in data centers and energy infrastructure
- John Kelly (BlackRock): "real urgent need to build infrastructure... to compete in technology"
- Philanthropy arm, not investment
- Source: NY Post

### OpenAI OSTP Submission
- US needs "20 percent of its current skilled trades workforce over the next five years"
- Specifically: electricians, mechanics, metal/ironworkers, carpenters, plumbers
- Stargate: 6 data centers across TX, NM, OH, WI — $400B+ investment
- Source: TechRepublic

### DEWALT/Stanley Black & Decker Study (April 2026)
- 90% believe AI will be indispensable within 5 years
- Only 8% currently using AI day-to-day
- 88% expect AI use to increase within next year
- 87% say AI education should be in trade schools/technical programs
- 86% feel "at least somewhat prepared" for AI
- Most rely on YouTube and online courses for AI learning
- 59% want hands-on, task-based training
- DEWALT + Associated Builders and Contractors (ABC) pilot training program launched
- Bill Beck (SBD president): "need for practical AI training has never been more important"
- Source: themanufacturer.com

### Skills England Report (Oct 2025)
- Construction is "least digitally mature" sector
- 65% of UK construction workers unable to complete 20 essential digital tasks (Lloyds Bank 2024)
- "Most existing AI training is not tailored to construction"
- AI uptake constrained by "workforce digital literacy and limited integration into vocational pathways"
- Source: Construction News UK

### ServiceTitan 2026 Report
- 74% of residential contractors view AI as efficiency engine
- Only 25% currently using AI
- 48% of early adopters report increased productivity
- 45% report time savings
- 73% believe starting early creates competitive advantage
- Source: GlobeNewsWire

### VR Training
- ITI/Interplay Learning: enhanced VR crane simulator, 10 crane types, 1,200+ scenarios
- CONEXPO finalist (out of 230 submissions)
- SafeXtend (UK): AI + 3D scanning → VR training environments, 13-15 min personalized sessions
- HTC VIVE + OutHere + Skanska: "Step Into Safety" VR behavioral change program
- Source: multiple

## Original Contribution Calculation

**Per-worker investment math:**
- BlackRock: $100M / 50,000 target workers = $2,000/worker (solid)
- But vs. total US construction workforce: 11.4M (BLS) → $100M / 11.4M = $8.77/worker
- vs. NABTU's own: 3M members → $100M / 3M = $33.33/worker
- NABTU already invests ~$2B/yr in training → $2B / 3M = $667/worker/year
- BlackRock's $100M over 5 YEARS = $20M/year → 1% of NABTU's existing training budget
- Microsoft and OpenAI: non-monetary (training programs, credentials, platform access)

**The gap math:**
- 90% believe AI important × 8% using it = 82 percentage point gap
- 87% want trade school AI education × ~0% of trade schools offering it = total disconnect
- 11.4M US construction workers × 8% adoption = ~912K currently using AI
- 11.4M × 20% (OpenAI's ask) = 2.28M workers needed just for data centers
- Total construction labor shortage: ~500K (NAHB ongoing)

## Kill Test
"Does this help someone building or buying a home?"
YES — the construction labor shortage directly affects home build timelines and costs. AI training programs could make workers more productive (48% say so per ServiceTitan). But the uncomfortable truth is these Big Tech investments are aimed at data center construction, not housing. The residential builder gets the spillover, maybe.

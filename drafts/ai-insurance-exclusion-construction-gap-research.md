# Research: AI Insurance Exclusion Creates Coverage Gap for Residential Construction

## Journalist: Jake "Jackhammer" Kowalski (Construction Technology)

## Angle
Insurance industry has quietly added AI exclusions to the same CGL policies that protect residential builders. The ISO forms (CG 40 47 and CG 40 48) became effective January 1, 2026 — and since ISO forms underpin 82% of US property and casualty policies, the adoption will be widespread. Meanwhile, 25% of residential contractors are already using AI meaningfully. The coverage gap is growing in both directions: more AI use + less AI coverage = exposed builders and homeowners.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** If your builder, architect, or engineer uses AI tools and something goes wrong — a structural miscalculation, a hallucinated spec, a code compliance error — the insurance that's supposed to protect you may now exclude AI-related claims entirely. This directly affects homeowners, contractors, and design professionals.

## Primary Sources

### 1. ISO/Verisk Standardized AI Exclusion Forms
- **CG 40 47** and **CG 40 48** — Generative AI Exclusion endorsements
- Effective: **January 1, 2026**
- Optional endorsement to commercial general liability (CGL) policies
- Defines generative AI as: "a machine-based learning system or model that is trained on data with the ability to create content or responses, including but not limited to text, images, audio, video, or code"
- Applies to **Coverage A** (bodily injury and property damage) AND **Coverage B** (personal and advertising injury)
- Source: Cohen Seglias Pallas Greenhall & Furman PC, "New Generative AI Insurance Exclusion: What the Construction Industry Needs to Know" (cohenseglias.com)

### 2. Adoption Scale
- **ISO forms underpin approximately 82% of US property and casualty insurance policies** — widespread adoption expected at renewal periods
- **18 largest US liability insurers** removing AI coverage from policies, with **80-100% regulatory approval rates** (LinkedIn analysis by Joel Morales)
- Major carriers filing for exclusions: **AIG, Great American, WR Berkley** (Lexology/Jones Day analysis)
- Berkley's "Artificial Intelligence Exclusion (Absolute)" for D&O and E&O policies — even broader than ISO, covering loss "based on, arising out of, or attributable to" AI use
- Source: Mondaq/Jones Day, "A-Eye On Coverage: Maximizing Insurance For AI Risks Amid Emerging Exclusions"

### 3. Construction AI Adoption Data
- **25% of residential contractors** using AI meaningfully (ServiceTitan 2026 Residential State of the Trades report, 1,000+ contractors surveyed)
- **50% of contractors lack trust in AI** — but adoption is growing regardless
- **1/3 of architecture firms** incorporate AI tools in daily operations (AIA, March 2025)
- Large firms lead at **61%** adoption, small firms at **27%**
- Generational divide: architects **under 35 are 66% likely** to use AI image generators vs **41% of those over 50**
- Source: HousingWire, ServiceTitan report; AIA Trust

### 4. AI Error Rates in Practice
- **Stanford Law School**: general-purpose AI tools produce hallucinations at rates between **58% and 88%**
- Even specialized "legal-grade" AI tools show error rates of **20-33%**
- **Mata v. Avianca** case: lawyer sanctioned for citing fake AI-generated cases — precedent for AI-generated errors being treated as practitioner negligence
- Source: San Diego Union-Tribune / Risk Specialty Group analysis

### 5. Real-World Construction Risk Scenarios
- AI-generated structural calculations that violate building codes
- Specifications for nonexistent materials (hallucinated product numbers, specs)
- Erroneous project schedules leading to delays and cost overruns
- AI plan review missing code violations
- AI cost estimates that mislead project budgeting
- Source: Cohen Seglias; Multi-Housing News (Segal McCambridge attorneys)

### 6. Industry Context
- AI construction market projected to reach **$24.3 billion by 2030** (Mordor Intelligence)
- DeWalt study: **90% of construction professionals** expect AI to be indispensable within 5 years, yet only **8% currently use it**
- **73% of customers** cite clear upfront pricing as primary reason for choosing contractor — AI estimating tools are directly answering this demand
- Home Depot expanded pro digital platform with AI-driven estimating tools
- Source: HousingWire; BusinessJournalDaily/DeWalt

### 7. Legal Analysis — What Builders Should Do
- Review existing insurance programs with agent/broker at renewal
- Negotiate to eliminate or narrow AI exclusions
- Ask about "carve-outs" for incidental AI use
- Demand clear definitions of "AI," "generative AI," and "use" in policies
- Consider standalone AI coverage products (emerging market)
- Establish AI usage protocols: approved tools, human review checkpoints, logging
- Source: Jones Day; Cohen Seglias

## Novel Contribution
No one has calculated the collision between the 25% residential contractor AI adoption rate and the 82% ISO form adoption rate to quantify the exposure gap for homeowners. The typical homeowner has no visibility into whether their builder's CGL policy now excludes AI-related claims, yet their builder may be using AI for the most consequential parts of the project (structural calcs, code compliance, cost estimates).

## Strongest Counterargument
The exclusion is optional — not all carriers will adopt it, and sophisticated builders can negotiate it away. Early-stage AI adoption is still low enough (25% residential, 8% using it deeply per DeWalt) that the practical exposure remains small. The market will likely develop standalone AI coverage products that fill the gap, much as cyber insurance emerged to cover cyber risks that were excluded from traditional policies.

## Limitations
- We don't have data on how many policies have already been renewed with the AI exclusion attached since Jan 1, 2026
- The 82% figure refers to ISO form usage broadly; actual adoption of this specific optional endorsement may be lower
- Residential-specific data is thin — most insurance analysis focuses on commercial/multi-family construction
- We don't know the premium impact of standalone AI coverage products (still emerging)

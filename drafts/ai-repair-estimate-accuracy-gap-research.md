# Research: AI Repair Estimate Accuracy Gap

## Angle
AI tools are flooding the home-buying process with instant repair cost estimates. The numbers they produce don't match what contractors actually charge — and nobody is talking about the gap.

## Core Tension
- **OfferWise** (15,000+ inspection reports processed): average repair cost estimate = **$63,688**
- **Real-world buyer negotiation average**: **$14,000** off sale price for inspection-related repairs (AZ Big Media / LLCBuddy 2025 data)
- That's a **4.5x gap**. Why?

## Kill Test
✅ Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — directly actionable for any homebuyer trying to decide whether to trust an AI repair estimate, how to negotiate, and when to get real contractor quotes instead.

## Why the 4.5x Gap?
Hypotheses to investigate/present:
1. **AI flags everything** — cosmetic, deferred maintenance, "nice to have" — inflating totals
2. **Buyers cherry-pick** — negotiate only structural/safety items, not cosmetics
3. **Contractor competition** — real quotes come in lower because contractors bid against each other
4. **Market variance** — OfferWise may skew toward expensive coastal markets
5. **Scope definition** — AI estimates "full replacement" while contractors quote "repair"
6. **Timeline discounting** — some flagged items are 5-10 year problems, not immediate

## Original Contribution (Required)
The 4.5x gap calculation between AI estimate averages and real-world negotiation averages. Nobody has explicitly surfaced this disconnect and asked what it means for buyers.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI overestimation might actually be GOOD for buyers — it creates negotiating ammunition. If the AI says $63K and you negotiate $14K off, you "won" even though the AI was wildly wrong. The inflated number serves as an anchor in negotiations.

## Limitations
- OfferWise's $63,688 is a total repair estimate, not what buyers are expected to negotiate — includes deferred maintenance and cosmetic items
- The $14,000 average is across all inspections including those with minimal findings
- No head-to-head accuracy study exists comparing AI estimates to actual contractor invoices
- Geographic variation is enormous — Bay Area AI estimates vs. rural Alabama are apples and oranges
- Small sample sizes on some tools (Binsr has only 50 users)

# Research: AI Permit Review — Cities Are Automating Plan Review. Who's Liable When the AI Misses a Code Violation?

**Journalist:** Catherine "Code" Chen (Policy & Regulation)
**Date:** 2026-07-16

## Core Angle
AI-powered building permit review is spreading rapidly across US cities. The speed gains are real and documented. But the liability question — when an AI prescreen clears a plan that later turns out to break code, who is on the hook? — remains largely unresolved. This is the policy story behind the tech story.

## Primary Sources & Data

### Honolulu CivCheck (Clariti)
- Launched December 2025 after 5-month pilot
- Cut reviewer time per residential plan by 70% (60-90 min → 15-20 min)
- Applications reach permit decision 55% faster: **32.5 days vs 73 days** for non-CivCheck
- Corrections per permit dropped 67%: **23.5 → 7.7 corrections**
- Prescreening backlog: **6 months → ~1 week**
- Going mandatory later in 2026
- Priority Review program incentivizes early adoption
- Free to applicants (residential only for now, commercial mid-2026)
- ~20,000 building permit applications/year, only ~1,400 new housing units authorized/year
- Source: Honolulu DPP official pages, GovTech Jan 2026, StateScoop Jun 2026, Clariti press release May 2026

### Naples, FL (Blitz AI + CityView)
- First in Florida to use Blitz AI for plan review
- Integration with CityView permitting system
- Mayor Teresa Heitmann quote about "innovation, efficiency, and service excellence"
- Source: HousingWire, Apr 2026

### AutoReview.AI (University of Florida)
- 10+ years of R&D at UF College of Design, Construction and Planning
- Based on entire Florida Building Code (~800 pages)
- Customers: Gainesville, Pasco County, Altamonte Springs
- Altamonte Springs: "AI can do it in as little as 30 minutes" vs "few weeks" for staff
- Uses NLP for ambiguous/vague code language interpretation
- Patent-pending technology
- Source: UF Warrington, Feb 2026

### Other City Deployments
- **Austin:** Archistar (Australia-based), adopted Oct 2024 for single-family permits
- **Los Angeles/LA County:** Archistar, launched Apr 30 2025 (post-wildfire rebuilding)
- **Seattle:** Mayor Harrell EO 2025-05 (Jun 2025), AI pilot, careful language: "designed to aide though not supplant human acumen"
- **Denver:** Selected Clariti for building permitting modernization (May 2026)
- **Calgary:** CivCheck pilot underway
- Source: CB Insights Jun 2026, various

### Baseline Permitting Problem
- Orlando avg review time: 2.5+ months
- Florida state law: municipalities must approve/deny within 120-180 days
- Survey of FL building professionals (2019): top delay causes = high volume, staff shortages, plan complexity, many corrections
- Most frequently violated code sections: means of egress, fire resistance, glazing, roof ventilation

### LLM-QueryBC (Academic)
- ASCE Journal of Construction Engineering, Vol 152 No 6
- AI system for natural language building code queries
- 24% better accuracy (text), 25% better (tables) vs existing methods
- 10-second response time for complex compliance questions
- Validated on 15-story mixed-use case study

## Key Tension: Liability
- CB Insights (Jun 14, 2026): "When an AI prescreen waves through a design that later turns out to break code, who is on the hook? The answer is less alarming than it first sounds, and more unsettled than the cities adopting these tools have publicly worked out."
- CivCheck: "designed to augment plan reviewers, not replace them"
- Honolulu DPP: CivCheck "does not replace the DPP's official review, nor does it guarantee permit approval"
- Seattle EO: "designed to aide though not supplant human acumen"
- Current legal framework: building officials personally certify code compliance. AI is positioned as pre-check tool, not the decision-maker.
- But as backlogs shrink and AI proves faster, political pressure to make AI the de facto reviewer increases.

## Strongest Counterargument
Current plan reviewers already miss things — human error rate in code compliance isn't zero. If AI catches 67% more errors upfront (Honolulu data), it may actually reduce liability exposure even if it occasionally misses something. The real risk isn't AI making mistakes; it's AI making *fast* mistakes at scale that take longer to discover.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES. If you're filing a residential permit, several cities now offer (or will soon require) AI pre-review that cuts your review time by 50-70%. Know which cities, what tools, what it covers, and what it doesn't guarantee.

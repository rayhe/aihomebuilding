# Research Notes: AI Training Language & Digital Access Gap in Construction

## Core Data Points

### Hispanic/Latino Workers in Construction (BLS 2025)
- 3,544,000 Hispanic/Latino workers in construction and extraction occupations
- Total US construction workforce: ~11.5M
- Hispanic/Latino share: ~30-33% of construction workforce
- Foreign-born workers: 19.1% of total US civilian labor force
- Median weekly earnings: foreign-born $1,059 vs native-born $1,236

### Language Gap (ACTFL, OSHA, NIOSH)
- Construction has LARGEST foreign language skills gap of all US industries (ACTFL report)
- 40% of contractors report "huge gap" in foreign language skills on jobsites
- 53% expect demand for foreign language skills to increase in next 5 years
- OSHA: Employers must present training "in a manner that employees are able to understand"
- NIOSH/ASSE study (Saf Sci 103:62-69): Smaller construction firms less likely to employ supervisors who speak workers' language (p < .001), provide fewer hours of safety training to non-native workers
- OSHA publishes materials in Spanish but has no English-only requirement

### Education/Literacy (BLS ORS 2025)
- 49.3% of construction workers: NO minimum education required
- 49.6%: high school diploma required
- 71.4%: on-the-job training required
- Only 1.1% can telework

### Digital Adoption
- JBKnowledge Construction Technology Report: 90% of construction workers use smartphones on-site
- 68% of contractors use smartphones in workday
- 49% use mobile apps for safety
- Smart Market Report: only 47-71% of construction businesses in wealthy nations have digital solutions
- Contractor Training Center survey (2025, n=683): 43% adopted construction management apps, 32% using AI tools
- 43% of contractors say it's difficult to find time to implement/train on new tech
- 38% cite employee resistance to technology

### AI Training Initiatives (2026)
- NABTU + Microsoft (April 21, 2026): 
  - Already trained 1,500 instructors
  - Launching free AI literacy courses + industry-recognized credentials
  - Delivered via LinkedIn Learning
  - TradesFutures extension: 34 states, union apprenticeship programs
  - Focus: AI literacy, data security, practical applications
  - Use cases: code compliance, safety updates, lesson plans
- DOL AI in Registered Apprenticeship Innovation Portal (May 2026):
  - Resources for AI training in apprenticeships
  - AI Literacy Framework: core competencies
  - "Make America AI-Ready": FREE text-message-based AI course for workers WITHOUT reliable internet
  - Construction, healthcare, manufacturing, finance sectors
- Hawaiian Dredging (HDCC): Uses Alice Technologies to capture retiring estimators' knowledge as AI "recipes"

### The Gap (Original Calculation)
- ~3.5M Hispanic/Latino construction workers
- NABTU represents ~3M workers in 14 unions
- Microsoft/LinkedIn Learning delivery: requires English literacy, internet access, digital fluency
- DOL "Make America AI-Ready" text-message course: addresses internet gap but still English-only
- ServiceTitan 2026: only 25% of residential contractors meaningfully using AI
- Harvard JCHS: >50% of residential remodeling businesses generate <$250K/year (too small for tech investment)

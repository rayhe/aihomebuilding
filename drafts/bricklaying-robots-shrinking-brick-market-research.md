# Research: Bricklaying Robots vs. The Shrinking Brick Market

## Angle
Three startups have built robots that can lay bricks at 800-1,000 per hour. The US residential market has been quietly abandoning brick for decades. Only 19% of new homes use brick as the primary exterior material. The robots are racing to automate a trade the industry is already routing around by choosing different materials.

## Kill Test
**PASS.** If you're building a home and considering brick exterior, should you wait for robots to bring costs down? If you're a mason, is your trade being disrupted — or is it already being disrupted by material substitution? Actionable for homeowners choosing exteriors and contractors evaluating whether to invest in masonry crews.

- 294,300 masonry workers employed (2024)
- Median wage: $56,600/year ($27.21/hr)
- Projected growth 2024-2034: 2% (slower than average)
- Most openings from retirements/exits, not new demand
- Source: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/Construction-and-extraction/brickmasons-blockmasons-and-stonemasons.htm

### 3. FBR Ltd (Hadrian X) — ASX:FBR
- Founded in Perth, Australia; created Hadrian X bricklaying robot
- Claims 1,000 bricks/hour vs human 300-500/day
- First US demo home completed Sept 2024 (CRH Ventures partnership)
- Recent: AUD 400-450K contract with AMOVEO for 5,400 sqm wall over 24 months
- MOU for ~200 homes over 12 months (non-binding)
- Critical: "material going-concern uncertainty due to cash burn" in financial disclosures
- Hadrian X built a 3BR/2BA structure "in under three days" (Nov 2018) — but that was 8 years ago and commercialization is still "scaling"
- Source: https://www.fbr.com.au; TradingView analysis; Wikipedia

### 4. Buildroid AI — San Francisco startup
- $2M pre-seed (Tim Draper), emerged from stealth Nov 2025
- Uses NVIDIA Omniverse digital twin simulations before deploying robots
- BLR (block-laying robot) handles blocks up to 40kg, walls up to 4m wide × 3m tall
- Targets blockwork and partition walls — "$13B segment" 
- Shared-savings model: Buildroid takes 50% of net efficiency gains
- Compatible with 40+ robot types; BIM ingestion via Autodesk Revit plugin
- Plans Q1 2026 commercial implementations — ZERO completed buildings to date
- Source: ENR, TheRobotReport (Jan 2026)

### 5. Monumental — Amsterdam, Netherlands
- Founded 2021 by Salar al Khafaji (ex-Silk/Palantir)
- $25M funding (Plural, Hummingbird, Northzone, Foundamental)
- Specializes in red clay brick (vs Hadrian's concrete blocks)
- Pilots in Netherlands including 15m office exterior, partnerships with 25 contractors
- Focus includes low-income housing
- Autonomous cart + mortar-spreading robot + brick-placing robot
- Source: TechCrunch (Feb 2024)

### 6. SAM100 (Construction Robotics) — Legacy system
- $500K per unit; 800-1,200 bricks/day vs human 300-500/day
- Requires human helper (corners, mortar cleanup, aesthetics)
- "Best suited for large swaths of flat walls" — commercial, not residential
- Source: MIT Technology Review (2015)

## Key Tensions

1. **Veneer vs. Structural**: Almost all US residential brick is veneer — a thin cosmetic layer over wood framing. These robots are designed for structural block/masonry. Different task, different economics.

2. **The Addressable Market Is Shrinking**: Brick went from dominant residential material to 19% nationally. Vinyl, fiber cement, and stucco gained. The trend continues.

3. **Commercial vs. Residential Mismatch**: SAM100 explicitly needs "large flat walls" (hospitals, universities). Residential facades have windows, corners, gables, dormers — all require human finesse. Even Buildroid's BLR does walls "up to 4m wide" — a single residential facade section.

4. **Graveyard Context**: Katerra ($2B raised, bankrupt 2021), Veev ($647M, failed). Construction tech has a body count. FBR's "going concern" warning echoes these patterns.

5. **Economics Don't Pencil for Residential**: At 19% brick share, ~1.35M single-family starts = ~257K brick homes/year. Robot systems cost $500K+ and need volume. A typical brick veneer house has maybe 1,500-2,500 sq ft of exterior wall. The setup/teardown for each custom home design eats the throughput advantage.

## Original Calculation
- At $27.21/hr median masonry wage × 8 hours × crew of 3 × ~5 days for a typical 2,000 sqft exterior = ~$3,270 labor cost for brick veneer
- Material: ~$6-12/sqft installed, so a 2,000 sqft exterior = $12,000-24,000 total
- Labor is only 25-35% of installed brick cost
- Even if robots cut labor to zero, savings are $3-5K on a $300K+ home — 1-1.5%
- Material cost (bricks, mortar, flashing, weep holes) stays the same regardless of who lays them

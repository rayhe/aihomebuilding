# Research: Plumbing Pipe Oversizing — Hunter's Curve vs. Modern Water Demand Tools

## Core Thesis
Every new home in America has oversized plumbing pipes because the formula plumbers use to size them — Hunter's Curve — was developed in the 1940s for fixtures that consumed 2-5x more water than today's code-mandated low-flow products. Two computational tools (IAPMO WDC, ICC DRIPS) use real usage data to right-size pipes, saving $2,000-$5,000 per single-family home, reducing Legionella risk, and delivering hot water faster.

## Key Data Points

### Hunter's Curve (the problem)
- Developed 1940s by Roy B. Hunter, physicist at National Bureau of Standards (now NIST)
- Uses "fixture units" — assigns fixed values regardless of fixture efficiency
- Assumes simultaneous use based on 99th percentile binomial distribution
- Energy Policy Act of 1992 cut flow rates 50%+: toilets 3.5→1.6 gpf, faucets 4→2.2 gpm, showerheads 5→2.5 gpm
- Pipe sizing formulas never updated to match reduced flows
- Studies over two decades: overestimates peak demand by 5-27x actual observed flow (PMM Magazine CE, multiple flow monitoring studies)
- Systems designed per Hunter's Curve see maximum demands at 10-20% of theoretical peak
- Both UPC and IPC still use Hunter's Curve principles
- Source: PMM Magazine, "Designing right-sized plumbing systems," 2025

### Consequences
- **Cost:** $2,000-$5,000 per 2,379 sq ft SFH (Stantec study, 3 markets: NYC, Pittsburgh, Oklahoma City). 45-unit MF: $100,000+
- **Meter fees:** In San Francisco, one pipe-size reduction saves $17,000 in connection fees ($26K→$9K). 60%+ of 50 fastest-growing metros charge >$1,500 for 1-inch meter connection (AWE study)
- **Health:** Oversized pipes → low velocity → stagnation → water aging → biofilm → Legionella. CDC confirms. EPA notes stagnant premise plumbing promotes pathogen growth
- **Hot water delay:** Bigger pipes = more cold water sitting between heater and tap. User waits, wastes water and energy
- Sources: JLC "Saving Water, Saving Cost"; ASPE Pipeline; CDC Legionella guidance; EPA premise plumbing

### IAPMO Water Demand Calculator (WDC)
- First update to pipe sizing in 80+ years
- Developed by IAPMO, ASPE, University of Cincinnati, Water Quality Research Foundation
- 1,000+ SFH study, 863,000 water use events, data loggers recording every 10 seconds
- Six fixture groups: faucets (75% of events, 21% volume), showers (<3% events, 24% volume), toilets (highest volume), clothes washers, dishwashers
- Up to 65% reduction in water demand estimates
- 1-to-3 pipe-size service line diameter reduction
- Free app (Android/iPhone) + Excel download
- Published as standalone IAPMO/UPC/Appendix M-2021
- Dan Cole, IAPMO Senior Director of Technical Services
- Source: Builder Online, JLC, PMM Magazine CE, ASPE Pipeline

### ICC DRIPS
- Design-Toolkit for Robust International Plumbing Systems
- Developed by ICC + University of Miami
- Advanced simulations modeling thousands of days of operation
- Regional data: geography, climate, cultural habits
- Auto-sizes piping networks, calculates pressure losses
- SPADE Guideline for standardized global data collection
- ICC 815 Standard for right-sizing
- Source: ICC Building Safety Journal

### Adoption Gap
- "The building industry tends toward slow and cautious adoption" — PMM
- Industry prioritizes "perceived robustness over demonstrated performance"
- Legacy spreadsheets, habit, perceived risk
- 80 years of code inertia
- Engineers fear insufficient water during peak demand

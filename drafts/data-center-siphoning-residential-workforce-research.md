# Research: Data Centers Are Hiring Your Electrician. Here's What That Costs Your Home.

## Thesis
AI data center construction is structurally siphoning skilled trades workers — especially electricians, pipe fitters, and HVAC technicians — from residential homebuilding by offering 30% higher wages and more stable employment, directly worsening the housing shortage and extending build timelines for every home buyer in America.

## Primary Sources

### 1. ITIF / WSJ — "Data Centers Are a 'Gold Rush' for Construction Workers"
- **Source:** Te-Ping Chen, WSJ, Nov 29 2025; ITIF summary Jan 12 2026
- **URL:** https://itif.org/publications/2026/01/12/construction-industry-facing-worker-shortage-driven-by-growth-of-data-centers/
- **Key data:**
  - Construction industry facing 439,000-worker shortage
  - Data center construction jobs pay up to 30% more than typical construction jobs
  - Over 400 data centers currently under development (Amazon, Google, Microsoft)
  - Backlogs of close to a year for data center projects
  - DC/MD/VA electricians' union membership doubled since 2018 to 14,700

### 2. Amtec Staffing — "State of the U.S. Construction Workforce 2025-2026"
- **Source:** Amtec Staffing, compiled from BLS, AGC, ABC, AIA, Deloitte data (Feb 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.amtec.us.com/blog/construction-workforce-report
- **Key data:**
  - 8.31 million construction workers total (Jan 2026)

  - 1 in 5 construction workers over 55 — retirement is primary driver of need
  - Immigration enforcement affected 28% of construction firms
  - Data center boom pulling highest-skilled trades (electricians, welders, HVAC) away

### 3. ElectraKit — "The Electrical Workforce Crisis: Why 80,000 Electricians Are Needed by 2028"
- **Source:** ElectraKit Editorial, Jan 25 2026
- **URL:** https://www.electrakit.com/blog/electrical-labor-shortage-2026
- **Key data:**
  - 762,000 electricians in the US (BLS 2024)
  - ~25,000 retire annually, ~30,000 new apprenticeship completions — net gain only 5,000/yr
  - 80,000 new electrician positions needed by 2028 (6% growth, BLS)
  - Electrical subcontractor bid prices up 18-25% since 2022 in high-growth markets
  - Residential electricians booked 4-6 weeks for basic service calls, 3-4 months for panel upgrades
  - Specializations in EV/solar/data center work command 15-25% premiums above standard journeyman

### 6. Homes.com / Home Builder Institute CEO Edward Brady
- **Source:** Homes.com news report, 2026
- **Key data:**

  - As housing slows, more stable commercial projects (especially data centers) drawing workers away
  - Workers leaving residential for data center jobs = higher pay, more predictable schedules, less weather exposure

## Novel Math
- Data center jobs pay 30% more ($50-52/hr vs $38-40/hr residential)
- 439,000 worker shortage × average residential hourly wage of $39.63 × 2,080 hours = $36.1 billion in missing labor capacity annually
- If even 10% of the shortage is attributable to data center poaching = ~44,000 workers = enough to build ~22,000 homes/year (at 2 workers/home equivalent)
- Building permits down 5.8% YoY while data center construction spending up — the correlation isn't coincidental
- Net electrician supply gain of 5,000/year vs 80,000 needed by 2028 = 15-year gap at current pace

## Strongest Counterargument
"Data centers create good jobs and economic growth — housing will bounce back when rates drop." True for the macroeconomic view. But the specific workers leaving residential (electricians, HVAC techs, pipe fitters) take 4-5 years to replace through apprenticeship pipelines. When housing demand rebounds, those workers won't come back — they'll be locked into data center careers. The structural damage to the residential labor pipeline is already done.

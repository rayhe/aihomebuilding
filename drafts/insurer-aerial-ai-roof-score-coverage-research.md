# Research: Insurer Aerial AI Roof Scoring

## Topic
Insurers are using AI-powered aerial imagery (satellites, drones, fixed-wing aircraft) to score residential roofs and make underwriting decisions — canceling, non-renewing, or demanding costly repairs — often without the homeowner's knowledge. The new-construction angle: even brand-new roofs can get flagged by AI that mistakes shadows, architectural features, or dark materials for damage. This creates a new, invisible design constraint for builders.

## Primary Sources

### 1. CAPE Analytics (Moody's company)
- Roof Condition Rating v5 launched Feb 2025
- 300+ approved rating/underwriting filings across 40+ states
- 100+ carrier customers
- 110 million buildings covered, 98% U.S. coverage
- Severe/poor condition roofs: 2.5x wind/hail claims frequency vs excellent roofs
- Homes with heavy tree overhang: 90% higher wind-related losses
- Supports multiple imagery vendors (Nearmap, etc.)
- Source: https://capeanalytics.com (multiple press releases)

### 2. Eagleview
- 3 billion+ image library, 94% U.S. coverage
- 98.77% measurement accuracy (CompassData independent benchmark, Denver Metro, April-May 2025)
- 300+ patents, 25+ year history

- Integration with Verisk Xactimate (Feb 2025)
- Source: https://www.eagleview.com, Globe Newswire June 2025

### 3. California Assembly Bill 75 (Calderon)

- Must explain basis for adverse decision
- Homeowner gets until effective date to remedy
- Commissioner Lara: CDI investigated "numerous complaints where flawed aerial imagery led to wrongful cancellations"
- Passed Assembly Insurance Committee 15-0-2
- Source: CA Insurance Commissioner press release March 28, 2025; Legiscan AB75

## New-Construction Angle
- Roof material selection now has aerial AI insurability implications
- Dark asphalt shingles can show "wear" patterns that are actually normal granule variance
- Tree proximity/landscaping affects scoring (90% higher wind losses per CAPE)
- Architectural features (dormers, valleys, complex geometry) create shadows AI can misread
- New homes SHOULD score well but aren't immune to false positives
- A bad AI score on a brand-new home can affect sale, refinancing, insurance availability
- Builder recommendation: light-colored standing seam metal or composite with clean lines scores best
- Clear tree setback from structure affects CAPE tree overhang scoring

## Strongest Counterargument
Aerial AI has legitimate actuarial value: CAPE's data shows poor-condition roofs genuinely have 2.5x claims frequency. The technology identifies real risk that human inspections miss. The problem isn't the technology — it's the lack of homeowner transparency and dispute mechanisms. Legislation should require transparency and accuracy standards, not ban the practice.

## Original Contribution
Nobody has published an analysis of how aerial AI roof scoring creates a feedback loop into residential design decisions. This article connects the insurance underwriting technology to the building/buying decision: what the AI sees from above is becoming an invisible design constraint, and most builders don't know the scoring criteria exist.

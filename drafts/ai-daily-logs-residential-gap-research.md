# Research: AI Daily Logs — The Residential Gap

## Angle
AI daily reporting tools (Raken, Procore, Constructable, FieldWire) are saving commercial construction superintendents 5-8 hours/week of paperwork. But residential builders — the ones who need it most — barely use them. The adoption gap between commercial and residential is the story nobody writes about.

## Key Data Points

### The Paperwork Problem
- Construction professionals spend **35% of their time on non-productive activities** — manual data entry, error correction, dispute resolution, rework from bad documentation (Rhumbix/industry research)
- Field foremen spend **5-8 hours per week** on paperwork (Rhumbix 2026 analysis)
  - Daily field reports: 30-60 min/day (2.5-5 hrs/week)
  - Timecard collection/verification: 45-90 min/week
  - Safety documentation: 30-45 min/week
  - Material tracking/coordination: 30-60 min/week
  - Change orders & T&M documentation: 1-2 hrs/week
- **50% of construction companies** experience monthly payroll errors, 60% acknowledge multiple errors/month (industry studies)
- Direct cost of paperwork for mid-size contractor: **$260K-$820K annually** per superintendent at $95K-$140K salary + 30-40% loaded costs

### Construction Timelines
- Average single-family home: **9.1 months** from permit to completion (Census Bureau 2024 Survey of Construction)
  - Built for sale: 7.6 months
  - Contractor-built: 11.9 months
  - Owner-built: 15.5 months
- Regional variation: South 8.1 months, Northeast 13.5 months
- 2024 average is **12.3% longer than 2019** — timelines getting worse, not better

### Project Overruns
- Large construction projects run **20% longer than scheduled** and **80% over budget** (McKinsey)
- Industry-wide, lack of real-time progress data is root cause (Buildots 2023 study)

### AI Daily Log Tools — What Exists
- **Constructable** — auto-fills hourly weather by zip code, 9 log categories, delay tagging by cause, photos auto-organize by week, flat fee unlimited users
- **Procore** — enterprise, daily log with customizable fields, described as "click-heavy" and hard for field staff
- **Raken** — daily reports, time cards, toolbox talks, production tracking; built for field use
- **Autodesk Construction Cloud** — daily reporting from merged acquisitions, fragmented architecture
- **Buildertrend/CoConstruct** — residential-focused project management with daily log features
- Key feature: voice-to-text reporting from field, auto-weather, GPS photo tagging

### The Residential Gap
- Most AI construction tools target commercial ($5M+ projects) — pricing, complexity, and onboarding reflect this
- Residential builders (especially custom home) often run 3-10 projects simultaneously with skeleton crew
- Per-seat pricing models punish small firms: $399-$749/month for Procore vs. a custom home builder with 4 projects netting $40K-$60K margin each
- Buildertrend and CoConstruct have residential focus but AI features are minimal compared to commercial tools
- The superintendents who need documentation most (1-3 person PM teams running multiple homes) are least likely to have it
- NYC DOB approved digital record-keeping (BuildPass.ai 2025) — regulatory push toward digital, but residential lags

### The Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home?
**YES** — If you're a custom home builder running 5 projects with a PM and two supers, you're losing 10-16 hours/week combined on paperwork. That's a full day of supervision nobody is doing. AI daily logs could recover that time — but the tools either don't fit residential workflows or cost more than the margin on a single home. This article maps the actual landscape.

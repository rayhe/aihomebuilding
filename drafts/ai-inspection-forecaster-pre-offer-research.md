# Research: AI Inspection Forecasting — Pre-Offer Defect Prediction

## Journalist: Jake Kowalski
## Beat: Construction Technology, tools, practical tech
## Angle: AI tools that predict home inspection problems before you make an offer

## The Problem

### Home Inspection Statistics
- **2.1 million** home inspections performed annually in US (InterNACHI)
- **$4.9 billion** US building inspection market in 2026 (IBISWorld)
- Median inspection cost: **$333** (Gitnux/ASHI data). Range $300-$600 for residential.
- **86%** of inspections uncover problems (AZ Big Media, citing inspection industry data)
- **18%** flag safety issues needing remediation or further evaluation (Gitnux)
- Buyers negotiate an average of **$14,000** off sale price based on inspection findings (AZ Big Media)

### Deal Cancellations
- **17.6%** of home deals fell through in December 2025 — highest outside pandemic (AZ Big Media)
- **13.7%** of deals fell through in February 2026 — record for the month (Redfin, March 2026)
- More than **42,000** US home-sale agreements fell through in February 2026 alone (Redfin)
- Inspection/repair issues rank among leading reasons deals fall through
- NAR Confidence Index: **6%** cancellation rate nationwide (as of recent data)
- Redfin shows **nearly 15%** for some months — gap suggests methodology differences

### Waived Inspections
- **21%** of buyers waived inspection contingency (NAR REALTORS® Confidence Index, September)
- Up from 18% one month earlier and 17% in September 2024
- This is the hidden risk: buyers absorbing unknown defects to win bidding wars

### Common Inspection Problems (frequency)
- Roof issues: **19.7%**
- Electrical problems: **18.7%**
- Window defects: **18.4%**
- Foundation repair average: **$5,165** (range $2,218-$8,111)

## AI Tools

### Alpine Intelligence (Alpine Building Performance, Denver CO)
- **Free** tool, launched March 2026
- ChatGPT-powered predictive analytics
- Founder: Andrew Sams (decade-old inspection company, founded 2016). Co-developer: Mason Minor
- **How it works:** Upload MLS data + additional docs (property disclosures, permit reports). Analyzes era-based risk factors, legacy construction materials, region-specific building trends. Returns system-by-system risk flagging.
- **Example output:** "Pre-1940s home → 87% chance of lead-based paint." "Pre-1930s Denver neighborhoods → brick masonry foundations → mortar deterioration and brick spalling."
- **Limitation:** Especially suited to Colorado Front Range inventory it was trained on. Regional specificity matters.
- **NOT a replacement** for physical inspection — "meant to help the agent set expectations"
- Alpine also uses: drone imaging, infrared scanning, detailed digital reporting

### Hosta a.i. (MIT spin-off)
- Analyzes photos for property assessments
- Produces: precise measurements, floor plans, 3D models, bills of materials
- Evaluates conditions of materials, assesses damage, identifies risks (flammable materials, inadequate sprinkler ratios)
- Working with insurers, contractors, mortgage lenders
- Co-founders: Henriette Fleischmann MBA '19, Rachelle Villalon SM '08, PhD '17
- Quote: "Contractor and insurance companies hardly ever come up with the same estimate."

### homegenius IQ (homegenius Real Estate)
- AI-powered listing photo analysis
- Automatically analyzes property photos to identify and assess condition
- Identifies objects, finishes, and materials impacting consumer value
- Evaluates property condition for more accurate value estimates
- Used for photo-based search matching

## Original Contribution: The $14,000 Surprise Tax

### Calculation
- 2.1M inspections/year × 86% finding problems = 1.806M inspections with issues
- Average negotiation: $14,000 off
- Total annual post-inspection price adjustments: ~$25.3 billion
- Deal cancellation rate: 13.7% (Feb 2026) — roughly 42,000 deals/month falling through
- Each failed deal = sunk costs: inspection ($333), appraisal (~$400), attorney fees, opportunity cost, seller relisting costs
- If AI pre-screening could predict 70% of major issues before offers are made:
  - Buyers adjust initial offers to account for known issues → fewer adversarial renegotiations
  - Buyers avoid bidding on properties with dealbreaker defects → fewer cancellations
  - Agents set expectations early → fewer emotional surprises
  - **But:** 21% of buyers waiving inspections entirely face unknown exposure. AI pre-screening could give them SOME defect intelligence even when skipping the physical inspection.

### The Strongest Counterargument
- AI prediction based on age/location/type is just actuarial tables with a chatbot interface. An experienced agent in a local market already knows "1950s Denver homes have X." The AI adds a veneer of precision to what is fundamentally pattern-matching from building vintages.
- Accuracy claims are untested — no independent validation of prediction accuracy
- Regional training bias: tools trained on Colorado data may not generalize to Gulf Coast, Southwest, or East Coast building stocks
- Risk of false confidence: buyers who get an "AI says it's fine" report may underinvest in physical inspection

---

## Skepticism / Graveyard
- Many AI real estate tools have been more marketing than substance
- No independent accuracy testing published for any of these tools
- The physical inspection still catches property-specific issues AI can't see (hidden plumbing, termite damage behind walls, etc.)
- Professional inspectors may view AI forecasting as either a threat or a glorified lookup table

---

## Kill Test
**Does this help someone building or buying a home?**
YES — directly helps homebuyers by:
1. Identifying likely defects before making an offer (saving time, money, emotional energy)
2. Helping buyers who waive inspections get some defect intelligence
3. Enabling agents to set realistic expectations early
4. Reducing the $14,000 surprise renegotiation cycle

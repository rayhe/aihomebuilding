# Research: AI Floor Plans vs. The Architect's Stamp

## Angle
AI design tools can now generate residential floor plans in minutes at $20/month. NVIDIA just demoed an AI agent doing full architectural design on a local PC chip. But in all 50 states, someone with a license still has to review and stamp those plans. The AIA says architects remain "accountable for all work products... including work produced with AI assistance." The gap between what the AI draws and who signs for it is where the money and the risk live.

## Journalist
Elena Vasquez — Architecture & Design

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — anyone considering using AI tools to design their home needs to know: the generation is cheap, the professional stamp is not, and the liability question is genuinely unsettled.

## Primary Sources

### 1. NVIDIA GTC Taipei 2026 (June 2, 2026)
- Jensen Huang demoed RTX Spark chip running an AI agent that:
  - Takes site selection, concept sketches, mood board
  - Opens Rhino, models terrain, setbacks, building envelope
  - Proposes forms optimized for cost, comfort, quality
  - Generates interior layouts (walls, circulation, rooms)
  - Self-corrects mistakes
  - Exports to Blender, renders photoreal images
- All running locally on a PC (not cloud)
- RTX Spark: 1 petaflop AI performance, available fall 2026
- Notebooks from ASUS, Dell, HP, Lenovo
- "For 40 years, you launched apps. Click. Type. With RTX Spark and Microsoft Windows, you ask — and the PC does the work." — Jensen Huang

### 2. Maket.ai — AI Floor Plan Generator
- Pricing: Free (50 credits), $20/month (300 credits), $30/month (Pro)
- Each floor plan generation uses 20 credits (so $20/mo gets ~15 floor plans)
- "Agentic editing" — describe changes in conversation ("make the kitchen bigger," "add a garage")
- "It really does 70 to 75 percent of the work. And then you get to a point where you meet an architect and you save a fair amount of time and money." — Patrick Murphy, CEO
- Competitor pricing: ArkDesign $180/mo, TestFit custom, Zenerate custom
- Key limitation: "The output is a schematic floor plan, not a construction document."
- Residential only

### 3. AIA Guidance for Responsible AI Use (October 2025)
- "AI is a design support tool — not a replacement for professional judgment."
- "Architects remain accountable for all work products, decisions, and representations made to clients, agencies, and the public — including work produced with AI assistance."
- "All AI-generated outputs must be reviewed and validated by qualified professionals."
- Guidance on value-based pricing: firms should move from time-based billing to outcome-based pricing
- Concern that "the value generated by AI tools benefits architects and their practices, rather than being diverted to clients seeking fee reductions"

### 4. California Business & Professions Code § 5536.25
- A licensed architect who stamps plans is NOT responsible for damage caused by subsequent unauthorized changes
- But signing/stamping does NOT impose a legal duty to observe construction
- The architect who stamps is responsible for the design itself

### 5. UpCodes Plan Review (June 3, 2026)
- AI-native QA/QC that checks drawings against 11 million locally adopted building codes
- Covers 6,000+ jurisdictions
- Discipline-specific: architecture, structural, MEP, fire protection, life safety, accessibility, energy
- Returns issues by severity, linked to drawing page and governing code section
- 800,000 AEC professionals already use UpCodes for code research
- CEO Scott Reynolds: "This transforms compliance review from a one-person bottleneck into a team-wide capability."

### 6. Bilzin Sumberg Legal Analysis — AI Liability in Homebuilding
- "Who bears responsibility?" for AI-generated design errors
- Clickwrap agreements typically cap AI vendor liability at software cost ($10-30/mo)
- If AI hallucinates a six-figure design error, vendor liable for maybe $30
- Contracts must explicitly allocate liability for AI errors
- IP concerns: AI designs draw on training data that may include copyrighted work
- No case law yet establishing liability framework for AI-generated architectural plans

### 7. Versur — Agentic AI for Architectural Design
- Modular AI platform using multiple models (GPT, Flux, Hunyuan3D)
- Can receive plans, run reasoning, output to Revit via API
- Compliance checking via RAG: upload building codes, agents evaluate designs against them
- "Compliance becomes an evaluative layer inside the workflow, not an external checklist."

### 8. Academic Research (Frontiers of Architectural Research, 2026)
- "Controllable generation of building representations" — Zhengyang Wang et al.
- 70.5% accuracy in vertical configuration
- 56 architecture students rated output quality above 4/5 on Likert scale
- Can "quickly revise schemes in response to client feedback during meetings"

## Cost Comparison (Original Contribution)
- AI floor plan generation: $20-30/month (Maket)
- Full residential architectural services: $5,000-$60,000+ depending on project scope
  - Typical custom home: 5-15% of construction cost
  - $500K home × 8% = $40,000 architect fee
  - Stamp-only review (plans prepared by others): $2,000-$5,000
- The AI handles the $20/month generation part
- The professional stamp (review + liability) is the expensive part
- AI doesn't reduce stamp cost — may increase it (architect must verify work they didn't draw)

## Counterarguments
- Architects argue AI-generated plans lack spatial intelligence (circulation flow, sight lines, proportional harmony)
- Many AI floor plans are technically "adequate" but lack the qualitative judgment of experienced designers
- "Stamp mills" already exist — architects who review and stamp plans they didn't draw for lower fees
- Some architects may refuse to stamp AI-generated plans due to liability concerns

## Limitations
- No comprehensive data on how many residential projects use AI-generated floor plans
- AI floor plan accuracy studies are limited to academic contexts (not real construction projects)
- Liability framework is theoretical — no case law exists for AI-generated architectural plans that caused construction defects
- UpCodes Plan Review is brand new (3 days old) — no track record
- Cost savings claims from AI vendors are self-reported

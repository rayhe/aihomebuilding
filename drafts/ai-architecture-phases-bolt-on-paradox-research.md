# Research: 97% of Architecture Firms Still Use Traditional Design Phases — AI Is a Bolt-On, Not a Redesign

**Journalist:** Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)
**Date:** 2026-05-10
**Kill test:** Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — homeowners hiring architects need to understand why AI hasn't lowered design fees despite firms claiming productivity gains. Architects need to confront why their workflow hasn't changed.

## Core Thesis
Architecture firms report massive AI experimentation (64%) and time savings (85%), yet 97% still work within traditional design phases unchanged since AIA standardized them decades ago. AI is being bolted onto a waterfall process designed for hand drafting. The phase structure itself — schematic design (15% fee), design development (20%), construction documents (40%), bidding (5%), construction administration (20%) — hasn't shifted despite supposed productivity gains. If AI really saved 85% of architects meaningful time, you'd expect the fee-phase distribution to change. It hasn't.

## Primary Sources

### 1. AIA/Deltek Architecture Billings Index — March 2026
- Source: https://www.aia.org/resource-center/abi-march-2026-architecture-firm-billings-approach-growth
- Key finding: **97% of firms report that they still work within traditional design phases**
- ABI score 49.8 — essentially flat, closest to 50 since Q1 2023
- Value of newly signed design contracts decreased for **25th consecutive month**
- Backlogs at 6.6 months (highest since Dec 2023) — multifamily residential jumped from 5.4 to 6.2 months
- Inflation 3.3% annual, energy prices spiked 10.9% in March
- Construction employment added 26,000 positions in March

### 2. Chaos/Architizer Global Architecture AI Survey 2026 (4th annual)
- Source: https://blog.chaos.com/the-state-of-ai-in-architecture-survey-insights
- Nearly 800 architects and designers surveyed globally
- 60% from small practices (≤19 people), 27% medium (20-99), 13% large (100+)
- 71% architectural design, 25% interior design
- **64% have experimented with AI tools, but only 20% fully embracing**
- **74% plan to increase AI use in next 12 months**
- 43% say concept/pre-design is where AI adds most value
- **69% only "somewhat" satisfied with AI output**
- 48% cite inconsistent/poor output quality as biggest challenge
- **85% report time savings**, mostly in concept design and image-based workflows
- 70% feel AI visuals reflect their intent "to a reasonable degree"
- AI most effective in early design where speed/iteration matter > precision

### 3. AIA Standard Design Phases (B101-2007 and subsequent)
- Source: https://www.aia.org/resource-center/defining-the-architects-basic-services
- Standard fee breakdown: SD 15%, DD 20%, CD 40%, Bidding 5%, CA 20%
- This structure has remained essentially unchanged for decades
- Originated in pre-digital workflows where each phase produced physical deliverables
- AIA's own schematic design phase checklist (quality management) doesn't mention AI at all

### 4. ArchDaily: What Architects Expect From AI Tools in 2026
- Source: https://www.archdaily.com/1040024/what-architects-expect-from-ai-tools-in-2026
- 64% experimenting daily, 73-93% planning to increase usage
- Time savings but challenges with output quality and integration

## Novel Analysis

### The Bolt-On Paradox
Cross-referencing AIA's 97% with Chaos's 64%/20%:
- 64% of architects experiment with AI
- Only 20% have "fully embraced" it
- **97% haven't changed their phase structure at all**
- This means even the 20% who "fully embraced" AI are running it inside the exact same waterfall

### Fee Distribution as Proof
If AI genuinely transformed architectural design:
- CD phase (40% of fees) should shrink as AI automates documentation
- SD phase (15%) should expand or compress depending on AI's role
- CA phase (20%) would shift as AI enables real-time field comparison
- **None of this has happened.** The fee structure is identical to pre-AI.

### The 25-Month Contract Decline
Design contracts have declined for 25 consecutive months. During this same period:
- AI experimentation rose from ~30% to 64%
- Time savings claims rose to 85%
- Yet contracts still shrinking — AI efficiency isn't translating to more work or lower client costs

### The Small Firm Squeeze
60% of survey respondents are small firms (≤19 people). These firms:
- Can least afford AI tool subscriptions
- Have the most to gain from productivity
- Are most locked into traditional phases because AIA B101 contracts define them
- The contractual structure IS the constraint — not the technology

## Skepticism / Strongest Counterargument
- Traditional phases exist for good reasons: liability boundaries, clear deliverables, payment milestones
- AIA B101 contracts define services by phase — changing phases means rewriting contracts
- Insurance (E&O) is tied to phase-based deliverables
- Some would argue 97% using traditional phases while 64% experiment with AI is HEALTHY — test before transforming
- Counter: but after 4 years of experimentation, "testing" is just stalling

## What's Missing / Limitations
- AIA survey doesn't break down WHICH traditional phases firms use (all 5? modified versions?)
- Chaos survey skews toward visualization-heavy practices (their product sells rendering tools)
- No data on whether AI has actually changed fee percentages at individual firms
- Self-reported "time savings" may be aspirational; no third-party validation
- International variation — US firms may be more conservative than European/Asian

## Actionable Takeaways
For homeowners:
- If your architect claims AI makes them more efficient, ask: did that lower your fee? (Probably not.)
- The phase structure determines your payment schedule — it hasn't changed
- AI-generated concepts in SD phase may LOOK innovative but don't mean the rest of the process is faster

For architects:
- The fee structure is the real bottleneck, not the technology
- AIA contract reform is more impactful than any AI tool
- Small firms should question whether traditional phases serve their clients or just their contracts
- 48% dissatisfaction with AI output means the tools aren't ready to replace phases anyway

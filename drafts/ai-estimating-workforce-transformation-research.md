# Research: AI Estimating Is Rewriting the Construction Estimator's Job Description

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES** — estimating directly affects bid accuracy, project cost, and timeline. Homeowners pay for estimating errors through change orders and budget overruns. Builders who adopt AI estimating can bid more competitively and win more work.

## Core Thesis
225,310 cost estimators work in the US (BLS May 2022). They spend 70% of their time on measurement and data entry. AI takeoff tools cut that 70% to near-zero. The question isn't whether these jobs disappear — it's whether the people in them can pivot to the 30% that requires judgment.

## Original Contribution — Cost-Per-Won-Job Calculation
- BLS median estimator wage: $34.23/hr (residential: $34.99/hr)
- Manual takeoff time per residential bid: 4-8 hours measurement + additional hours for data entry, pricing, proposal
- Total estimate time (manual): ~20-40 hours per complete bid package
- At $34.99/hr: $700-$1,400 in labor per residential bid
- Typical residential bid win rate: 15-25% (industry standard)
- Cost of estimating labor per WON job (manual): $2,800-$9,333
- With AI takeoff (70% of time eliminated): $210-$420 per bid → $840-$2,800 per won job
- Annual savings per estimator: if doing 8-12 bids/month, saving 15-25 hours/bid = 120-300 hours/month recovered
- But: the recovered hours aren't all productive. Estimators trained on measurement aren't necessarily skilled at scope analysis, risk assessment, or client communication.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI takeoff accuracy drops significantly on complex plans, MEP-heavy work, and low-quality drawings. The 90-95% residential accuracy means 5-10% error — on a $500K home, that's $25,000-$50,000 in potential miscounts. An experienced manual estimator at 95-99% accuracy catches things AI misses: unusual site conditions, code quirks, material substitution opportunities. The "time saved" may be illusory if the review phase balloons.

## Limitations
- BLS data is from May 2022 (latest available OES); actual employment may have shifted
- Most accuracy claims come from vendors (Togal, Eano) — limited independent verification
- Win rate data is industry lore, not from a single authoritative study
- The 70/30 time split (busy work vs. strategy) comes from vendor marketing, not academic research
- No longitudinal data on what happens to estimators whose jobs transform — displacement vs. upskilling outcomes are speculative

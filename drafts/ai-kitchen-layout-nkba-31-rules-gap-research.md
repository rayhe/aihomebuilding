# Research: AI Kitchen Layout Tools vs. NKBA's 31 Design Guidelines

## Journalist: Elena Vasquez (Architecture & Design)

## Angle
AI kitchen layout generators (Planner 5D, HomeByMe, Coohom, IKEA Kitchen Planner, etc.) produce visually stunning renders but check zero of the NKBA's 31 kitchen design guidelines. Homeowners use these AI layouts as their "design" and hand them to contractors — who then discover violations during installation. The result: change orders, rework, and kitchens that look great in renders but fail in daily use.

## Kill Test
**YES** — directly helps someone planning a kitchen remodel. Tells them exactly which rules to check before handing an AI layout to a contractor, and what the rework costs if they don't.

## Key Data Points

### NKBA's 31 Kitchen Design Guidelines (specific violations AI misses)
Source: CRD Design Build / NKBA Planning Guidelines 4th Edition

Critical guidelines AI tools typically ignore:
1. **Work triangle**: Sum of distances between cooking, cleanup, and refrigerator ≤ 26 feet. Each leg 4-9 feet. No leg crosses island >12".
2. **Work aisle width**: 42" minimum for one cook, 48" for two cooks (most common violation — AI optimizes counter space, not aisle width)
3. **Sink landing area**: 24" on one side, 18" on the other — AI often places sinks flush against walls or next to fridges
4. **Prep area**: 30" wide × 24" deep continuous countertop adjacent to sink — AI may fragment this
5. **Door interference**: No entry door should interfere with appliance operation; no appliance doors should interfere with each other
6. **Traffic patterns**: No major traffic should cross the work triangle — AI doesn't model human circulation
7. **Dishwasher placement**: Within 36" of cleanup sink — AI treats dishwashers as aesthetic elements
8. **Cooktop landing area**: 12" on one side, 15" on the other (IRC requires 30" clearance above)
9. **Seating clearances**: 32" (no traffic), 36" (slide past), 44" (walk past) behind seated diners
10. **Walkway width**: 36" minimum, 42" if perpendicular walkways intersect

### The Gap
- Chief Architect software (professional tool) has NKBA Auto Dimension feature — consumer AI tools don't
- Professional kitchen designers ($150-$300/hr) check all 31 rules manually
- AI tools check: aesthetics, approximate dimensions, visual balance
- AI tools don't check: work triangle geometry, aisle clearances, landing areas, door swing conflicts, traffic patterns, appliance spacing, code-required ventilation clearances

### Work Triangle History
Source: Wikipedia / NKBA
- Concept dates to Lillian Gilbreth's 1920s efficiency research (formalized 1940s)
- Modern critique: work triangle less relevant for open-plan kitchens with multiple work zones
- But the underlying ergonomic principles (clearances, landing areas, traffic patterns) still critical
- AI tools have thrown out the baby with the bathwater — they skip both the triangle AND the clearance rules

### What STC Ratings Are to Acoustics, NKBA Rules Are to Kitchens
- Just as AI floor plan tools ignore STC ratings for interior walls (we covered this), they ignore NKBA rules for kitchens
- The pattern: AI optimizes what's visible (aesthetics, layout geometry) and ignores what's experiential (workflow, clearances, ergonomics)

## Original Contribution
A checklist analysis: which of the 31 NKBA guidelines can AI kitchen layout tools actually verify? Answer: approximately 3-5 of 31. The remaining 26 require either human judgment, physical measurement context, or ergonomic understanding that no consumer AI tool currently encodes.

## Strongest Counterargument
AI tools democratize design — they get homeowners 80% of the way there for $0, vs. $2,000-$5,000 for a professional kitchen designer. The remaining 20% (code compliance, ergonomics) is the professional's value-add. The problem isn't the AI tool; it's homeowners who mistake the render for the design.

## Limitations
- No systematic study has tested AI kitchen tools against all 31 NKBA guidelines
- Adoption figures are self-reported surveys
- Change order costs are estimates from industry sources, not controlled data
- Professional kitchen design costs vary significantly by market

# Research: AI Aerial Imagery Insurance Scoring — The New Closing Risk for Builders

## Angle
Insurance companies are using AI-powered satellite and aerial imagery to score properties for underwriting — sometimes dropping coverage or refusing to renew without ever visiting the home. For builders, this creates a new closing risk: if your buyer can't get insured, the deal dies. For buyers, an algorithm they never consented to may be setting their premium from space.

**Journalist:** Catherine "Code" Chen (policy/regulation beat)
**Kill test:** Yes — this directly affects anyone building or buying a home. If insurance falls through, the closing falls through.

## Primary Sources

### Company/Technology Data
1. **CAPE Analytics** — AI Roof Condition Rating (RCR) using geospatial data. 34% of all homeowner claims linked to wind/hail damage (CAPE research). Integrated into major carrier underwriting systems.
2. **Nearmap + Zurich North America** — AI-enhanced aerial imagery integration for property underwriting. Nearmap provides roof scoring directly into Zurich's underwriting workflow via Betterview platform. Measurable improvement in risk classification accuracy during pilot.
3. **EagleView** — "EagleView Horizon" AI engine built on 25+ years of verified property data. Drone-based roof claims processing (EagleView Assess). Hastings Mutual case study: eliminated ladder-assist costs, improved adjuster safety.
4. **Vexcel** — High-resolution aerial imagery + AI property attributes. "Gray Sky" program captures disaster damage imagery for rapid response.
5. **Intelligent AI + Guidewire** — Integration of property risk data directly into insurer workflows, reducing underinsurance.
6. **Stand Insurance** — California startup using AI for real-time risk reassessment, aiming to expand coverage to high-risk areas.

### Regulatory/Legal
7. **California AB 75** (2025-2026): Requires 30-day advance notice before aerial imagery inspections, mandates insurers share images with homeowners upon request. Supported by California Insurance Commissioner Ricardo Lara and CA Association of Realtors. Passed Senate July 2025.
8. **California AB 1559**: Requires insurers to disclose specific aerial images used in underwriting decisions. Sponsored by Assemblymember Lisa Calderon.
9. **Pennsylvania** — 60-day notice period for aerial imaging use in insurance decisions.
10. **Troutman Pepper Locke analysis** — Emerging regulatory themes: quality, transparency, validation. Insurers must distinguish material risks from cosmetic flaws. Consumer dispute mechanisms required when adverse actions based on imagery.

### Impact/Statistics
11. **Texas investigations:** At least a dozen homeowner cases since 2023 of coverage dropped/non-renewed based on aerial imagery. Homeowners report inaccurate assessments — minor issues like roof age triggering cancellation.
12. **Florida non-renewal rates:** Up to 279% increase (Insurify).
13. **US Treasury report:** ZIP codes in high-risk areas pay 82% more in premiums. Premium increases outpacing inflation by 8.7%.
14. **Insurify 2026 projection:** Home insurance rates to rise 4% to avg $3,057/yr. Cumulative 40.4% increase over 6 years (LendingTree). Louisiana saw 58% increase.
15. **1 in 6 homeowners** in most climate-vulnerable states now skip insurance entirely (Insurify).
16. **Brookings/Treasury data:** ZIP code-level nonrenewal analysis showing concentrated impacts in FL, CA, SC.
17. **Kiplinger report:** California homeowners losing insurance over drone/satellite photos, with disputes over whether images reflect actual conditions.

### Builder Impact
18. If buyer can't get insurance, mortgage lender won't fund → closing fails.
19. New construction SHOULD score well (new roof, new systems), but AI may flag:
    - Tree proximity to structure
    - Lot grading/drainage patterns
    - Neighboring property conditions
    - Wildfire zone classification
    - Materials classification errors (metal roof scored as damaged asphalt)
20. Some production builders (DR Horton, Lennar) now pre-coordinate with insurers during planning.

## Original Analysis Opportunity
Calculate: What does a 30-day insurance delay cost a builder? (Carrying costs on a $650K home: ~$2,000-$3,500/month in financing, taxes, insurance, maintenance. A single delayed closing from an AI flag = $2K-4K direct cost.)

## Strongest Counterargument
AI aerial scoring actually HELPS new construction. A brand-new roof scores well. The real victims are owners of older homes with aging roofs. Builders should be the beneficiaries, not the casualties. Counter: the AI doesn't just score roofs — it scores the entire property context (vegetation, exposure, neighboring structures), and new homes in wildfire/hurricane zones still get flagged.

## Limitations
- No public data on how many closings have failed specifically due to AI aerial scoring
- CAPE/Nearmap/EagleView don't publish false positive rates
- Most data is from Texas and California — may not generalize to other states
- AB 75 passed Senate but final implementation details unclear
- Stand Insurance's model is too new to evaluate outcomes

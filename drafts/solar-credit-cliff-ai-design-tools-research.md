# Research: Federal Solar Credit Cliff — How AI Design Tools Got the Math Wrong Overnight

**Journalist:** Priya Greenwood (sustainability, green building, evidence-based)
**Date:** May 21, 2026
**Slug:** solar-credit-cliff-ai-design-tools

## Angle
The 30% federal residential solar tax credit (Section 25D) was killed by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act, signed July 4, 2025. Credits expire June 30, 2026 — 40 days away. Every AI solar design tool (Aurora Solar, Enphase, SolarEdge platforms) that generates ROI projections for homeowners was calibrated around the 30% ITC. Those projections are now $9,000+ wrong. For a homeowner building or buying a home right now, the decision calculus has fundamentally shifted, and the tools haven't caught up.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? **YES.** This directly affects the economics of every new home being designed with solar. A homeowner using AI design tools to evaluate solar is getting financially misleading projections if the tool hasn't updated its tax incentive models.

## Primary Sources

### 1. One Big Beautiful Bill Act (H.R. 1, 119th Congress)
- **Signed:** July 4, 2025 by Trump
- **Key provision:** Repeals Section 25D Residential Clean Energy Credit (30% ITC for residential solar PV, energy storage, other renewables)
- **Also eliminates:** Tax benefits for leased solar systems (PPAs, leases)
- **Phase-out:** Clean energy credits expire June 30, 2026
- **Source:** Wikipedia (OBBBA entry), Congress.gov, Sol-Ark analysis, Free Press (May 10, 2026)
- **Cost to deficit:** $2.8 trillion increase
- **Senate passed with bipartisan opposition to clean energy cuts, but law signed anyway**

### 2. Solar Installation Cost Data (2026)
- **Average installation cost:** $30,505 before incentives (EnergySage, 2026)
- **ConsumerAffairs estimate:** $13,962–$27,924 range
- **With 30% ITC:** ~$21,354 net cost
- **Without ITC:** Full $30,505 out of pocket
- **Delta:** $9,152 per average installation
- **25-year savings:** $37,000–$154,000 depending on location/rates (EnergySage)
- **Payback shift:** ~3.8 additional years at $2,400/year savings

### 3. Industry Impact Projections
- **Wood Mackenzie:** 40% decline in residential solar installations projected if ITC removed
- **SEIA:** 1% decrease in solar adoption = ~14,000 job losses
- **250,000+ people** employed in residential solar workforce
- **Solar captured 91% of new U.S. grid capacity in 2025** (ACP Annual Market Report)
- **S&P Global:** Solar, wind, storage = 93% of new power capacity in 2026

### 4. State Response — Scramble to Fill the Gap
- **Colorado:** HB26-1007 signed May 7, 2026 — enables plug-in solar, removes HOA restrictions, effective Jan 1, 2027
- **Connecticut:** HB 5340 — extends renewable incentives, streamlines permitting, authorizes plug-in solar
- **Section 25C still alive:** Home energy retrofit credits (30% up to $3,200/year) for envelope, mechanical, electrical upgrades still active
- **State programs vary widely:** Colorado tax exemptions, New York 25% state credit, Silicon Valley Clean Energy rebates ($6,700 for heat pumps)
- **But no state can fully replace 30% of $30,505**

### 5. Restoration Efforts
- **American Energy Dominance Act:** Republican House bill to restore wind/solar credits curtailed by OBBBA
- **Political reality:** Unlikely to pass this Congress; anticipated if Democrats take House/Senate (Free Press, May 10, 2026)
- **Bipartisan support for clean energy** exists, especially in energy-exporting states

### 6. Perovskite Panel Breakthrough (Timing Problem)
- **Tandem PV (Fremont, CA):** First mass production of perovskite-silicon tandem panels
- **65,000 sq ft facility, up to 40 MW annual capacity**
- **Theoretical efficiency:** ~45% for tandem vs ~30% for silicon alone
- **Durability progress:** <1% annual power loss (10x improvement year-over-year)
- **Targeting 25+ year performance** consistent with industry warranties
- **Reality check:** Still at demonstration scale. Residential availability is 2-3 years out minimum.
- **Also in race:** Oxford PV, Swift Solar

### 7. Heat Pump and Electrification Context
- **RMI study:** Heat pumps reduce bills up to $1,530/year for single-family homes
- **60 million+ U.S. homes** still use electric resistance heating
- **Section 25C credits still available** for heat pump installations ($2,000 cap) — but this is also phasing
- **Bay Area homeowner case study:** 20-25% winter bill reduction with heat pump (Gree Flexx Ultra)

## Strongest Counterargument
Solar panel costs have dropped 90% since 2010. Even without the federal credit, solar may still make financial sense in high-electricity-cost markets. The ITC was always meant to be temporary, and the industry needs to stand on its own. States with serious clean energy commitments (CA, NY, CT, CO) are already backfilling. The real bottleneck isn't incentives — it's interconnection queues and grid infrastructure.

## Limitations
- Payback calculations use national averages; actual savings depend on roof orientation, shading, local utility rate structures, net metering availability
- State incentive stacking evolves monthly; some programs may have exhausted funding
- Perovskite commercial timeline is speculative; manufacturer claims are pre-verification
- The political situation is fluid — credits could be restored by future legislation
- Leased/PPA solar still gets some tax treatment through 48E commercial credit (through 2027), making the picture less binary than "credit gone = solar dead"

# Research: AI Aerial Insurance Scoring & New Construction

## Angle
Insurance companies now use AI-powered aerial/satellite imagery to score roofs and property conditions from above. Algorithms from Cape Analytics (acquired by Moody's), Nearmap, and EagleView generate 80+ property insights instantly — roof condition, age, defensible space, hazard indicators. These scores influence underwriting, pricing, and non-renewal decisions. For someone building a new home, every construction choice (roof material, landscaping, solar panel placement) directly affects what the insurance AI will "see" from space.

## Kill Test
Does this help someone building or buying a home? YES — understanding what insurance AI scores means you can make construction decisions that save thousands per year in premiums and avoid non-renewal.

## Novel Contribution
Calculate the cost difference between homes that score well vs poorly on aerial AI assessment. Connect:
- IBHS Fortified savings (up to 55% off wind premiums in Alabama)
- Class 4 impact-resistant roofing discounts
- New construction premium advantage (33% less than 20-year homes)
- Cost of non-renewal (being forced to a more expensive insurer)
Cross-reference against what aerial AI actually scores to identify which construction choices have the highest insurance ROI.

## Strongest Counterargument
Aerial AI scoring, despite its flaws, has made insurance more accurately priced overall. Before it, insurers relied on self-reported data (which homeowners often got wrong) or expensive in-person inspections (which happened rarely). The technology catches genuine hazards — a tree limb over the roof, missing shingles, clogged gutters — that would have gone unnoticed until they caused a claim. The false positive problem is real but arguably a lesser evil than the alternative: pricing based on no property-specific data at all.

## Limitations
- Cape Analytics does not publicly disclose false positive/negative rates for roof condition scoring
- IRC survey is industry-funded (Insurance Research Council)
- IBHS Fortified discount data is concentrated in coastal/hurricane-prone states; inland savings are less dramatic
- State regulatory responses vary widely; some have no aerial imagery guidance at all
- We lack systematic data on how many non-renewals are driven specifically by aerial AI vs other factors
